**This Lead Generation Service Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of the date of the last signature below ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Provider:**
Second Chair LLC, Los Angeles, CA ("Second Chair" or "Provider")

**Client:**
West Coast Trial Lawyers, Contact: Robert Davtyan ("WCTL" or "Client")

Each individually a "Party" and collectively the "Parties."

## 1. Scope of Services

1.1 **Services.** Provider shall generate and deliver personal injury ("PI") leads to Client through paid digital advertising campaigns, proprietary qualification funnels, and automated verification systems.

1.2 **Territory.** All lead generation activities under this Agreement shall target the state of Arizona, including the Phoenix and Tucson designated market areas ("Territory").

1.3 **Case Types.** Provider shall deliver leads for the following non-soft-tissue case types only:

- Moderate-to-severe motor vehicle accidents (MVA)
- Trucking and commercial vehicle accidents
- Motorcycle accidents
- Pedestrian and bicycle accidents
- DUI/impaired driver victim claims
- Rideshare (Uber/Lyft) accidents
- Wrongful death

1.4 **Exclusions.** Provider shall not deliver leads for:

- Soft tissue injuries (whiplash only, minor sprains, minor bruising)
- Parking lot accidents
- Minor fender-benders
- Property-damage-only incidents

## 2. Territory Exclusivity

2.1 **Exclusive Territory.** For the duration of this Agreement, Provider shall not sell, license, or deliver Arizona PI leads to any competing personal injury law firm or legal services provider.

2.2 **Scope of Exclusivity.** Exclusivity applies to the state of Arizona only. Provider retains the right to operate in all other states and territories without restriction.

2.3 **Termination of Exclusivity.** Territorial exclusivity terminates automatically upon termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## 3. Term

3.1 **Pilot Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until the earlier of: (a) delivery of all leads purchased under this Agreement, or (b) ninety (90) calendar days from the Effective Date ("Pilot Term").

3.2 **Renewal.** Upon satisfactory completion of the Pilot Term, the Parties may negotiate a renewal or expanded engagement under a separate agreement or amendment.

## 4. Payment Terms

4.1 **Pilot Fee.** Client shall pay Provider a total of **Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00)** ("Pilot Fee"), prepaid in full upon execution of this Agreement.

4.2 **Cost Per Lead.** The Pilot Fee is based on a cost of **Four Hundred Seventy-Five Dollars ($475.00) per qualified lead** delivered.

4.3 **Estimated Lead Volume.** Provider estimates delivery of approximately twenty-one (21) qualified leads under this Agreement. This is an estimate and not a guarantee. Actual lead volume may vary based on advertising performance, platform optimization, and market conditions.

4.4 **Payment Method.** Provider prefers payment via direct bank deposit (ACH/wire). Payment by credit card is accepted with an additional processing fee of three percent (3%) of the total amount charged.

4.5 **Payment Due.** The Pilot Fee is due within seven (7) business days of the date of invoice. Creative development and campaign launch will not commence until payment is received in full.

## 5. Lead Specifications

5.1 **Qualification Criteria.** Each lead delivered to Client shall have passed through the following qualification process:

(a) **Accident Verification** — Confirmed involvement in a qualifying accident within the past eighteen (18) months.

(b) **Fault Determination** — Lead indicates they are not the at-fault party, or were involved in a shared-fault scenario where they are not the primary at-fault party.

(c) **Accident Type** — Accident falls within the case types enumerated in Section 1.3.

(d) **Injury Severity** — Lead indicates non-soft-tissue injuries including but not limited to: broken bones, surgical intervention, hospitalization, herniated or bulging disc, traumatic brain injury, concussion, lacerations requiring medical treatment, burns, or comparable injuries.

(e) **No Existing Representation** — Lead confirms they do not currently have an attorney for the accident in question.

(f) **Contact Validation** — Lead's phone number has been verified through automated verification APIs prior to delivery.

(g) **TCPA Consent** — Lead has provided one-to-one consent explicitly naming West Coast Trial Lawyers via a TrustedForm-certified consent mechanism.

(h) **Fraud Detection** — Lead has passed automated bot and fraud detection screening.

5.2 **Delivery Method.** Leads shall be delivered via email to a designated email address provided by Client. Each lead shall include: full name, phone number, email address (if available), accident type, injury description, fault status, accident date, and TCPA consent proof.

5.3 **Delivery Timeline.** Provider estimates delivery of all leads within approximately four to six (4-6) weeks of campaign launch.

## 6. Lead Credit Policy

6.1 **Eligible Credits.** A delivered lead qualifies for a credit if any of the following conditions are confirmed:

(a) **At-Fault Lead** — The lead is determined to be the at-fault party as confirmed by police report or Client's intake discovery.

(b) **Soft Tissue Only** — The lead's injuries are determined to be exclusively soft tissue, despite having passed Provider's injury severity screening.

(c) **Already Represented** — The lead has existing legal representation for the accident in question, despite having indicated otherwise during the qualification process.

(d) **Outside Recency Window** — The lead's accident occurred more than eighteen (18) months prior to the date of delivery.

(e) **Outside Territory** — The lead's accident did not occur in Arizona and the lead does not reside in Arizona.

6.2 **Credit Mechanism.** Credits shall be issued as additional replacement leads at no charge. Credits are not redeemable as cash refunds.

6.3 **Dispute Process.** Client must submit a credit request within fourteen (14) calendar days of lead delivery. Provider shall review and respond within forty-eight (48) hours.

## 7. Creative Approval

7.1 **Pre-Launch Review.** Provider shall submit all advertising creative to Client for review and approval prior to campaign launch.

7.2 **Approval Timeline.** Client shall approve or provide written feedback within three (3) business days of receipt. Failure to respond within this period shall be deemed approval.

7.3 **Creative Ownership.** Provider retains ownership of all creative materials, campaign structures, funnel designs, and proprietary systems developed in connection with this Agreement.

## 8. Compliance

8.1 **Provider Responsibilities.** Provider shall comply with all applicable advertising laws, TCPA requirements, Meta platform policies, and Arizona Rules of Professional Conduct applicable to legal advertising.

8.2 **Client Responsibilities.** Client shall provide accurate firm information, maintain responsive intake operations, and comply with all applicable laws governing the practice of law in Arizona.

## 9. Confidentiality

9.1 **Confidential Information.** Each Party acknowledges that it may receive confidential information from the other Party. Neither Party shall disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent.

9.2 **Survival.** The obligations of this Section shall survive termination for a period of two (2) years.

## 10. Limitation of Liability

10.1 **No Outcome Guarantees.** Provider makes no guarantee regarding Client's conversion rates, signed case volume, or revenue generated from delivered leads.

10.2 **Liability Cap.** In no event shall either Party's total aggregate liability exceed the total amount paid by Client under this Agreement ($10,000.00).

## 11. Termination

11.1 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate upon seven (7) calendar days' written notice.

11.2 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Provider shall cease all advertising activities, Client shall pay for all delivered leads, and any unused portion of the Pilot Fee shall be refunded pro-rata.
