## 1. SCOPE OF SERVICES

1.1 **Services.** Provider shall generate and deliver personal injury ("PI") leads to Client through paid digital advertising campaigns, proprietary qualification funnels, and automated verification systems.

1.2 **Territory.** All lead generation activities under this Agreement shall target the state of Arizona, including the Phoenix and Tucson designated market areas ("Territory").

1.3 **Case Types.** Provider shall deliver leads for the following non-soft-tissue case types only:
- Moderate-to-severe motor vehicle accidents (MVA)
- Trucking and commercial vehicle accidents
- Motorcycle accidents
- Pedestrian and bicycle accidents
- Rideshare (Uber/Lyft) accidents
- Wrongful death

1.4 **Exclusions.** Provider shall not deliver leads for:
- Soft tissue injuries (whiplash only, minor sprains, minor bruising)
- Parking lot accidents
- Minor fender-benders
- Property-damage-only incidents

## 2. TERRITORY EXCLUSIVITY

2.1 **Exclusive Territory.** For the duration of this Agreement, Provider shall not sell, license, or deliver Arizona PI leads to any competing personal injury law firm or legal services provider.

2.2 **Scope of Exclusivity.** Exclusivity applies to the state of Arizona only. Provider retains the right to operate in all other states and territories without restriction.

2.3 **Termination of Exclusivity.** Territorial exclusivity terminates automatically upon termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## 3. TERM

3.1 **Pilot Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until the earlier of: (a) delivery of all leads purchased under this Agreement, or (b) ninety (90) calendar days from the Effective Date ("Pilot Term").

3.2 **Renewal.** Upon satisfactory completion of the Pilot Term, the Parties may negotiate a renewal or expanded engagement under a separate agreement or amendment.

## 4. PAYMENT TERMS

4.1 **Pilot Fee.** Client shall pay Provider a total of **Nine Thousand Nine Hundred Seventy-Five Dollars ($9,975.00)** ("Pilot Fee"), prepaid in full upon execution of this Agreement.

4.2 **Cost Per Lead.** The Pilot Fee is based on a cost of **Four Hundred Seventy-Five Dollars ($475.00) per qualified lead** delivered.

4.3 **Lead Volume.** Provider shall deliver twenty-one (21) qualified leads to Client under this Agreement.

4.4 **Payment Method.** Provider prefers payment via direct bank deposit (ACH/wire). Payment by credit card is accepted with an additional processing fee of three percent (3%) of the total amount charged.

4.5 **Invoicing.** Upon execution of this Agreement by both Parties, Provider shall issue an invoice for the Pilot Fee with payment instructions. The Pilot Fee is due within ten (10) business days of the date of invoice. Creative development and campaign launch will not commence until payment is received in full.

## 5. LEAD SPECIFICATIONS

5.1 **Qualification Criteria.** Each lead delivered to Client shall have passed through the following qualification process:

  (a) **Accident Verification** — Confirmed involvement in a qualifying accident within the past twenty-four (24) months.

  (b) **Fault Determination** — Lead indicates they are not the at-fault party, or were involved in a shared-fault scenario where they are not the primary at-fault party.

  (c) **Accident Type** — Accident falls within the case types enumerated in Section 1.3.

  (d) **Injury Severity** — Lead indicates non-soft-tissue injuries including but not limited to: broken bones, surgical intervention, hospitalization, herniated or bulging disc, traumatic brain injury, concussion, lacerations requiring medical treatment, burns, or comparable injuries.

  (e) **No Existing Representation** — Lead confirms they do not currently have an attorney for the accident in question.

  (f) **TCPA Consent** — Lead has provided one-to-one consent explicitly naming West Coast Trial Lawyers via a TrustedForm-certified consent mechanism.

  (g) **Fraud Detection** — Lead has passed automated bot and fraud detection screening.

5.2 **Delivery Method.** Leads shall be delivered via email to a designated email address provided by Client. Each lead shall include: full name, phone number, email address (if available), accident type, injury description, fault status, accident date, and TCPA consent proof.

5.3 **Delivery Timeline.** Provider estimates delivery of all leads within approximately four to six (4-6) weeks of campaign launch. This timeline is an estimate and may vary based on advertising platform approval timelines, creative optimization pace, and market conditions. Campaign launch occurs after creative approval (see Section 7).

---

## 6. LEAD CREDIT POLICY

6.1 **Eligible Credits.** A delivered lead qualifies for a credit if any of the following conditions are confirmed:

  (a) **At-Fault Lead** — The lead is determined to be the at-fault party as confirmed by police report or Client's intake discovery.

  (b) **Soft Tissue Only** — The lead's injuries are determined to be exclusively soft tissue, despite having passed Provider's injury severity screening.

  (c) **Already Represented** — The lead has existing legal representation for the accident in question, despite having indicated otherwise during the qualification process.

  (d) **Outside Recency Window** — The lead's accident occurred more than twenty-four (24) months prior to the date of delivery.

  (e) **Outside Territory** — The lead's accident did not occur in Arizona and the lead does not reside in Arizona.

6.2 **Credit Mechanism.** Credits shall be issued as additional replacement leads at no charge. Credits are not redeemable as cash refunds.

6.3 **Dispute Process.** Client must submit a credit request within fourteen (14) calendar days of lead delivery, specifying the lead and the applicable condition from Section 6.1. Provider shall review and respond within forty-eight (48) hours.

6.4 **Non-Refundable Conditions.** Leads are not eligible for credit in the following circumstances:
- Client's intake team was unable to contact the lead, provided contact information was valid at time of delivery
- Lead decided not to proceed after initial contact with Client
- Lead retained a different law firm after delivery to Client
- Case value is lower than Client expected, provided injuries are non-soft-tissue
- Lead did not respond within Client's follow-up window, provided contact information was valid at time of delivery

## 7. CREATIVE APPROVAL

7.1 **Pre-Launch Review.** Provider shall submit all advertising creative (including ad copy, images, video, landing pages, quiz funnel flow, and TCPA consent language) to Client for review and approval prior to campaign launch.

7.2 **Approval Timeline.** Client shall approve or provide written feedback on submitted creative within five (5) business days of receipt. Failure to respond within this period shall be deemed approval.

7.3 **Revisions.** If Client requests changes, Provider shall make reasonable revisions and resubmit within three (3) business days. Delayed approval by Client extends the estimated delivery timeline accordingly.

7.4 **Creative Ownership.** Provider retains ownership of all creative materials, campaign structures, funnel designs, and proprietary systems developed in connection with this Agreement. Client is granted a non-exclusive license to use approved creative solely in connection with the services provided under this Agreement.

7.5 **Ongoing Approval.** Any new creative developed after campaign launch shall be submitted to Client for approval before going live, following the same process described in this Section.

## 8. COMPLIANCE

8.1 **Provider Responsibilities.** Provider shall:
- Obtain TCPA-compliant consent explicitly naming Client on all leads
- Comply with Meta platform advertising policies, including Special Ad Category requirements
- Comply with Arizona Rules of Professional Conduct applicable to legal advertising (ER 7.1, 7.3, 7.5)
- Maintain automated bot detection and fraud filtering systems
- Maintain TrustedForm certificates as evidence of consent for all delivered leads
- Use third-person language in all advertising creative as required by platform policies

8.2 **Client Responsibilities.** Client shall:
- Provide accurate firm information for use in advertising and consent language
- Maintain responsive intake operations to contact delivered leads in a timely manner
- Provide timely feedback on lead quality and status to support campaign optimization
- Comply with all applicable laws and ethical rules governing the practice of law in Arizona

8.3 **Mutual Obligations.** Neither Party shall engage in misleading, deceptive, or non-compliant advertising in connection with this Agreement.

14.3 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination:
- Provider shall cease all advertising activities in the Territory
