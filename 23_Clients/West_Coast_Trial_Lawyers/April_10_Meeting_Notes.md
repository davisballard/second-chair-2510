## Decisions Made

### Pilot Test Budget: $10K

- **Decision:** Robert proposed a $10K pilot test (not the standard 100-lead buy)
- **Made by:** Robert
- **Rationale:** $5K-$10K is WCTL's standard test range for new lead vendors. At $10K, they need 3-4 sign-worthy leads to consider it successful.
- **Implication:** Second Chair must work within a tighter budget than their typical 100-lead onboarding. Davis acknowledged results will be suboptimal during ramp-up due to pixel learning.

### Target Market: Washington State (Tentative)

- **Decision:** Washington is the likely target DMA for the pilot
- **Made by:** Davis (proposed), Robert (supported)
- **Rationale:** Robert indicated Washington and Arizona are performing better than California for WCTL right now. Davis felt Washington offered the strongest market fit given Second Chair's capabilities and cost structure.
- **Implication:** Second Chair will need to build Washington-specific creative and funnel. Davis emphasized campaigns must be localized, not one-size-fits-all.

### Lead Delivery Format: Email First, Then CRM Integration

- **Decision:** Start with email delivery, upgrade to Salesforce integration if pilot succeeds
- **Made by:** Robert
- **Rationale:** Robert wants to keep it simple for the test. If CPA hits target, he'll integrate into Salesforce and set up 12-24 hour status reporting back to Second Chair.
- **Implication:** Second Chair's firm portal is available but not needed initially. Webhook/CRM integration is Phase 2.

### No Live Transfers Initially

- **Decision:** Standard lead delivery, not live transfers
- **Made by:** Robert
- **Rationale:** WCTL has a solid speed-to-contact ratio with regular leads. Their intake team operates \~8AM to midnight. Robert prefers concentrating efforts in high-conversion windows rather than 24/7 live transfer.
- **Implication:** Second Chair's live transfer and hybrid intake model ideas are parked for now. Could revisit in Phase 2.
