## The finding (in one paragraph)

Meta's Special Ad Category framework formally restricts targeting for advertisers running ads about: **Housing, Employment, Credit (HEC)**, plus **Social Issues, Elections, or Politics**. Personal injury law firm services advertising — i.e., a PI firm advertising "we represent accident victims" / "free case review" / "see what your case is worth" — is **not on Meta's official Special Ad Category list.** Such ads run in the *standard* category with full access to demographic targeting, detailed interests, lookalikes, ZIP-level geo, age and gender targeting. PI law firm ads do receive *enhanced manual review scrutiny* (graphic-injury / "shock-scare-disgust" rejection risk is high), but that's regular Meta ad-policy review, not Special Ad Category restrictions. **Source:** [Meta Business — Special Ad Category guide](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/298000447747885); [Data Axle — 2025 Meta Special Ad Categories Rules](https://www.data-axle.com/resources/blog/meta-special-ad-categories-rules/).

## Where it gets blurry

### Mass tort plaintiff recruitment IS different

Ads recruiting plaintiffs for specific litigation ("Were you injured by [drug/device]?" / "Have you been affected by [class action]?") have historically tripped Meta's Social Issues review and been pulled. Most-recent reference event: **April 2026, Meta pulled ads recruiting plaintiffs for social media addiction lawsuits**. Reference: [MoLawyers Media — Meta pulled SM addiction lawsuit ads](https://molawyersmedia.com/2026/04/10/meta-pulls-ads-recruiting-plaintiffs-social-media-addiction-lawsuits/).

WCTL's Phase 1 creative is **PI services advertising, not mass tort plaintiff recruitment**. The hooks are general PI-vertical ("Hit by a semi on I-10?", "Hit while crossing the street?") — not specific to a litigation, drug, device, or class action. Under Meta's documented policy, this should not require Social Issues classification.

### Some practitioners self-categorize defensively

Some PI advertisers self-classify as Social Issues / Special Ad Category as a *defensive* posture — to avoid potential mid-campaign delivery pauses if Meta retroactively flags ads as Social Issues. Trade-off:
- **Self-classify as SAC:** lose detailed targeting, lookalikes, ZIP-level geo, age/gender — but eliminate retroactive-flag risk
- **Standard category:** full targeting unlocks — but operational risk if Meta flags during campaign run

Davis's verbatim from the scope discussion: *"I feel like its gotta be special ad category it was when I ran it before."* — suggests the prior decision was made on operational/defensive grounds, not Meta-required grounds. **This is the exact question worth re-examining.**

## AZ Bar interactions (independent of Meta)

ER 7.1 / 7.2 / 7.3 are AZ State Bar requirements that apply regardless of Meta classification:
- **ER 7.1:** No false/misleading claims; can't guarantee results
- **ER 7.2:** Every ad must include name + office address of at least one responsible lawyer/firm
- **ER 7.3:** Live person-to-person solicitation prohibited (cold-calling known-need individuals — Meta retargeting / Custom Audiences may interact with this if the audience is identified by recent injury status)

**Re-categorizing on Meta does not change AZ bar requirements.** All current compliance discipline (third-person rules, "results not guaranteed" disclaimers, responsible firm name + address in every ad) continues to apply.

Source: [AZ Bar — ER 7.1 / 7.3](https://www.azbar.org/for-legal-professionals/ethics/best-practices/er-7-1-communications-concerning-a-lawyer-s-services-and-er-7-3-solicitation-of-new-clients/); [Lab Coat Marketing — AZ Attorney Advertising Guide](https://www.labcoatmarketing.com/insights/an-attorneys-guide-to-advertising-regulations-in-arizona).

## Risks of re-categorizing to standard

1. **Mid-campaign retroactive flag.** If Meta's automated review system later classifies an ad as Social Issues despite advertiser self-classification as standard, the ad may be paused without warning. Operational risk for active campaigns.
2. **Inconsistency with creative content.** If creative drifts toward language Meta interprets as "social issue" (e.g., systemic injustice frames, anti-corporate rhetoric, identity-grievance framing), retroactive flag risk increases. WCTL's existing register avoids this — empathy-driven, not anti-corporate-grievance — but Phase 2 register expansion (especially J2 Bold Sister-Line wild swings if any get flagged) could shift that.
3. **Litigation-defensive posture.** Some firms maintain SAC classification to argue "we held ourselves to the more restrictive standard" in TCPA / consumer-protection litigation defense. Whether this is meaningful litigation defense is a question for WCTL counsel, not the agency.
4. **Robert's prior decision.** If Robert specifically requested SAC classification for reasons WCTL counsel hasn't documented to the agency, that decision should be preserved until the rationale is examined.

1. **Read Meta's official SAC documentation** in full: [Special Ad Category guide](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/298000447747885). Look for explicit mention of "personal injury", "law firm services", or "legal services advertising" in the SAC criteria. The current documentation does NOT include these.

2. **Review Meta's Social Issues definition** specifically: [Social Issues, Elections, Politics policy](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/167836590566506). The definition centers on "ads that advocate for or against issues of public importance, intended to influence public opinion." PI services advertising does not match this definition.

3. **Investigate mass tort vs PI distinction in Meta enforcement history.** Look for cases where Meta pulled standard PI services ads (not plaintiff recruitment). The April 2026 SM addiction case is plaintiff recruitment; not the same.

4. **Compare peer AZ PI firms.** Lerner & Rowe, Phillips Law Group, Sweet James, Sokolove run Meta ads. Use Meta Ad Library to inspect — are they showing demographic targeting that would only be available outside SAC? (Meta Ad Library shows audience details only for Social Issues ads; absence of audience disclosure is consistent with standard classification.)
   - [Meta Ad Library](https://www.facebook.com/ads/library) — search by firm name

5. **Check WCTL's existing classification rationale.** Why was SAC chosen previously? Was it documented? Was it counsel-directed or operational-default? This is the most important question for Davis to answer.

6. **Review ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct** on attorney advertising. The Model Rules don't speak to Meta classification but provide context on ethical standards for PI services marketing.
