## Decisions made

### 1. Motorcycle crash imagery — GREEN on Ty only, with three guardrails (Saul)

Prior "no crash" position was a **brand-discipline choice**, not an AZ legal mandate. AZ ER 7.1 permits reenactments with disclosure. Meta SAC permits implied-impact without gore or victim focus.

**Saul's three guardrails:**
1. **Ty only.** Sarah, Diana, Laura get zero crash-adjacent imagery — their personas are identity-loss, system-loss, place-loss, and crash footage kills the creative.
2. **Pattern B (Moment-Before) or Pattern C (Sound-Suggestion) only.** Never show impact or injury. The cut-to-black *is* the crash.
3. **Arizona-licensed counsel signs off separately** — route the dramatization variant to WCTL counsel flagged for heightened review, not bundled into the standard package sweep.

One live quote, Saul:
> *"Davis. You're right, and the previous call was a brand-discipline choice dressed up as a legal one. Let me put the guardrails on it clean... GREEN with three guardrails."*

### 2. Package structure — 24 hook units, 1 primary + 3-4 variants per concept (Julian)

Julian's package discipline:
- Each of 6 concepts gets: locked primary + 3-4 variants tuned to the same formula beat
- Each unit gets: hook, formula breakdown (Hook/Tension/Turn/CTA timed), visual direction, rationale, production tier, compliance checkpoint
- Ratio: 1 locked + 3 variants × 6 concepts = **24 creative units in the approval package** (not all produced for launch — see Nigel's correction below)

Julian's filter rule, verbatim:
> *"Every variant must pass the three-word gate. First three words either self-qualify (identity, severity, situation) or repel the wrong person. If a variant hook gets to word four without filtering, it's not a variant — it's a different ad."*

### 3. UGC / Elevated A/B test inside the ad set (Barry)

Barry's discipline on UGC register:
- Diana ×4 all UGC-Native (locked)
- Add Ty M2-V1 UGC version (dual register on "Bills don't stop because you can't work")
- Add Sarah S1-V3 UGC version (dual register on "The first offer is rarely the fair offer")
- = 6 UGC units vs. 18 Elevated — statistically meaningful A/B test inside the consolidated ad set

Barry's non-negotiable:
> *"UGC variants are NOT 'Elevated shots downgraded.' Different prompt system, different Higgsfield/Kling settings, different camera lens (phone wide, ~28mm equivalent, not 50mm cinematic). The uncanny-valley version of UGC is worse than either pure register."*

The only real cost is Giuseppe's time on prompting and QA, which is already budgeted. Produce all 24 upfront. The discipline shifts to rotation — which 6-8 go live this week, next week — not production volume. Revised verdict: GREEN, not amber."*
