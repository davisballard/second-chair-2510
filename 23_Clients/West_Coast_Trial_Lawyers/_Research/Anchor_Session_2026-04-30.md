**Indigo (the standing-overlay tension):** "Davis is asking for the opposite of our discipline — a persistent anchor on every frame. Both can be true. The standing overlay IS earning its place — its job is comprehension lock for half-listeners and 0.5-second skimmers. That's a legitimate conversion job that the existing earned-Peak-Card pattern doesn't do. It's not a violation; it's a SIXTH pattern that serves a job the other five don't."

**Massimo (pulled in):** "I buy it conditionally. The Standing Anchor earns its place IF it does three things the others don't: (1) carries the Fair Case brand identity continuously so the spot reads as a *brand* spot not a *content* fragment; (2) holds the CTA promise visibly during the entire scroll-window; (3) renders with the typographic and color discipline of a designed object. If those hold, it's a sixth signature pattern. What it CANNOT do is steal the editorial palette. The Anchor is a Fair Case-branded element by definition; Anchor in Roman Navy + Torch Amber + white. Editorial overlays in Cream/Burgundy/Charcoal stay separate, layered above. Two layers, two palettes, two jobs."

**Ed McCabe (pulled in on copy):** "Three lines on the Anchor and they need to do different jobs. Headline (largest, white) = the *promise*. Secondary (smaller, off-white or muted) = the *qualifier* that adds credibility. Disclaimer (smallest) = the *compliance* line. One promise, one credibility, one compliance. If a fourth line creeps in, kill it."

**Davis (correction after first plan draft):** "Also the budget should fit the addressable market so how the split of the socials and formats has to do with that and where those people are and in those formats. Also this is for the AZ WCTL campaign."

## The Davis correction → AZ-addressable-market rebuild

Davis's correction reframed the whole format-mix recommendation. The room had been reasoning from generic Meta strategy (60-70% IG / 30-40% FB on Advantage+). The audience research files (`../Audience/01_AZ_Non_Soft_Tissue_Demographics.md` §10 + §9, `../Audience/04_Platform_Behavior_And_Reach.md`) tell a sharper story:

- AZ non-soft-tissue PI 30-54 commuter core: 80% FB adoption / 58% daily use
- Phoenix Hispanic 32% (Maryvale 75%, South Phx 65-70%): FB primary + Spanish FB
- Motorcycle (Ty): IG/FB Reels + YouTube
- Rideshare 18-34: IG dominant
- Davis's intuition "this is a big FB demo" → confirmed

Implication: the Anchor (designed/editorial register) is FB-Feed-native. UGC + cinematic motion is IG-Reels-native. Deployment isn't all-or-nothing — it's per-persona-platform-fit.

**Refined static-as-video rule:** by surface, not blanket. FB Feed renders pure static at full weight (it's print-ad-native). Render-as-video only for IG Reels/Stories. Davis or Alex builds two versions of each static unit; per-placement customization in Advantage+ serves the right version to the right surface.
