## How SC Starter Packages Work

First packages are sold as pre-paid lead packages. **100 leads per Market** is the minimum that gets past Meta's algorithm learning phase (first ~30 leads, elevated CPL, least-precise targeting) and into real optimized performance. At 100 leads, WCTL sees the full arc — learning, stabilization, and early optimization — not just ramp noise.

Leads are delivered as we generate them. A 100-lead package in a single Market fills in roughly 5–7 weeks depending on market competitiveness.

**Why 100 and not 50:** at 50 leads, you'd be evaluating us almost entirely on pre-optimization performance. The first 30 leads are always the worst (learning phase). At 100, the campaigns are dialed in and you're seeing what they actually produce when they're working. That's a fairer test for both sides.

**Why upfront, not per-lead:** algorithms need budget certainty to commit media spend. A guaranteed package lets us run efficient optimization across the full ramp curve. It's also how we know whether to lock the territory.

## What Happens After The First Package

When a starter package fills, WCTL has real data:

- Actual cost per signed case per Market
- Sign rate by market (with methodology)
- Creative performance (which archetypes resonated)
- Lead quality scores across the full distribution
- Intake team feedback on lead arrival state

With that data, WCTL can:

1. **Renew at 50+ leads per Market** — campaigns are already optimized, renewals benefit from everything the first 100 taught the system
2. **Expand to more Markets** — add Riverside, Sacramento, Vegas, Phoenix, or Seattle based on where WCTL sees the strongest opportunity
3. **Walk away** — if the numbers don't work, the relationship ends cleanly with no ongoing obligation

Renewals typically cost less per lead than first packages because the algorithm is dialed in, the pixel is trained on real behavior, and retargeting becomes possible. Second-package economics are meaningfully better than first-package economics.

## Projected Attorney Fee → Projected ROAS (Day One)

The primary metric Second Chair reports is **Projected Attorney Fee per Lead → Projected ROAS** on the cohort. Every lead arrives with a projected dollar value attached — built from the Fit Score × sign probability × median attorney fee by case type. This is visible on day one, before any case has signed.

**Defensible range: 5-8× projected ROAS** on California Markets at SC's rates. See `Abracadabra/08_Brands/Second_Chair/01_Identity/Projected_Attorney_Fee_Model.md` for the full lookup tables and formula.

**Example (100-lead LA cohort at $36,000 spend):**
- Projected Attorney Fee per Lead: $1,800-$2,800 (depending on case mix)
- Aggregate Projected Attorney Fees: ~$180,000-$280,000
- Projected ROAS: **~5-8×**

## What's Included In Every Package

- Territory exclusivity in the purchased Markets
- Market-specific creative built from scratch for each market
- SB-37 compliant creative (CA markets)
- FCC 2025 one-to-one consent with TrustedForm certificate on every lead
- Full data package per lead: contact, accident details, injury type, qualification score, consent proof
- Real-time dashboard with CPL, volume, lead quality, CPSC tracking
- Ad visibility — every ad running under WCTL's name is visible in the dashboard
- Optional approval workflow (ads reviewed before launch) on standing basis
- Portal / email / webhook delivery — whatever fits WCTL's intake workflow
- Low-inventory alerts before packages run out
- Full audit trail on every consent moment (5-year retention)

## What's NOT Included
