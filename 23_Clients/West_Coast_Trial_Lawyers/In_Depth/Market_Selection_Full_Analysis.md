# Full Market Selection Analysis: WCTL Pilot Campaign
## Which Market Should Second Chair Target for the $10K Test?

**Prepared:** April 13, 2026
**Purpose:** Evaluate every Market where WCTL operates to determine the optimal market for a $10K non-soft-tissue PI lead gen pilot.

---

## WCTL Footprint

From the April 10 call, Robert confirmed WCTL has presence in:
- **California** — HQ in downtown LA, offices across the state
- **Arizona** — Physical office, staffed
- **Washington** — New lease in Seattle, people working there
- **Nevada** — "Pretty big office" *(excluded from analysis per Robert: "too volatile")*

Robert gave Second Chair full Market choice: *"Whatever you guys think and deliver the best success, I want to give you guys that opportunity."*

He also signaled preference: *"Arizona, Washington... we're seeing better markets at the moment [vs California]."*

---

## The Scorecard

Each Market is evaluated on 5 factors critical to pilot success:

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **Volume** | More non-soft-tissue incidents = larger ad pool = easier to hit 3-4 sign-worthy leads |
| **Competition** | Lower PI ad saturation = lower CPL = more leads per dollar |
| **WCTL Readiness** | Established office with staff = faster intake = higher conversion |
| **Regulatory** | SB37 (CA only) adds compliance burden but also clears out competitors |
| **Pilot Fit** | $10K budget constraint — needs concentrated Market, not spread thin |

#### 1. Phoenix, AZ — THE TOP PICK

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total crashes (2024) | 37,472 |
| Injuries (2024) | 10,449 |
| Fatal crashes (2024) | 265 |
| Est. non-soft-tissue incidents/month | ~574–764 |
| Cumulative unrepresented pipeline | ~1,720–4,590 |
| Crash rate per 1,000 residents | 3.2 |
| Motorcycle crashes (statewide, mostly Phoenix) | 264/month |
| DUI injury crashes (statewide) | 298/month |
| WCTL office | Yes — established, staffed |
| PI ad competition | High (billboard wars on I-10, I-17, Loop 101) |
| Statute of limitations | 2 years |

**Volume: A+** — By far the largest addressable pool of any single Market. Phoenix alone produces more injury crashes than the entire state of Washington.

**Competition: B-** — Phoenix PI advertising is saturated on billboards and Google. BUT paid social (Meta/TikTok) is a different battlefield. Most PI firms are still focused on traditional channels and Google PPC. Second Chair's social-first approach sidesteps the worst of the competition.

**WCTL Readiness: A** — Established office with staff. Robert explicitly called AZ a "better market." Intake team is operational and experienced in this geography.

**Regulatory: A** — No SB37. Standard advertising rules. Clean operating environment.

**Pilot Fit: A** — Massive pool means the $10K budget has maximum room to find qualified leads even with pixel ramp-up inefficiency. A 0.7–1.8% capture rate from the unrepresented pipeline is needed — very achievable.

**Overall: STRONGEST PICK.** Volume and WCTL readiness make this the highest-probability market for hitting 3-4 sign-worthy leads on $10K.

**Volume: C+** — Smaller pool, but not disqualifying. ~64–86 non-soft-tissue incidents per month with a 6-12 month cumulative pipeline of ~192–516 unrepresented people.

**Competition: A** — This is Tucson's killer advantage. Far fewer PI firms competing for attention. Lower CPCs, lower CPMs, less ad fatigue. The $10K goes significantly further here.

**WCTL Readiness: B+** — WCTL operates across Arizona. Tucson isn't their HQ but they have presence and can service leads from here.

**Regulatory: A** — No SB37.

**Pilot Fit: B+** — Lower volume means tighter margins, but dramatically lower competition could compensate. If CPL is 40-50% lower than Phoenix, the effective lead volume per dollar could be comparable.

**Overall: DARK HORSE.** If the concern is that Phoenix PI competition will eat through the $10K too fast, Tucson offers a less contested battlefield. Worth presenting as an alternative. Could also run a Phoenix + Tucson split since they're in the same state and WCTL services both.

#### 4. Los Angeles, CA — HIGH RISK, HIGH REWARD

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total crashes (2023, LA County) | 40,717 |
| Injuries (2023) | ~14,779 |
| Fatalities (2023) | 336 |
| Est. non-soft-tissue incidents/month | ~370–493 |
| Cumulative unrepresented pipeline | ~1,110–2,958 |
| WCTL office | **Headquarters** — fully staffed, maximum readiness |
| PI ad competition | **Extreme** — most saturated PI market in the country |
| Statute of limitations | 2 years (CA) |
| SB37 impact | **Active — effective Jan 1, 2026** |

**Volume: A-** — Massive market, comparable to Phoenix in raw numbers.

**Competition: D** — LA is ground zero for PI advertising. Jacobi & Meyers, Sweet James, and dozens of billboard-heavy firms dominate. Google CPC for PI keywords in LA is among the highest in the country. Social media is also heavily contested.

**WCTL Readiness: A+** — This is their headquarters. Maximum intake speed, maximum conversion potential.

**Regulatory: C** — SB37 is a double-edged sword:
- **Risk:** Strict compliance requirements, $5K–$100K per violation private right of action, expanded definition of "advertise" covers social media posts
- **Opportunity:** SB37 has caused a *"mass exodus of lead generation companies, affiliate networks, and marketing aggregators"* from California. Compliant operators face less competition than before. Second Chair's compliance-first positioning is a genuine differentiator here.

**Pilot Fit: C** — The $10K will get chewed up fast in LA's auction environment. Even with SB37 clearing out some competitors, the remaining ones are spending heavily. Not the right market for a budget-constrained test.

**Overall: SAVE FOR LATER.** LA's combination of extreme competition and regulatory complexity makes it a poor pilot market. But once Second Chair has proven results elsewhere and can command a larger budget, LA — as WCTL's headquarters with maximum intake readiness — becomes the highest-value target. SB37 compliance is a moat that will keep growing.

## Recommendation

### Primary: Phoenix, AZ

The highest-probability path to hitting Robert's 3-4 sign-worthy leads on $10K. Massive volume, established WCTL office, no regulatory headaches, and Robert's explicit preference for Arizona. The PI competition is real but concentrated on billboards and Google — Second Chair's social-first approach operates in a less contested channel.

### Alternative: Phoenix + Tucson Split (Same State)

If there's concern about Phoenix competition eating through budget, consider a 70/30 or 60/40 split between Phoenix and Tucson. Both are serviced by WCTL's Arizona office, both fall under the same state regulations, and Tucson's lower competition could produce cheaper leads that subsidize Phoenix's higher-value but more expensive ones. This is a one-state strategy that gives pixel data from two different competition environments.

### Why Not California (For Now)

SB37 is actually an *opportunity* for Second Chair long-term — the mass exodus of non-compliant lead gen companies has created a gap that compliant operators can fill. But for a $10K pilot where the goal is proving performance to earn a second round of spend, adding regulatory complexity is unnecessary risk. **Prove it in Arizona first, then expand to CA with a compliance moat as the selling point.**

### Why Not Washington (For Now)

Washington works but offers ~40% less volume than Phoenix with comparable or higher ad costs (Seattle tech economy inflates CPMs). WCTL's Seattle office is new and still building out, meaning intake response times may lag Arizona's — directly impacting conversion rates. It's a viable Phase 2 market once the partnership is established.
