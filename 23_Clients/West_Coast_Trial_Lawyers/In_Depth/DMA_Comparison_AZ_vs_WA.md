# DMA Comparison: Arizona vs. Washington
## Non-Soft-Tissue PI Lead Gen — Pilot Market Selection for WCTL

**Prepared:** April 13, 2026
**Purpose:** Compare Arizona and Washington as potential pilot markets for the WCTL $10K test campaign, factoring in incident volume, category mix, cumulative pipeline, and market dynamics.

## Cumulative Pipeline Analysis

The monthly incident rate is not the ad-targetable pool. People who had a qualifying accident in the past several months and haven't retained an attorney are all potential leads. The cumulative pipeline is what paid social actually reaches.

| Factor | Arizona | Washington |
|--------|---------|------------|
| PI statute of limitations | **2 years** | 3 years |
| Realistic action window | 6–12 months | 6–12 months |
| Non-soft-tissue incidents/month (DMA) | 574–764 | 325–430 |
| × 6-month backlog | 3,444–4,584 | 1,950–2,580 |
| × 12-month backlog | 6,888–9,168 | 3,900–5,160 |
| Minus ~50% already represented | | |
| **Unrepresented pipeline (6-mo, DMA)** | **1,722–2,292** | **975–1,290** |
| **Unrepresented pipeline (12-mo, DMA)** | **3,444–4,584** | **1,950–2,580** |

*Arizona's shorter statute of limitations (2 years vs 3) is a minor disadvantage, but the realistic action window is the same (6–12 months). The raw volume advantage overwhelms the statute difference.*

**What this means for ads:** At any given time in the Phoenix DMA, there are an estimated **1,700–4,600 unrepresented people** who had a non-soft-tissue accident in the past 6–12 months. Needing ~30 qualified leads from this pool is a **0.7–1.8% capture rate** — well within realistic paid social performance.

### Phoenix DMA Advantages for Pilot
- **Dense, urban population** = high social media saturation and ad delivery efficiency
- **Year-round driving and riding** = no seasonal crash dip (unlike WA's winter motorcycle drop-off)
- **Major interstate corridors** = consistent trucking accident volume
- **WCTL has a physical, staffed office** = faster intake response times than their new Seattle lease
- **Robert called AZ a "better market"** = internal buy-in already exists

## Recommendation

**Arizona (Phoenix DMA) is the stronger pilot market.** The case is clear on multiple dimensions:

1. **Volume:** ~77% more addressable non-soft-tissue incidents per month in the primary DMA
2. **Pipeline depth:** 1,700–4,600 unrepresented people in the pool at any time vs. 970–2,590 in Seattle
3. **Capture rate needed:** 0.7–1.8% to hit lead targets (vs. 1.2–3.1% in WA) — significantly more achievable
4. **WCTL readiness:** Established AZ office with staff vs. brand-new Seattle lease still building out
5. **Client preference:** Robert explicitly said Arizona is a "better market" for them right now
6. **Seasonal stability:** No winter drop-off in motorcycle/cycling categories
7. **Category richness:** Massive motorcycle volume (264/month), high pedestrian/bicycle (288/month), strong trucking corridor presence

The only advantage Washington holds is a longer statute of limitations (3 years vs. 2), which has minimal practical impact on the 6–12 month action window that matters for lead gen.

**For a $10K pilot where the margin for error is thin, Arizona's larger pool gives Second Chair significantly better odds of hitting the 3–4 sign-worthy lead target and earning a second round of spend.**
