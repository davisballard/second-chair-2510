## 1. SMP → S5 Hook Translation

The SMP from §00: *Diana's entire life is organized around being the person who manages things. She's not 'at home on medical leave.' She is a family system that has quietly stopped functioning because the one invisible piece holding it together is on a couch with a herniated disc and a concussion — and everyone else just discovered how much she was actually doing.*

**For S5 (video 15–30s, 1:1 + 9:16, UGC Native):**

The S5 asset is the **broadest-catch volume slot** in the entire Phase 1 campaign. Its job is different from Sarah's (premium specialty) or Ty's three slots (rider-identity activation). **S5 has to convert at scale — the lead-volume engine of Phase 1.**

**Hook direction (from McCabe's 25-option pool in `04_Media_Reach.md` §8):**

- **Lead:** "Broken bones. Bills don't stop." (Current — works. Severity-filter in first two words. Direct, no-artifice.)
- **Alt A:** "Three kids. Two incomes. One crash." (Diana-specific emotional; bolder filter that self-qualifies to parent + family-stakes audience.)
- **Alt B:** "Serious car accident in Arizona? Months of recovery. Bills don't wait." (Information-forward; retargeting-friendly.)

**What each does psychologically:**

- **Lead** is the Steel/Doc 2 canonical hook. Severity filter + loss-forward. Tight. Works for cold traffic.
- **Alt A** activates family-system awareness directly. Higher emotional weight. Best for warm traffic retargeting — Diana who's already seen the Lead hook and is now primed.
- **Alt B** is the longest but most information-forward. Best for consideration-stage retargeting where she's tapped and bounced.

**A/B discipline:** Launch all three in Week 1 at equal weight. After 10 days (~150 impressions per creative per week minimum for signal), scale the winner and retire the two losers. Expected: Lead wins on cold; Alt A wins on warm; Alt B dies.

## 2. Body Copy Direction (for Meta feed text)

**Template (current from Week_1_Launch_Concepts.md — validated for Diana):**

> Serious injuries don't pause because you can't work. Arizona drivers injured in a crash — surgery, hospital stays, months of recovery — may be entitled to more than what insurance first offered. No fee unless we win.

**Why this template works for Diana:**

1. **Opens with a universal truth** ("Serious injuries don't pause...") that Diana reads as lived reality, not slogan.
2. **Specifies severity thresholds** ("surgery, hospital stays, months of recovery") = filters hard on non-soft-tissue.
3. **Names the adjuster-anchor insult** ("more than what insurance first offered") = activates her specific irritation at the $18K offer.
4. **"Find out" framing is investigative**, not advocacy — fits Diana's research-before-decide style.
5. **"No fee unless we win"** — Diana partially knows contingency, but this belongs above-the-fold.

**Alternative body copy angles for A/B testing:**

- *"Your family is trying. Your bills aren't waiting. Arizona drivers injured in a crash — free case review to see what the law says about your case. No fee unless we win."* (Family-system emphasis; matches Diana's emotional architecture.)
- *"Rear-ended on an Arizona freeway? The recovery is longer than the crash. Find out what your case is actually worth. Free review — 60 seconds."* (Specific to her crash type; activates the "one event → months of impact" frame.)

**CTA text direction:**

- "See Options → quiz. Free case review — 60 seconds."
- "Learn what the law says. Free — 60 seconds."
- "Get a free case review — no obligation, no fee unless we win."

**NEVER use:**
- "Call now — don't wait!" (Diana scrolls past; scare urgency triggers her billboard-firm pattern match)
- "You may be entitled to MILLIONS!" (triggers her volume-mill alarm)
- "Get the compensation you deserve!" (pitying; vague)
- "Our aggressive legal team!" (counterproductive)

## 4. Compliance Considerations Specific to Diana

- **AZ Bar ER 7.3 (direct solicitation):** Hook is third-person ("Broken bones. Bills don't stop."). No "you" in headlines. Body copy can shift to second-person after the third-person frame is established. Compliant.
- **AZ Bar ER 7.1 (truthfulness):** Body copy references "surgery, hospital stays, months of recovery" — factually accurate for Diana-type MVA cases; filters non-soft-tissue. "More than what insurance first offered" is truthful (initial offers are typically 30–50% of final settlement value). **No specific dollar amounts promised.**
- **AZ Bar ER 7.5 (firm naming):** WCTL logo + firm name in end-card.
- **Fault-presumption rigor note** (`00_Evidence_Register.md` §14): If Robert raises fault concerns for S5, answer is strong: standard rear-end collision = driver-presumed-at-fault in virtually all states including AZ. Clean liability. This is one of the simplest liability cases in the entire Phase 1 campaign.
- **Medical lien considerations:** Banner Gateway will file a hospital lien on Diana's settlement. Intake team should proactively explain lien-negotiation strategy (similar to Sarah but less complex — Diana's medical costs are likely lower than Sarah's $100K+ range).
- **TCPA consent language:** Required on quiz-funnel lead-capture.

## 5. Creative Acid Tests (before shipping any Diana creative)

Three questions. 3/3 = ship. 2/3 = revise. 1/3 = wrong audience.

1. **Would Diana read this line and feel recognized, not pitied?** Not "would a suburban mom archetype" — specifically *would Diana, the 42-year-old Senior Office Manager running a household CFO operation, read this and think "oh, they actually see me"*?
2. **Would Diana screenshot this and send it to her Chandler Mom Crew group chat** with an *"is this too real?"* caption? Does it read as documentary-true, or would it embarrass her to share because it's melodramatic?
3. **Does it name the invisible labor + the system failure without lecturing?** Does the creative's visual or subtext signal that WCTL sees her as a *family operating system* — not as a generic injured person?

Three rules for Diana:

1. **Sound-off test at 9:30 PM on a 6-inch phone screen in FB Reels.** Captions required. First-2-second hook has to land without audio.

## 7. Binet's 40/60 Effectiveness Lens (volume-slot discipline)

Per `02_Emotional_Architecture.md`:

**Diana's slot is volume-floor, not premium-value.** Binet's 60/40 frame inverts for volume segments: **activation ≥ brand-signal** in the short-term optimization.

**Practical implications:**

- **Hook filter can be sharper.** "Broken bones. Bills don't stop." is tight; we're comfortable losing soft-tissue self-identifiers because volume math still works.
- **Creative refresh cadence can be faster.** Diana's audience has higher baseline mental availability for PI (she's seen billboard ads her whole career). We can rotate UGC variants every 2 weeks without fatigue.
- **Direct-response metrics weight heavily.** CTR, LPV, quiz-completion-rate are the operational optimization signals.
- **Brand signal is lower-stakes.** If WCTL's S5 creative doesn't build massive long-term brand recall, that's fine — this slot's job is volume, not brand.
- **BUT the UGC-vs-Elevated style test runs through S5.** If the UGC creative underperforms badly, we've lost not just S5's leads — we've lost the Phase 2 style-direction signal. **Protect the UGC integrity even while tuning direct-response.**

## 8. Research Gaps (what this persona hasn't validated)

Items to validate during or after the pilot:

- **Ben's actual role in the decision.** We modeled him as an active co-decision-maker (reads the firm's website, has opinions). WCTL intake data will validate. If Ben is more passive than modeled, creative should shift toward Diana as sole-voice.
- **The concussion-insomnia window.** 2:00 AM – 4:00 AM is our hypothesis — verify with Meta delivery data in Week 1–2. If Diana-type profiles don't disproportionately tap in that window, reallocate spend.
- **UGC style performance vs. Elevated.** This is the big bet. Phase 1 data from S5 (UGC) vs. S1/S2 (Elevated) will tell us if UGC is the Phase 2 direction or if Elevated holds.
- **"Broken bones" comprehension among actual Diana-type audience.** We assume the audience reads "broken bones" as severity filter. Some viewers with herniated-disc-only (soft-tissue-adjacent but non-fracture injuries) may NOT self-identify — missing this niche. Validate via quiz Step 2 injury-type data; if too many "soft tissue" responses clear the filter, tune the hook.
- **Diana's analysis-paralysis as a modeled pattern.** We assumed she compares 6 firms. If intake data shows she's actually converting on 2–3 firms compared, the "make the decision easy" creative discipline is right. If she's converting on more, re-tune.
- **The "invisible labor" theme's actual resonance.** We argue it's load-bearing. A/B test a hook that names it indirectly ("Three kids. Two incomes. One crash.") against the severity-forward Lead. If the family-system hook out-performs, Diana's emotional architecture layer is the right long-term campaign direction.
