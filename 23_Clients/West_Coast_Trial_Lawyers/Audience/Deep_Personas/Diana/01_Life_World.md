## 1. The Professional Role

**Senior Office Manager at a 7-person property management company** in central Phoenix — near 7th Street and Camelback. Been with the firm 9 years; promoted to "Senior" 6 months ago (quiet title change, small raise, more accountability).

**The company:** A mid-sized property management firm handling ~180 residential rental units across Phoenix metro — mostly 2BR/3BR single-family homes in Ahwatukee, Phoenix (central + 7th Street corridor), Tempe (ASU-adjacent rentals), a handful of small multi-family properties in Chandler and Gilbert. The firm has two property managers (her boss Karen and Karen's colleague Greg), a maintenance coordinator, and a team of four admin/accounting staff including Diana. **Diana is effectively the operational spine of the firm.**

**What her role actually covers (day-to-day):**
- **Accounts payable / receivable** — she reconciles rent rolls, handles tenant payment issues, manages vendor payables (HVAC, plumbers, landscapers, pest control)
- **Tenant escalations** — the upset-tenant-call that Karen and Greg don't want to take, Diana takes
- **Vendor scheduling** — she runs the dispatcher role for maintenance trades; keeps the calendar where landlord-approved repairs get assigned
- **Lease renewals + new-tenant paperwork** — coordinates signing windows, deposit handling, move-out inspections
- **Compliance + reporting** — AZ LLC annual reports, TPT tax filings, 1099 generation for vendors, ADP payroll admin
- **Firm's Google Business Profile + a modest social presence** — she's the one who responds to negative reviews
- **The monthly "state-of-the-book" meeting** with the principals — she's the one who knows where every property stands

**Why "Senior" was earned 6 months ago:** Her predecessor left. Diana had been doing 80% of the senior role unofficially for 3 years. Karen finally admitted it and promoted her. Small raise ($3K base increase to $48K) but real title change. It's important to her identity — the 6-month-old plaque from Karen is on her desk. It's the kind of professional recognition a 42-year-old working mom doesn't take for granted.

**Hours:** 7:30 AM – 4:30 PM, Monday–Friday. 30-minute lunch at her desk. Occasionally bleeds late (tenant crisis on a Friday = she's still on the phone at 6 PM).

**Pay band:** $48,000/yr base + occasional $500–$1000 bonus for quarterly performance = **~$49K gross**. Solid for her title and experience in AZ, low for someone with her actual operational leverage.

**The commute corridor:**

- Occasional midday errands: maintenance vendor visits, bank, post office
- **No major side trips** — Chandler-Phoenix I-10 is her commute; she doesn't do the I-17 corridor (that's Sarah) or Tempe (though ASU-adjacent rentals take her there once a month)

**The crash location:** **Westbound I-10, just past the I-17 interchange, in stop-and-go traffic (the classic afternoon commute congestion).** She was at a complete stop in backed-up traffic. A **Ford F-250** rear-ended her at approximately 35 mph. The driver of the F-250 was looking at his phone (confirmed by the AZ DPS officer who took her statement). The CR-V's rear crumple zone absorbed some impact but Diana's head snapped backward then forward. Sharp pain shot from her neck down her right arm. She couldn't turn her head. **The airbags did NOT deploy** (rear collisions at 35 mph often don't trigger front airbags).

**Injuries (confirmed at Banner Gateway ER the next morning — she refused the ambulance at the scene because she was thinking about who would pick up her kids):**
- **Herniated disc C5-C6** (the primary injury — radiculopathy into her right arm)
- **Grade 2 concussion** (mild TBI — screen-sensitivity + headaches + cognitive fog)
- **Soft-tissue trauma, upper back + neck** (whiplash, confirmed)
- **Contusion along her right ribs** (seatbelt-related, minor)
- **Right-side facial abrasion** (from the rear-view mirror knocking her cheek on the recoil) — small bruise along her right jawline

**Ongoing treatment:**
- **Neurosurgeon consult at Banner Phoenix** — herniated disc, conservative management (physical therapy + epidural steroid injection) for 12 weeks; if no improvement, discectomy (surgery)
- **Concussion protocol** — no screens for 2 weeks, gradual reintroduction with headaches as a governor
- **Physical therapy** — 3×/week at a Chandler PT practice
- **Cannot drive for 4 weeks minimum** (neck immobility + concussion combo)
- **Recovery timeline:** 3–6 months to full function, longer if surgery needed

**Her arm sling (the visual anchor continuity prop):** She has her **right arm in a medical-grade black fabric sling** because the radiculopathy causes weakness + nerve pain down her right arm — keeping it supported reduces pain. The sling will be on her in every S5 frame. Strap runs diagonally across her body from her left shoulder down to her right hip, cradling her right forearm at her chest.

## 6. Daily Rhythm (pre-crash — the machine)

This is the machine Diana ran. The crash broke it.

**Weekday (the commuter-mom machine):**
- **5:40 AM** — Alarm. Shower. Hair into a ponytail. Minimal makeup.
- **6:00 AM** — Coffee. Begins the morning system: unload dishwasher while coffee brews; check the fridge calendar for the day's overlaps.
- **6:10 AM** — Mia's lunchbox packed (turkey sandwich, apple, goldfish, a small note on a post-it about the school day). Emma's lunchbox packed (salad, pretzels, her "no note please, mom"). Aiden's lunch money handed to him.
- **6:30 AM** — Kids fed and moving. Ben does breakfast while Diana does logistics.
- **6:40 AM** — Ben handles school drop-off (he's already in work mode, drives the F-150 with kids — drops them on his way to his crew yard)
- **6:50 AM** — Diana out the door. Commute begins. Podcast or NPR Morning Edition. Coffee in the cup holder.
- **7:30 AM** — At her office desk. She starts the day by reading the overnight tenant-service voicemails and flagging the three most urgent.
- **12:00 PM** — Lunch at her desk. Usually a salad she packed the night before. 20 minutes.
- **4:30 PM** — Leaves the office. Commute home.
- **5:00 PM** — Picks up one or more kids from after-school (the rotation varies). Ben meets her at home or the kids' activities.
- **5:30–8:00 PM** — Dinner + homework + activities drop-off + one load of laundry. Mia's reading time. Emma's passive-aggressive comments about something at school. Aiden's soccer-practice-related questions.
- **8:30 PM** — Mia to bed.
- **9:00 PM** — Emma + Aiden toward bed. Some TV with Ben if she's up for it (Succession, Ted Lasso when it was around).
- **10:30 PM** — Bed. She's done maybe 40% of tomorrow's mental prep.

**Weekend (the catch-up system):**
- Saturday morning: kids' activities (Aiden's soccer), grocery run (Costco + Target), family errands
- Saturday afternoon: downtime, some kid-solo time with each kid (she rotates)
- Saturday evening: date night with Ben ~1×/month (usually at a Chandler restaurant near their house — The Keg or a Pita Jungle)
- Sunday: chores, kids' homework catch-up, meal prep for the week, Linda (her mom) check-in by phone

**Post-crash rhythm (broken machine):**
- Ben has taken emergency leave at his subcontractor (he has some PTO banked — 10 days — which runs out Week 2; unpaid leave after that is not viable)
- Linda (grandma) comes up from her apartment 2–3 days/week to help manage the kids, but her own stroke recovery makes her limited
- Sister-in-law Lien drops off meals 2×/week
- **The kitchen magnetic wall calendar has been frozen since the week of the crash** — Ben is trying to update it but doesn't know the conventions Diana used
- **Emma is making her own lunch** (grumpily) and Aiden's (mostly poorly)
- **The dentist appointment Diana had scheduled for Aiden at Week 3 was missed** because Ben didn't know about it
- **The vendor scheduling for Diana's office is covered by a temp** (Karen hired someone for 6 weeks). The temp is adequate, not excellent, and Karen knows it.
