- Emotional: worried about bills, quietly frustrated at the system-failure in the household, tired of being the one who knows where everything is.

- Emotional: anxiety peak. The mental-load is processing everything that's broken. The insult of the $18K offer is active. Ben is asleep; she's alone with thoughts.
- Cognitive: surprisingly sharp for short bursts (4–8 minutes before brain fog returns). Will Google. Will tap ads. Will read. Will close tabs fast when they don't deliver.

**Saturday 10:00 AM morning scroll:**
- Physical: fresher. Coffee in system.
- Emotional: calmer. The week is paused.

**Creative implications:**
- **9:30 PM creative:** emotionally resonant, visually simple, image-first. **S5 core creative window.**
- **2:30 AM creative:** pairs with Google search results. Landing page UX must be concussion-friendly (clean, fast-loading, minimal cognitive load).
- **Saturday morning creative:** retargeting window — deeper-funnel creative targeting those who've seen S5 top-of-funnel.

## 4. Meta Targeting Spec (Depesh's layer)

Meta's **Special Ad Category: Social Issues** constraint applies — no demographic or interest targeting for legal services.

**What we CAN target:**
- **Location:** Arizona statewide; heavier weight to Maricopa County (64% of AZ MVA crashes). Particular weight to the I-10 corridor communities: Chandler (85225, 85248, 85249, 85286), Ahwatukee (85048, 85044, 85045), Tempe (85281, 85283, 85284), central Phoenix (85020s, 85016, 85014) — Diana's commute corridor is also the crash corridor for her demo.
- **Age:** 18–65+ broad (SAC prohibits narrow age bands).
- **Placement:** **Facebook Feed + FB Reels + FB Stories + Instagram Feed + Instagram Stories.** NOT Audience Network. NOT Messenger.
- **Language:** English primary.
- **Device:** Mobile primary (Diana is 90%+ mobile, especially post-crash).

**What we CAN'T target:**
- Motherhood / parent interests
- Chandler-specific residents (we can target Chandler geographically but not "Chandler Moms" as an interest)
- Property management profession
- Recent car accident lookalikes (not until we have 50+ converters)

**Creative as targeting — the workaround (same principle as Ty + Sarah):**

Because we can't target Diana directly, the **creative hook does the qualifying work.** S5's "Broken bones. Bills don't stop." filters hard on severity. The UGC visual signals "this is for real people like me, not a firm trying to impress you." The combination filters to Diana's profile without direct targeting.

**Key insight for Diana vs. other personas:**
- Diana's audience is larger than Sarah's (trucking) — broader filter, more reach possible
- Diana's audience is also larger than Ty's (motorcycle) — non-motorcycle MVA is the biggest pool
- **S5 should achieve the lowest CPM of any Phase 1 creative** because the addressable audience is broadest
- **Sign-rate per lead should be similar to other MVA slots** — not as premium as Sarah's trucking but higher volume compensates
- Expected outcome: S5 generates the largest share of raw leads + signs, with lower per-case value than Sarah's trucking

**Lookalike seed strategy (Phase 2 if data):**
- 1% LAL from S5 quiz-completers (once 50+ data points)
- 1% LAL from S5 lead-form submitters
- Retargeting pool: everyone who engaged with S5 creative (views ≥ 50% of video, tapped but didn't convert)

**Frequency cap:** **4 impressions/person/week** (matches Ty; slightly higher than Sarah's 3 because Diana's broader audience tolerates more exposure before fatigue — she's not a clinical-skeptical professional like Sarah, she's a general-consumer mom).

**UGC-specific delivery note:**
S5 runs UGC Native style. Meta's algo treats UGC-aesthetic ads differently from polished Elevated ads — often delivering to slightly different audience slices based on engagement patterns. **Monitor whether S5's delivered audience differs meaningfully from Elevated S1/S2 audiences.** If the UGC style systematically pulls younger or less-affluent viewers, the sign-rate math could shift.

## 5. Language Patterns (USE / AVOID — full set)

How Diana actually talks and thinks. **These are the words that should appear in S5 copy + landing page + intake. Words NOT in these lists should not appear.**

### How she describes the crash:
- ✅ "I got rear-ended on I-10." / "A guy in a truck hit me from behind." / "Stopped traffic and this F-250 just plowed into me."
- ❌ "I was involved in a motor vehicle accident." (Legalese.) / "Another driver struck my vehicle." (Bureaucratic.)

### How she describes herself (her identity):
- ✅ "I'm a mom of three." / "I run an office." / "I'm the one who keeps everything going."
- ✅ "I'm 42." (She doesn't hide it.)
- ❌ "I'm a caregiver." (Too clinical.) / "Working mom." (She's beyond this stock phrase.)

### How she describes the other driver:
- ✅ "The guy in the truck." / "The driver who was looking at his phone." / "A big Ford pickup."
- ❌ "The at-fault party." (PI-speak.) / "The defendant." (Premature.) / "The negligent driver." (Legalese.)

### How she describes her injuries:
- ✅ "Herniated disc." / "C5-C6." / "Bad concussion." / "My neck is jacked." / "I can't look at screens."
- ✅ "Broken bones" (hook-compatible — though technically her injury is disc + concussion, Diana would still mentally file herself in the "yeah, I've got serious injuries" camp)
- ❌ "Catastrophic injuries." / "Devastating trauma." (Dramatic.) / "Life-altering accident." (Generic.)

### How she describes the adjuster:
- ✅ "They offered eighteen thousand. It's not even close." / "The insurance company called on day ten." / "They want me to go away."
- ❌ "The insurance company is acting in bad faith." (Would think, wouldn't say yet.)

### How she describes lawyers (before hiring):
- ✅ "I need to get a lawyer." / "I've been putting it off." / "I don't want to deal with it but I have to." / "The billboard guys feel wrong."
- ❌ "I need an aggressive attorney." / "I want to find a good advocate." (Abstract.)

### How she describes her recovery:
- ✅ "Six to eight weeks on PT, maybe longer." / "They're talking surgery if I don't improve." / "I can't drive yet." / "The concussion is worse than the disc honestly."
- ❌ "I'm fighting every day." / "Grateful to be alive." (Not her register.)

### How she describes her household situation:
- ✅ "Ben is doing everything. He's burnt out." / "The kids are missing their routine." / "My sister-in-law has been a saint." / "The bills are starting to pile up."
- ❌ "We're struggling." (Too vague.) / "Everyone is devastated." (Too emotive.)

### Language we should USE in S5 creative:
- "Bills don't stop because you can't work." (The Diana line — direct, lived, specific.)
- "Serious injuries don't pause because you can't."
- "Arizona drivers injured in a crash"
- "Months of recovery"
- "Free case review — 60 seconds"
- "No fee unless we win"
- "Find out what the law says"
- "Your family shouldn't have to carry this alone"

### Language we should NEVER use in S5 creative:
- "Entitled to MILLIONS"
- "Fight for your rights"
- "Our aggressive legal team"
- "We fight the insurance companies" (emotive + redundant)
- "You deserve..."
- "Injured in an accident" (too generic — S5 hook specifies)
- "Free consultation with our caring attorneys" (infantilizing)
- "Get the compensation you're entitled to" (PI-speak)
- Any phrase a billboard-firm would use

## 6. Creative Format Preferences

Based on Diana's platform behavior + mood states + UGC style:

**9:16 vertical UGC video (FB Reels + IG Reels + Stories):** PRIMARY. UGC native aesthetic. 15–30 seconds max. Sound-off required (Ben or kids might be nearby). Captions mandatory.

**1:1 square UGC (FB Feed):** SECONDARY. For static variants of S5. A single compelling frame — the falling envelopes, Diana's arm-sling close-up, the frozen wall calendar.

**4:5 portrait (IG Feed):** Tertiary.

**Video lead-form ads (Meta's native format):** **VERY WORTH TESTING for Diana.** Pre-fills her name/email via FB; lowers friction given her concussion-limited screen time. Test in Phase 1 if possible. Trade-off: lead quality may be lower than quiz-completer leads.

**UGC style specifics for S5:**
- **Handheld phone feel** (not Steadicam, not gimbal)
- **Natural daylight through kitchen window** (no motivated cinematic lighting)
- **Flat phone color** (no color grading, no film grain)
- **Imperfect composition** — subject slightly off-center is a feature, not a bug
- **As if filmed by Ben from across the kitchen** with one hand on his phone
- Per `Creative/Style_Sheets/UGC_Native.md` — double-check every scene prompt against the style sheet

**NOT formats for her:**
- Carousel (too much cognitive load)
- Playable ads
- Long-form text posts
- Stories with 10+ frames (Diana won't tap through)

**Creative length:** 15 seconds may outperform 30 for her because concussion-related attention limits. A/B test.

## 7. Frequency + Fatigue Guardrails

Diana will see S5 across the 12-week window.

- **Frequency cap:** 4 impressions/person/week.
- **Creative refresh:** swap new UGC variants every 2 weeks to combat fatigue. The UGC format tolerates more variation than polished — different kitchen-scene permutations, different time-of-day captures, different emotional beats — all reading as "another real-person-filmed this" rather than "same ad we've seen."
- **Retargeting refresh:** if she hit the landing page but bounced, serve a deeper-funnel creative — "Ready to talk? Schedule your 20-minute call" + calendar CTA.
- **Burnout signal:** CTR drop + CPM climb. Swap the asset.
