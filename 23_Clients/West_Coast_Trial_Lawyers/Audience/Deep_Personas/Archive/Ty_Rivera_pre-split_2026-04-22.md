## Why the pivot (read this first)

**Supersport riders have a fatal crash rate ~4x higher than cruiser riders** — 22.5–22.6 fatalities per 10,000 registered bikes vs. 5.6–5.7 for cruisers (Dulaney, Lauer & Thomas; Motorcycle Industry Council data). Sportbikes represent a small share of registered motorcycles but are disproportionately represented in fatal and serious-injury crashes. AZ specifically: 258 motorcycle deaths in 2023 (an 11.2% increase over 2022), fatality rate per registered motorcycle up 15.4% in one year, and AZ ranks 9th in US for nighttime motorcycle speeding fatalities (26% above national average). Maricopa County alone accounts for 64% of AZ motorcycle crashes.

**The PI case-quality concern is manageable, not disqualifying.** The obvious worry: 57% of supersport fatalities involve speed, which could trigger comparative fault reductions. But research confirms — the left-turn driver is *presumptively* at fault in intersection crashes, independent of the oncoming vehicle's speed. AZ's pure comparative-negligence rule (A.R.S. § 12-2505) reduces a rider's recovery proportionally to fault, but never eliminates it. A sportbike rider found 20% at fault on a $500K claim still recovers $400K. The real WCTL targeting strategy: **reach sportbike riders who were hit at intersections by left-turning drivers** — this is a large subset, clean liability, higher injury severity, higher case value, and 4x the per-capita incidence rate vs. cruisers.

## 1. The Bike

**2020 Yamaha YZF-R6 in Team Yamaha Blue and white** — the final year of road-legal R6 production (Yamaha discontinued the road R6 after 2020 in favor of the R7). A 2020 is now a collector-grade bike for supersport guys who want the last 600cc true race-replica inline-four; **prices in Phoenix run $11,000–$14,000 used** on Cycle Trader and CycleTrader private listings. Ty bought his 3 years ago at 26 from a private seller in Gilbert for $11,800 — his first supersport after stepping up from a 2015 Honda CBR500R (the common "starter sportbike" ladder).

**Why R6 holds (validated):** The R6 is the iconic middleweight supersport, ubiquitous across AZ canyon and track-day culture. Per Yamaha's own demographic research the middleweight sportbike buyer median is 27 years old — Ty (29) fits exactly. The R6 is also the single most common sportbike at Inde Motorsports Ranch track days (AZ's primary road-race circuit, 3 hours southeast in Willcox). For a working-class AZ guy at 29 with 8 years of riding, a used 2020 R6 is both the peak of desire and a reachable stretch purchase.

**Stock and mods:**
- **118 hp at 14,500 rpm 600cc inline-four**, ride-by-wire throttle (factory).
- **Puig Naked New Generation double-bubble windscreen (smoke)** — the single most common R6 mod for reducing wind blast on 70+ mph canyon runs.
- **Frame sliders (black T-Rex or Woodcraft)** — crash protection, added at purchase.
- **Driven Racing rear sets** (track-ready, allows more aggressive foot position for canyon carving).
- **Rizoma aftermarket levers** (blue anodized to match tank).
- **Stock Akrapovič slip-on exhaust** (the "race can" — louder and lighter than stock, added by prior owner).
- **Michelin Power 5 tires** — the standard AZ canyon tire choice, grippy in desert heat.
- Small Yamaha tuning fork decal on the tail.

*Sources: [Yamaha YZF-R6 buyer's guide — Motofomo](https://motofomo.com/yamaha-r6-buyers-guide/); [Why R6 is the best supersport — TopSpeed](https://www.topspeed.com/reasons-why-the-yamaha-r6-is-the-best-supersport/); [2026 Yamaha R7 demographic notes — RideApart](https://www.rideapart.com/reviews/792423/2026-yamaha-r7-sportbike-first-ride-motorcycle-review-specs-price-features/); [Cycle Trader Phoenix sportbikes](https://www.cycletrader.com/motorcycles-for-sale?make=Yamaha%7C2317008&model=R6&city=phoenix&state=Arizona%7CAZ); [RideNow Powersports Phoenix](https://www.ridenowsurprise.com/).*

## 2. The Riding

**The dawn patrol — his weekly routine (pre-crash):** Saturday 5:00 AM alarm, roll the R6 out of the garage by 5:30 AM to beat the desert heat and the traffic. Meets 2–3 riding friends at the **Circle K at Rural & Warner in Tempe** for fuel-up and a Red Bull. Wave out by 5:45 AM.

**The canyon ride — the weekly classic:** **Bush Highway → Saguaro Lake → Beeline Highway loop** run aggressively. The same East Valley loop the cruiser archetype did, but at a 15–25 mph faster pace through the curves, leaned over to the pegs on every apex. Back at a shaded coffee shop in Fountain Hills by 8:00 AM before it hits 100°F.

**The long canyon day — monthly:** **Mt. Lemmon Scenic Byway (AZ-198)** — 27 miles of mountain switchbacks from Tucson to the top of the Santa Catalinas. 2.5 hours from Tempe each way. Leaves 4:00 AM Saturdays to get first tracks on the mountain before bicycles and tourists swamp it. Lunch at Summerhaven at the top. This ride is the canyon-rider's religion in AZ.

**Occasional big-day rides:** Yarnell Pass (AZ-89 from Congress to Prescott), Oak Creek Canyon (Sedona to Flagstaff via AZ-89A), the Devil's Highway (US-191 from Clifton to Alpine, for the masochists).

**Track days:** Maybe 3–4 per year at **Inde Motorsports Ranch, Willcox AZ** — AZ's primary road-racing circuit, 3 hours southeast of Phoenix. Saturday novice/intermediate group. Camps overnight in his truck with the bike loaded in the back.

**Community structure:** Small rotating group of 2–4 other sportbike riders in the East Valley. Mostly solo or duos. He's in a Facebook group "**Sportbike Riders of Arizona**" and a Discord server. Has casual familiarity with **Sportbike Riders of Tucson** (real meetup group for Mt. Lemmon runs) but doesn't make it down often. **Not in a club.** No HOG. No cruiser bars. Sportbike culture in AZ is loose — groups of 3–4 who meet up via text thread, not formal chapters.

**Riding philosophy:** He'd tell you he's not a squid. He wears full gear every ride, rides predictably in traffic, saves the speed for the canyon where he knows the road. He's been riding 8 years, 3 years on the R6. He's not the guy splitting lanes at 90 on the 101 — he's the guy railing the Bush Hwy twisties at 7 AM while most of Phoenix is still asleep.

Lemmon Scenic Byway — GreatMotorcycleRoads](https://greatmotorcycleroads.com/arizona/); [Sportbike Riders of Tucson](https://www.meetup.com/sportbike-riders-of-tucson/); [Inde Motorsports Ranch track days](https://www.indemotorsportsranch.com/); [RideNow Surprise Arizona best rides](https://www.ridenowsurprise.com/blog/the-best-motorcycle-rides-in-arizona-top-routes-for-every-rider--88420).*

## 3. The Gear (head-to-toe)

AZ has **no universal helmet law for riders 18+** (ARS § 28-964) — but sportbike culture wears full gear. Full-face, armored jacket, pants, boots, gloves, back protector. Ty is a full-gear rider every ride. It saved his life in the crash.

- **Helmet:** **Shoei X-Fourteen** in Team Yamaha blue-white-red colorway (matches the R6). $800+ new. Full-face, race-derived, pinlock antifog insert. 3 years old, small scuff on the chin bar from a low-side in a parking lot his first month. Replaced after the crash — the old one went into the trash with fractures along the crown.
- **Eyewear:** Integrated with helmet; no separate glasses. Wears Oakley Holbrook XL off-bike.
- **Jacket:** **Alpinestars GP Plus R v2 leather jacket**, black with white accents. Armor at shoulders, elbows, back. Perforated panels for AZ heat. Saved the skin on his torso in the crash.
- **Pants:** **Alpinestars Missile v2 leather pants**, black. Armor at knees and hips. Connected to the jacket via zipper. Saved his skin on his right leg in the crash — though the femur fracture happened anyway because leather can't stop a 50 mph impact with a car's front bumper.
- **Back protector:** **Alpinestars Nucleon KR-2 Level 2** — worn under the jacket. Very common in sportbike culture; required for track days.
- **Boots:** **Alpinestars SMX-6 v2 boots**, black with red accents. Armored ankle, reinforced shin plate, sliders on the toe. Steel-toe optional (he has the non-steel version for lighter weight).
- **Gloves:** **Alpinestars GP Plus R v2 gauntlet gloves**, black leather with perforation and kevlar stitching. Knuckle armor. Sliders on the palm.
- **Airbag vest (new this year):** **Alpinestars Tech-Air Street 2** — electronic airbag system that triggers on crash impact, inflates around the torso and collarbone. Deployed in his crash — cracked rib but no punctured lung (what would have happened without it).
- **Backpack for canyon days:** Kriega R25 (sportbike-specific, tight against the back, no wind drag).

**Summer 115°F (June–September):** Switches to **Alpinestars Air-Flo mesh pants** for commute rides. Keeps the leather for canyon runs even at 115°F — because the hospital doesn't care how hot you were. Drinks electrolytes before and after. Never a cotton t-shirt as the only layer (that's squid territory).

**Winter 40°F canyon mornings:** Adds an Alpinestars Windstopper base layer under the leather, and heated grips (factory accessory for R6) turned to medium.

**Cost of gear** (pre-airbag): ~$2,800 head-to-toe. Airbag adds another $800. Total ~$3,600 in gear vs. $11,800 bike = roughly 30% gear-to-bike ratio, which is the sportbike norm (cruiser guys run 5–10%).

*Sources: [AZ Motorcycle Helmet Law ARS § 28-964](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/28/00964.htm); Alpinestars product lineup + rider forum consensus on AZ-heat gear; Shoei X-Fourteen sportbike standard; Tech-Air airbag adoption among sportbike riders.*
