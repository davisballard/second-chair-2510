## 1. The Bike

**2019 Harley-Davidson Street Glide in Vivid Black.** Bought new at **Desert Wind Harley-Davidson** (922 S Country Club Dr, Mesa AZ 85201 — his hometown dealer, 12 minutes from his house). Financed over 72 months through Eaglemark (HD's lending arm); payoff around $12K when the crash happened. Insurance settled the totaled bike for ~$18K — which is consistent with 2026 used Street Glide values in Phoenix ($15–18K for '18–'20 model years per Cycle Trader, Buddy Stubbs, and HD-of-Scottsdale listings).

**Why Street Glide holds up (validated, not changed):** The Street Glide is Harley's most popular full-dress touring bike and the "I made it" purchase for working-class Harley guys who upgraded from a Sportster. Marco's riding timeline in Doc 2 (Sportster → two upgrades → Street Glide new) mirrors the classic Harley owner's ladder. On a $78K household with $2,100/mo mortgage, financing a $22K new Street Glide on a 6-year note is tight-but-realistic for a rider whose identity is wrapped in the bike. The wife's "look" (Doc 2) reads true.

**Trim and mods:**
- **107 Milwaukee-Eight engine** (stock 2019 trim — he didn't pay up for the 114 upgrade).
- **Vance & Hines Monster Round slip-on exhaust** — the single most common Harley mod among Phoenix-area working-class riders; changes the sound without changing the bike's essence.
- **Stock windshield** (not replaced — he likes the factory height for I-10 wind).
- **Bagger saddlebags** stock — used for his lunch Yeti + rain gear on long rides.
- **Stock mid-controls** (not forward controls — he rides more than he poses).
- Small American flag pinstripe decal on the tank he added himself.

## 2. The Riding

**Weekend routine (pre-crash):** Saturday mornings, 6:00 AM alarm, out the door by 7:00 AM. Meets "the guys" (6–7 regulars) at the Circle K parking lot on Signal Butte & US-60 in east Mesa — a standard pre-ride gas stop. Wave out between 7:15 and 7:30.

**The home ride — the weekly classic:** **Bush Highway → Saguaro Lake → Beeline Highway loop.** ~40 miles of scenic two-lane starting just east of Mesa, flowing curves along the Salt River, Usery Mountain in the rearview. The Mesa Forum trip guides and Phoenix New Times both describe this as the definitive East Valley ride. Breakfast stop: Sunflower Café in Fountain Hills, or a carne asada burrito at a roadside stand on Bush Hwy if they ride early.

**The big day ride — once a month:** **Apache Trail (AZ-88) to Tortilla Flat and Roosevelt Lake.** Tight twisties, Canyon Lake overlook, lunch at the Tortilla Flat Superstition Restaurant & Saloon (prickly-pear ice cream for the 7-year-old's sake of Instagram; for him it's a burger and a root beer).

**The long haul — 4x/year:** **Beeline Highway (AZ-87) north to Payson**, breakfast at **Tiny's Restaurant on US-260** (the Lets Just Ride meetup group's standard Payson breakfast stop per their published routes). Continues to Pine, Strawberry, Mogollon Rim if weather holds. Home by late afternoon.

**HOG affiliation:** **East Valley Chapter #4507** — the Desert Wind-sponsored HOG chapter. Marco's a paid member but not an officer; he attends maybe 4–5 chapter rides a year and skips the monthly 3rd-Wednesday meeting at "the Hog Pen" (behind the dealership) unless there's a free BBQ. His Saturday rides are with "the guys," not the chapter — same faces every week, no formal structure.

**NOT in a club:** Marco is an independent rider. He's not Hells Angels, not Mongols, not Vagos, not the AZ Bandidos. His leather vest has no club patches, no 1%er diamond, no rockers. Just a plain working vest with a small American flag and maybe a Harley shield pin. He's seen the club guys at gas stations, nods, keeps moving.

*Sources: [Best Motorcycle Ride Near Phoenix — Bush Highway, Apache Trail, Tortilla Flat](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZOEzFw5jN6U); [Bush Hwy Saguaro Lake Payson Mesa Forum](https://www.tripadvisor.com/ShowTopic-g31281-i1076-k8265138-Bush_Highway_Saguaro_Lake_Payson-Mesa_Arizona.html); [Lets Just Ride Motorcycle Group meetup](https://www.meetup.com/lets_just_ride_motorcycle_group/); [East Valley HOG Chapter #4507](https://www.eastvalleyhog.com/); [Kelly's Kawasaki Top 10 AZ Rides](https://www.kellyskawasaki.com/arizonarides).*

## 3. The Gear (head-to-toe)

AZ has **no universal helmet law for riders 18+** (ARS § 28-964) — riders can choose. Marco chooses a helmet in the summer because of the sun, not the law. Eye protection is required regardless of helmet.

- **Helmet:** **Bell Custom 500 in matte black.** Low-profile half-shell with leather trim. The most popular working-class Harley half-shell in AZ. ($165 new; Marco's had this one for 3 years — small scuff on the right side from a garage knock.)
- **Eyewear:** **Gatorz Magnum** aluminum-frame sunglasses, smoke lens. Built for desert glare and wind; favorites among AZ riders. When he's off the bike he wears them around his neck on a retainer.
- **Vest:** **Interstate Leather plain brown full-grain leather vest** — no club patches, no rockers, no 1%er diamond. Small American flag pin on the left lapel. Small Harley shield pin on the right. Inside left pocket carries his wallet and phone. Weathered from 4 summers of daily wear.
- **T-shirt:** Plain black cotton tee under the vest on cooler days; an old **Desert Wind Harley-Davidson dealer tee** (from the 2022 Arizona Bike Week) on hotter days. The dealer tee is thin and faded at the collar.
- **Jeans:** **Levi's 514 straight-fit in dark wash**, worn in, small grease smudges at the knees from HVAC work. Not Kevlar-lined — too hot for AZ summers, and honestly he never saw the point.
- **Boots:** **Harley-Davidson Jerry men's motorcycle boots** — black leather, lace-up, scuffed to hell from 3 years of daily wear. Steel toe (HVAC job requirement, double-duty on the bike).
- **Gloves:** **Biltwell Bantam short-cuff gloves in black** — ventilated, summer-weight, no armor. He carries heavier winter gloves (HD Factor leather) in the saddlebag November–February.
- **Belt:** Plain brown leather with a small chrome buckle — not one of the turquoise-stone Southwestern buckles (he's not trying).
- **Watch:** Timex Weekender with a brown leather strap. Replaced once in the last 5 years.
- **Wedding band:** Plain silver, left hand, worn smooth.

**Summer 115°F variation (June–Sept):** Drops the vest on the hottest rides — just the faded dealer tee. Still wears the Bell and Gatorz. Switches to a mesh Alpinestars riding shirt once or twice a summer for Payson runs where elevation change matters.

**Winter 45°F variation (Dec–Jan mornings):** Adds a lined leather jacket over the vest, switches gloves to HD Factor, adds a thin scarf wrapped once around the neck. He doesn't own heated gear — too expensive, AZ winter rarely justifies it.

*Sources: [AZ helmet law ARS § 28-964](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/28/00964.htm); [Arizona Motorcycle Helmet Laws — Phillips Law](https://phillipslaw.com/phoenix-az-motorcycle-accident-lawyer/safety-tips/helmet-laws/); Bell Helmet Custom 500 + Biltwell gear standard — common across AZ rider forum threads.*

## 5. The Work

**Employer archetype:** Mid-size East Valley **commercial HVAC shop** — roughly 25–40 techs, services strip malls, restaurants, small office buildings, light industrial across the Valley. Plausible archetype: **Midstate Mechanical (founded 1986)** or **Howard Air's commercial division**. Doc 2 locks "small commercial HVAC company" — this is a ~30-person outfit, not Parker & Sons (residential giant) or George Brazil (residential). Commercial work = more consistent schedule, less emergency-call chaos than residential, but heavier equipment lifting.

For AI prompting purposes, treat the employer as a representative regional commercial shop — a realistic placeholder name is **"Valley Commercial Mechanical"** (archetype, not a real business) with logo livery in white + red + black.

**Work truck:**
- **2022 Ford Transit 250 cargo van** in fleet white.
- **Company logo** on both driver and passenger doors (simple sans-serif, company name + phone number + contractor license number).
- **Roof rack** with a copper pipe storage tube strapped on.
- **Inside the cab:** Service Titan tablet on a RAM mount bolted to the dash. Yeti Rambler tumbler (coffee in winter, ice water in summer) in the center console. Milwaukee M18 drill case on the passenger floor. Clipboard with work orders. A small American flag decal on the rear windshield.
- **Cargo area:** Adrian Steel shelving system, bins labeled (capacitors, contactors, filters), a section for copper pipe and flex duct.

**Daily routine:**
- **5:30 AM:** Alarm. Coffee. Out the door by 6:00.
- **6:15 AM:** At the shop (Mesa / Tempe yard), loads parts for the day.
- **7:00 AM – 4:30 PM:** 3–5 service calls across the Valley. Summer work = roof units at 135°F on the deck, attic work at 150°F. Winter = chasing heat-pump defrost issues at strip malls in Chandler.
- **Summer overtime:** When a compressor dies on a 112°F day at a restaurant, he's the on-call. Time-and-a-half. He takes the calls. It funds the Street Glide payment.

**Pay:**
- **Base:** $62,000/yr (mid-career commercial service tech, 15 years experience)
- **Overtime + commission on parts:** ~$14,000/yr on a normal summer
- **Total:** ~$76K pretax. Wife at the dental office (part-time, 2 days/week, ~$15/hr admin, not hygienist) brings in ~$12K. Combined household ~$78K — tracks Doc 2.

**Certifications:** EPA 608 Universal (required for refrigerant handling). NATE Core + Commercial Air Conditioning. No degree — high school and the Mesa-area trade school (East Valley Institute of Technology, EVIT) pathway. He's proud of being self-made. Walks onto a roof and knows exactly what's wrong inside 90 seconds.

**Self-image about the trade:** He doesn't call himself "blue-collar" but it's the identity. He respects the work. He laughs at guys who think they're better than a guy who fixes their AC in August. His truck is clean inside — a professional's truck, not a mess.

*Sources: [Phoenix HVAC tech salary — Glassdoor $74K avg](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/phoenix-hvac-technician-salary-SRCH_IL.0,7_IM678_KO8,23.htm); [Parker & Sons salaries — $59–83K base, $100K total with commission](https://www.indeed.com/cmp/Parker-and-Sons-1/salaries/HVAC-Technician/Phoenix-AZ); [Midstate Mechanical — commercial HVAC Phoenix](https://midstatemechanical.com/); [Howard Air & Plumbing commercial Phoenix](https://howardair.com/commercial-and-business-hvac-services/); [Fleet Wrap HQ HVAC van wraps](https://fleetwraphq.com/industry/hvac-vehicle-wraps/).*

## 8. Voice & Media Diet

**How he talks about the crash:**
- "Got hit." Not "was in an accident" or "the incident." *Got hit.*
- "She didn't see me." Not "failed to yield." His exact framing — echoed in the M3 hook "A driver didn't see the rider."
- "Laid it down at 40." Technical rider language.
- "Doc says 6 months before I'm on a bike again." Not "the physician advises."
- About insurance: "They called the next day. Offered me thirty-five to go away." He's suspicious, not yet angry.
