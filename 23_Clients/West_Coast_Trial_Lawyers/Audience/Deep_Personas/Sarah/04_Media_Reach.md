## 4. Meta Targeting Spec (Depesh's layer)

Meta's **Special Ad Category: Social Issues** constraint applies — no demographic or interest targeting for legal services. Our precision comes from **creative filtering + placement + location + lookalikes (post-launch)**.

**What we CAN target:**
- **Location:** Arizona statewide; higher weight to Maricopa County (64% of AZ trucking crashes occur there — Evidence Register §2.4) and Coconino County (Flagstaff, where she lives and where I-17 corridor commuters live). Yavapai County (Prescott Valley, Sedona, Cottonwood) also relevant for the I-17 corridor.
- **Age:** 18–65+ broad (SAC prohibits narrow age bands).
- **Placement:** **Facebook Feed + FB Reels + Instagram Feed + Instagram Stories.** NOT Audience Network (quality concerns). NOT Messenger (wrong register for PI services).
- **Language:** English primary.
- **Device:** Mobile primary (90%+ of her reachable attention).

**What we CAN'T target (SAC restriction):**
- "Nurse" interest categories.
- "Recently in an accident" lookalikes (not pre-launch).
- Narrow age bands like 35–45.
- Gender (Sarah's demo is 55–60% female form-fillers, but SAC blocks gender targeting here).

**Creative as targeting — the workaround:**
Because we can't target Sarah directly, our **creative hook does the qualifying work.** S1's "Hit by a semi on I-10?" self-qualifies: if you weren't hit by a semi, you scroll past; if you were, you stop. The algorithm learns who's stopping (Sarah-type profiles) and delivers to more of that demographic.

**Key insight vs. Ty's S1 targeting:** For Sarah, the creative filter is *even harder* (trucking is a smaller pool than motorcycle), so expect:
- Higher CPM than motorcycle creative (smaller addressable audience).
- But higher sign-rate per lead (because the creative filters hard and the leads who respond are genuinely trucking-relevant).
- Net CPA could be favorable despite the CPM premium — that's the trucking-segment value equation.

**Lookalike seed strategy (Phase 2 if we have data):**
- 1% LAL from S1 quiz completers (once we have 50+).
- 1% LAL from S1 lead-form submitters (more sensitive signal).
- Retargeting pool: users who engaged with S1 creative (views ≥ 50% of video, tapped but didn't convert).

**Frequency cap:** **3 impressions/person/week** — slightly tighter than Ty's (4) because Sarah's analytical-processing style means repeated exposure reads as "aggressive marketing." She'd notice 4 hits per week and tag the firm as "desperate." 3 is the ceiling.

## 5. Language Patterns (USE / AVOID — full set)

How Sarah actually talks and thinks. **These are the words that should appear in S1 copy + landing page + intake. Words NOT in these lists should not appear.**

### How she describes the crash:
- ✅ "A truck hit me on I-17." / "A Freightliner lost a tire and the trailer swung." / "I was southbound at Sunset Point."
- ❌ "I was involved in a motor vehicle incident." (Legalese — she'd groan.) / "A reckless trucker crashed into my car." (Emotion without fact.)

### How she describes herself professionally:
- ✅ "ICU nurse." / "I run a unit." / "I'm at Flagstaff Medical Center — charge nurse." / "Trauma ICU." / "I do some outreach down to Verde Valley."
- ❌ "Healthcare professional." (Too vague.) / "Registered nurse." (Accurate but she'd specialize.) / "Medical caregiver." (Impersonal.)

### How she describes the trucking company:
- ✅ "The logistics company." / "The Bakersfield outfit." / "The carrier." / "The company that owned the rig."
- ❌ "The defendant." (Premature legalese.) / "The at-fault party." (Generic PI-speak.) / "The trucker." (She separates the driver from the corporation — driver isn't the deep-pocket.)

### How she describes the crash-mechanics (clinical):
- ✅ "Front-left tire blew. Overcorrection. Trailer swung right. I braked and swerved. 1.5 seconds of reaction time."
- ✅ "Compound mid-shaft humerus. Clavicle. Three ribs. Spleen lac, Grade II, managed non-op."
- These are direct clinical quotes. **Honor them in her copy voice.**

### How she describes her injuries:
- ✅ "Compound humerus." / "Left arm." / "Ribs." / "Spleen lac." / "Concussion."
- ❌ "Devastating injuries." / "Life-altering wounds." / "Brutally injured." (Emotive adjectives she'd find performative.)

### How she describes the adjuster:
- ✅ "The adjuster called on day three." / "They offered thirty-five to close." / "I know what my ER bill was — and it was forty-seven."
- ❌ "The insurance company is acting in bad faith." (She'd think it — wouldn't say it yet without counsel.)

### How she describes lawyers (before hiring):
- ✅ "I probably need a lawyer." / "I've been putting it off." / "I don't know which one to pick." / "The billboard guys feel wrong." / "I want someone who knows trucking specifically."
- ❌ "I need an aggressive attorney to fight for me." (She'd never say "aggressive" — that's a red flag for her.)

### How she describes lawyers (after hiring one she respects):
- ✅ "My attorney handles it." / "They're specialists." / "They sent me the plan of action." / "They explained the medical liens."

### How she describes her recovery:
- ✅ "Six to eight months out on full function." / "External fix for six weeks, then internal." / "PT three times a week." / "I'm back on admin-only at sixty percent pay."
- ❌ "I'm so blessed to be alive." (Not her register.) / "Every day is a miracle." (Not her.)

### How she describes her family situation:
- ✅ "Marcus is carrying more right now." / "Oliver's five, he's been great." / "My sister flew in." / "We're burning through the emergency fund."
- ❌ "My family is devastated." (Too emotive.) / "My poor husband..." (Pitying tone she'd reject.)

### Language we should USE in S1 creative:
- "Commercial trucks carry $1M+ insurance policies"
- "Their companies have legal teams ready from day one"
- "Hit by a semi on I-17" (or I-10)
- "Arizona truck crash victims"
- "Find out what the law says"
- "Free case review — 60 seconds"
- "No fee unless we win"
- "Commercial insurance is different"

### Language we should NEVER use in S1 creative:
- "Entitled to MILLIONS" (triggers her billboard-lawyer alarm)
- "Fight for your rights" (empty phrase she'd scroll past)
- "Our aggressive legal team" (counterproductive — signals desperation)
- "We fight the insurance companies" (redundant + emotive)
- "You deserve..." (pitying frame)
- "Injured in an accident" (too generic)
- "Free consultation with our caring attorneys" (infantilizing)
- Any phrase a late-night TV lawyer would say

## 6. Creative Format Preferences

Based on Sarah's platform behavior + mood states:

**9:16 vertical video (FB Reels + IG Reels + Stories):** PRIMARY. Consumption format for her 9:30 PM scroll. **15–30 seconds** max. Sound-off mandatory (she might be next to sleeping Marcus). Captions required. **Slightly higher production value than Ty's** — she'll read "UGC handheld" as unprofessional; she wants documentary-realism elevated style (her style sheet preference).

**1:1 square (FB Feed):** SECONDARY. For static variants of S1. She responds to strong composition — a single compelling frame she can parse in 2 seconds.

**4:5 portrait (IG Feed):** Tertiary.

**Video lead-form ads (Meta's native format):** **Worth testing for S1 specifically.** Pre-fills her name/email via FB; she's more likely to complete a form than a landing-page quiz because of her time-pressure. Trade-off: lead quality may be lower than quiz-completer leads; test in Phase 2.

**NOT a format for her:**
- Carousel ads (too much work to consume).
- Playable ads.
- Shopping ads (wrong category).
- Long-form text posts (she won't read).

**Creative length:** keep it short. 15-second ads may outperform 30-second for her because she's impatient with production she deems inefficient. Test both.

## 7. Frequency + Fatigue Guardrails

Sarah will see our S1 creative repeatedly over the 12-week window.

- **Frequency cap:** 3 impressions/person/week (tighter than Ty).
- **Creative refresh:** Swap new variants at Week 3 once CTR declines 15%+ from launch baseline. Because S1 is 1-of-6 creatives, she'll see other assets in rotation — this reduces fatigue naturally.
- **Burnout signal:** Sarah + her demo segment — watch for CTR drop paired with CPM climb. She'll stop engaging with repeated creative before she stops engaging with varied creative.
- **Retargeting refresh:** if she hit the landing page but bounced, serve her a deeper-funnel creative (not more of the same top-of-funnel S1). The deeper-funnel creative should be intake-specialist-voice ("Can we answer questions about your commercial-trucking case?") — calendar booking CTA.
