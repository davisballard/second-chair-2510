## 1. The Professional Role

**Trauma ICU Charge Nurse + Clinical Outreach Specialist at Flagstaff Medical Center (FMC)** — the only Level I Trauma Center north of Phoenix, operated by **Northern Arizona Healthcare (NAH)**. FMC is at 1200 N Beaver St, Flagstaff, AZ 86001.

**What that title actually means operationally:**

- **Charge nurse on 3× 12-hour shifts/week** in the medical-surgical ICU (managing 6–10 critical beds during her shift — running the floor, not at bedside)
- **Outreach consult role** 1–2 days/month, traveling to NAH's affiliate **Verde Valley Medical Center in Cottonwood** for trauma ICU specialist consultation + occasional coverage
- **Ad-hoc collaboration trips** to Banner Health sites in Phoenix (Banner-University Medical Center Phoenix for trauma case collaboration; Banner Desert for occasional coverage)
- **Occasionally flies to Payson Regional Medical Center** for consults — but usually drives the Beeline Highway route

**Credentials (all verifiable, industry-standard):**

- **BSN** — Bachelor of Science in Nursing, Northern Arizona University (2010)
- **RN** — Arizona Board of Nursing licensed since 2010
- **CCRN** — Critical Care Registered Nurse (AACN certification)
- **TNCC** — Trauma Nursing Core Course (Emergency Nurses Association)
- **Currently pursuing CNML** — Certified Nurse Manager and Leader (AONE) — 80% through the program, exam scheduled for June 2026 (now postponed due to crash)

**Why a charge nurse commutes:** NAH's rural-affiliate outreach structure means specialist-grade clinical coverage is shared across their Flagstaff and Verde Valley facilities. A senior trauma ICU RN at FMC is exactly the profile they'd tap for rotating consult days in Cottonwood (1 hr 45 min south via I-17 → AZ-260). Banner Phoenix trips are separate — occasional multi-system coordination on specific cases.

**Pay band:** $82,000/yr base at FMC + \~$3K/yr outreach stipend = \~$85K gross. Solid for AZ nursing; below Phoenix-metro nursing (Banner-Mayo charge nurses hit $100K+) but standard for rural-regional charge role.

*Sources: [Flagstaff Medical Center — Northern Arizona Healthcare](https://www.nahealth.com/); [FMC Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagstaff_Medical_Center) — Level I trauma center, only one north of Phoenix; [Trauma Survivors Network — FMC](https://www.traumasurvivorsnetwork.org/trauma-centers-27/); [AACN CCRN certification](https://www.aacn.org/certification/get-certified/ccrn-adult); [ENA TNCC](https://www.ena.org/education/tncc); standard AZ nursing salary benchmarks per Glassdoor + BLS.*

## 2. The Commute + Vehicle (the crash context)

**Vehicle:** **2022 Subaru Outback in Crystal White Pearl.** AWD. Paid off in 2024 — she and Marcus bought it when Oliver was born because the commute + winter in Flagstaff demanded it. Equipped with roof crossbars (no cargo box), a "I Brake for Saguaros" sticker on the back windshield (Marcus put it there, she kept it), a FMC parking permit hangtag in the rearview mirror, a Dharma rock-climbing sticker (her brother's gym in Tempe), an NAU Lumberjacks decal. Interior: reusable grocery bags always in the cargo, a Yeti Rambler 20oz in the cup holder, a trail running shoe in the passenger footwell from the last time she did the Schultz Loop Trail pre-crash, a well-worn copy of *The Patient Will See You Now* by Eric Topol in the backseat door pocket.

**The commute corridor:**

- **Regular weekly:** Flagstaff home → FMC (12 min drive, I-40 exit, downtown Flagstaff)
- **1–2×/month outreach trips:** Flagstaff → Verde Valley Medical Center, Cottonwood via I-17 southbound (1 hr 45 min). Leaves 5:30 AM on outreach days, back by 6:30 PM.
- **2–3×/year Banner Phoenix trips:** Flagstaff → Banner-University Medical Center Phoenix via I-17 all the way down (2 hr 30 min). Usually multi-day, stays with her sister in Peoria.
- **Winter concern:** snow on I-17 between Flagstaff (7,000 ft elevation) and the Verde Valley descent (\~4,000 ft) makes her respect the road. She's driven it in whiteouts. **This is why she's on it.** Another RN wouldn't — she does because she's the kind of nurse who reliably shows up.

**The crash location:** **Southbound I-17, mile marker 268, the Sunset Point rest area descent.** Real stretch of AZ highway. Technical decline with switchbacks, trucks climbing slow on the north grade, cars descending fast southbound. **AZ DOT has logged Sunset Point as a multi-year motorcycle and commercial-vehicle incident hotspot.** 10:40 AM Monday, 2026-04-06 (approximately — persona timeline, not real date).

**Crash mechanics (rendered from Doc 2, retained verbatim for continuity):** An 18-wheeler Freightliner pulling a dry van (registered to a mid-size Bakersfield CA logistics company) was in the left lane southbound, also going south. The truck's front-left tire blew. The driver overcorrected. The trailer swung across the right lane. Sarah had \~1.5 seconds to react. She braked and swerved right. The trailer clipped the driver's side of the Outback, crushing the front quarter panel, shoving her into the guardrail at \~50 mph. Side curtain airbags deployed. **Left arm pinned between door and center console.** Conscious the whole time. Airlifted to Banner-University Medical Center Phoenix (60 miles closer than FMC ironically — her own hospital).

**Injuries:**

- **Compound mid-shaft left humerus fracture** — the career-defining injury
- **Fractured left clavicle**
- **3 broken ribs (left side)**
- **Grade II lacerated spleen** — managed non-surgically (angio embolization)
- **Mild concussion** (Grade II MTBI)
- **Multiple contusions, left side**
- Surgery Day 1: external fixation of humerus
- Surgery Week 6: internal fixation (plate + 8 screws)
- Physical therapy: ongoing, months out
