## 1. The Bike

**2020 Yamaha YZF-R6 in Team Yamaha Blue and white** — the final year of road-legal R6 production (Yamaha discontinued the road R6 after 2020 in favor of the R7). A 2020 is now collector-grade for supersport guys who want the last 600cc true race-replica inline-four; **Phoenix used prices run $11,000–$14,000** on Cycle Trader and private listings. Ty bought his 3 years ago at 26 from a private seller in Gilbert for $11,800 — his first supersport after stepping up from a 2015 Honda CBR500R (the common "starter sportbike" ladder).

**Why R6 fits** — iconic middleweight supersport, ubiquitous in AZ canyon and track-day culture. Yamaha's own demographic research says the middleweight sportbike buyer median age is 27 → Ty (29) fits exactly. The R6 is the single most common bike at Inde Motorsports Ranch track days. For a working-class AZ guy at 29 with 8 years of riding, a used 2020 R6 is both peak desire and reachable stretch purchase.

**Stock and mods:**
- 118 hp at 14,500 rpm 600cc inline-four, ride-by-wire throttle (factory)
- **Puig Naked New Generation double-bubble windscreen (smoke)** — the single most common R6 mod for reducing 70+ mph canyon wind blast
- **Black T-Rex frame sliders** — crash protection added at purchase
- **Driven Racing rear sets** — aggressive foot position for canyon carving
- **Rizoma aftermarket levers** — blue anodized to match tank
- **Akrapovič slip-on exhaust** — louder and lighter than stock, added by prior owner
- **Michelin Power 5 tires** — standard AZ canyon choice, grippy in desert heat
- Small Yamaha tuning-fork decal on the tail

*Sources: [Motofomo R6 Buyer's Guide](https://motofomo.com/yamaha-r6-buyers-guide/); [TopSpeed R6 supersport review](https://www.topspeed.com/reasons-why-the-yamaha-r6-is-the-best-supersport/); [RideApart 2026 R7 demographic notes](https://www.rideapart.com/reviews/792423/2026-yamaha-r7-sportbike-first-ride-motorcycle-review-specs-price-features/); [Cycle Trader Phoenix R6 inventory](https://www.cycletrader.com/motorcycles-for-sale?make=Yamaha%7C2317008&model=R6&city=phoenix&state=Arizona%7CAZ).*

## 2. The Riding

**Dawn patrol — his weekly routine (pre-crash):** Saturday 5:00 AM alarm, roll the R6 out of the garage by 5:30 AM to beat the heat and traffic. Meets 2–3 friends at the **Circle K at Rural & Warner, Tempe** for fuel-up and a Red Bull. Wave out by 5:45 AM.

**The canyon ride — weekly classic:** **Bush Highway → Saguaro Lake → Beeline Highway loop** run aggressively. Same East Valley loop as the cruiser archetype did, but 15–25 mph faster through the curves, leaned over to the pegs on every apex. Home at a shaded Fountain Hills coffee shop by 8:00 AM before it hits 100°F.

**Long canyon day — monthly:** **Mt. Lemmon Scenic Byway (AZ-198)** — 27 miles of mountain switchbacks from Tucson to the top of the Santa Catalinas. 2.5 hours each way from Tempe. Leaves 4:00 AM Saturdays to get first tracks before bicycles and tourists swamp it. Lunch at Summerhaven at the top. **The canyon-rider's religion in AZ.**

**Other big-day rides:** Yarnell Pass (AZ-89), Oak Creek Canyon (AZ-89A, Sedona → Flagstaff), Devil's Highway (US-191, for the masochists).

**Track days:** 3–4/year at **Inde Motorsports Ranch, Willcox AZ** — AZ's primary road-racing circuit. Saturday novice/intermediate group. Camps overnight in his truck with the bike loaded in the back.

**Community structure:** Small rotating group of 2–4 East Valley sportbike riders. Mostly solo or duos. In the Facebook group "Sportbike Riders of Arizona" and a Discord. Casual familiarity with **Sportbike Riders of Tucson** for Mt. Lemmon runs but not a regular. **Not in a club.** No HOG, no cruiser bars. Sportbike culture in AZ is loose groups of 3–4 meeting up via text thread, not formal chapters.

**Riding philosophy:** He'd tell you he's not a squid. Full gear every ride, predictable in traffic, saves the speed for the canyon where he knows the road. 8 years riding, 3 on the R6. Not the guy splitting lanes at 90 on the 101 — he's the guy railing Bush Hwy twisties at 7 AM while most of Phoenix is still asleep.

*Sources: [Mt. Lemmon Scenic Byway](https://greatmotorcycleroads.com/arizona/); [Sportbike Riders of Tucson Meetup](https://www.meetup.com/sportbike-riders-of-tucson/); [Inde Motorsports Ranch](https://www.indemotorsportsranch.com/); [RideNow Surprise best rides list](https://www.ridenowsurprise.com/blog/the-best-motorcycle-rides-in-arizona-top-routes-for-every-rider--88420).*

## 3. The Gear (head-to-toe)

AZ has **no universal helmet law for riders 18+** (ARS § 28-964) — but sportbike culture wears full gear. Ty is a full-gear rider every ride. **It saved his life in the crash.**

|---|---|---|
| Helmet | **Shoei X-Fourteen**, Team Yamaha blue-white-red | $800+ new; full-face race-derived; 3 years old; scuffed chin bar from an old low-side; replaced after crash (old one cracked along crown) |
| Eyewear | Integrated with helmet; Oakley Holbrook XL off-bike | — |
| Jacket | **Alpinestars GP Plus R v2**, black w/ white accents | CE Level 2 armor at shoulders/elbows/back; perforated AZ-heat panels; saved torso skin in the crash |
| Pants | **Alpinestars Missile v2**, black | Armor at knees/hips; zips to jacket; saved right leg skin (femur fracture happened anyway) |

| Airbag | **Alpinestars Tech-Air Street 2** | Electronic airbag; deployed in crash; cracked a rib but prevented punctured lung |

**Summer 115°F (Jun–Sep):** Alpinestars Air-Flo mesh pants for commute; full leather on canyon runs regardless of heat ("hospital doesn't care how hot you were"). Electrolytes before/after. Never just a cotton tee ("squid territory").

**Winter 40°F canyon mornings:** Alpinestars Windstopper base layer under leather + heated grips (R6 factory accessory) on medium.

**Cost ratio:** ~$2,800 gear + $800 airbag = ~$3,600 total vs. $11,800 bike = **~30% gear-to-bike ratio** (sportbike norm; cruiser guys run 5–10%).

*Sources: [AZ Helmet Law ARS § 28-964](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/28/00964.htm); Alpinestars product lineup + sportbike-rider forum consensus on AZ-heat gear.*

## 4. The Home

**Tempe, AZ (85282)** — closer to the younger rider scene, ASU proximity, cheaper housing than Gilbert/Chandler, faster freeway access to canyon rides. 85282 is working-to-lower-middle-class, majority white with significant Latino and Asian populations, median household income lower than east Mesa. **Starter-adult demographic.**

**The home:**
- **Rental 2-bedroom / 1-bath stucco duplex or small standalone rental**, 1970s build, beige stucco, flat roof, concrete slab, no real yard (small fenced patio)
- **Attached 1-car garage** — critically important; the R6 lives here (won't park a $12K sportbike in a Tempe apartment lot overnight: theft + sun damage). Garage has Abba Superbike stand ($150), peg board with small tools, mini-fridge with White Claw and Modelo, WD-40, chain lube shelf.
- **Rent:** ~$1,650/month (Tempe 2025 market)
- **Neighborhood archetype:** Broadway & McClintock area. Stucco duplexes, some small older houses, ASU rental stock mixed with starter-adult couples. Mandatory desert landscaping (Tempe water rules). Renter-transient neighbors (change every 2–3 years).
- **The street:** Arterial-adjacent side streets, sidewalks one side, narrow cul-de-sacs, scattered palm trees, block walls painted different colors per era.

**Not Mesa.** Ty commutes to East Valley job sites from Tempe. AZ sportbike demographic lives Tempe / Chandler / Ahwatukee — Mesa is cruiser / family territory.

**Distances:**
- Inde Motorsports Ranch (Willcox): 3 hrs SE
- Mt. Lemmon (Tucson): 2.5 hrs SE
- Bush Highway trailhead: 20 min E
- HVAC shop yard (Mesa): 15–25 min E
- Banner Desert (where he recovered from surgery): 15 min E

*Sources: [ZIP 85282 demographics](https://www.zipdatamaps.com/85282); [Tempe Redfin rental market](https://www.redfin.com/city/17830/AZ/Tempe); [Tempe Neighborhood profiles](https://www.neighborhoodscout.com/az/tempe/demographics).*

## 5. The Work

**Employer archetype:** Mid-size East Valley commercial HVAC shop, ~25–40 techs. Services strip malls, restaurants, small office buildings. Plausible archetype: **Midstate Mechanical** or **Howard Air's commercial division**. Representative fictional name for AI prompting: **"Valley Commercial Mechanical"** (red + white + black livery).

**Experience:** 8 years in trade (started at 21 out of EVIT — East Valley Institute of Technology). No college. Mid-career commercial service tech. Gets the technical calls; not the supervisor calls. **NATE-Core certified, EPA 608 Universal.**

**Work truck:** 2021 Ford Transit 250 cargo van in fleet white. Company logo on both doors. Inside the cab: Service Titan tablet on RAM mount, Yeti Rambler tumbler, red Milwaukee M18 drill case on passenger floor, crumpled Red Bull can in door pocket, Sportbike Riders of Arizona sticker on the rear window. Cargo: Adrian Steel shelving, parts bins, copper pipe tube on the roof.

**Daily routine:**
- 5:30 AM alarm (summer); 6:00 AM (winter)
- 6:00 AM cold brew from the night before, out the door
- 6:30 AM at the Mesa shop yard loading parts
- 7:00 AM – 4:00 PM: 3–5 commercial HVAC service calls. Summer = rooftop units in 140°F deck heat. Winter = gas-heat issues at chain restaurants.

**Pay:** $62K base + $8K summer overtime/commission = **~$70K gross personally**.

**Pre-crash household finances:** Ty $70K + fiancée Rachel $28K (vet tech part-time) = **~$98K household pretax**. Tight because of: wedding savings ($18K target, $12K saved); R6 paid off in 2024 (quiet pride point); $1,650 rent; student loans from trade school. Crash disaster: 3–6 months off work, $3,600 gear to re-buy, totaled $11K bike (insurance paying ~$9K), no short-term disability. Wedding postponed.
