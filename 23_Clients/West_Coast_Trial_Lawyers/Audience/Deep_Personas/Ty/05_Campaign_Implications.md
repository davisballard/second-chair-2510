## 1. SMP → Hooks (how the single-minded proposition translates to M1/M2/M3)

The SMP from §00: *Ty doesn't want sympathy. He wants the record set straight: he was doing the speed limit, wearing the right gear, in the right lane, and a woman making a left turn didn't see him.*

Each hook is a different emotional entry point into the same SMP. They don't compete — they triangulate.

### M1 — Identity hook

**Direction:** Third-person rider-identity. Signals *this is for you specifically* in the first three words. No crash reference; the absence is the point.

**Example hook direction (not locked copy — craft team writes final):**
- "Riders don't ask for sympathy. They ask for what's fair." (Existing from cruiser-era Creative brief — works for Ty too. Tone is identity-forward, not loss-forward.)
- Alternates: "For riders in Arizona who know they were doing everything right." / "If you ride, you already know what happened."

**What it does psychologically:** Activates identity belonging (Ferrier's motivation layer). Bypasses the adjuster-induced shame (Loss 5). Pre-empts the "you're just another biker" presumption.

**Visual direction:** Ty in the garage with the empty R6 spot (§01 §9 — "The empty R6 spot: Abba Superbike stand still there, oil stain on the concrete, Shoei on the workbench"). NOT crash imagery. The absence of the bike carries the emotional weight.

### M2 — Loss hook

**Direction:** Deprivation-forward. What the crash took, quiet and specific.

**Example hook direction:**
- "What the crash took is still being taken." (Existing direction from cruiser-era — still operative.)
- Alternates: "A bike. Nine weeks of work. A wedding pushed to fall. Still counting." / "The bills show up faster than the therapy does."

**What it does psychologically:** Names Loss 4 (the timeline) and Loss 3 (the body) + the financial reality. Makes the $35K adjuster offer feel insufficient without directly naming it (Shotton's loss-aversion reframe).

**Visual direction:** Ty in his Tempe kitchen at morning light (§01 §9 — "Medical bills from Banner Desert on the kitchen table next to a paused Service Titan tablet"). Bandit the dog asleep in a patch of shade. Coffee going cold. Bills specific — visible *Banner Desert Medical Center* letterhead. **Still eye of the storm principle** (Style Sheet §6): Ty composed, environment carries the loss.

### M3 — Injustice hook

**Direction:** The fault-flip. The moment that makes the SMP explicit.

**Example hook direction:**
- "A driver didn't see the rider." (Existing — perfect for Ty, same or stronger than cruiser-era because sportbike visibility is even lower against traffic.)
- Alternates: "She made a left turn. He was doing the speed limit. Guess who got blamed." / "In Arizona, drivers don't see riders. Insurance adjusters don't care."

**What it does psychologically:** Directly refutes the fault presumption (Loss 5, the shadow loss). Gives Ty the *permission* to believe what he already knows in his gut.

**Visual direction:** Southern Avenue & Dobson Road in Mesa at dusk (§01 §9). Ty standing at the curb looking at where it happened. Empty intersection, painted left-turn arrow, Circle K on the corner, amber streetlight switching on. **No crash scene in frame.** (Rejects the exploratory crash-frame direction from earlier in session — stays compliant with the `Week_1_Launch_Concepts.md` "no crash footage" brand rule.)

## 2. Copy Direction (tone + vocabulary)

**Register:** Grounded, competent, specific. The lawyer Ty would hire talks like a tradesman who respects other tradesmen.

**Vocabulary — USE these words (drawn from §04 language patterns):**
- "Riders" · "Got hit" · "A driver didn't see the rider" · "If you ride in Arizona..." · "No fee unless we win" · "Free case review — 60 seconds" · "Bills don't stop because you can't work" · "What the law says" · "Arizona riders" · "Seriously injured"

**Vocabulary — NEVER use (§04):**
- "Entitled to MILLIONS" · "Fight for your rights" · "Our aggressive legal team" · "We fight the insurance companies" · "You deserve..." · "Biker" (cruiser connotation Ty rejects) · "Motorcyclist" (legal term, not his speech) · "The accident" (he says "got hit") · "Victim" · Any late-night-TV-lawyer phrase

**Sentence construction:**
- Short sentences. Tradesman-pacing, not lawyer-pacing.
- Active voice. "A driver didn't see the rider" — not "the rider was not seen by a driver."
- Specific numbers over vague claims. Say "60 seconds" not "quick"; say "No fee unless we win" not "affordable."
- Never a rhetorical question that pities. "Have you been injured?" fails. "Hit by a driver who didn't see you?" works.

**Body-copy template (for reference):**
> A driver didn't see you. An insurance company's first offer isn't designed to. For Arizona riders seriously injured in a motorcycle crash, the law says more. No fee unless we win.

This template hits: fault-flip (first sentence) + adjuster call-out (second) + community + severity filter (third) + risk-reversal (fourth). Under 50 words. Survives the 11PM scroll.

## 4. Compliance Considerations Specific to Ty

- **AZ Bar ER 7.3 (direct solicitation):** All hooks must be third-person. "Riders" / "the rider" / "Arizona riders" — never "you" in headlines. Body copy can shift to second-person once the creative has established the third-person frame (the existing Week_1_Launch_Concepts.md body copy pattern handles this correctly).
- **AZ Bar ER 7.1 (truthfulness):** No specific dollar-amount promises. "The law says more" is compliant; "get millions" is not.
- **AZ Bar ER 7.5 (firm naming):** WCTL name must be identifiable in every ad (logo or end-card).
- **Fault-presumption rigor note (Evidence Register §14):** If Robert at WCTL raises the "aren't bikers mostly at fault?" concern for the supersport demographic — §14.5 of the Evidence Register has the sourced pitch language. Hurt Report: 60–66% of multi-vehicle moto crashes are the other driver's fault. AZ ADOT: 62.6% of AZ moto crashes are multi-vehicle. Pure comparative negligence (A.R.S. § 12-2505) preserves recovery even when speed is alleged. This persona targets the clean-liability subset within a 4x-higher-crash-rate population.
- **TCPA consent language:** Required on the quiz funnel lead-capture. Not our per-ad concern but flagged for the intake team.

## 5. Creative Acid Tests (run before any Ty creative ships)

Three questions. If 3/3 = ship. 2/3 = revise. 1/3 = wrong audience.

1. **Would Ty stop scrolling at 11 PM on the couch to read it?** Not "would a 38-year-old middle manager" — specifically *would Ty at 11PM with his leg propped up, scrolling past Jake's canyon photos and the adjuster's voicemail notification*?
2. **Would Ty forward it to the dawn-patrol group chat?** "You guys see this one?" — does it read as something a rider respects, not something that pities him?
3. **Does it refute the fault presumption without arguing?** Does the line, visual, or moment quietly say *a driver did this* without ever defensively *arguing* the point?

## 7. Open Research Gaps (what this persona hasn't validated)

The persona is a rigorous archetype build but not primary ethnography. Items to validate during or after Phase 1 pilot:

- **Rachel's actual role in the decision.** We modeled her as co-owner of the household admin + a veto stake on lawyer choice. WCTL intake data (percentage of intake calls where the fiancée/spouse is present or named) can validate. If Rachel is more decision-lead than co-owner, creative should shift to dual-register (speak to the rider AND the partner).
- **Actual Meta cost-per-lead for this demographic segment.** Our forecasts in `00_Evidence_Register.md` §§6–8 are internal estimates. Week 1–3 of the pilot gives us real numbers. Update Evidence Register first if they land off.
- **Language testing.** The vocabulary in `04_Media_Reach.md` §5 is reasoned from forum + subreddit observation, not primary interviews. A/B test hook variants across Weeks 2–4 — "Riders don't ask for sympathy" vs. "For riders in Arizona who know they were doing everything right" vs. "If you ride in Arizona" — to validate which specific framing lands on real Ty-like people.
- **The $35K anchor specificity.** Doc 2 assumed a $35K opening adjuster offer. If WCTL intake data shows the real typical opening is $20K or $50K, the framing shifts. Not a creative-killer but a credibility check.
- **Supersport retargeting pool size.** Once we have 50+ quiz completions, check if the supersport-rider share of lookalike sources warrants expanding the creative set (maybe a fourth M-asset for sportbike-specific niches — canyon crashes, track-day adjacent, etc.). Phase 2 consideration.
- **"No fee unless we win" comprehension.** We assumed Ty doesn't know contingency fees well. Quiz Step 1 could include a comprehension gate — does the ad actually build the contingency-fee schema or assume it? A/B test that explicitly.
