- Emotional: quietly angry at the insurance adjuster, missing the bike, aware of the bills.

## 4. Meta Targeting Spec (Depesh's layer)

Given Meta's **Special Ad Category: Social Issues** constraint (no demographic/interest targeting for legal services), our targeting is intentionally broad. We rely on **creative filtering + placement + location** — not interest targeting — to reach Ty specifically.

**What we CAN target:**
- **Location:** Arizona statewide. Heavier delivery to Maricopa County (64% of AZ moto crashes), Pima County secondary.
- **Age:** 18–65+ broad (SAC prohibits narrow age targeting).
- **Placement:** Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed + IG Reels + IG Stories. Not Audience Network (quality concerns), not Messenger.
- **Language:** English primary (Phase 1 is English-only; Spanish Miguel creative is Phase 2).
- **Device:** Mobile primary (desktop secondary but lower weight — Ty is 95%+ mobile).

**What we CAN'T target (SAC restriction):**
- Motorcycle-interest categories directly.
- "Recently in an accident" lookalikes.
- Narrow age bands like 25–35.
- Gender (Ty's demo is 92% male for motorcycle victims but SAC blocks gender targeting here).

**Creative as targeting — the workaround:**
Because we can't target Ty directly, our **creative hook does the qualifying work**. M1's "Riders don't ask for sympathy" self-qualifies: if you're not a rider, you scroll past; if you are, you stop. The algorithm learns who's stopping and delivers to more of that profile. **Creative is the targeting in an SAC campaign.**

**Lookalike seed strategy (Phase 2 if available):**
- 1% LAL from lead form completions (once we have 50+).
- 1% LAL from quiz-complete events (richer signal than lead-form).
- Retargeting pool: anyone who engaged with M1/M2/M3 creative (Phase 2, once sample size is meaningful).

## 5. Language Patterns (full set — deepened from `01_Life_World.md` §8)

How Ty actually talks about the crash, the case, and the decision. **These are the words that should appear in creative and landing-page copy; words NOT in this list should not appear.**

### How he describes the crash:
- ✅ "Got hit." / "A driver didn't see me." / "She left-turned me."
- ❌ "I was involved in a motor vehicle accident." / "My motorcycle was struck by a negligent driver." (Legalese.)

### How he describes himself:
- ✅ "A rider." / "I ride." / "Sportbike guy." / "R6 guy."
- ❌ "Biker." (Cruiser connotation he rejects.) "Motorcyclist." (Legal term, he doesn't use it conversationally.) "Enthusiast." (Too soft.)

### How he describes the other driver:
- ✅ "The lady in the Nissan." / "The woman who turned." / "She didn't see me."
- ❌ "The at-fault motorist." / "The negligent party." / "The defendant." (Not his vocabulary.)

### How he describes the bike:
- ✅ "The R6." / "My bike." / "The Yamaha."
- ❌ "The vehicle." / "The motorcycle." (Context-dependent — in casual speech he says "bike.")

### How he describes the crash context:
- ✅ "4:45 in the afternoon." / "Full sun." / "Southern and Dobson." / "Doing the limit." / "Forty-five."
- ✅ "The skid marks say I wasn't." (Specificity + receipts energy. This is Ty's brain.)

### How he describes recovery:
- ✅ "Femur's healing." / "Doc says 6 months before I'm on a bike again." / "Light duty at work."
- ❌ "Experiencing severe mobility limitations." / "Undergoing physical rehabilitation." (Not his words.)

### How he describes the adjuster:
- ✅ "They wanted me to say I was speeding." / "They called on day 3." / "Thirty-five to go away."
- ❌ "The insurance company is acting in bad faith." (Legal framing — he wouldn't use this yet, though he'd recognize it as true.)

### How he describes his financial situation:
- ✅ "Bills don't stop because I can't work." / "We pushed the wedding." / "Insurance paying nine on a twelve-thousand-dollar bike."
- ❌ "I've incurred significant financial damages." (Legalese.)

### How he describes lawyers (before vs. after):
**Before hiring one:** "I probably need a lawyer, right?" / "I don't know how this works." / "I don't want to get screwed."
**After hiring one he respects:** "My lawyer said..." / "They're handling it." / "Best decision I made all year."

### Language we should USE in creative:
- "Riders" (not motorcyclists)
- "A driver didn't see the rider" (third-person, compliant)
- "If you ride in Arizona..." (community + geographic)
- "No fee unless we win" (direct, addresses the cost anxiety)
- "Free case review — 60 seconds" (action + low-friction promise)
- "Bills don't stop because you can't work" (emotional, lived reality)

### Language we should NEVER use in creative:
- "Entitled to MILLIONS" (billboard-lawyer energy he rejects)
- "Fight for your rights" (abstract, combative, empty)
- "Our aggressive legal team" (he's tired; he doesn't want aggressive, he wants competent)
- "We fight the insurance companies" (another agg-and-empty phrase)
- "You deserve..." (triggers pitying-victim frame he actively scrolls past)
- Any phrase a late-night TV lawyer would say

## 6. Creative Format Preferences

Based on Ty's platform behavior + mood states:

**9:16 vertical video (IG Reels + FB Stories):** PRIMARY. His most common consumption format. **15–30 second** runtime max; he scrolls if the hook isn't in the first 2 seconds. Sound-off primary (captions required). Subtle handheld feel outperforms slick cinematic for him (he's seen every slick PI ad; authentic rider-adjacent aesthetic cuts through).

**1:1 square image (IG Feed + FB Feed):** SECONDARY. For M3 (static intersection image). He pauses on strong single-frame compositions more than on videos sometimes. **Aspect ratio 1:1 + 4:5 variants.**

**Stories + Reels crossposted** lets Meta arbitrage placement without manual optimization.

**NOT a format for him:**
- Carousel ads (too much effort).
- Link ads in the text (he doesn't read preview text).
- Messenger ads (he's never there).
- Playable ads, AR ads, shopping ads (wrong category).

## 7. Frequency + Fatigue Guardrails

Ty will see our creative across the 12-week window. Thresholds:

- **Frequency cap:** 4 impressions per person per week (Meta recommendation for PI is 3–5). Going higher erodes trust and reads as harassment.
- **Creative refresh:** swap new variants weekly once CTR declines 20%+ from launch baseline. With 6 Phase 1 creatives (M1/M2/M3 + S1/S2/S5), we have a natural rotation pool.
- **Burnout signal:** CTR drop + CPC climb simultaneously = creative fatigue. Kill that creative, push impression share to the next one.
