## 4. Meta Targeting Spec (Depesh's layer)

Meta's **Special Ad Category: Social Issues** constraint applies.

**What we CAN target:**
- **Location:** Arizona statewide; heavy weight to Maricopa County (64% of AZ crashes) and specifically the East Valley (Gilbert, Chandler, Mesa, Tempe, Ahwatukee — suburban-mom-walker demographics concentrated here).
- **Age:** 18–65+ broad (SAC).
- **Placement:** **FB Feed + FB Stories + IG Feed + IG Stories.** Laura engages more with FB groups, so FB placements may perform better than IG.
- **Language:** English (Miguel is Spanish Phase 2).
- **Device:** Mobile 90%+.

**What we CAN'T target:**
- PTA / school-librarian / teacher interest categories
- Walking / walker / pedestrian interest
- Specific community groups (Gilbert Neighborhood Watch, Highland Elementary PTA)

**Creative as targeting:**

Per our established discipline for all Phase 1 personas: the **creative hook does the qualifying work.** S2's "Hit while crossing the street?" + the visual of a suburban crosswalk at dusk self-qualifies to pedestrian victims. Anyone in a vehicle at their own crash moment scrolls past instantly.

**Key insight for Laura's reach:**

Laura's audience overlaps with Diana's (both suburban-mom-East-Valley-FB-heavy) but differs:
- Diana-types are typically at home more, more screen-heavy, tech-adjacent families
- Laura-types are PTA-involved, walking/fitness-engaged, school-community-oriented
- These differ in what Meta's algo will identify as engagement patterns
- **The S2 creative will over-index with Laura-types because the VISUAL (crosswalk + walker-woman) pattern-matches to their self-identification**

**Frequency cap:** **3 impressions/person/week.** Laura's conflict-aversion means higher frequency reads as "marketing harassment" to her. Stay tight.

**Static-image-specific delivery note:**
- S2 is a single frame, not a video. Meta sometimes under-delivers static ads vs. video in mixed-format campaigns (video ads get favored algorithmically for eyeballs). Manual optimization may be needed to protect S2's impression share.
- Consider running S2 as both 1:1 (FB Feed) + 9:16 (FB Stories + IG Stories) to maximize placement options.

## 6. Creative Format Preferences

**S2 is a STATIC IMAGE asset.** Different discipline from video creative — every pixel carries weight.

**Primary format: 1:1 square (Facebook Feed).** Works on mobile. Strong composition suited to suburban still-life.

**Secondary format: 9:16 vertical (FB Stories + IG Stories).** For stories placement — same image re-cropped.

**Tertiary format: 4:5 portrait (IG Feed).** Good for Laura's Instagram surface area.

**Static-image composition discipline for Laura:**
- **Single emotional subject** — Laura at the crosswalk at dusk. No multi-frame story.
- **Strong warm-to-cool color contrast** (warm sand foreground + cyan dusk sky + amber streetlight) — per the existing Creative brief.
- **Off-center composition** — Laura in the right third of the frame, crosswalk lines leading the eye from lower-left to upper-right.
- **Negative space** (the empty crosswalk) is the emotional subject.
- **Hook + headline in the top third** of the 1:1 frame, CTA in the bottom third.

**NOT formats for her:**
- Carousel (too much cognitive load)
- Video (not the S2 asset; reserved for S1/S5/M1/M2)
- Playable / shopping / messenger

## 7. Frequency + Fatigue Guardrails

- **Frequency cap: 3 impressions/person/week.** Laura's conflict-aversion + suburban-politeness inhibition means higher frequency is counterproductive.
- **Creative refresh:** swap alt S2 variants every 3 weeks. Static images tolerate somewhat less rotation than video but still benefit from alternate angles / alternate headlines.
- **Retargeting refresh:** if she hit the landing page but bounced, serve a deeper-funnel creative — "Ready to talk through your case? 20 minutes, no pressure." Specifically calibrated to her conflict-aversion.
- **Burnout signal:** CTR drop + CPM climb. Swap variant.
