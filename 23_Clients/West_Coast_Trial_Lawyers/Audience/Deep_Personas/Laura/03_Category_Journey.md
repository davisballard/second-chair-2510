## How She Thinks About PI Lawyers (Before, During, After)

## 1. Laura's Category Priors (what she ALREADY thinks)

**What "PI lawyer" means to Laura:**

1. **The neighbor-who-told-a-story.** Her cousin Emily (6 houses down) had a car accident 5 years ago and hired a lawyer. Emily was happy with the outcome (a modest settlement, ~$30K) but said the process "took forever." Laura has a positive-ish reference but a tempered expectation.
2. **The occasional AZCentral article.** She reads AZ news, skims stories about major crash cases. She vaguely recognizes WCTL-adjacent firm names from these articles.
3. **The I-10 + Gilbert Rd billboards.** Common sight, she's tuned them out — but unlike Ty who finds them tacky and Diana who finds them volume-mill, Laura mostly finds them *loud.* She's a quiet person. Loud advertising is its own disqualifier.
4. **The PTA-parent Facebook group whispers.** One of the other PTA moms, Jennifer, mentioned "my attorney" once in a passing comment — she'd been hit by a distracted driver at a Gilbert intersection 2 years ago. Laura didn't ask. She remembers the mention.
5. **The "don't sue unless you have to" suburban ethos.** Laura's Gilbert-PTA-church-community has a quiet consensus: litigation is for serious wrongs, not for making a fuss. This cultural prior is the single strongest barrier to Laura calling a PI lawyer. **She'll have to overcome an internal "am I that person?" moment first.**

**What Laura thinks PI lawyers do:**

**Accurate:**
- Negotiate with insurance companies on your behalf
- Take a contingency fee (she knows "about a third")
- Handle cases in a 12–24 month timeline
- Deal with medical liens (vaguely)
- Some firms specialize (she knows this from Jennifer's casual mention)

**Gaps:**
- She doesn't know AZ's pure comparative negligence rule
- She doesn't know policy-limits demand dynamics
- She doesn't know uninsured/underinsured motorist stacking (which matters since Ryan Perez has a $100K policy that might be insufficient for tibial-plateau surgery + long-term PT)
- She doesn't know her OWN underinsured motorist coverage on the Hendersons' State Farm policy (which is probably ~$100K-$250K — stackable)

**The landing page + intake needs to fill these gaps gently** — Laura hates feeling underinformed but also hates being lectured. Meet her at peer-tone.

**What Laura thinks a GOOD lawyer looks like:**
- **Someone who treats her like a reasonable adult, not a paying client.** This is the core test.
- **Local (Gilbert/East Valley, she trusts).** She's more local-minded than Diana or Sarah.
- **Recommended by someone she trusts** (Emily's referral, Jennifer's passing mention, an Amanda-type warm intro).
- **Professional tone on the website.** Not loud. Not thumbs-up. Not "call now!"
- **Not the billboard firms on I-10.**
- **Willing to explain things without condescending.**

## 2. The Decision Journey (Laura's path to hiring)

### Stage 1 — Awareness (Weeks 1–5 post-crash)

**What's happening:** Laura is in acute recovery. Non-weight-bearing, then crutches, then cane. She's NOT looking for a lawyer actively. Her focus is physical.

**What she's seeing:**
- Mike mentions "we should talk to a lawyer" around Week 3 after the $22K adjuster offer. Laura says "soon."
- Her Facebook feed (she's on it 30–60 min/day) starts serving PI ads by Week 3. She scrolls past most. She pauses on one that has a crosswalk visual (an archetype close to ours). She doesn't click.
- Emily (cousin) texts at Week 3: "hey when you're ready, I have my lawyer's number. No pressure." Laura texts back "thanks, maybe later."
- Jennifer-from-PTA sends a casual DM at Week 4: "saw your family's update post, so sorry. if you want my attorney's name, let me know." Laura appreciates it but doesn't act.
- She reads an AZCentral article about crash-victim rights in AZ. Doesn't click the link for the firm quoted.

**Barriers at Awareness:**
- **Conflict-aversion.** The hardest barrier for Laura. "I don't want to sue anyone" is her reflex.
- **"I should handle this myself" competence-bias.** She's PTA Treasurer; she can manage the insurance adjuster.
- **Recovery-mode attention fatigue.** She's in physical therapy 3x/week, managing pain meds, making sure her kids' lives keep moving. Legal stuff is lowest priority.

**What moves her forward:** A creative that reframes legal action as *accountability* + *accurate-recording-of-facts* — not as *fighting* or *combat.* **"Find out what the law says"** hits exactly this register. It's investigative, informational, neutral. Exactly Laura.

### Stage 2 — Consideration (Weeks 5–10)

**What's happening:** She's walking with a cane. The $22K adjuster offer still sits. Her medical bills total ~$48K so far and she hasn't even finished PT. Around Week 7–8, the math becomes undeniable: $22K doesn't cover what she's already paid out (even with her health insurance covering most).

**What she's doing:**
- **Searching:** "pedestrian accident lawyer gilbert arizona," "tibial plateau fracture settlement value," "arizona pedestrian hit by car rights," "how long does a pedestrian accident case take." She's on laptop or phone at 8–10 PM.
- **Asking:** finally texts Emily: "hey, would you send me your lawyer's info?" (Gets a name.) Texts Jennifer: "if your attorney is local, can you send me their name?" (Gets another name.) Has a phone call with Amanda where she talks through the situation; Amanda recommends Googling "Gilbert AZ pedestrian accident attorney."
- **Comparing:** Maybe 4–5 firms in her head. Slower comparison than Diana's 8, faster than Ty's 2–3. She's reading Google reviews but also weighting Emily's and Jennifer's referrals heavily (social proof dominates Laura's decision-making).
- **Reading websites.** She'll spend 10–15 minutes on a well-designed firm site. Closes cagey-fee-pages instantly.

**Barriers at Consideration:**
- **Conflict-aversion, now escalated.** Seeing firm websites that use words like "aggressive" makes her close the tab reflexively.
- **"Should I be doing this?" moral uncertainty.** She wants to make sure she's not being greedy. A good PI firm signals *we're ensuring fairness,* not *we're maximizing payout.*
- **Social-proof anchoring.** Laura weights personal referrals (Emily, Jennifer) above Google reviews. **Any firm with low friction for "let me just follow up with my cousin's lawyer" is winning over WCTL unless WCTL gets to her first.**
- **Fee-transparency demand.** She's PTA Treasurer. She wants to see the math. Cagey websites lose her.

**What moves her forward:** A low-friction path to a real conversation where someone professional and warm explains what to expect. **She's the most conversation-friendly of the four personas** — a 20-minute call with a lawyer who listens, asks relevant questions, and outlines a plan = she signs that week.

### Stage 3 — Decision (Weeks 10–16)

**What's happening:** She's had 1–3 consultations. She's narrowing.

**What flips her from consideration to decision:**
- **Warm, competent intake on the first call.** Laura isn't evaluating legal acumen specifically — she's evaluating trust + fit. The firm that makes her feel *heard* wins her.
- **Clear fee explanation, plainly.** She's an administrator; she wants the math visible.
- **Proactive on medical liens + UIM stacking.** If the lawyer brings up her own underinsured motorist coverage (which she doesn't know she has) — that's the specialist-tier signal.
- **No pressure to sign on call 1.** She needs to tell Mike. She needs to sleep on it. Pressure kills.
- **A plain-language engagement letter.** 4 pages, clear sections. Laura reads contracts for the PTA — she'll smell bad drafting.
- **Someone who doesn't make her feel embarrassed about her conflict-aversion.** If the intake specialist says "people often hesitate to call a lawyer — it's normal; let's talk through whether it makes sense for your case" — Laura's defenses relax.

**Barriers at Decision:**
- **Sign-anxiety (fairness variant).** She wants to make sure she's not taking advantage of Ryan Perez specifically. She'll want to understand: "is this about him or about his insurance company?" — the right answer (the insurance company, who's underwriting his policy) matters to her.
- **Spousal consultation.** Mike will weigh in. He's engineering-trained, wants the math to check out. The firm that survives his "does this make sense?" review wins.

## 3. Adam Ferrier's Trigger Moments (Laura-specific)

Ranked by conversion weight. **Note: Laura's triggers are slower and quieter than Sarah's or Diana's** — pedestrian cases have different activation tempo.

1. **Week 3: $22K adjuster offer.** Not immediately insulting (unlike Diana's $18K or Sarah's $35K where the audience has clinical/CFO fluency) — but when Laura adds her surgery + hospital + ongoing PT, she realizes this won't cover the real costs. **Trigger lands quietly around Week 3–4.**
2. **Week 5: First clear Physical Therapy timeline.** Her PT tells her "expect 4–6 months to full function; right ankle strength may not fully return." **The prognosis-specificity lands.** She realizes this isn't a 6-week thing.
3. **Week 7: First day she can walk to the end of the cul-de-sac with her cane.** Emotional — small triumph — but also a REALITY CHECK that "walking again" is a very long ladder. She's on rung 2 of 40.
4. **Week 8: Emily's offhand comment.** Over coffee: *"You should talk to a lawyer soon, Laur. Don't wait like I almost did."* Emily's own case had a moment where she almost accepted a lowball offer out of exhaustion; she remembers and gently warns. **Laura takes Emily's trust seriously.**
5. **Week 9–10: Mike asks her directly what she wants to do.** Not pressuring — checking in. Laura says "I'll call Emily's lawyer this week." She does.

**Campaign activation window:** Weeks 6–14 post-crash. Earliest = Week 6 (physical recovery enabling attention-resources); deepest engagement = Week 8–11; latest save = Week 13–14. **Outside this window: she's accepted the offer + moved on, or already hired Emily's/Jennifer's referral.**

## 4. Competitive Set (Laura's alternatives)

Ranked by her likely preference order:

1. **Emily's attorney (cousin's referral).** Highest warmth. Emily's endorsement is weighty. Laura would take this call first.
2. **Jennifer's attorney (PTA mom referral).** Second-warmth. She'd follow up if Emily's doesn't feel right.
3. **A local Gilbert-specific PI firm (WCTL's natural position if we can position correctly).** Ideal. Laura is local-minded — she'd love a Gilbert-first firm.
4. **A larger East Valley / Phoenix firm she finds via Google (this is where WCTL would actually win her).** Probably her third-tier option after referrals — but the one most likely to convert if the creative + intake + website are right.
5. **Large billboard firm.** Unlikely unless all others fail. Their aesthetic pushes her away.
6. **Accept the $22K + move on.** Default if nothing else converts. She'd regret it around Month 6 when she realizes her PT costs alone outran the settlement.

**WCTL's job:** beat options 1–2 via creative + specialty-signal + warm-professional intake. Option 3 is where WCTL should try to position. The billboard firms (option 5) are aesthetic competition — if WCTL's creative reads billboard-y, Laura files us in that category.

**Time-based competitor (option 6) is the real race.** Every week past Week 14 without WCTL outcompeting Emily's/Jennifer's referrals, the family-referral option wins by default.

## 5. Creative Implications (preview for `05_Campaign_Implications.md`)

- **Opening visual must break PI-ad pattern by being QUIETER, not louder.** The S2 static image — dusk, crosswalk, Laura at the curb — is already calibrated for this. The aesthetic signal is "documentary, not billboard."
- **"Hit while crossing the street?" is the current hook.** Works for Laura. Filter-sharp. Non-combative tone.
- **Body copy must reframe legal action as accountability, not combat.** "Find out what the law says" ✅. "Fight for your rights" ❌.
- **"No fee unless we win" still critical** — but less about fee-anxiety (Laura understands contingency) and more about *risk-reversal signal* (removes the "am I making a bad financial decision?" barrier).
- **Landing page must be quick + warm.** Laura has better attention than Diana (no concussion) but still prefers efficiency.
- **Intake call tone: warm + competent + unhurried.** Not salesy. Not hurried. Not billboard.
- **Address the conflict-aversion.** Landing page or intake should normalize "hesitating to call a lawyer." Laura will feel seen.
- **UIM/UM coverage proactive raise.** Specialist-tier signal. Laura doesn't know she might have it. Mentioning it = she trusts WCTL knows the terrain.
