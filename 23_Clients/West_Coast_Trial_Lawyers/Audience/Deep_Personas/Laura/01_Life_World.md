## 2. The Walking Life (the identity core)

**This is Laura's emotional spine. The crash took this.**

Laura has walked the same three-mile morning loop through her Gilbert neighborhood for **15 years**, since she was 27 and they bought the house. She started walking when Mike worked the 7-to-7 engineering shift at Intel and she had the mornings alone. She walked through her pregnancy with Caleb. Through his toddler years with a jogging stroller. Through Olivia's newborn colic at 5:30 AM. Through a breast biopsy scare at 37 that came back benign. Through her dad's death in 2020. The walk was her constant.

**The morning route** (15 years, same path, roughly):

- **5:45 AM** (in cooler months — fall, winter, spring) or **5:15 AM** (in summer to beat the heat): alarm, Maggie the Golden gets up, Laura puts on walking shoes.
- **6:00 AM:** out the door. Maggie on a leash. Mike often still in bed (he leaves for Intel at 6:45). Kids asleep.
- **6:00–7:00 AM:** three-mile loop through her subdivision and the adjacent neighborhood. Passes:
  - The Johnson's Australian shepherd (Barkley, 11, barks once from the yard every time)
  - The elementary school crossing guard's house (Martha, retired teacher, waves from her porch if she's up)
  - The corner house with Halloween decorations that never come down (a running joke she shares with Mike)
  - The park at the midpoint where Maggie gets a 3-minute sniff break
  - The Starbucks drive-thru corner (she doesn't stop — just the smell of coffee)
  - Back home via the small street where her kids' friend Emily lives
- **7:00 AM:** home. Coffee. Get the kids moving. Start the day.

**The evening route** (added 7 years ago when Maggie was getting older and needed a second shorter walk):
- **6:30 PM** after dinner (in cooler months) or **7:30 PM** (summer, after heat breaks)
- Shorter — 1.5 miles through the same neighborhood
- Maggie + Laura + sometimes Mike
- **This is the walk where the crash happened.**

**What she knew about her neighborhood** (the craft of her walking):

Laura knew her block the way a good property manager knows a portfolio. She knew which house had the landscape crew coming on Thursdays. She knew whose kid had just started driving. She knew that the house on the corner of Houston & Sagebrush had been for sale for 9 months because they'd overpriced it. She knew the house across from the park had a new roof in 2023. **She knew her street.**

**The block-captain role:**
- Runs the Gilbert Neighborhood Watch email list for her subdivision (~180 homes)
- Coordinates the annual block party in October
- Flags HOA items
- Knows the majority of her immediate-area neighbors by first name
- Has been the de facto "neighborhood information broker" for years

**Her professional symmetry:** the elementary school librarian who knows her library inside-out is also the neighborhood walker who knows her three-mile loop inside-out. Same temperament — observational, community-oriented, rhythmic. The crash broke both of these, but the walking-identity broke harder.

## 3. The Crash (the context)

**Location:** Crosswalk at **Gilbert Road & Warner Road**, Gilbert AZ (a real arterial intersection — mile-square grid commercial corridor, heavy traffic, big-box retail on the corners). She was walking home from the small neighborhood shopping strip (had picked up a prescription from the Walgreens on the corner).

**Time:** Evening, **approximately 6:30 PM**, early-spring dusk. Warm weather (70°F), long twilight. Still reasonably bright but with amber streetlights flickering on. Perfect walking weather.

**Mechanics:** Laura was at the crosswalk with the walk signal (green pedestrian, her light). A driver — **Ryan Perez, 29, in a 2019 Toyota Tacoma, coming home from a sales job at a Gilbert auto dealership** — was stopped at the red light intending to turn right onto Gilbert Rd. **He looked LEFT for a gap in the Gilbert Rd traffic, timed his right-turn-on-red for the gap, and started the turn without looking right toward the crosswalk.** Laura was in the second step of her crossing. He hit her at approximately 15–20 mph (he braked partway through).

**Impact:** Right side of her body made contact with the front left corner of the Tacoma's bumper + hood. She was thrown back onto the pavement, right hip first. Her right ankle twisted severely in the initial impact. Her head hit the pavement (helmet-less — pedestrian). Tacoma stopped immediately, driver got out, called 911.

**Injuries:**
- **Right tibial plateau fracture** (Schatzker type II) — **surgical repair required** (open reduction internal fixation with plate + screws). Banner Gateway ER → surgical admission → 2-night stay. Surgery Day 1.
- **Severe right ankle sprain / Grade 2 lateral ligament tear** — no surgery, immobilization and PT
- **Right hip contusion** with deep bruising (no fracture)
- **Grade 2 concussion** (mild TBI — 7–10 days of screen sensitivity, residual balance issues for weeks)
- **Multiple abrasions** right side from pavement contact
- **Soft-tissue trauma** right side / back

**Recovery timeline:**
- **Surgery:** Day 1
- **Hospital stay:** 2 nights
- **Non-weight-bearing:** 6 weeks (crutches)
- **Weight-bearing progression:** Weeks 6–12 (cane)
- **Back to full walking without cane:** 4–6 months
- **Back to her 3-mile loop:** uncertain. Possibly 6–9 months. **Possibly never the same route** — a psychological question more than a physical one.
- **Physical therapy:** 3×/week at a Gilbert PT practice for 4+ months

**Legal picture:**
- Clean liability case. Ryan Perez's auto insurance (State Farm, $100K policy limit) admits fault. The crash was witnessed by two vehicles stopped at the adjacent traffic signal.
- Gilbert Police Department took the report. Ryan got a citation for failure to yield to pedestrian + reckless right-turn-on-red.
- Laura's medical costs so far: $18K surgery + $6K hospital + $3.5K ER + ongoing PT = will probably total $35–50K before she's done.
- Potential policy-limits demand + claim against her own uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage stacking.
