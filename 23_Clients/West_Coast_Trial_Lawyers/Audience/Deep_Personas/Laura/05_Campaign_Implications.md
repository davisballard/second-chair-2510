## 1. SMP → S2 Static Frame Translation

The SMP from §00: *Laura didn't lose her body — she got most of it back. She lost her relationship with her own street. For fifteen years she was the woman who walked this neighborhood; for the rest of her life, she's the woman who used to walk this neighborhood. The ordinary became unsafe. The familiar became a trigger. And there is no physical therapy for the loss of a place you thought belonged to you.*

**S2 is ONE frame.** Unlike S1 (Sarah's 5-beat video sequence) or M1/M2 (Ty's 5-beat sequences) or S5 (Diana's 5-beat UGC), S2 has to say everything in a single still image + a short text overlay. **The discipline is radically different from video.**

**Hook direction (from McCabe's 25-option pool in `04_Media_Reach.md` §8):**

- **Lead:** "Hit while crossing the street?" (Current brief lock. Filter-sharp. Non-combative. Three words.)
- **Alt A:** "You were walking. He was turning. Everything else is negotiable." (More distinctive; breaks billboard-firm pattern. Best for cold traffic scroll-stops.)
- **Alt B:** "The crosswalk was marked. The signal was yours. So is your case." (Fact-forward, accountability-framing, matches Laura's neighborly register.)

**A/B discipline:** Launch all three at equal weight in Week 1. After 10 days of ~150 impressions/creative/week minimum for signal, scale the winner. Expected: Lead (simplicity) wins cold traffic; Alt A (distinctiveness) wins the middle-funnel; Alt B is retargeting-warm creative.

## 2. Body Copy Direction (for Meta feed text — shorter than video body)

Static ads typically run shorter body copy than video ads because the format rewards scannability.

**Primary body copy (current from `Creative/Week_1_Approval_Package/02_S2_Laura_Pedestrian.md` — validated for Laura):**

> You weren't the one driving. Arizona pedestrians injured by a driver — broken bones, hospitalization, surgery — may be entitled to compensation. No fee unless we win.

**Why this template works for Laura:**

1. **Opens with the innocence filter** ("You weren't the one driving") — Laura's single strongest self-qualifier.
2. **Severity thresholds named** ("broken bones, hospitalization, surgery") = screens soft-tissue self-identifiers. Laura's tibial plateau + surgery fits.
3. **"May be entitled to compensation"** — cautious, neutral, non-promising. Laura's conflict-aversion responds well to non-combative framing.
4. **"No fee unless we win"** — risk-reversal. Important for her but she already half-understands contingency.
5. **Under 30 words.** Static-image body copy must be scannable.

**Alternative body copy for A/B:**

- *"A driver didn't see you. An insurance company's first offer isn't designed to. Arizona pedestrians hit by a driver — broken bones, hospitalization, surgery — may have options beyond the first offer. Free case review."* (Slightly longer; addresses the adjuster-anchor insult which activates around Week 4.)
- *"Arizona pedestrians injured by a turning driver may be entitled to more than an insurance adjuster's first offer. Find out what the law says about your case. No fee unless we win."* (Fact-forward, accountability register — Laura's preferred voice.)

**CTA text direction:**
- "Learn More → free case review"
- "Find out what the law says — 60 seconds"
- "Free review. No obligation."

**NEVER use for Laura:**
- "Call NOW!" (pushy — triggers her conflict-aversion)
- "You may be entitled to MILLIONS!" (billboard-firm energy)
- "Get the settlement you deserve!" (pitying; she doesn't want to be a "deserving" victim)
- "Our aggressive legal team will fight for you!" (wrong voice for her)

## 3. Visual Direction (S2 — the single static frame)

**CRITICAL:** S2 is Elevated Documentary per `Creative/Style_Sheets/Elevated_Documentary.md`. Alec Soth / Nadav Kander portraiture + Roger Deakins atmospheric light. Desaturated with lifted blacks, subtle film grain, motivated natural lighting, shallow depth of field, NOT UGC.

**The S2 frame:** A single composition that holds The Stolen Neighborhood (Loss 2 — Laura's emotional front-line) without explaining it.

**Composition brief:**

- **Setting:** Crosswalk at Gilbert Road & Warner Road, Gilbert AZ. Suburban arterial intersection. Dusk, late spring — approximately 7:00 PM, blue hour transitioning to twilight. An amber streetlight just switched on in the upper-left quadrant. Warm sand-colored asphalt foreground. Painted white crosswalk lines running from lower-left to mid-right, leading the eye. A Walgreens corner signage faintly visible in the deep background-left. A mature palo verde tree silhouetted in background-right against a cyan-fading sky.

- **Subject placement:** Laura stands on the near-side curb (viewer's lower-right third of the frame), three-quarter angle, looking across the crosswalk toward the opposite sidewalk (away from the camera). Her cane in her LEFT hand, tip resting on the sidewalk near her left foot. She is NOT walking. She is standing, still, looking.

- **Wardrobe:** Soft cream or pale-gray cardigan over a plain white t-shirt. Worn medium-wash jeans. Simple slip-on sneakers (not athletic). Reading glasses pushed up in her hair. Thin silver chain at the collar. Small pearl stud earrings. Plain white-gold wedding band on her LEFT hand. Hair in a loose low ponytail, shoulder-length light-brown with sun-lightened ends, 2–3 pieces fallen loose at the temples.

- **Face:** Three-quarter profile from the camera's perspective (not full-frontal). Her expression is quiet — not crying, not grim, not stoic — present. Thoughtful. A woman looking at a place she's lost. Subtle freckle pattern across the nose. Faint laugh lines. Green-blue eyes (barely visible at three-quarter angle).

- **Continuity props (LOCKED):**
  - **Cane in LEFT hand** (struck on right side → weight on left)
  - **ACE-style wrap visible at her RIGHT ankle beneath the jean cuff** (subtle, may not be clearly visible in this dusk frame but must be renderable if re-crop or detail shots are needed)
  - **Small healing bruise at right temple** (3 weeks healing, yellowing — subtle, may not read at dusk but present)
  - **Small old scar on her LEFT forearm** (cardigan may cover it; fine)
  - **Wedding band on LEFT hand**

- **Light direction:** Motivated natural — fading dusk sky light from above + the amber streetlight from upper-left casting a soft warm glow on the right side of her face. Her left side in cooler cyan-twilight shadow. This light-contrast IS the emotional structure of the frame — warm (past) meeting cool (present).

- **Color palette (per Creative brief):** Warm sand / amber / cyan. No over-saturation. Desaturated-with-lifted-blacks grade.

- **Text overlay (top-third of 1:1 frame):** *"Hit while crossing the street?"* — serif or slab typeface, large, left-aligned, white with a subtle drop shadow. Below in smaller weight: *"You weren't the one driving."*

- **CTA (bottom-fifth of frame):** *"Arizona pedestrians injured by a driver — find out what the law says about your case. Free — 60 seconds."* + WCTL logo end-card treatment.

**NOT in frame:**
- The driver
- The Tacoma (or any car in the actual impact position)
- The crash moment (no simulation)
- A hospital, a doctor's office, a cane in its wrong hand, an ACE wrap on wrong ankle
- Any billboard-firm aesthetic (no giant phone number, no loud call-out text, no thumbs-up)

**Aspect ratios to produce:** 1:1 (Facebook Feed) + 9:16 (FB Stories + IG Stories). Consider a 4:5 portrait crop for IG Feed.

## 4. Compliance Considerations Specific to Laura

- **AZ Bar ER 7.3 (direct solicitation):** Hook is a third-person question ("Hit while crossing the street?"). Body copy uses "you" after the third-person frame is established via the hook — acceptable pattern per previous Laura creative draft. Compliant.
- **AZ Bar ER 7.1 (truthfulness):** "Broken bones, hospitalization, surgery" is factually accurate for Laura-type pedestrian cases (her tibial plateau fracture = surgery). "May be entitled to compensation" is non-promising. "No fee unless we win" is truthful under AZ contingency rules. **No specific dollar amounts promised.**
- **AZ Bar ER 7.5 (firm naming):** WCTL logo + firm name on end-card treatment.
- **Right-turn-on-red liability** (Laura-specific): AZ law requires drivers to yield to pedestrians in the crosswalk before completing a right-turn-on-red (A.R.S. § 28-645). Ryan Perez's failure to yield + police citation = STRONG liability. This is an extremely clean case by PI standards. Reinforces that the creative doesn't need to over-sell — the law is clearly on Laura's side.
- **Policy limits + UIM stacking** (insurance specific): Ryan Perez's State Farm policy is likely $100K. Laura's tibial plateau surgery + PT + long-term ankle prognosis could exceed $100K in total economic damages. Her household's State Farm auto policy almost certainly carries underinsured-motorist (UIM) stacking. **WCTL's intake team should proactively raise UIM stacking on the first call** — this is the specialist-tier signal Laura is watching for.
- **Medical liens:** Banner Gateway will file a lien on Laura's settlement for her surgery + hospital bills. Standard but must be addressed in intake.
- **TCPA consent language:** Required on quiz-funnel lead-capture.

## 5. Creative Acid Tests (before shipping any Laura creative)

Three questions. 3/3 = ship. 2/3 = revise. 1/3 = wrong audience.

1. **Would Laura read this line and feel it in her neighborhood-specific way?** Not "would a generic suburban mom" — specifically *would Laura, the 42-year-old Gilbert school librarian who walked three miles every morning for fifteen years past the Johnson's Australian shepherd, read this and think "yes, they see what I actually lost"?*
2. **Would Laura share this with her Gilbert PTA group chat** with a *"this made me cry a little but in the good way"* caption? Does it read as quietly-true rather than performatively-sad? Would it embarrass her to share — or would her friends see it and say "that's exactly right"?
3. **Does it honor the place-loss without being sentimental?** Does the creative let Laura's relationship with her street be the emotional subject — without over-explaining, without voice-over monologue, without forcing her to name it? **Does the composition hold the Stolen Neighborhood without lecturing?**

## 8. Research Gaps (what this persona hasn't validated)

Items to validate during or after the pilot:

- **Mike's role in the decision.** We modeled him as deferent (defers to Laura's judgment on lawyer choice, reviews the engagement letter with an engineering eye). If WCTL intake shows Mike is more active than modeled, creative may need a secondary-voice variant.
- **The $22K adjuster anchor accuracy.** We assumed her opening offer is in this range; real WCTL intake data will validate. If actual pedestrian offers in AZ cluster around $15K or $40K, the emotional-math in `02 §2 Loss 6` shifts.
- **The "place-loss" emotional-resonance hypothesis.** We argue Laura's pedestrian audience will respond to the Stolen Neighborhood vector more than to generic victim-creative. A/B test: run S2 with Stolen-Neighborhood-oriented imagery vs. a more generic "pedestrian hit by driver" standard alternative. If Stolen Neighborhood wins on engagement + quiz-completion, Steel's emotional-architecture call is validated and should influence Phase 2 creative.
- **Right-turn-on-red crash concentration.** We chose Ryan Perez's right-turn-on-red mechanics because it's the most common pedestrian-intersection crash pattern in AZ urban arterials. ADOT data + WCTL intake can validate: if most real Laura-cases involve different mechanics (left-turn instead, mid-block instead), the visual may need tuning.
- **Conflict-aversion as load-bearing barrier.** We argue this is Laura's #1 barrier — higher than Diana's analysis-paralysis or Ty's billboard-distrust. A/B test creative that explicitly acknowledges hesitation ("people often hesitate to call a lawyer — it's normal") vs. creative that doesn't. If the acknowledgment-variant out-converts, we've validated the hypothesis.
- **Static-image-performance vs. video-performance on Meta.** S2 is the only static asset; S1/S5/M1/M2 are video. Meta delivery algorithms sometimes under-serve static ads in mixed-format campaigns. Monitor S2's impression-share vs. the videos — if S2 systematically under-delivers, consider boosting with manual bid adjustments.
- **The 8-10 PM peak-tap window.** Assumed. Validate via Week 1–2 delivery data — if Saturday 9-11 AM outperforms, reallocate spend.
