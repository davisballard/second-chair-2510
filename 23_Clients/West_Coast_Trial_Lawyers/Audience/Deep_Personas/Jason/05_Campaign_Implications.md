## 1. SMP → Hook Translation

The SMP from §00: *Jason Reardon's entire adult identity is built on a body that works and a crew that listens. A non-soft-tissue MVA hasn't just injured him — it has removed him from the role he built every other part of his life around. WCTL's job is to be the one specialist who treats him like he's still the competent man in his own room — because he is.*

**For the AZ Male MVA general-volume slot (Cinematic Documentary primary, 15–30s, 1:1 + 9:16):**

Jason's slot pairs with Diana's to cover the largest addressable pool (~400–600 moderate-severe MVA incidents/month). His job is to convert the male half of that pool — currently unactivated by the female-protagonist creative line. **He is the male volume engine of Phase 1.**

**Hook direction (from McCabe's 25-option pool in `04_Media_Reach.md` §8):**

- **Lead:** *Broken bones. Bills don't stop.* (Severity-filter forward. Tight. The same hook that anchors Diana, but lands tighter for Jason because he is *literally* the broken-bones case. Cold traffic.)
- **Alt A:** *You did the work. Make sure the math is right.* (Ferrier's earn-it register; respects Jason's competence; activates the role-inversion subtext. Warm retargeting.)
- **Alt B:** *Workers' comp dragging? You may have other options.* (Jason-specific differentiator; converts on the Week-6+ workers'-comp-frustrated subsegment. Late-funnel close.)

**What each does psychologically:**

- **Lead** is the campaign's canonical severity-filter hook. Loss-forward. No artifice. Jason reads it as documentary truth — the bills he's looking at on the kitchen counter.
- **Alt A** speaks competence-to-competence. The earn-it framing replaces fight metaphors without losing the activation gravity. This is the hook that displaces the billboard-mill prior most directly.
- **Alt B** names a Jason-specific pain that no other persona's creative will name. It's the differentiator — a firm that *coordinates with workers' comp* rather than fighting it. Converts the workers'-comp-disputed subsegment that the broader hooks don't reach.

**A/B discipline:** Launch all three in Week 1 at equal weight. After 10 days (~150 impressions per creative per week minimum for signal), scale the winner of each cohort and retire the losers. Expected: Lead wins on cold; Alt A wins on warm; Alt B wins on workers'-comp-frustrated retargeting.

## 2. Body Copy Direction (for Meta feed text)

**Template (validated against Diana's pattern, Jason-specific tuning):**

> Serious injuries don't pause because you can't work. Arizona drivers seriously injured in a crash — surgery, hospital stays, months of recovery — may be entitled to more than what insurance first offered. Free case review. No fee unless we win.

**Why this template works for Jason:**

1. **Opens with a universal truth** ("Serious injuries don't pause...") that Jason reads as lived reality, not slogan. Same first line that works for Diana, but the audience reads it through a different deprivation architecture.
2. **Specifies severity thresholds** ("surgery, hospital stays, months of recovery") = filters hard on non-soft-tissue. Jason is squarely in this filter; the words describe him.
3. **Names the adjuster-anchor insult** ("more than what insurance first offered") = activates his specific irritation at the $38K verdict.
4. **"Free case review" framing** is plain disclosure, not advocacy. Fits Jason's competence-to-competence register.
5. **"No fee unless we win"** — Jason knows about contingency directionally; this puts it above the fold.

**Alternative body copy angles for A/B testing:**

- *"You did the work. Make sure what they offer is what you're owed. Arizona drivers seriously injured in a crash — surgery, months of recovery, bills stacking. Free case review. No fee unless we win."* (Earn-it framing.)
- *"T-boned at the light. Surgery. Months out of work. Insurance closes fast — we slow them down until the math is right. Free case review for serious injury MVA. No fee unless we win."* (Crash-specific + math-language.)
- *"Workers' comp dragging? Insurance closing fast? Arizona drivers with surgical injuries from a crash — find out what the law actually says about your case. Free case review. No fee unless we win."* (Workers'-comp differentiator.)

**CTA text direction:**

- *"Get a free case review. 60 seconds."*
- *"Talk to a specialist. Free — 60 seconds."*
- *"Find out what the law says about your case. No fee unless we win."*

**NEVER use:**
- *"Call now — don't wait!"* (pattern-matches billboard-mill; Jason scrolls past)
- *"You may be entitled to MILLIONS!"* (kills credibility instantly)
- *"Get the compensation you deserve!"* (pitying advocacy register)
- *"Don't let them take what's yours!"* (fight-metaphor; aggression register)
- *"Our aggressive legal team!"* (try-hard alpha; counterproductive)
- *"Hard-working Arizonans deserve..."* (class-coded condescension)

## 3. Visual Direction (Cinematic Documentary scene-by-scene)

**CRITICAL:** Jason's hero asset is **Cinematic Documentary primary**, NOT UGC. Do not default him to UGC — that's Diana's slot. Per `Creative/Style_Sheets/Elevated_Documentary.md` (or equivalent): natural light, 35mm or 50mm prime feel, restrained color grading, real-room-tone audio, no artifice. The visual world is grounded in `01_Life_World.md` §§8–10.

**The Cinematic Documentary hero lives between the kitchen counter and the empty truck. Scene-by-scene direction (15s + 22s versions):**

**0:00–0:04 (Hook frame):**
- Medium shot of Jason at his kitchen counter on a stool, morning light from the window to his left. Plain heather-grey t-shirt, unbuttoned Carhartt overshirt over it. Five-day stubble. **Walker visible to his LEFT** (the femur side), strap visible across his lap from the boot brace. He's looking down at the counter — at a stack of unopened envelopes from Banner Gateway and the at-fault driver's insurance. Not at camera. Not performing.
- **Text overlay:** *"Broken bones."*
- Documentary feel. 35mm prime. Natural kitchen window daylight from camera-left. **Slight static handheld breath, no dolly.** Color is restrained — Cooper-style natural skin, no orange-and-teal grade.

**0:04–0:10 (The bills + the empty truck):**
- Cut to a close on the kitchen island surface. The stack of envelopes — Banner Gateway letterhead clearly visible on top, then a credit card statement, then a Hardin Concrete pay stub envelope (stamped). Jason's calloused right hand rests on the counter edge, motionless. **He's not reaching for them.**
- Quick cut to a wide shot through the kitchen window: the F-250 sits in the driveway, washed, keys visible on the hook just inside the open garage door. **No one in the frame.** The truck is the subject.
- **Text overlay:** *"Bills don't stop."*
- Documentary stillness. The truck is what's moved by not moving. Shotton's "unexpected motion in a still domestic scene" trigger — here, the motion is the *envelopes the camera notices* and the *empty driveway*.

**0:10–0:16 (The shop calendar + the crew text):**
- Cut to the detached shop. Tight on the paper desk-pad calendar pinned to the workbench. **Frozen on the first week of the month.** A pencil resting on the calendar where Jason left it. Dust on the pencil.
- Cut to an iPhone in Jason's calloused right hand. Text thread "CREW." Marco's message: *"Pour went clean today boss. Hector held the corner like you taught him. We got u."* The reply field is empty. Jason hasn't typed.
- **Text overlay:** *"Months of recovery."*
- Jason remains off-frame for both beats. **The system is the subject.** The shop without him. The crew without him.

**0:16–0:22 (Return to Jason's face):**
- Cut back to Jason at the kitchen counter, held. Tired but composed. Five-day stubble. Right hand still calloused on the counter. The walker still visible to his LEFT. **He's the same man as frame one, after the audience has seen what he's lost.** Quietly competent, weight accumulating in his face but not breaking.
- **Text overlay:** *"Find out what the law actually says."*

**0:22–end (CTA):**
- Final text card: *"Arizona drivers seriously injured in a crash — free case review. No fee unless we win."*
- WCTL logo end-card.
- AZ Bar compliance disclaimer (tiny text): *"Attorney advertising. Past results do not guarantee future outcomes."*

**Aspect ratios to produce:** 1:1 (FB Feed) + 9:16 (Reels + Stories).

**Character lock for Jason** (from `01_Life_World.md` §§8 + 10 — these are MANDATORY in every Cinematic frame):

- 45, 5'10"–6'0", solid working-man build, mid-brown hair short and slightly grey at the temples
- Five-day stubble (the visual marker that he's lost his shaving routine)
- **Calloused right palm** — visible in any shot where his hand is in frame
- Wedding band, plain titanium, **left ring finger**
- Casio G-Shock watch on left wrist
- Small white scars on knuckles (forming-and-rebar work)
- Plain heather-grey t-shirt (or navy in some frames)
- **Carhartt B11 brown duck dungarees** OR sweatpants on bad-pain frames; never jeans
- Carhartt overshirt unbuttoned over t-shirt
- **Boots replaced by Crocs slip-ons (early frames) or unlaced Red Wing Iron Rangers (later frames)** — never neatly laced; he can't bend down
- Faded Hardin Concrete cap NOT on his head (sits on the counter or on a hook)
- **Walker on his LEFT side (Weeks 1–5) or wooden cane on his LEFT side (Weeks 6+) — MANDATORY in every applicable frame**. Left femur fracture. Flipping the cane to the right side breaks the character lock.
- Faint bruising along left ribs through the t-shirt (fades by Week 6)
- No visible facial bruising (the face damage was minimal; the structural injuries are below the neck)

**Cinematic Documentary style discipline:**
- Natural light only (kitchen window light, garage daylight, dawn outside the truck)
- 35mm or 50mm prime equivalent — minimal lens distortion
- Restrained color grade — natural skin, slightly warm but not orange
- No artificial smoke, no haze, no rain (this is AZ — clear sky 320 days/year)
- No film-look heavy grain
- Static or slight breathing-handheld; **no dolly moves, no Steadicam swoops, no slow-motion**
- Real room tone audio; no music in the cinematic hero (music optional in the UGC variant)

## 4. UGC Variant (retargeting, 15s, 9:16)

**Purpose:** pattern-break for retargeting cohort that saw the cinematic but didn't tap. Different visual world.

**Setup:** Jason at the kitchen counter, phone-camera handheld feel (slight wobble, flat phone color, no cinematic grading). 15s, 9:16 only. Single take, blunt.

**Hook line on screen:** *"You did the work. Make sure the math is right."*

**Body:** Jason looks at the camera (yes, here he looks — UGC convention). **One sentence, one beat:** *"I got hit by a guy looking at his phone. The insurance company offered me thirty-eight thousand dollars. I'm a foreman. I do the math for a living. That's not the math."*

**CTA:** *"Free case review. No fee unless we win."* — text overlay only, no voiceover CTA.

**Why this works:** the UGC variant is permission to break the documentary fourth wall. Jason addresses the camera as if texting his cousin Pete. The math language is in his voice. **It is the only frame in the campaign where Jason speaks directly.** Use it sparingly.

**Risk to police:** UGC drift toward "performative testimonial." If Jason's read on camera feels trained or scripted, the asset reads fake. Direct him to deliver as if explaining the situation to Pete or Marco — flat, fact-stating, slightly tired. **Not acting.**

## 6. Compliance Considerations Specific to Jason (Saul's gate)

- **AZ Bar ER 7.3 (direct solicitation):** Lead hook *"Broken bones. Bills don't stop."* is third-person. Alt A *"You did the work. Make sure the math is right."* uses second-person — **Saul flag: this is targeting-adjacent.** Mitigation: the headline is content-driven (a statement of competence), not solicitation; the body copy stays third-person; review with WCTL's AZ-licensed counsel before launch. **Recommendation: keep Alt A in the test pool but tag it for compliance review at Day 3 of the launch.**
- **AZ Bar ER 7.1 (truthfulness):** body copy references "surgery, hospital stays, months of recovery" — factually accurate for Jason-type cases. *"More than what insurance first offered"* is truthful (initial offers are typically 25–40% of final settlement value; aligns with `00_Evidence_Register.md` §9). *"We coordinate with workers' comp"* must trace to actual WCTL service capability — confirm with Robert before launch. **No specific dollar amounts promised.**
- **AZ Bar ER 7.5 (firm naming):** WCTL logo + firm name in end-card. AZ Bar # in disclosures.
- **Substantiation:** any WCTL outcome claim ("more than $1.7B recovered") traces to the canonical aggregate per Davis's substantiation note. **Do not break this figure into per-case ranges unless Robert confirms the supporting data.** No "$1M+ settlements!" claims for Jason's slot.
- **Fault-presumption rigor** (per `Audience/00_Evidence_Register.md` §14): T-bone with the other driver running a red is among the cleanest liability cases in AZ. Police report + traffic-cam footage = clean liability. **No fault-rebuttal language is required** ("the law is on your side" type framing is unnecessary for Jason and reads defensive). **Skip it.**
- **Workers'-comp coordination claim:** Saul's specific gate. *"We coordinate with workers' comp, not against it."* — this claim reads true for WCTL's service (per `In_Depth/Internal_Strategy_Memo.md`), but should be vetted with Robert/Neama as part of the substantiation pass. If they cannot substantiate, replace with a softer line: *"workers' comp questions? We can help."*
- **Medical lien considerations:** Banner Gateway will file a hospital lien on Jason's settlement. Intake script should proactively explain lien-negotiation strategy. Lien complexity for Jason is moderate (likely $100K–$130K total medical against $120K–$180K settlement projection). Net to Jason after fees + liens: estimated $55K–$95K, materially better than the $38K + $0 lien-handled outcome of accepting the adjuster's offer.
- **TCPA consent language:** required on quiz-funnel lead-capture, standard.

## 7. Creative Acid Tests (before shipping any Jason creative)

Three questions. 3/3 = ship. 2/3 = revise. 1/3 = wrong audience.

1. **Would Jason read this line and feel recognized, not pitied?** Not "would a generic blue-collar man" — specifically *would Jason, the 45-year-old Apache Junction concrete foreman who's been reading PI billboards on US-60 for fifteen years, read this and think "oh, they actually see me"?*
2. **Would Jason screenshot this and send it to his crew text thread or his cousin Pete with no caption?** Does it read as documentary-truthful, or would it embarrass him to share because it's melodramatic, advocacy-ish, or trying too hard?
3. **Does it name the role inversion without naming it directly?** Does the creative's visual or subtext signal that WCTL sees him as *a man whose competence has been temporarily relocated to a recliner* — not as a generic injured person? Does it avoid the four register failure modes (pickup-truck-and-sunset, country-music montage, aggression copy, Hollywood alpha)?

## 9. Binet's 40/60 Effectiveness Lens (volume-slot discipline)

Per `02_Emotional_Architecture.md` and parallel to Diana's volume-slot framing:

**Jason's slot is volume-floor for the male half of the largest addressable pool.** Per-case value is moderate-to-good ($120K–$180K projected; $55K–$95K net after fees + liens). Binet's 60/40 frame inverts for volume segments: **activation ≥ brand-signal** in short-term optimization.

**Practical implications:**

- **Hook filter can be sharp.** *"Broken bones. Bills don't stop."* is tight; we're comfortable losing soft-tissue self-identifiers because volume math still works. The filter is *especially* tight for Jason because he is *literally* the broken-bones case.
- **Creative refresh cadence can be faster.** AZ trades-male audiences have high mental availability for the PI category (US-60 / I-10 billboard saturation has trained them). Rotate UGC variants every 2 weeks; cinematic hero every 3 weeks. Don't worry about brand-signal erosion; Jason isn't being sold on WCTL brand long-term — he's being sold on competence today.
- **Direct-response metrics weight heavily.** CTR, LPV, quiz-completion-rate are the operational signals. Quiz-completion-rate especially — Jason is the persona most likely to bail at quiz step 3 due to focus issues at 2 AM. **A short quiz wins.**
- **Brand signal is lower-stakes** for Jason's optimization. WCTL doesn't need long-term brand recall in Apache Junction; it needs Jason to tap → quiz → consult → sign within 5 weeks.
- **BUT — the campaign's male-recognition test runs through Jason.** If he significantly outperforms a generic male creative, the four-persona Meta architecture is validated. If he underperforms, we either re-tune the trade slot (HVAC instead of concrete?) or accept that the AZ male audience converts on a different lever entirely. **Protect the persona integrity even while tuning direct-response.**

## 10. Research Gaps (what this persona hasn't validated)

Items to validate during or after the pilot:

- **Trade-slot accuracy.** Concrete foreman is the current lock. If pilot data shows under-conversion in non-concrete trades (HVAC, electrical, plumbing), the visual-world specificity may be too narrow. Validate by reading quiz-step-1 occupation data; if "concrete" or "construction" dominates while "HVAC" and "plumbing" underperform, broaden the next creative cycle.
- **The 2 AM concussion-insomnia window.** Modeled as 2:00–3:30 AM. Verify with Meta delivery data Week 1–2. If Jason-type profiles don't disproportionately tap in that window, reallocate spend to the 8:30–11:30 PM window only.
- **Cinematic vs UGC performance for the male audience.** Diana is the UGC test. Jason is the Cinematic test. If Jason's UGC variant out-performs the cinematic hero, the production assumption that "men prefer cinematic" is wrong — flag and revise.
- **Workers'-comp differentiator resonance.** Alt B *"Workers' comp dragging?"* is a Jason-specific hook. If it converts above projection, the workers'-comp coordination service is a Phase 2 creative anchor for the entire male trades audience — not just Jason. If it underperforms, the differentiator isn't market-load-bearing; the campaign reverts to severity-filter Lead.
- **Kristy as form-filler.** We modeled Kristy as deferential-but-influential; the consult is for Jason but Kristy reads the firm's site. Intake data will validate. If Kristy is a more active form-filler than modeled, retune the landing-page tone for husband-and-wife co-review.
- **The role-inversion theme's actual resonance.** We argue the empty-truck + shop-calendar + crew-text-thread visual triple is load-bearing. A/B test the cinematic hero against a simpler kitchen-counter-only cut to isolate which beat carries the activation.
- **Ben/Marco-style shadow figure resonance.** Marco-as-acting-foreman is Jason's specific deprivation parallel to Diana's invisible labor. The campaign creative does not name Marco directly but the empty-truck + crew-text frames imply his presence. If Week-3 retargeting data shows the crew-text frame disproportionately drives quiz starts, Marco is a hidden activation lever to lean into for Phase 2.
