## Production-Room Pivot — Concept 04 (Bring-A-Foreman) — 2026-04-29

**Trigger:** Davis fired Giuseppe's production room (Giuseppe + Massimo + Fink + McCabe + Indigo) after seeing the first Higgsfield candidates for Slides 2 (calendar) and 3 (boots + cane). His diagnosis (verbatim): *"these are boring me, giuseppe these need help these arent going to hook him the lead is burried."*

Followed by the deeper concept-architecture diagnosis: *"why would he stay on this ad its gonna take 4 fucking images to get to the hook he would scroll immediately especially because its not actually a carousel."*

**Production-room failure analysis (post-fire):**

The Reels-as-carousel framing inherited from Beat 4 (Davis's "Reels only" lock) was a wrong delivery vehicle for this register. Diagnosis on three counts, all valid:

1. **Lead buried.** $38K landed on slide 5 of 6 — eternity in Reels grammar. Slide 1 (clean F-250 listing-frame) read to Jason as "truck for sale, irrelevant" because he already has a truck; he scrolled before the frame ever pivoted.
2. **Mechanism mismatch.** The auction-listing format-bend ("is this a truck listing? wait, what kind of listing?") REQUIRES viewer-driven discovery. Real BAT/IG-listing carousels work because the user controls the scrub — the wrongness compounds at the user's pace, the punch lands when they're ready. Forced linear video kills the mechanism.
3. **Subject ≠ violation.** The first two slides shipped were register-correct but descriptive — boots-by-the-door, calendar-on-bench. Each photo described the inventory when it needed to indict the violation. Even with surgical Fink/McCabe rewrites (cane-as-subject, freeze-edge macro, dated headlines), the architecture remained broken.

**Davis's call (Fork A):** re-cast Concept 04 as a **single static feed image** (4:5 portrait, 1080×1350) — full-info-density auction-listing graphic-design post, the way real classic-truck IG accounts publish when they want all listing data baked into the image. The format-bend completes in one frame, in 2–4 seconds of feed-scroll comprehension, the way it would on a real BAT IG post.

**What this changes:**

- Format scope: violates the Beat 4 "Reels only" lock for Concept 04 only. The other four concepts (01/02/03/05) remain Reels-native.
- Production cost: drops from ~$10 across six stills to ~$3–6 across iterations of one composite still.
- Mechanism: format-bend now operates natively (single-image scroll-stop) instead of fighting Reels grammar.
- Cinematic-pairing logic in `00_Concept_Set_Overview.md` still holds — Bring-A-Foreman remains the cold-prospect first-impression pair for the Cinematic Hero, just as a static-image variant rather than a Reel.

**Killed artifacts (no longer in scope):**

- The 6-slide carousel sequence (slides 1–6 with progressive listing-detail reveal)
- Slide-advance Foley + 2.5s-per-slide pacing + Kling slow-tighten on slide 6
- Slide 6 kinetic Tiempos pivot ("FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD" as a kinetic-text resolve)
- Per-slide Higgsfield character-lock continuity (slides 4/5/6 kitchen-counter chain)
- Carousel-as-Reels assembly mechanic (page-indicator dots, swipe transitions, swipe Foley)

The "FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD" line and the $38K asking price are PRESERVED — they now both live as rows inside the listing-block panel of the single static image. The format-bend resolves in the same place it always did; it just happens in one frame instead of six slides.

**Updated deliverables:**

- `04_Concept_Bring_A_Foreman.md` — concept doc updated 2026-04-29 (format / register-bend / visual treatment / hook-overlay / production spec sections rewritten)
- `04_Bring_A_Foreman_Higgsfield_Prompts.md` — fully rewritten as single-still spec with full-info-density listing-block typography baked into the image (or composited in CapCut if Image 2 typography legibility fails)
- `00_Concept_Set_Overview.md` — Concept 04 row updated (format = single 4:5 static feed image; cost = ~$3–6)

**Why this is the right call (room consensus, post-pivot):**

- **Hegarty:** the format-bend was always the work; the Reels framing was a delivery error from the room. Static restores the mechanism.
- **Massimo:** the listing-graphic-design typography composite is closer to the actual BAT-IG-listing register Jason consumes than a video edit ever was.
- **Fink:** the visual mischief lives where it should now — in the format-bend operating natively in feed, not in slow-burn carousel transitions.
- **McCabe:** every line in the listing is fact-density. The "wait, what?" cycle is built into the listing rows themselves; copy didn't need to change, just the surface it lives on.
- **Ferrier:** the voice test still scores 3/3. Recognition mechanism is unchanged — Jason's algorithm-trained pattern recognition for BAT/IG-listing posts; role-inversion still lands sideways via the listing rows; sendable to Pete with no caption (Pete reads classic-truck IG too).
- **Saul:** all substantiation flags hold. The compliance gate is unchanged.

**Lesson for the wider concept set:** Beat 4 format scope ("Reels only") was a strategic constraint Davis applied uniformly, but the Bring-A-Trailer register specifically depends on user-controlled scrub. If a register's mechanism depends on agency, locking it into a forced-linear delivery vehicle breaks the mechanism even if every other element is correct. Going forward: register-bends get a delivery-vehicle compatibility check at concept-room close, not at production-room time.
