## CODA — Anchor pressure-test (2026-05-01 PM)

After the closing session, Davis pressure-tested the Anchor (persistent bottom-third designed overlay) hypothesis directly: "is that even something that works?" The room reconvened (Depesh chairs, Simon + Hegarty fold in) with deep web research as input — Reddit threads, marketing forums, paid-social practitioner content, PI-vertical case studies, Motion benchmarks, agency blogs.

### Research findings (load-bearing)

1. **No A/B isolating the persistent-panel pattern exists in the public record.** Motion 2026 benchmarks measure hook *title cards* (frame-1 text that exits) — a different mechanism. Hook cards leave the frame; Anchors stay. Different result expected.
2. **Practitioner consensus runs against persistent designed overlays.** Hott / Denney / Common Thread Collective / Sanchez camp argues native-ugly creative outperforms designed/polished branded creative on Meta. A 35-40% panel from t=0 telegraphs "ad" before cinematic content can do hook work. Likely *reduces* hook rate even if it lifts comprehension downstream — opposite-direction funnel mechanisms.
3. **PI vertical evidence cuts against us specifically.** Morgan & Morgan flagship = character-driven comedy (not designed-overlay-heavy). Zahavian case = $800 → $297 CPL via "simple image of lawyers on the team" (not designed-overlay-heavy). Rankings.io / INSIDEA carousel data outperforms video by 20-25% in PI. The visible "designed-overlay PI ad look" is plausibly explained by compliance signaling + low-budget production + older audience — not by competitive testing wins.
4. **Structural finding (kills the original two-surface design):** bottom 35% of IG Reels is Meta's UI safe zone (Loomer / adsuploader / billo confirmed). A persistent panel pinned there collides with Meta's chrome. **Anchor cohort is FB-Feed-only by physics, not choice.** "Clean wins on Reels" wasn't a hypothesis — it was the only option there.

### Verdict (Depesh)

> **AMBER on the Anchor pattern, not RED.** The hypothesis is reasonable — sound-off comprehension on FB Feed older skew, Special Ad Category creative-must-qualify, trust signaling in a scammy vertical. The evidence doesn't justify a 13-unit production commitment up front.

### Revision (Davis approved)

- **Anchor build cut from 13 → 4 units.** One per copy register: A1-S1-Primary (Promise), A2-S2-Primary (Specificity), A5-S5-Primary (Speed), A5-S5-Cinematic-V1 (Reassurance).
- **FB Feed deployment only** — IG Reels UI safe-zone collision is structural.
- **Day 5 hook-rate gate** against matched Clean variants. Within 15% = GO, scale to remaining ~9 in Week 2. Drop >15% with no CPL offset = KILL pattern entirely.
- **Freed Mon-Tue hours (~5h)** banked as buffer: J2 Saturday-iteration absorption, Sunday work overrun, optional M3 Ty Injustice fresh build, or early-start on Day 14 J2-B Trade Tutorial swap-pool build.

### Hegarty's protective note

> "The Anchor pattern dilutes the cinematic register we spent two weeks building. S5 Diana cinematic *is* the elevated documentary register. Pinning a 40% Roman Navy panel at the bottom converts that into a designed thumbnail. Whatever the algorithm does with it, the *creative* loses register integrity. Test 4 to know. Don't pre-commit 13."

### Simon's ops math

> "13 composites is roughly a full Mon + half of Tue of Davis's time. That's load-bearing capacity in the build sequence right before launch. Cutting to 4 frees Mon afternoon + most of Tue. Ops flexibility worth more than 13 vs 4 composites."

### Nigel commercial check on the revision
