## Why pedestrian, why Laura, why static

Pedestrian is the **highest-empathy vertical** in the pilot — 268 incidents/month in AZ, strongest emotional resonance among form-fillers (disproportionately female family members or friends of the injured pedestrian). The case type is also the most emotionally delicate: pedestrians are almost always innocent parties, and the creative has to release them from self-blame ("I shouldn't have crossed there") without tipping into pity or rage at the driver.

Laura is the archetype. 15 years walking the same three-mile loop with her dog Maggie. Hit by a 29-year-old driver making a right turn at Gilbert & Warner, 5:45 AM. Right tibial plateau fracture + Grade 2 concussion. The crash didn't break her body as much as it broke her *relationship with her street*.

**Her voice from the persona file:**
> *"I've walked the same three-mile loop for 15 years. I know this place. I know every dog, every family, every change."*
> *"The crash didn't break my body. It broke my relationship with my street."*
> *"Hit while crossing the street? The one you crossed ten thousand times?"*
> *"The neighborhood I knew is still there. I'm not sure I am anymore."*

**Why static only for S2:** Laura's emotional register is stillness — place-loss, composure, the quiet after. A 15-second video of her isn't *more* than a single composed frame; in many cases it's less. Static image + 5-word hook fits her audience's scroll pattern (Saturday mornings, evenings after kids in bed). Also: static is a disciplined A/B test against the video-heavy rest of the set — Meta tells us whether feed static outperforms Reels video for this specific audience.

## Locked primary — S2

**Hook (on-image text):** *"Hit while crossing the street?"*

**Primary text (Meta body copy):** "You weren't the one driving. Arizona law says you have options even if insurance says otherwise. Free case review."

**CTA button:** Learn More

**Formula (HPR, static):**
- **Hook (top of frame, large text):** Question form, hard situation filter.
- **Problem (mid-image, visual):** Laura at Gilbert & Warner crosswalk at dusk, three-quarter profile, cane in L hand, ACE wrap visible at R ankle. She's looking at the painted lines, not at camera.
- **Reveal (small text, bottom third):** *"You weren't the one driving."* — a single line that reframes the blame.
- **CTA (button, bottom):** "Learn More" + "Free case review" supporting text.

**Visual direction (Alec Soth / Nadav Kander composition):** Dusk light at Gilbert & Warner crosswalk. Warm sand-colored asphalt, cyan dusk sky, amber streetlight. Laura standing at the curb, three-quarter profile, looking down at the crosswalk lines. No vehicle in frame (per brand standard — no crash). Her cane (L hand, wooden or matte-black aluminum) and the ACE wrap (R ankle, beneath jean cuff) are continuity props. Pale-gray cardigan over white t-shirt, well-worn medium-wash jeans, simple slip-on Sperrys.

**Self-qualification:** "Crossing the street" is the hard situation filter. Vehicle occupants self-exclude. Third-person copy respects Meta SAC Personal Attributes policy.

**Rationale:** This is the locked primary from the April call. Works because Laura is composed, not crying, not pleading — the composition reads as *quiet agency*, which is exactly her psychology. The "you weren't the one driving" body line releases the self-blame without saying "you deserve" (Laura rejects pity framing).

## Variant S2-V1 — Place-identity door

**Hook:** *"The crosswalk you crossed ten thousand times."*

**Pull:** Place-loss door, not situation-loss door. Opens Laura's deepest emotional wound: the 15-year ritual that broke in 1.5 seconds.

**Formula (HPR, static):** Hook (top). Problem/visual (Laura at crosswalk, same composition as primary). Reveal (small text): *"Should still be yours."*

**Visual direction:** Same scene architecture as primary. Text swap + slight camera move (wider shot showing more of the street she walked). **Tier A** production (text swap from primary).

**Rationale:** No other PI ad in AZ is pulling the place-loss door. This variant positions WCTL as the firm that understands injury is not just physical — it's the loss of the ordinary routines that made your life yours. Scroll-stopping for anyone who has a place-identity ritual (walkers, joggers, dog-walkers, runners) and sees themselves in the "ten thousand times" specificity.

## Variant S2-V2 — Quiet agency (no-pity release)

**Hook:** *"You weren't the one driving."*

**Pull:** Isolates the line that was body copy in the primary and elevates it to the hook. Releases the self-blame immediately in the first three words — ideal for pedestrians who are stuck in the *"maybe I shouldn't have crossed there"* loop.

**Formula (HPR, static):** Hook on top. Image: Laura looking slightly up and away (hopeful, not defeated). Reveal (small text): *"Arizona law still has your back."*

**Visual direction:** Same scene, slight composition adjustment — Laura's face angle lifted from the primary's "looking at lines" to three-quarter up-and-out. **Tier A** production (same background, adjust subject angle).

**Rationale:** Strongest filter + strongest release in one hook. The line is doing two jobs (filter + reframe) in three words, which is the tightest we can run.

## Variant S2-V3 — Poetic restraint (TTC, challenger)

**Hook:** *"The street is still there. She isn't, yet."*

**Pull:** The most cinematic and highest-risk variant. Third-person, two-beat structure (sentence + comma + one word). The "yet" is the TTC turn.

**Formula (TTC, static):**
- **Tension (0–3s equivalent in static = dwell time on composition):** The line itself + a wide composition of the empty crosswalk at dawn (no Laura in frame).
- **Turn (middle of composition):** Laura in a corner of the frame, not centered — small, present but not dominating. A glass of the word *"yet"* carries the hope.
- **Conversion (bottom text):** *"Arizona trial attorneys. Free case review."*

**Visual direction:** Alec Soth / Nadav Kander at their most restrained. Dawn light on Gilbert & Warner, empty crosswalk, Laura in the lower third of the composition, very small in the frame, leaning on her cane. **Tier B** production (new composition, different framing from V1/V2 primary).

**Rationale (Byron's caveat flagged to Robert):** This is the elegant variant — the kind of copy only Second Chair would write. Nobody else in AZ PI is operating at this register. But Byron Sharp's mental-availability principle flags a risk: restraint this deep may test *below* V1/V2 on click-through because it demands more cognitive work from a scrolling viewer. We run it as a conscious challenger, not a flagship. If it wins, we learn the audience ceiling for restraint is higher than we assumed. If it loses, we learn where that ceiling is.

## Variant S2-V4 — Neighborhood specificity

**Hook:** *"Gilbert & Warner changed in 1.5 seconds."*

**Pull:** Hyperspecific geographic + temporal. Scroll-stopping for anyone who *knows* that intersection — which is a meaningful portion of East Valley suburbanites.

**Formula (HPR, static):** Hook (top). Image: Laura at the intersection, same composition as primary. Reveal: *"One driver, one left turn, one life changed."*

**Visual direction:** Same scene architecture. Text swap. **Tier A** production.

**Rationale:** Tests whether hyperspecific geography lifts regional engagement (Gilbert/Chandler/Mesa quadrant). Valuable even if it loses — it tells us the audience responds to abstract framing over specific geography, which shapes Phase 2 targeting.

**Compliance:** Factual naming of an intersection is permitted under AZ ER 7.1. "Changed in 1.5 seconds" is not a causation claim against any party; it describes the event generically.

## Compliance notes

- All 5 Laura variants are static images, which reduces Meta review time and risk of motion-based sensationalism flags.
- No crash imagery in any variant. The crosswalk is the place; the absence of a vehicle in frame is the discipline.
- Third-person copy throughout. "You weren't the one driving" is permitted per Meta guidance because it's a reframe of agency (releasing blame), not a personal-attribute assertion.
- Body copy emphasizes "seriously injured — fracture, surgery, hospitalization" to filter soft-tissue pedestrian cases out of the funnel per Doc 5 §A.
- Laura's cane and ACE wrap are compliant proof-of-severity without graphic content.
