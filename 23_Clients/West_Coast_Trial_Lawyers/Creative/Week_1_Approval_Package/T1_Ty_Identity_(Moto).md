## ⚠ Production reality (T1 primary actual cut)

Original brief specified **Absence visual doctrine** — no bike, no helmet on body, no crash-adjacent imagery. **Final production diverged**: the T1 primary opens with the bike (garage), uses a cracked helmet on asphalt with skid marks (crash aftermath), and includes a comparative-negligence determination letter as the Agitate beat. **AI crash imagery is in-bounds for AZ pilot** (no SB-37) so this is on-rule.

The variants below are restructured to follow the actual production arc — **Identity → Cause → Loss → Systemic Unfairness → Funnel CTA** — so they slot into the same 5-visual production pattern Davis already proved out on the primary.

## Why motorcycle, why Ty, why Identity frame

Motorcycle = **highest-self-qualification vertical**. Riders recognize themselves in the first three words of an identity-framed hook; non-riders self-exclude. Cleanest filter we have, audience small (~120-160 incidents/month statewide) and high-value.

Ty: 32, HVAC tech, engaged to Rachel (wedding postponed), hit by Nissan Rogue making a left turn at Southern & Dobson at 4:45 PM. Full sun, doing the speed limit. Femur fracture, intramedullary nail, six screws. **Injury is real; deeper crisis is social** — everyone presumes he was speeding. Shame of presumed guilt IS the scroll-stop.

Ty carries two produced motorcycle concepts: T1 (this file) **identity** and T2 **injustice** (`T2_Ty_Injustice_(Moto).md`).

> *"I was doing the limit. Forty-five. Full sun. She didn't see me."*
> *"The R6 was the first thing in my adult life that was entirely mine."*
> *"Nobody close to me has said out loud 'this wasn't your fault.'"*

### Script (17.6s · 5 visuals)

**Visual 1 (0–5s)** — Yamaha R6 supersport in Ty's home garage, parked on rear stand. Cracked tank paint visible (post-crash). Shoei helmet rests on the seat (also visibly cracked). Tools + Craftsman toolbox in background. Warm interior light, low ambient.

**VO + caption:** *"Riders don't ask for sympathy, they ask for what's fair."* (caption builds word-by-word across the clip)

**Visual 2 (5–7s)** — Cracked motorcycle helmet on asphalt at AZ suburban intersection at golden-hour. Skid marks lead toward camera into the foreground. Empty road. Stoplight + palm trees + low desert houses in the deep distance.

**VO + caption:** *"A driver didn't see him."*

**Visual 3 (7–9s)** — Ty at his kitchen table. L-leg in matte-black brace stretched out across the chair. Stack of medical bills strewn across the table; **PAST DUE** stamp visible in red. Wedding ring on his hand. Worn dark t-shirt. Diffuse afternoon window light.

**VO + caption:** *"The bills don't stop."*

**Visual 4 (9–13s)** — Close-up: Ty's hand holding an insurance letter. Letterhead reads *"MERIDIAN ASSURANCE GROUP / RE: COMPARATIVE NEGLIGENCE DETERMINATION — 30%"*. Addressed to Mr. Tyler Rivera, Mesa AZ. Body text states they're applying 30% comparative negligence and won't honor the full claim. Signed *"Daniel P. Whitman, Claims Adjuster."* PAST DUE bills stacked beneath.

**VO + caption:** *"The presumption doesn't either."* (~9.5–11s)

**VO + caption:** *"No fee unless we win."* (~11.5–13s)

**Visual 5 (13–17.6s)** — Over-shoulder: Ty on couch in living room (lamp glow behind), holding iPhone showing the Fair Case intake quiz. **FAIR CASE** logo (torch + Tiempos wordmark) at top. Question: *"What type of motor vehicle accident were you in?"* with 6 options (Car / Truck / Motorcycle / Rideshare / Pedestrian or Bicycle Hit by Vehicle / Bus — note: no DUI option per Service Agreement §1.3). Progress bar shows *"Step 1 of 7 · 14% complete"*. Disclaimer at form bottom: *"This ad is sponsored by West Coast Trial Lawyers."*

**VO + caption:** *"Find out what fair looks like today."*

### Visual direction
**Cinematic motion + slow camera moves**, not high-motion. Each clip is a held composition with subtle drift — bike rests on stand, helmet sits on asphalt, hand holds letter, phone screen glows. Warm interior light + golden-hour exterior. **No Fair Case torch in the bike/helmet/table/letter clips** — branding lives only on the quiz form (Visual 5). This is the Davis production rule: branding added in final CapCut pass, not baked into footage.

### Rationale
First three words ("Riders don't ask") = identity self-qualification. Non-riders scroll past in 1.5 seconds. The arc reframes the standard PI "fight for your rights" energy into **dignity + factuality**: rider doesn't want sympathy → driver caused it → bills are real → insurance company's "30% comparative negligence" presumption is the unfair part → here's the door (quiz form). The comparative-negligence letter is the load-bearing prop — it shows the *exact* documentary form the systemic injustice takes for a working tradesman who wasn't speeding.

### Compliance
- AZ ER 7.1 clean — "No fee unless we win" is the AZ-permissible contingency-fee disclosure, not a guarantee
- Meta SAC: third-person; "Riders don't ask for sympathy" = community assertion, not personal attribute
- Insurance letter prop is generic (Meridian Assurance Group is fictitious; claims adjuster Daniel P. Whitman is fictitious; address is realistic Mesa, AZ but generic apt) — no real carrier defamation
- Funnel disclaimer ("This ad is sponsored by West Coast Trial Lawyers") satisfies TCPA 1-to-1 consent attribution at first touch
- AI-generated crash aftermath imagery (helmet on asphalt, skid marks) is in-bounds for AZ (no SB-37); craft bar met (golden-hour, photographic, no shock factor)
