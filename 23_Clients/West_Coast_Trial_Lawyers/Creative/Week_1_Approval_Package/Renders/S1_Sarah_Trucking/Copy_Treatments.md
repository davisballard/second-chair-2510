## Word budget discipline (load-bearing — read before the variants)

A Meta Feed creative is read at thumb-scroll, ~6 cm tall on a phone. **In-image text budget caps:**

- **Photographic with dark-gradient overlay:** ~15–25 words main type + ~12 words tiny disclaimer footer
- **Designed-static editorial (Hero Number, Exhibit):** ~25–35 words including hero element supporting type + ~10–15 words footer
- **Chart-led (Exhibit pattern):** ~15 words text + chart carries the rest

**This is not "wall of text" → "tiny copy."** It's variety across the six — some ultra-lean (V5: headline + closing, ~15 words), some hero-number-anchored (V3), some chart-led (V4), some hook + subhead + closing (V1, V2, V6). Each variant has a target density McCabe + Giuseppe locked together. Density is a compositional choice, not a copy-length problem.

The "no fee unless we win" beat + past-results disclaimer **always live as an 8pt IBM Plex Mono footer**, not as body text. The "ten minutes free" / "what happens after click" beat is **either folded into the closing line OR dropped** depending on the variant — if the layout is too tight, the Meta system context (Sponsored ad, Learn More button) carries enough signal that "no commitment" implication holds without explicit text.

## Trigger Window

**Month 2-4 post-crash, 9-11 PM evening scroll.** Slow-burn data-specific clinical anger. Sarah scrolls past urgency / billboard energy — competent calm wins her.

**Hooks that fail her:** "Are you injured?" (banned + lazy) · "Don't let them take advantage of you!" (billboard) · "Get the settlement you deserve!" (pity) · "Time is running out!" (manufactured) · "Fight for your rights!" (combat).
