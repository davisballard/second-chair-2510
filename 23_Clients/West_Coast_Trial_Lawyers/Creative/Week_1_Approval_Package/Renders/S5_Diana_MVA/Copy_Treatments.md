**Week 6-10 post-crash, 9 PM – 2 AM evening + concussion-insomnia couch-scroll** (off cervical collar, PT three times a week, 30-min screen tolerance, employer's short-term disability kicked in at Week 6 dropping her to 60% pay, $18K adjuster offer sitting unanswered, Ben asking if they should hire someone, household running on Ben's emergency-mode admin which is slowly cracking). **2:00 AM – 4:00 AM is unusually strong** because of concussion-related sleep disturbance + anxious mental-load.

- Anything with manufactured urgency or pity-loaded language

**Hooks that work for her:**
- The locked S5 hook: *"Broken bones. Bills don't stop."* — household-CFO factual, no melodrama
- System-seeing copy: *"We handle the legal side. The household side is yours."*
- Specific dollar anchors that match the math she's running ($18K vs. $33K-on-the-table, 52% income drop)
- Investigative phrasing ("Find out where the case stands." / "See what the case is worth.")
- Fault-shift facts that are quietly stated, not argued (distracted-driving language, A.R.S. § 28-914)
