## Iteration targets (apply to every render)

For every Higgsfield generation, regenerate until all of these land:

- **Side-specific lock holds** — cane in Laura's OWN LEFT hand (viewer's RIGHT side of frame). ACE wrap on her RIGHT ankle visible under the jean cuff (where ankle is in frame). Wedding band on LEFT ring finger. Small pink healed scar on LEFT forearm if forearm is bare. **The single most common Higgsfield failure on this character is cane-side flip — reject any frame where the cane is in her right hand.**
- **No vehicle of any kind in the frame** — no car, truck, SUV, motorcycle, bicycle, scooter, bus. The crosswalk is the place. The absence of the vehicle is the discipline.
- **No crash, no debris, no skid marks, no broken glass.** Laura is post-recovery, standing at the curb composed.
- **Mouth closed, natural breath only.** No speech-shape. No mid-word pose. No "addressing camera." Eyes follow the scene direction (down at lines / up-and-out / etc. per variant).
- **Honest 42-year-old face** — visible laugh lines, freckle pattern across nose bridge, fine pores, subtle asymmetry. **No** glamour-shot smoothing, **no** youthful model face.
- **Suburban East Valley read** — sun-lightened hair, slight tan, working-mom register. Not Beverly Hills, not boutique-styled.
- **Asymmetric composition** — rule-of-thirds anchored. Reject centered-symmetric defaults.
- **No AI tells** — no waxy skin, no uncanny eyes, no extra fingers, no floating hair, no HDR, no over-sharpening, no over-saturation, no airbrushed finish.
- **No text in image, no logo, no watermark, no UI overlay, no CTA button visible.** Branding is added in CapCut.
