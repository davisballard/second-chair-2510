## Trigger Window

**Week 8-10 post-crash, evening 8-10 PM** (PT progress started, mentally re-engaging, Mike has been suggesting "maybe talk to a lawyer" since Week 3 and she's been putting it off, around Week 8 she starts Googling). Saturday morning 9-11 AM is a secondary window.

**Hooks that fail her:**
- "Are you injured?" — banned (Meta SAC) + impersonal
- "Make them pay!" / "Get the settlement you deserve!" — billboard energy + combat framing
- "Don't be a victim twice!" — exploitative
- "Fight for your rights!" — combat (her conflict-aversion will reject)
- "Sue the driver!" — uses banned vocabulary (per Fair Case banned-phrases list)
- Anything with manufactured urgency

**Hooks that work for her:**
- The law as factual reference ("Arizona pedestrians have right-of-way at marked crosswalks.")
- Quiet accountability ("Drivers must yield. Most don't know what happens when they don't.")
- Neighborly observational truth ("The crosswalk you crossed ten thousand times is still there.")
- "Find out what the law says" as investigative, not combative
