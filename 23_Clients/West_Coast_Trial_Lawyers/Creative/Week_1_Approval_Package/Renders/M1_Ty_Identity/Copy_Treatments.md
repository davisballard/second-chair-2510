- Third-person factual statements that match his lived experience ("A driver didn't see the rider.")

- Specific dollar anchors that match the adjuster math he's already running
- Quiet structural truths ("Most riders are hit by drivers who don't see them.")
- Investigative phrasing ("Find out what the case is worth." / "See what the law says.")
