**The job this frame does:**

**Prompt:**

```

Warm amber identity color register. Kodak Vision3 film grain subtle. Desaturated with lifted blacks, honest color, no HDR, no over-saturation.

Negative: no people, no faces, no watermark, no HDR, no waxy finish, no over-sharpened edges, no dealership or product-shoot quality, no glossy commercial shine, no showroom lighting, no artificial fill, no stock-photo flatness, no cartoon look, no CGI render quality.
```

**Iteration notes:**
- **The crack on the helmet is load-bearing.** If the Shoei looks pristine, regenerate — the crack is what creates the micro-mystery that holds the eye past 1 second.
- **R6 silhouette must be readable.** If it looks like a generic sportbike, reinforce "Yamaha R6 supersport, angular fairings, twin underseat exhausts."
- **"Lived-in, not showroom"** is the tone lock. If the bike looks too clean/new, reinforce chain-lube marks, swingarm scuffs, working-rider bike.
- **Watch for commercial shine** — product-shoot aesthetic kills the documentary register. Reject anything that looks like a Yamaha ad.
- **Golden-hour light must rake** — if light looks flat or even, reinforce "long horizontal shadow stripes from doorframe across concrete floor."

**REVISION 2026-04-24 — Added left-side crash damage to bike.**
First-pass generations had pristine bike, which undercut the premise — viewer couldn't clock that this was a serious (non-soft-tissue) crash case. Saul compliance GREEN-LIGHT: damaged bike at rest = aftermath doctrine, same category as cracked helmet / recovery props. FORBIDDEN is crash footage (the impact itself), not aftermath imagery.

Damage spec (all left-side, matching Ty's crash pattern — hit by Nissan left-turning across his lane, goes down on his left):
- Left fairing cracked and scraped, long horizontal road-rash streaks exposing plastic
- Left flank of tank gashed, paint scraped through to bare metal
- Left mirror broken and dangling / missing (biggest scroll-visible damage cue)
- Left footpeg bent back, scratched raw
- Left frame slider chewed and blackened — "did its job" (rider-detail tell)
- Front left fork impact scars

Read target: "aftermath, not wreckage." Bike still dignified, still recognizable as HIS BIKE — but clearly went down in a serious impact.
