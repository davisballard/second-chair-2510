## ⚠ Production reality (T2 primary actual cut)

T2 was originally framed as a **static-only concept with 4 photographic stills**. **Production added a Cinematic Dashcam-Evidence Video** (`WCTL - T4(2).mov`, 17.2s, 5 visuals) — first motion unit in the T2 concept, **now elevated to primary**. The dashcam-as-witness register is a natural extension of T2's "witness: the skid marks" injustice framing. T2 now has mixed format: 1 produced video (IG Reels + FB Reels) + 4 planned statics (FB Feed + IG Feed). Fills the Reels reach gap that was previously zero for T2.

The original empty-mirror static (which was the planned primary before production) is **demoted to a planned static variant** alongside V1-V3.

## Why T2, why Injustice frame

T1 = identity. **T2 = injustice.** Closes the presumption-of-fault wound directly. Audience = rider who has been *told* or *treated as if* they caused their own crash, and is carrying that alone.

> *"A driver didn't see the rider."*
> *"Nobody close to me has said out loud 'this wasn't your fault.'"*
> *"The ER nurse asked 'were you speeding' before she asked 'how much pain.'"*

**VO + caption:** *"Find out."*

**Visual 3 (5–10s)** — **Motorcycle dashcam POV** from rider's perspective — handlebars + R6 dashboard reading **32 MPH** in foreground. Suburban AZ road, sunset glow ahead, palm trees lining the boulevard. Side mirror visible right side showing car behind. **VO + caption:** *"Find out what the law says about your case."*

**Visual 4 (10–13s)** — Same dashcam-POV continued. Camera moves through intersection.

**VO + caption:** *"They said the record was wrong."* *(approximate — caption sequence partial)*

**Visual 5 (13–17.2s)** — WCTL office consultation interior, golden-hour. **Ty in burgundy leather chair**, dark t-shirt + jeans + leg brace knee-down, crutches leaning against the chair. Attorney in suit sitting across, paralegal taking notes on yellow legal pad, partner standing by window with AZ desert sunset behind. Coffee mugs + notepad on coffee table, dashcam-screenshot photo on the table (the record from V3-V4 now part of the case file).

**VO + caption:** *"The law reads the record."*

**VO + caption:** *"Free 60-second case review."*

### Visual direction
**Cinematic motion with embedded dashcam-POV register.** V1-V2 are crash-aftermath wides (golden-hour suburban AZ). V3-V4 are first-person motorcycle POV with realistic dashcam UI overlay (recording timestamp, speed indicator, resolution-rate). V5 is the WCTL consultation interior — burgundy leather + wood paneling + sunset desert through window. The dashcam-screenshot prop on the consultation table closes the loop — the rider's own record IS the legal evidence.

**No Fair Case torch in the crash + dashcam clips.** Branding lives only on the V5 consultation visual (case file + WCTL room + sunset).

### Rationale
**Dashcam-as-witness IS the T2 injustice frame in motion.** Static T2 variants pull "witness: the skid marks" (V3) and "the helmet didn't fail" (V4) — both object-evidence. The primary video extends the same evidence-led register to motion: the rider's own dashcam is legal evidence that proves the rider's case. Scroll-stops riders who have dashcams (large + growing AZ subset) and want to know how to use them. Closes on consultation, not phone quiz — Laura-style ending grounded in human-relationship of being heard by attorneys.

### Compliance
- AI-generated crash-aftermath imagery (crashed bike + Nissan Rogue) in-bounds for AZ (no SB-37); craft bar met (sunset photographic, no impact, no injured bodies)
- Dashcam UI overlay = generic mobile-app-style mockup, not impersonating a specific dashcam brand
- "32 MPH" speed reading = generic speedometer display, not specific to Ty's actual case (Ty's case was 45 in a 45)
- Nissan Rogue rendered with damage visible but no plate / no driver identification
- "Free 60-second case review" = standard adjacent disclosure
- AZ ER 7.1 clean
- Meta SAC: question-form hook + third-person body permitted
