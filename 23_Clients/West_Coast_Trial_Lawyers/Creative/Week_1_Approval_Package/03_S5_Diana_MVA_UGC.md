## Why general MVA, why Diana, why UGC

General MVA (car-on-car, non-commercial) is the **volume vertical** — 400-600 incidents/month in AZ, the largest case-type pool and the catch-all for the ad set. It's also the **most-advertised-to vertical**, which means the creative has to break pattern. Every PI ad in this category looks the same: billboard-energy lawyer face, "MILLIONS WON" text, aggressive CTA. That's the category mean. Diana's 5-asset UGC cohort is the zag.

Diana is the archetype: Senior Office Manager at a property management firm. Rear-ended by an F-250 on I-10. 52% of household income on her $48K salary (Ben's $44K is variable). Three kids (14, 11, 8). The crash injury is real (C5-C6 disc + concussion), but the deeper crisis is **system failure** — she's the invisible operating system of the household, and when she can't work, the whole system stops.

**Her voice from the persona file:**
> *"Bills don't stop because you can't work."*
> *"Three kids. Two incomes. One crash."*
> *"I'm 52% of household income. I'm the stable half."*
> *"Broken bones heal. Bill collectors don't wait."*
> *"Who knew where the permission slips were?"*

**Why UGC-Native for all Diana variants:** Diana is the form-filler archetype Meta needs to reach for volume. The UGC register — handheld iPhone, candid unposed, natural daylight, no cinematic grading — reads as *authentic*, which is the register that breaks through category-mean billboard-energy PI ads. Barry Hott's discipline: UGC is NOT "Elevated footage downgraded." Different lens (phone wide ~28mm equivalent), different lighting (auto-white-balance phone color), different framing (handheld micro-wobble, re-frames mid-clip).

## Locked primary — S5

**Hook (0–3s on-screen):** *"Broken bones. Bills don't stop."*

**Primary text (Meta body copy):** "Broken bones, surgery, months of recovery — insurance companies know most people take the first offer. You don't have to. Arizona trial attorneys. Free case review."

**CTA button:** See Options

**Formula (HPR, 15s, UGC):**
- **0–3s Hook:** Diana at kitchen island, filmed phone-style from across the counter. Sling visible R arm. Text overlay of hook. Auto-white-balance warm kitchen light.
- **3–10s Problem/Narrative:** Envelopes fall onto the island, one at a time (bills). Cut to laptop showing paused work document (she can't focus through the concussion). Cut to kids' homework abandoned on the counter. Text: *"Your family shouldn't carry this alone."*
- **10–13s Reveal:** Cut to Diana's face, tired but composed. Text: *"Arizona law says you have more options than the first offer."*
- **13–15s CTA:** "See Options" button. Phone number. WCTL logo.

**Visual direction (UGC-Native):** Ben's phone feel — filmed from the hip, slight handheld wobble, camera re-frames when Diana moves. Natural morning or evening kitchen light, no studio illumination. Diana's reading glasses pushed up in her hair (ICONIC continuity). Pale-yellow cardigan over white t-shirt OR gray sweatpants (rest frames). Medical bills piled on the counter. NO cinematic grading — flat phone color. "As if filmed by Ben with one hand on his phone."

**Self-qualification:** "Broken bones" is a hard severity filter in the first two words. Soft-tissue self-identifiers scroll past. "Bills don't stop" is universally empathic — the tension-relief pivot happens in three seconds.

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Three kids. Two incomes. One crash."*

## Variant S5-V2 — Invisible labor

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Who knew where the permission slips were?"*

**Pull:** The invisible-labor door. No other PI ad in any category is pulling this frame. It says: when you can't work, it's not just income that's gone — it's the whole operating system of your family that nobody else saw you running.

**Formula (HPR, UGC):** Hook as text. Problem visuals — frozen calendar on the kitchen wall, a missed permission slip on the counter, a child's field-trip flyer dated Tuesday with today's date showing. Cut to Diana's husband looking lost at the same counter (optional — or just the empty space where she would be). Reveal = *"You ran everything quietly. We'll take one thing off your plate."*

**Visual direction:** New UGC scene. Heavy on object-frames (calendar, permission slip, field-trip flyer) before cutting to Diana or Ben. **Tier B** production.

**Rationale:** Highest empathy-weight in the Diana set. Scroll-stops women who run households. Works especially well on Saturday-morning or 2-4AM scroll windows when the concussion-insomnia and mental-load pressure is highest for Diana's audience.

**Compliance:** "We'll take one thing off your plate" is a soft-help framing, not a guarantee. AZ ER 7.1 clean.

## Variant S5-V3 — Numeric shock

**Hook (0–3s):** *"They offered eighteen thousand. The ER alone was six."*

**Pull:** Mathematical scroll-stop. Numbers are mute-compliant — they hit even with sound off. Reaches viewers who have already been *made an offer* and suspect it's low (Week 6+ post-crash funnel stage).

**Formula (HPR, UGC):** Hook as text. Problem = close-up of an $18,000 settlement offer letter (prop) on kitchen counter, then close-up of an ER bill summary showing $5,847. Reveal = Diana's tired-but-steady face + *"The first offer is rarely the fair offer."*

**Visual direction:** Same kitchen, same UGC handheld. Text-swap on primary scene architecture + one new close-up of prop documents. **Tier A** production.

**Rationale:** Quantified tension beats abstract tension. Anyone who has ever been handed a lowball insurance number recognizes the math immediately. Scroll-stops for the offer-validation audience.

**Compliance:** Prop numbers ($18,000 offer, $5,847 ER) are framed as *Diana's example, not WCTL results*. No claim about what WCTL can achieve. AZ ER 7.1 clean. The numbers are generic enough to avoid specific-case identification.

## Variant S5-V4 — Day-parted 2-4AM

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Concussion won't let you sleep. Neither will the bills."*

**Pull:** Hyper-specific to Diana's 2-4AM insomnia scroll window. Reaches people at the exact moment concussion-driven insomnia has them on their phone, mental-load running loops.

**Formula (HPR, UGC):** Hook as text. Problem = Diana on couch in low light (phone-screen glow on her face), bills on the couch beside her, household asleep. Reveal = *"Arizona trial attorneys. Here when you are."*

**Visual direction:** New UGC scene — low-light living room at 3AM. Phone glow on Diana's face. Household dark. Bills within reach. Same locked character. **Tier B** production.

**Rationale:** Scheduled delivery — this variant is *bid up* during 2-4AM ET hours and *bid down* during daytime. Meta's day-parting controls allow this. The hook loses power in daylight; in insomnia-hours it feels like someone understood.

**Compliance:** "Here when you are" is permissible availability framing, not a direct solicitation. AZ ER 7.3 clean (not unsolicited contact — audience self-selects via form).

## UGC register discipline (Barry Hott)

**UGC is NOT "Elevated desaturated."** That reads as low-quality cinematic, not authentic UGC, and uncanny-valley fails both audiences.

UGC prompt settings:
- **Lens:** ~28mm equivalent (phone wide), not 50mm cinematic
- **Lighting:** Auto-white-balance phone color, no studio illumination, natural daylight only
- **Framing:** Handheld with micro-wobble, subject re-frames mid-clip, camera moves with the subject not against them
- **Grading:** Flat phone color, no cinematic LUT, no film grain
- **Acting register:** Intimate, un-performed. Diana does NOT look at camera. She's mid-life, mid-scroll, mid-moment.

Injection block lives in `Style_Sheets/UGC_Native.md` — paste verbatim into every Diana prompt.

## Compliance notes

- All 5 Diana variants are UGC-Native, which *reads as more authentic* to Meta's algorithm but also triggers **more rigorous review** if anything reads as impersonating a real user (Meta policy: ads cannot appear to be organic posts from a real person). Disclosure: "Sponsored" label is mandatory on all ad placements; we confirm this is surfacing via Meta's ad format, not suppressed.
- No graphic injury. Sling on R arm is the only recovery prop, visible but not emphasized.
- "Broken bones, surgery" severity language in primary text body for all 5 variants filters soft-tissue at the ad level.
- Numeric variants (V3) use prop documents, not WCTL case data. No guaranteed-results implication.
- All third-person framing. Diana's "Bills don't stop" is observational, not a personal-attribute assertion.
