## Why trucking, why Sarah

Trucking is the **highest-case-value vertical** in the pilot — commercial carriers operate under $1M+ policies, and a compound humerus fracture from a Freightliner rear-end is a policy-limits case. It's also the **smallest audience pool** (~91 incidents/month in AZ), so the hook has to hyperspecifically filter in the right person without needing broad-reach volume.

Sarah is the archetype: Trauma ICU charge nurse at AZ's only Level I trauma center north of Phoenix, 71% household income anchor, knows commercial insurance better than most attorneys because she's seen the claims process from the hospital side. She's not reachable through "fight for your rights" energy — she's reachable through *clinical competence*. The hook has to meet her at her register.

**Her voice from the persona file:**
> *"I know what my ER bill was — and it was forty-seven."*
> *"The compound-humerus isn't just a broken arm. It's a career threat."*
> *"Truck accidents are not car accidents. The lawyer should be different."*
> *"Commercial carriers have legal teams on call 24/7. You should too."*

## Locked primary — S1

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Hit by a Freightliner on I-17?"*

**Primary text (Meta body copy):** "Commercial insurance is different. If you were seriously injured — broken bones, hospitalization, surgery — in a crash with a commercial truck, the law says you have options. Arizona trial attorneys fight insurance companies every day. Free case review."

**CTA button:** Learn More

**Formula breakdown (PAS, 15s):**
- **0–3s Problem:** Hook text overlaid on wide shot of **I-17 at dusk** — the Sunset Point descent or a long-haul slope south of Flagstaff, a Freightliner in frame far ahead going the same direction. Cut to Sarah at kitchen table, arm brace visible on L forearm, medical bills stacked.
- **3–8s Agitation:** Cut sequence — corporate boardroom table (empty, cold fluorescent), a legal team's framed diplomas, adjuster's letter face-down on Sarah's counter. Text: *"Their legal team was working before the ambulance arrived."*
- **8–13s Solution:** Cut to Sarah's face, composed, looking slightly off-camera. Quiet VO or text: *"Commercial carriers have legal teams on call 24/7. You should too."* Fair Case combination mark appears bottom-right.
- **13–15s CTA:** Torch Amber CTA button slides bottom. Phone number 11pt bottom-right. Logo holds.

**Visual direction:** Elevated Documentary. Motivated natural light (kitchen lamp golden-hour spill, dusk blue ambient outside). Shallow depth of field. Sarah's arm brace (L forearm, black canvas, Velcro straps) and the small fading bruise on L temple are continuity props — visible in every frame she's in. No crash footage. The *absence* of the truck in Sarah's domestic space is the frame; the boardroom contrast is the weight.

**Character-lock note (from deep persona):** Sarah's face must read as **professionally composed** — NOT crying, NOT defeated, NOT seeking sympathy. The weight of the crisis is visible in the arm brace + medical bills + the fading temple bruise, never in her expression. Her composure IS the character lock. This is Sarah's psychology (Loss 2 from `Deep_Personas/Sarah/02_Emotional_Architecture.md`: "The calm becomes the patient" — 15 years as the composed voice; now she's the patient and doesn't know how to accept help, but she's still not performing grief on camera).

**Self-qualification:** Dual-lock filter on "Freightliner" (commercial vehicle brand) + "I-17" (Sarah's corridor, Flagstaff↔Phoenix). Non-commercial-vehicle, non-north-AZ audiences self-exclude in the first five words. Hyperspecific — the wrong person scrolls past instantly.

**Rationale — why I-17 leads (updated 2026-04-23):** Primary now leads with **persona-accurate geography** per `Deep_Personas/Sarah/01_Life_World.md` — Sarah's actual crash was on I-17 southbound, Sunset Point descent, mile marker 268. Leading with her corridor makes the creative feel *specific* rather than generic, and the Freightliner+I-17 double-lock is the tightest self-qualification filter in the S1 variant set. **V4 below** (*"Hit by a semi on I-10?"*) is the **broader-reach alternative** for Phoenix-Tucson commuter and long-haul audiences outside Sarah's specific geography. Meta learns which pulls harder; Sarah-type audiences (north AZ, clinical professionals, commercial-vehicle crash victims) should respond more to primary; broader Maricopa audiences should respond more to V4.

## Variant S1-V1 — Authority / competence door

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Commercial insurance is different. So is the lawyer."* *(Updated 2026-04-23 per Agency Creative Review — original ended with "you need"; trimmed to clear Meta SAC Personal Attributes.)*

**Pull:** Different emotional door — not situation-first, *case-type sophistication* first. Reaches the segment of the audience that already knows they have a trucking case and is now sorting signal from noise on lawyer choice.

**Formula (PAS):** Problem = "commercial insurance is different" (implicit — you need specialized help). Agitation = 3-5s of quick cuts showing commercial policy paperwork ($1M limit visible on a document), the legal team montage, Sarah's kitchen with bills. Solution = Sarah's composed face + WCTL logo. CTA same.

**Visual direction:** Same scene architecture as primary (text swap + one additional shot of the policy document). **Tier A** production.

**Rationale:** Filters differently than the primary. Where the primary asks *"were you hit by a semi?"*, this asks *"do you know why that matters?"* — it targets the slightly later-funnel viewer who has already connected the case to commercial insurance in their own head. Sarah's register.

## Variant S1-V2 — Power asymmetry

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Their legal team was working before the ambulance arrived."*

**Pull:** The 2am-adjuster-anxiety door. Factually true and clinically unflinching. Opens a specific Sarah-state: the moment she realized the adjuster's tone meant "we've been preparing for this call since the crash."

**Formula (PAS):** Problem = stark sentence. Agitation = shot of boardroom at night (empty but lit), then shot of Sarah awake at midnight at kitchen table, arm brace visible, looking at bills. Solution = "You don't have to face them alone. Arizona trial attorneys." CTA same.

**Visual direction:** Same character, same scene architecture, swap boardroom-at-night shot for one shot in the sequence. **Tier A** production.

**Rationale:** This hook scroll-stops late at night. It's mute-compliant — the whole weight is carried by the text and the boardroom-vs-kitchen visual contrast. Works especially well as day-parted placement (9PM–2AM bid lift on the ad set).

## Variant S1-V3 — Offer-validation door (DUAL REGISTER)

**Hook (0–3s):** *"The first offer is rarely the fair offer."*

**Pull:** Different funnel stage — reaches the audience that has *already been made an offer* and is second-guessing it. Week 6–10 post-crash viewer, not Week 1.

**Formula (PAS):** Problem = the line. Agitation = close-up of Sarah on phone (adjuster call), her face steady but reading the number. A glimpse of a written offer ($35,000) on the counter. Solution = "The first offer is rarely the number you're entitled to. Arizona trial attorneys know what a case is worth." CTA same.

**Visual direction — Elevated version:** Same Sarah, kitchen table, adjuster-call composition, Elevated Documentary register. Motivated natural light.

**Visual direction — UGC-Native version:** Phone-camera-style, handheld, Sarah sitting at kitchen table with one hand on phone, bills visible, no cinematic grading. Feels like Marcus filmed this with his phone for a moment, not a production. **Tier B** production — new scene for both registers.

**Rationale:** This is the one Sarah variant Barry Hott flagged as UGC-appropriate. The hook is conversational, the scene is domestic, and the offer-validation moment is exactly the kind of intimate thing a UGC-native viewer would trust from an "authentic" source over a polished one. Dual-register production lets Meta tell us whether UGC or Elevated pulls harder on *this specific hook* — other Sarah variants stay Elevated because her overall register is clinical.

**Compliance:** Do not show specific dollar amounts as "results" — the $35,000 is a visible number on a prop letter, framed as the insurance offer (not a WCTL result). AZ ER 7.1 clean.

## Variant S1-V4 — Broad-reach alternative (I-10)

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Hit by a semi on I-10?"*

**Pull:** Swap primary's Freightliner+I-17 double-lock for generic semi + I-10 corridor. Broader reach — I-10 is Arizona's most-recognized interstate statewide (Phoenix → Tucson → west to CA), pulls Maricopa commuter and long-haul trucking victims outside Sarah's specific geography. Pairs with primary to let Meta learn which audience slice responds to persona-accurate specificity vs. broad-reach generic.

**Formula (PAS):** Same structure as primary, with V4 geography swap to I-10 wide shot replacing the I-17 descent.

**Visual direction:** Same scene architecture as primary. Text swap + one b-roll shot of I-10 corridor (flat-desert context to distinguish from primary's I-17 mountain-descent). **Tier A** production.

**Rationale:** Direct A/B against primary. If primary (I-17/Freightliner) wins on CTR with north-AZ audiences, it validates the persona-specificity thesis. If V4 (I-10/semi) wins on total impressions and leads, it tells us the broader commercial-vehicle audience doesn't need Sarah's corridor specificity — valuable Phase 2 signal for scaling.

**Compliance:** Generic "semi" + "I-10" — no vehicle-brand or specific carrier identification. Clean under AZ ER 7.1. Meta SAC question-form hook is permitted with third-person body copy.

## Variant S1-V5 — FMCSA-literacy / ultra-filter

**Hook (0–3s):** *"FMCSA violations. Maintenance records. A $1M policy."*

**Pull:** Ultra-filtered variant for the segment of the audience that already knows commercial trucking has its own legal framework. This hook doesn't explain — it confirms. Lower volume than primary / V1 / V2 / V4, higher sign rate because anyone who recognizes "FMCSA" is already deep in the case-type awareness funnel. Sarah is this audience (per `Deep_Personas/Sarah/03_Category_Journey.md`: *"she already understands FMCSA; the creative should confirm not educate"*).

**Formula (PAS):** Problem = hook as factual three-phrase list (no question mark, no sales energy — just terms of art). Agitation = close-ups of actual-looking commercial-trucking evidence artifacts: a DOT-numbered tractor door, a driver's-log pages, a maintenance-inspection sticker with a failure date, a policy document page header "$1,000,000 Commercial Auto Liability." Solution = Sarah at kitchen table, composed, text: *"Arizona trial attorneys. Commercial truck cases require commercial lawyers."* CTA same.

**Visual direction:** Elevated Documentary. This variant is **evidence-led, not person-led** — Sarah appears only at the end. The first 10 seconds are tight shots of trucking documentation. Motivated office lighting for documents, kitchen-table return for Sarah's final composed frame. **Tier B** production (new evidence-composition scenes — DOT door number, driver log, maintenance inspection sticker, policy doc).

**Rationale:** This is the variant for the clinical-professional segment of Sarah's audience who will scroll past "Hit by a semi" hooks because they read as pitched at amateurs. Nurses, paralegals, insurance adjusters, fleet managers, former-truckers, legal-services professionals — anyone whose job has exposed them to commercial-vehicle mechanics. The hook signals *specialist firm*, which Sarah explicitly wants (per `Deep_Personas/Sarah/05_Campaign_Implications.md`: *"she needs someone who speaks that language back to her without condescending"*).

**Compliance:** "FMCSA violations" / "maintenance records" / "$1M policy" are factual terms of art, not claims against a specific carrier or case outcome. AZ ER 7.1 clean. Evidence artifacts are generic/illustrative (no real DOT numbers, no real carrier names, no specific case documents).

## Compliance notes

- All 6 Sarah variants are **AZ Bar ER 7.1 compliant** — no guaranteed results, no false expectations, no direct solicitation.
- Meta Special Ad Category: clean. Third-person framing throughout. Question-form hooks ("Hit by a Freightliner on I-17?" / "Hit by a semi on I-10?") are permitted per Meta's guidance when paired with third-person solution copy (see 08_Compliance_And_Counsel.md).
- No graphic injury content. Arm brace is the only visible recovery prop. Bills on counter are non-specific (no real names visible).
- "Broken bones, hospitalization, surgery" severity language appears in primary text body for all 6 to filter soft-tissue self-identifiers out of the quiz funnel.
- No crash footage in any Sarah variant. I-17 and I-10 wide shots show commercial vehicles in transit, not collisions.
- V5 evidence artifacts (DOT numbers, maintenance stickers, policy doc) are generic/illustrative, not specific to any real carrier or case.
