## Why motorcycle, why Ty, why Identity frame

Motorcycle is the **highest-self-qualification vertical** in the pilot — riders recognize themselves in the first three words of an identity-framed hook, and non-riders self-exclude immediately. That makes motorcycle creative the cleanest filter we have, which matters because the audience is small (~120-160 incidents/month statewide) and high-value (average settlement far above general MVA).

Ty is the archetype. 32, HVAC technician, engaged to Rachel (wedding postponed), hit by a Nissan Rogue making a left turn at Southern & Dobson at 4:45 PM. Full sun, doing the speed limit. Femur fracture, intramedullary nail, six screws. **The injury is real; the deeper crisis is social**: everyone — ER nurse, adjuster, Rachel's mother — assumes he was speeding. The shame of being presumed guilty is the scroll-stop.

Ty carries three separate sub-concepts (M1/M2/M3) because his motorcycle audience has three distinct emotional doors — **identity** (who riders are), **loss** (what the crash took), and **injustice** (the presumption of fault). Each sub-concept gets its own file. This file is M1, the identity frame.

**His voice from the persona file:**
> *"I was doing the limit. Forty-five. Full sun. She didn't see me."*
> *"A driver didn't see the rider."*
> *"The R6 was the first thing in my adult life that was entirely mine."*
> *"Nobody close to me has said out loud 'this wasn't your fault.'"*

**Why Absence visual:** No crash footage in M1. The visual anchor is *the empty parking spot where the bike used to be*, or the helmet on a shelf, or the garage door half-open on a space that should be occupied. The absence carries more weight than any footage of the bike or the crash could — viewers complete the story themselves.

## Locked primary — M1

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Riders don't ask for sympathy. They ask for what's fair."*

**Primary text (Meta body copy):** "When a motorcyclist is seriously injured — bones broken, surgery, months off the bike — insurance companies know most riders take the first offer. They shouldn't. Arizona trial attorneys. Free case review."

**CTA button:** Learn More

**Formula (PAS, 15s):**
- **0–3s Problem:** Hook text over wide shot of empty parking space at dawn (the Circle K where Ty used to meet his riding group). Asphalt, faint oil stain where the bike sat. No rider, no bike.
- **3–8s Agitation:** Cut sequence — Ty's helmet on a shelf in his garage (the cracked Shoei), dirt-bike boots beside it, leather jacket on a hook. Cut to Ty on the couch, leg elevated (the recovery frame). Text: *"The presumption was always that he was speeding. He wasn't."*
- **8–13s Solution:** Cut to Ty's face, composed — not angry, not defeated. Text: *"Arizona law hears riders. The court should too."*
- **13–15s CTA:** "Learn More" button. Phone number. WCTL logo.

**Visual direction (Elevated Documentary, Absence doctrine):** Dawn or dusk light. Desaturated palette. Shallow depth of field. Ty's leg brace or crutch is the continuity prop but is never the subject — the *space* is the subject. Helmet with a visible hairline crack sits on the shelf, never on Ty's head. The Yamaha R6 appears ONLY in garage-rest frames (bike covered or bike parked, never moving). No crash footage.

**Self-qualification:** First three words ("Riders don't ask") are identity-based. Non-riders scroll past. Third-person copy respects Meta SAC Personal Attributes policy.

**Rationale:** This is the locked primary from the April call. Works because it opens with *community* (riders) rather than situation (crash). It says: *if you're one of us, you already know.* Ty's composure and the absence visual carry the emotional weight without any scare-tactic imagery. This is the hook that filters hardest for the rider audience.

## Variant M1-V1 — Community call-out (record axis)

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Every rider knows what happened. The court should too."*

**Pull:** Same identity frame, shift from the sympathy axis to the *record* axis. Opens Ty's injustice wound while staying in the M1 identity register.

**Formula (PAS):** Problem = hook as text. Agitation = same helmet / bike / garage sequence + a shot of dashcam-style debris on asphalt (far away, no crash visible). Solution = *"Arizona trial attorneys get the record straight."* CTA same.

**Visual direction:** Same scene architecture as primary. Text swap + one new shot of asphalt with skid-mark context (no crash). **Tier A** production.

**Rationale:** Variant for the rider who has been through the "was he speeding?" gauntlet and is looking for *vindication* more than *help*. Works alongside the primary, not as a competitor — primary pulls the sympathy-rejection door, V1 pulls the record-straighten door.

## Variant M1-V2 — Tradesman dignity

**Hook (0–3s):** *"A rider doesn't need pity. He needs a receipt."*

**Pull:** Working-class respect register. Ty is a tradesman (HVAC); his audience is working-class. This hook speaks to guys who respect competence and want a tangible outcome, not a hug.

**Formula (PAS):** Problem = hook text. Agitation = same garage / helmet / recovery sequence + a close-up of a medical bill with the amount visible ($47,000 ER cost, prop). Solution = *"Arizona trial attorneys. Numbers, not pity."* CTA same.

**Visual direction:** Same scene architecture + one new close-up of prop medical bill. Text swap + detail shot. **Tier A** production.

**Rationale:** Targets the specific psychographic of the working-class rider who scrolls past "fight for your rights" energy but stops on *"numbers, not pity."* Scroll-stops in a register the rest of the PI category isn't writing in.

**Compliance:** $47,000 ER bill is a *prop illustrating Ty's cost, not a WCTL case result*. No guaranteed-results implication. AZ ER 7.1 clean.

## Variant M1-V3 — Peer-group / statistical vindication

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Six thousand Arizona riders. One got hit this month. He wasn't speeding."*

**Pull:** Statistical + third-person. Puts Ty in a community of *six thousand Arizona riders*, says one of them got hit, and closes the presumption-of-fault door in the third sentence. Three-beat structure in 15 words.

**Formula (PAS):** Problem = hook text. Agitation = wide shot of a group ride at dawn (riders on bikes in a parking lot, helmets, leathers — no Ty yet). Cut to Ty's bike parked alone. Cut to Ty on the couch. Solution = *"Every rider's story is different. The law should be the same."* CTA same.

**Visual direction:** New scene (group ride wide shot). Ty appears in final frames. **Tier B** production (new composition, group shot requires generation work).

**Rationale:** Variant for the rider who identifies with the *community* before the individual story. "Six thousand" anchors Ty in a peer group that makes his case feel like a known statistical type, not a one-off. "He wasn't speeding" is the quiet vindication.

**Compliance:** "Six thousand Arizona riders" is approximately accurate against AZ DOT registered motorcycle count (~180K, but active weekly riders ~5-10K in any given metro). Conservative enough to pass AZ ER 7.1 truthfulness. We cite the estimate as "Arizona riders" not "WCTL clients."

## Compliance notes

- All 4 M1 variants are AZ Bar ER 7.1 compliant — no guaranteed results, no false expectations.
- Meta Special Ad Category: clean. All third-person copy. "Riders don't ask for sympathy" is a community assertion, not a personal attribute.
- No crash footage in any M1 variant. The cracked helmet is present as aftermath object; never shown in the crash moment.
- Numeric claims (V2 $47K, V3 6,000 riders) are framed as illustrative examples / registered-rider estimates, not WCTL results.
- No motorcycle-brand denigration. Yamaha R6 appears in rest frames only; driver's vehicle type (Nissan Rogue) is named in later sub-concepts (M2, M3) but never in M1.
