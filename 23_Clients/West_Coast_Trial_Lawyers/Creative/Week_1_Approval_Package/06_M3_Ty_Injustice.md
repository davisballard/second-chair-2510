## Why M3, why Injustice frame, why Static

M1 = identity, M2 = loss, M3 = *injustice*. This frame closes the presumption-of-fault wound directly. The audience is the rider who has been *told* or *treated as if* they caused their own crash, and is carrying that alone.

**Why Static for M3:** Two reasons. First, static forces the hook and the image to do all the work — which is perfect for the injustice frame because the argument is *factual*, not emotional. A hook like *"A driver didn't see the rider"* paired with a single composed image of an empty intersection mirror is more powerful than any motion sequence could be. Second, static is the disciplined A/B test — Meta's feed placement favors video, but static that beats video in CTR/CPL on this specific audience teaches us something about where the rider's scroll happens (feed vs. Reels).

**Ty's voice from the persona file, M3 register:**
> *"A driver didn't see the rider."*
> *"Nobody close to me has said out loud 'this wasn't your fault.'"*
> *"The ER nurse asked 'were you speeding' before she asked 'how much pain.'"*

## Locked primary — M3

**Hook (on-image text):** *"A driver didn't see the rider."*

**Primary text (Meta body copy):** "Most motorcycle crashes aren't the rider's fault. Insurance companies assume otherwise. Arizona trial attorneys get the record straight. Free case review."

**CTA button:** Get Quote

**Formula (HPR, static):**
- **Hook (top of frame):** Third-person factual assertion. Six words.
- **Problem (center of frame, visual):** Empty driver's-side mirror of a parked Nissan Rogue (or generic SUV), reflecting an empty intersection. No rider visible. No bike visible. The *emptiness* of the mirror is the argument.
- **Reveal (bottom third, small text):** *"Arizona law hears riders."*
- **CTA (button):** "Get Quote" + "Free case review" supporting text.

**Visual direction (Elevated Documentary, object composition):** Dusk or golden-hour light. Desaturated palette, lifted blacks, film grain. Shallow depth of field. The mirror is the subject — framed tight enough that the reflection is legible. The intersection visible in the reflection is a suburban AZ intersection (evoking Southern & Dobson without naming it). No human subject in the primary. Ty appears only in V1/V2 variants.

**Self-qualification:** "Rider" in the hook + "motorcycle crashes" in body text. Non-riders self-exclude. Third-person copy clean under Meta SAC.

**Rationale:** This is the locked primary from the April call. Works because the *absence of the rider from the driver's mirror* is the argument in one frame. The hook names the cause (inattentive driver), the visual proves it (empty mirror), the reveal commits the firm (Arizona law hears riders). Clean HPR static in a single composition.

## Variant M3-V1 — Inverted (sharper contrast)

**Hook:** *"The rider saw the car. The car didn't see the rider."*

**Pull:** Same frame, sharper contrast. Two-beat parallel structure. The rider was paying attention; the driver wasn't. Closes the presumption-of-fault wound through symmetry.

**Formula (HPR, static):** Hook top. Visual = side-by-side or overlaid composition — left half shows rider's POV (helmet visor reflection showing the intersection, car visible), right half shows driver's empty mirror. Reveal: *"Arizona law knows the difference."*

**Visual direction:** New composition (split-frame or overlay requires generation work). **Tier B** production (new visual architecture).

**Rationale:** Scroll-stops because the structure is immediately comprehensible — two sentences, two perspectives, one truth. The split/overlay composition is visually distinctive against the single-image primary and acts as an A/B on visual approach.

## Variant M3-V2 — Skid-mark evidence (factual)

**Hook:** *"Forty-five in a forty-five. Witness: the skid marks."*

**Pull:** Pure forensic framing. The rider was doing the speed limit. The skid marks prove it. Graphic-free proof-of-innocence — mute-compliant numbers + factual testimony.

**Formula (HPR, static):** Hook top. Visual = close-up of skid marks on asphalt at dusk. Nothing else in frame. Reveal: *"Arizona trial attorneys read the record."*

**Visual direction:** Tight shot of skid marks on pavement. No vehicles, no rider. Just the marks. Dusk light, desaturated, slight film grain. **Tier A** production (simple composition, generateable fast).

**Rationale:** The most reduced variant in the M3 set — hook carries all meaning, visual is pure evidence. No people, no bikes, no story — just *proof*. Scroll-stops because the reduction is distinctive; every other PI ad has a person in it. This one doesn't need one.

**Compliance:** "Forty-five in a forty-five" is factually accurate for Ty's case (speed limit at Southern & Dobson is 45 MPH; Ty was at 45). Framed as illustrative of the non-speeding rider category broadly, not a specific case result. AZ ER 7.1 clean.

## Variant M3-V3 — Helmet still-life

**Hook:** *"The helmet didn't fail. The driver did."*

**Pull:** Object-story. A helmet with a visible hairline crack on a shelf. The helmet did its job — it absorbed the impact and kept Ty alive. The *driver* failed. Reframes the conversation from "motorcycles are dangerous" to "inattentive drivers are dangerous."

**Formula (HPR, static):** Hook top. Visual = Shoei helmet with visible hairline crack on a shelf, working-class garage ambient (tool shelf, garage floor). Reveal: *"Arizona law knows what failed. And what didn't."*

**Visual direction:** Tight still-life of helmet. Garage ambient lighting (overhead fluorescent or natural spill). Helmet shows hairline crack but is not destroyed — it's clearly a helmet that did its job. Working-class aesthetic (tools, workbench in soft-focus background). **Tier B** production (new object composition).

**Rationale:** Working-class rider pride + liability reframe in one image. Scroll-stops the rider who has been told *"well, at least you were wearing a helmet"* (implying the helmet was what saved them, not their own caution). This hook says: the helmet was the backup. The primary failure was the driver's.

**Compliance:** Shoei is a motorcycle-helmet brand; named generically via the helmet brief, not endorsed. Helmet visual is not graphic — cracked but intact, no blood, no hair, no fragments. AZ ER 7.1 clean.

## Compliance notes

- All 4 M3 variants are static, which reduces Meta review time and zero risk of motion-sensationalism flags.
- No crash footage in any M3 variant. Skid marks (V2) and cracked helmet (V3) are aftermath objects, not crash moments.
- Third-person copy throughout. "A driver didn't see the rider" is a generic causation statement, not a personal-attribute assertion.
- No vehicle-brand denigration. The Nissan Rogue mirror in primary is rendered generically; driver is never identified.
- "Forty-five in a forty-five" (V2) is permissible factual speed reference, not a claim against any specific case outcome.
