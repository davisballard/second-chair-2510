## Why M2, why Loss frame

M1 carries *identity* (who riders are); M3 carries *injustice* (the presumption of fault); M2 carries the middle weight — **loss**. What the crash took that's still being taken. Ongoing deprivation, not acute shock.

This frame reaches riders at a different funnel stage than M1. M1 speaks to the rider who wants vindication (record-straightening). M2 speaks to the rider who is *living with the accumulation* — weeks of unpaid leave, the wedding that was postponed, the $12K savings that went to the surgical-nail cost, the bike gone, the Rachel's-mom silence that means "I told you so."

M2 uses the **TTC formula** (Tension-Turn-Conversion) because the loss-frame is inherently paced — tension builds across multiple beats, the turn has to arrive at the right moment, and the conversion is the relief.

**Rachel as secondary emotional axis (M2 specifically):** M1 is rider-community-identity, Ty solo. M3 is factual-injustice, Ty solo (or Ty-less object-stories). **M2 is the only sub-concept where Rachel belongs in frame** — she's Ty's fiancée, the wedding was supposed to be this spring, the postponement is the concrete loss that makes Ty's silent-conviction land. Per `Deep_Personas/Ty/02_Emotional_Architecture.md`, Rachel is also the household's decision co-lead: she's the one who finally gets Ty to call a lawyer (he's too proud to; she's the pressure release valve). **Directive for M2 production:** Rachel should be present in at least one variant's frame — M2-V2 (wedding-fund math) is the natural home (show her hand next to his on the wedding-fund envelope, or a framed engagement photo on the counter beside the medical bills). Rachel does NOT appear in M2-V3 dramatization (that's Ty's solo intersection-vindication moment) or M2 primary. **M3 also holds Rachel out** — that's injustice-frame, which is Ty's solo wound.

**Primary text (Meta body copy):** "Medical bills keep coming. Wages stopped. The first offer was low. Arizona trial attorneys fight insurance companies for motorcyclists. Free case review."

Continuity props: crutch or leg brace visible in Ty's frames, Yamaha R6 visible in a garage-rest frame briefly. No crash footage. The *accumulation* is the visual weight.

**Self-qualification:** "The crash" + "taking" severity language filters soft-tissue self-identifiers. Paired with primary text severity language ("medical bills, wages stopped").

## ⚠ Variant M2-V3 — Moment-Before Dramatization (STRETCH / COUNSEL-FLAGGED)

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Southern and Dobson. 4:45 PM. Full sun. She never looked right."*

**Pull:** The scroll-stop variant of the entire package. Dashcam-style reconstruction of the exact intersection where Ty was hit, told from the moment-before. No crash shown. No injury shown. The *cut to black* is the story.

**Formula (TTC, 15s):**
- **0–4s Tension:** Dashcam-POV approaching Southern & Dobson intersection at 4:45 PM. Full sun, clear visibility. Text appears line by line: *"Southern and Dobson. / 4:45 PM. / Full sun."* Ambient road sound.
- **4–7s Tension (escalating):** POV continues. A Nissan Rogue enters frame from the right side, approaching the intersection from the opposite direction. Driver's head visible through windshield — turning *left* not *right*. Text: *"She never looked right."*
- **7–8s THE CUT:** Sudden cut to black. No impact shown. 0.5-second black frame. Sound cuts.
- **8–11s Turn:** Cut to Ty on his couch, leg elevated, composed. Text: *"The crash wasn't his fault. Arizona law agrees."*
- **11–14s Conversion:** Text: *"Free case review. No fee unless we win."* WCTL logo.
- **14–15s CTA:** "See Options" button. "Dramatization" overlay visible bottom-right throughout entire spot, 11pt minimum.

**Visual direction (Elevated Documentary, Pattern B Moment-Before):** Real Arizona intersection aesthetic — Southern & Dobson in Chandler, 4:45 PM golden-hour full sun (key detail: *full sun*, proves visibility wasn't the issue). Dashcam POV is simulated (AI-generated camera perspective). Nissan Rogue is rendered generically (any silver/white SUV — no real plate, no identifying details). Driver is rendered with head visible turning left but face never clearly shown (avoids impersonation). **No impact. No injury. No post-crash gore.** The cut-to-black is absolute.

**"Dramatization" overlay:** Bottom-right corner, white sans-serif on 40% opacity black card. 11pt minimum. Visible from frame 1 to frame 14-15s. Never obscured.

**Self-qualification:** Intersection specificity + "full sun" + "didn't look" filters for riders who have experienced an inattentive-driver left-turn crash specifically (the single most common cause of motorcycle fatalities in AZ, per NHTSA and AZ DOT data).

**Rationale:** Every other PI ad in this category shows either generic highway footage or pity-victim imagery. A dashcam-reconstruction of the *exact* intersection where Ty was hit, with his "I wasn't speeding" conviction behind it, is the kind of creative nobody else in AZ PI is making. It's *proof-of-innocence* not scare tactics — the visual argument is that the crash was the driver's fault, not that being a rider is terrifying.

**Risk profile:**
- **AZ Bar compliance:** Reenactments-with-disclosure are permitted under AZ Rules. "Dramatization" overlay is the disclosure. GREEN with counsel sign-off.
- **Meta Special Ad Category:** Implied-impact-without-gore-or-victim is permitted under Meta's Violent-Graphic-Content policy. The cut-to-black is the industry-standard compliant treatment. GREEN but rate-limiting risk: Meta's algorithm may deprioritize reach even after human review passes. Budget for the possibility.
- **WCTL brand:** This is the most aggressive creative in the package. If Robert or WCTL counsel decide it crosses the Second Chair "honest persuasion over shock factor" doctrine, it comes out. Our position: done right (full sun, cut to black, no injury, proof-of-innocence frame), it's MORE honest than the category mean — it shows the actual cause of the crash rather than hiding behind abstraction.

**Counsel routing:** This variant goes to WCTL counsel in a **separate email**, flagged for heightened review. Storyboard frames (5-6 key frames hand-drawn or AI-rough'd) go in that email alongside the 08_Compliance_And_Counsel.md citations.

## Variant M2-V4 — Wedding-postponement / Rachel perspective

**Hook (0–3s):** *"Your wedding was supposed to be this spring. Your recovery might not let that happen."*

**Pull:** Extends M2-V2's wedding-fund math (Ty's numbers) into the **relationship cost** (Rachel's experience). M2-V2 says "$12K was for the wedding, now it's for the surgery" — the money. M2-V4 says "the wedding itself is moving" — the life milestone. Reaches riders in serious relationships (engaged, newly-married, pre-house-buying) whose crash doesn't just break their leg, it breaks the timeline of a life they were planning.

**Formula (TTC, 15s):**
- **0–4s Tension:** Slow dolly on **Rachel's hand** adjusting an engagement photo frame on a shelf, then her hand on Ty's knee (leg brace visible) on the couch. Text appears line by line: *"Your wedding was supposed to be this spring. / Your recovery might not let that happen."* Domestic light.
- **4–11s Turn:** Cut to Rachel's face (three-quarter profile, composed, not crying — she's the one holding it together), then to Ty's face beside her. Voiceover (Rachel, quiet): *"The crash wasn't his fault. And it shouldn't cost us what comes next."* Text: *"A driver didn't see Ty. A lawyer needs to see what comes next for them."*
- **11–14s Conversion:** Text: *"Arizona trial attorneys. Free case review."*
- **14–15s CTA:** Torch Amber button. Fair Case combination mark bottom-right.

**Visual direction (Elevated Documentary):** Warm domestic interior — Ty and Rachel's living room. Engagement photo in frame (no faces shown, or generic / can't-identify). Rachel composed and steady. Ty's leg brace visible, his hand on hers. No crash footage, no wedding-day imagery (they haven't had one yet). **Tier B** production (new scene with Rachel as named character in the frame).

**Rachel as named character (new to this package):** M2-V4 is the first variant in the package where Rachel is *depicted* rather than implied.
