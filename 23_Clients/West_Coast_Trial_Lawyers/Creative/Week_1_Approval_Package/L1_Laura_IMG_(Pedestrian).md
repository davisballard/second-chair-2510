## ⚠ Production reality (L1 primary actual cut)

Original brief was **static-only with 6 photographic stills**. **Final production diverged**: the primary is now a **motion-still video (MS-Laura, the net-new from Agency Creative Review §2)** that uses the static-photo register at the open and close but with multiple compositions + black-screen reframe transitions + a closing in-office consultation scene at WCTL. The 5 static variants (V1-V5) remain photographic stills as planned.

L1 also closes on an **in-office consultation scene** (Laura in leather chair at WCTL with attorneys + her case file labeled "HENDERSON, L. — Pedestrian / Crosswalk Incident" on the coffee table) — **not the Fair Case phone quiz**. Different CTA register than the Sarah/Diana/Ty primaries. The consultation-as-ending grounds Laura's emotional restraint and matches her FB-Feed-leaning audience.

---

## Why pedestrian, why Laura, why mixed register

Pedestrian = **highest-empathy vertical** (~268 incidents/month AZ). Form-fillers skew female family/friends. Most emotionally delicate case type — pedestrians are almost always innocent parties. Creative has to release self-blame ("I shouldn't have crossed there") without tipping into pity.

Laura is the archetype. 15 years walking the same three-mile loop with her dog Maggie. Hit by a 29-year-old driver making a right turn at Gilbert & Warner, 5:45 AM. Right tibial plateau fracture + Grade 2 concussion.

> *"The crash didn't break my body. It broke my relationship with my street."*
> *"The neighborhood I knew is still there. I'm not sure I am anymore."*

**Mixed register decision:** L1 motion-still primary covers IG Reels + Stories surface (static-only would be suppressed there). V1-V5 remain photographic stills for FB Feed where Laura's quiet-dignity register reads native at full impression weight.

### Script (17.7s · 6 visuals)

**Visual 1 (0–3s)** — Laura at Gilbert & Warner crosswalk at dusk. Three-quarter profile. Cane in R hand. Pale-gray cardigan over white t-shirt, worn medium-wash jeans, slip-on Sperrys. Looking down at the painted crosswalk lines. Streetlight amber + cyan dusk sky. Suburban AZ residential houses + palm trees in deep distance. Gentle ambient motion (slight breeze on cardigan, subtle hair drift) — feels like a held photograph.

**VO + caption:** *"Hit while crossing the street?"*

**Visual 2 (3–6s)** — Different woman (blonde ponytail, white blouse) at the crosswalk dusk, looking ACROSS the street toward an approaching vehicle in the distance (car in transit, no impact shown). Different angle from V1 — she's a parallel pedestrian victim, suggesting the audience is wider than just Laura.

**VO + caption:** *"Find out what the law says about your case."*

**Visual 3 (6–10s)** — Black screen, white text only. Two beats in succession (each held ~2s):

**VO + caption:** *"You weren't the one driving."*

**VO + caption:** *"They blamed her anyway."*

**Visual 4 (10–13s)** — WCTL law office interior, daytime. Two attorneys at a wooden conference table reviewing a case file labeled **"HENDERSON, L. — Pedestrian / Crosswalk Incident"**. Legal books on shelves behind, downtown skyline visible through window, golden lamp on desk. One attorney standing pointing at a highlighted passage, other seated taking notes on yellow legal pad. Coffee mug.

**VO + caption:** *"The law says you have options."*

**Visual 5 (13–15s)** — Same WCTL office, attorneys still working through file. Subtle camera shift / different framing of the same scene.

**VO + caption:** *"Free 60-second case review."*

**Visual 6 (15–17.7s)** — Different WCTL room at night (Phoenix skyline lit through window). Laura sitting in burgundy leather wingback chair facing two attorneys (paralegal + lead attorney). HENDERSON case file on the wooden coffee table between them. Coffee mugs, water glass, attorney standing in soft-focus background reviewing notes.

**VO + caption:** *(no caption — closing consultation visual carries its own meaning)*

### Visual direction
**Cinematic Documentary motion-still.** Compositional stillness with ambient micro-motion (breath, breeze, slight hair drift) — feels like a photograph that breathes. **V3 black screens** are a deliberate "let the text breathe" CapCut pattern — gives the reframe ("You weren't the one driving / They blamed her anyway") full attention. **Closing on an in-office consultation** (V6) is the variant CTA — instead of the Fair Case phone quiz used in the Sarah/Diana/Ty primaries, Laura's primary closes on the human-relationship moment of being heard by attorneys. Matches her FB-Feed-leaning audience and emotional register.

### Rationale
**The reframe is the breakthrough.** Standard PI ads for pedestrians lean on victim-pity. Laura's primary instead does **release-of-self-blame** ("You weren't the one driving") + **acknowledge-of-injustice** ("They blamed her anyway") + **lawful-counter** ("The law says you have options") + **quiet-action** (consultation). The black-screen reframe section is the load-bearing emotional move — it forces the viewer to sit with the words alone before getting visual relief.

### Compliance
- AZ ER 7.1 clean — "The law says you have options" is a generic-class assertion
- "They blamed her anyway" = Laura-character third-person frame, not viewer assertion (passes Meta SAC)
- HENDERSON case-file label = generic, no real client identification
- AI-generated crash-corner imagery (no actual impact shown — Visual 2 just shows car approaching crosswalk in distance) in-bounds for AZ (no SB-37); craft bar met (dusk photographic, no impact, no injured bodies)
- "Free 60-second case review" = standard contingency-fee adjacent disclosure
- Meta SAC: third-person throughout
