## Why Jason — and why a wild track

Jason is the **male volume slot** — biggest single addressable market in the Severe MVA cohort. The Cinematic Documentary line (J1, pending build) does the credibility work for him cleanly. **What it does NOT do** is stop a thumb in a feed already saturated with Toyota Tacoma ads, Ridge Wallet, Mike Rowe Foundation, and three other PI billboard mills working in similar grammar.

J2 is the scroll-stop sister to J1. Each direction below lives in a register Jason **already consumes** (per `Audience/Deep_Personas/Jason/01_Life_World.md` §7) and bends the wrapper to land the same SMP through different frequency. The principle: *a register-bend so culturally precise to the audience it reads as recognition, not advertising.*

**SMP recap (`Jason/00` §4):** Jason's identity is built on a body that works and a crew that listens. A non-soft-tissue MVA hasn't just injured him — it has removed him from the role he built every other part of his life around. WCTL's job is to be the one specialist who treats him like he's still the competent man in his own room. Each direction honors that without ever naming it.

All 5 already cleared **3/3 on Ferrier's voice test** (recognized-not-pitied / send-to-Pete-with-no-caption / role-inversion-without-naming-it) and Saul's concept-set-level compliance gate per `Bold_Sister_Line_Severe_MVA/00_Concept_Set_Overview.md`. Production-stage gates land at build-out.

**Cultural touchpoint:** Mike Rowe / *Dirty Jobs* documentary segment. *"Watches Mike Rowe podcasts (Tyler sends him clips)"* per `Jason/01` §7. Handheld doc-cam grammar, plain-language VO that respects the work, the camera as a third party observing competence.

**Sample hook:** *"Twenty years on construction sites taught me one thing about how insurance companies handle a pelvic fracture."*

**Why it works:** Authority-from-tenure is the trades male's native trust signal. "Twenty years" is a number Jason runs his own life by (he's been doing this for twenty-two). The *Dirty Jobs* register treats him as a worker rather than an injured person — that recognition IS the SMP delivered visually. Per `Jason/02` Loss 3 (Crew Authority) — the doc form treats him as still on the job even though the cut tells you he isn't.

**Format:** 15s short-form video, 9:16. Tier B production, ~$15. Mike Rowe register is aesthetic — copyright fence: NO *Dirty Jobs* assets, NO Mike Rowe voice.

### J2-C — Season Over

**Cultural touchpoint:** PS2-era (2001–2005) cel-shaded sports-game cutscene + broadcast-chyron grammar. Per `Jason/01` §7: *"TV — ESPN, Sunday football, Cards games."* Jason's twenties were Madden + NFL on FOX every Sunday. The PS2 cel-shaded polygon look + period-accurate broadcast chyron is **the** muscle-memory cultural register from his early adult years.

**Sample hook (chyron, not VO):** *"WEEK 22 // CONCRETE FOREMAN OUT FOR SEASON."*

**Why it works:** Sports-broadcast injury graphics are how Jason has had every NFL game's stakes communicated to him for two decades. Reading those exact words about a *concrete foreman* is a register-collision. Per `Jason/02` Loss 3 — Marco's promotion to acting foreman *is* the same dynamic as an athlete being replaced. Per Loss 6 — chyron stat lines (BILLS OWED $96,400 / OFFER ON TABLE $38,000 / MONTHS OUT OF WORK 5 / WORKERS COMP STATUS DISPUTED) surface the case math in the exact format Jason watched stats appear during NFL Sundays for 20 years. The number isn't low; the number is *what they think he's worth*. Format makes the verdict visible.

The pivot — GAME OVER → live-action cut at frame 4 — is the load-bearing structural moment. Without it the spot reads as flippant. With it the playful register pivots into substantive content and Jason reads the whole piece as deliberate.

**Format:** 12s short-form video, 9:16 only. Tier C production (highest in the set; dual-register production), ~$30.

**⚠ Heightened-review flag if selected for build-out:** PS2 + sports-broadcast register has the most active copyright fence of any direction in the set. Counsel sign-off required at build-out (akin to how M2-V3 dramatization was previously routed before that frame was dropped). Specific exclusions: NO Madden / EA Sports / NFL 2K branding, NO Cleatus / FOX Sports asset, NO real broadcast graphic-package elements, NO NFL team logos. Aesthetic register only.

### J2-D — Bring-A-Foreman

**Cultural touchpoint:** Bring-A-Trailer / classic-truck Instagram auction-listing post. Per `Jason/01` §7: *"Instagram (rarely posts, follows: tools, classic trucks, two AZ surf-fishing accounts)."* Classic-truck listing content is in his daily feed — utilitarian listing typography, photo-grid layout, lot-detail captions in a specific terse register.

**Sample lot title (the hook):** *"LOT 2026-04: ONE YEAR OF A 45-YEAR-OLD AZ FOREMAN."*

**Why it works:** The format-bend completes in a single feed-native image, the way real classic-truck IG accounts post. Eye lands on the BAT-style F-250 photo → drops to the listing block → reads through the rows (TRADE TENURE: 22 YEARS / ROLE: CONCRETE FOREMAN / CRASH: T-BONE COLLISION, US-60 / CURRENT STATUS: OUT OF WORK 5+ MONTHS / **AT-FAULT INSURANCE OFFER: \$38,000** / WORKERS' COMP: DISPUTED / **FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD**) → realizes the lot is the year, not the truck. Whole cycle happens in 2–4 seconds of feed-scroll comprehension.

Closing action lines (panel bottom, above Meta's Learn More button): *"Hurt in a crash on the job? Find out what your year is worth. →"* in Tiempos italic Torch Amber, plus *"Free case review for AZ trade workers."* in smaller Cream type beneath. Click destination unambiguous; landing page mirrors the listing register at the top.

**Format:** Single 4:5 static feed image (1080×1350) — IG feed-native. NOT 9:16 Reels. Tier A production (cheapest in the set), ~$3–6. **This direction does NOT follow the package's 15-second video formula** — it's the only static-image direction in the inventory.

Listing copy revised 2026-04-30 by Hegarty/McCabe/Massimo room — lot title commits up front; "$38,000" attributed to the at-fault insurance offer (not a truck price); closing action line tightened so click destination reads unambiguously as a case review, not a truck-valuation tool.

Copyright fence: NO Bring-A-Trailer logo, NO Hemmings logo, NO BAT branding. Generic insurer names. \$38,000 attributed to at-fault driver's insurance offer (Loss 6 substantiation in `Jason/02`, ER 7.1 cleared).

### J2-E — Drive Time Dashboard

**Cultural touchpoint:** KMLE 107.9 country-radio morning-DJ patter. Per `Jason/01` §7: *"KMLE 107.9 (country) in the truck most mornings."* This was the radio station Jason listened to from 5:30–6:45 AM in his F-250 for 22 years — the cadence is muscle memory.

**Sample hook (DJ patter, two voices via ElevenLabs TTS):**

> **DJ A:** *"Morning, Apache Junction. Our man at Hardin Concrete is on day a hundred and forty out of work — pelvis, femur, ribs, the whole load."*
>
> **DJ B:** *"Yeah, partner. That one hits home."*
>
> **DJ A:** *"Insurance company's offered him thirty-eight grand to make it go away."*
>
> **DJ B:** *"...funny thing about the law."*
>
> **DJ A:** *"It doesn't think thirty-eight grand is the number either."*

**Why it works:** Audio register is in Jason's auditory muscle memory. Two-DJ format with "yeah-yeah" interjections and slight conversational overlap is how country morning shows have sounded for the entirety of his career. Hearing his own life narrated in that exact cadence is the deepest scroll-stop possible — audio bypasses the ad-skepticism filter because it arrives in a non-ad wrapper.

Visual is utilitarian: a parked F-250 dashboard at 5:50 AM (his old commute time), keys in the ignition, radio dial lit, light through the windshield. Static shot held ~14 seconds with two micro-motion edits. The truck does not move — the visualization of his paused life. Per `Jason/02` Loss 3, the DJ talking *about* him in third-person is the role inversion — he was the man other people called; now he's the subject the morning show is reporting on.

**Format:** 18s static-with-micro-motion + audio-led, 9:16. Tier B production, ~$8. Most format-experimental of the set (audio-led on a sound-OFF-default platform; CapCut captions are load-bearing). Indigo's gate: track sound-on rate over 14 days; if <40%, retire.

Copyright fence: KMLE NOT named in audio or visual. Radio dial scrubbed of any KMLE asset. Generic country-radio bumper synthesized, NOT a real KMLE asset. ElevenLabs TTS voices are AI — not real DJs.

## Selection criteria (the call Davis is making)

The selection drives both build-out and hook-variant development. Likely factors:

- **Cultural-precision fit.** Which register lands hardest for Jason specifically? J2-A (Mike Rowe doc) and J2-E (KMLE radio) score highest on muscle-memory frequency. J2-D (BAT listing) scores highest on feed-native scroll-stop in a static format.
- **Production cost / time.** J2-D is cheapest by far (~\$3–6, single static image). J2-E is second (~\$8). J2-A and J2-B mid (~\$15–18). J2-C heaviest (~\$30, dual-register polygon + live-action production + heightened counsel review).
- **Format diversity vs the rest of the package.** Most of the package is 15s video; J2-D would add a static image to the mix and J2-E would add an audio-led format. Either creates more diverse format coverage in the ad set.
- **Risk profile.** J2-C is highest-risk / highest-reward (the playful-register-pivot is structurally fragile; if the GAME OVER cut doesn't land, the spot reads as flippant). J2-D and J2-A are lowest-risk.
- **Robert's gut + cohort A/B test signals** post-Week-1 launch. Cinematic primaries from S1/S2/L1/D1/T1/T2 will produce CTR/VTR/CPL data; the bend that pairs best with the underperforming cohort moments becomes the canonical pair.
