## ⚠ Production reality (D1 primary actual cut)

Original brief specified pure UGC-Native (handheld phone-camera, kitchen island, envelope drops, flat phone color). **Final production diverged**: opens with Diana standing **outside an Auto Body shop next to her crashed silver SUV** (real-feel outdoor framing — UGC flavor only), then escalates through an **iPhone iOS incoming call screen from "Insurance Adjuster"** as a scroll-stop prop, into a Cinematic phone-call interior, into the **shared WCTL conference room with the $1.7B plaque** (same asset as the Sarah trucking primary), ending on the Fair Case quiz form. This is hybrid Cinematic-Documentary register, not pure UGC.

The variants below stay pure UGC for register diversity in the ad set — the dual register helps Meta learn which Diana audience responds to UGC vs Cinematic. Primary now spans both.

## Why general MVA, why Diana

General MVA = **volume vertical** (~400-600 incidents/month AZ). Most-advertised category — every PI ad here looks the same (billboard lawyer face, "MILLIONS WON", aggressive CTA). Diana's variants are the zag.

Diana = Senior Office Manager at a property management firm. Rear-ended by F-250 on I-10. 52% of household income on $48K (Ben's $44K is variable construction). Three kids. The deeper crisis is **system failure** — invisible operating system of the household.

> *"Bills don't stop because you can't work."*
> *"Three kids. Two incomes. One crash."*
> *"Who knew where the permission slips were?"*

**Visual 4 (10–15s)** — Shared WCTL conference room interior at golden-hour. Wood-paneled wall with framed case-result certificates around central plaque **"$1.7 BILLION RECOVERED"**. Attorneys in suits at table reviewing case maps + laptop showing case file + thick legal binders ("FMCSA INSPECTION" book visible). AZ desert mountains through window.

**VO + caption:** *"Arizona trial attorneys fight insurance every day."*

**Visual 5 (15–21.2s)** — Over-shoulder: Diana on couch in living room (lamp glow behind), holding iPhone showing Fair Case intake quiz. **FAIR CASE** logo + question + 6 accident-type options + Step 1 of 7 progress + WCTL sponsorship disclaimer.

**VO + caption:** *"Get your free case review in 60 seconds."*

**VO + caption:** *"No fee unless we win."*

### Visual direction
**V1 has UGC framing** (outdoor parking lot, real-feel daylight, friend-with-phone composition). **V2-V5 are Cinematic Documentary** (controlled lighting, designed compositions, branded conference room). The hybrid is intentional — V1 establishes the recent-crash victim register at street level; V2-V5 layers in the systemic-knowledge content (adjuster procedural trap → counter-force at WCTL → action via quiz). Per Davis production rule: branding lives only on the V5 quiz frame.

### Rationale
**The procedural-trap framing is the breakthrough.** Most PI ads sell "I got hit, give me a lawyer" — Diana's primary teaches: *"They want a recorded statement before you have a lawyer."* That's actionable knowledge that builds trust by warning of an actual adjuster tactic. The iOS incoming-call screen is mute-compliant (reads in any feed without sound) and pattern-interrupts because users see incoming calls daily. WCTL room provides the counter-force scale; quiz delivers action.

### Compliance
- "$1.7 BILLION RECOVERED" = canonical WCTL aggregate per `project_wctl_substantiation.md`
- "Arizona trial attorneys fight insurance every day" = generic-class assertion
- "They want a recorded statement before you have a lawyer" = factual procedural knowledge, not defamation of a specific adjuster
- AZ ER 7.1 clean — "No fee unless we win" = permitted contingency disclosure
- AI auto-body-shop + crashed-vehicle imagery in-bounds for AZ (no SB-37); craft bar met (daytime photographic, no impact, no injured bodies)
- Funnel disclaimer satisfies TCPA 1-to-1 attribution
- Meta SAC: third-person throughout
