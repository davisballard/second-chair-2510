## ⚠ Production reality

This concept ships as a **standalone late-funnel hook** for the Sarah audience that has *already received a settlement offer* and suspects it's structurally low. The "$18,000 for $74,000 in bills" mismatch is the entire scroll-stop — math hits sound-off and the documentary props (X-ray + insurance letter + hospital bill + side-by-side comparison + empty trash can) build the case for permission-to-refuse.

This sits **alongside `S1_Sarah_Trucking_(Trucking).md`** as Sarah's second concept — same persona, different journey position. S1 reaches active-victim audiences (recent crash, looking for the right lawyer). S2 reaches buying / late-funnel audiences (already have an offer, second-guessing it).

---

## Why this concept, why now

The S1 trucking primary handles **early-funnel scroll-stop** — recent victims who don't know commercial cases need different legal handling. S2 handles **late-funnel scroll-stop** — Sarah-type viewers 6–10 weeks post-crash who have *already* fielded an adjuster's settlement offer and feel something's off, but don't realize they can refuse.

The numerical mismatch ($18K offer vs $74K hospital bill) does the heavy lifting in three seconds. The escalation through documentary props establishes the proof. The empty trash can is the metaphor pivot — visualizes the *throwing-away-the-offer* action without saying it. Then the quiz frame is the alternative.

**Strongest late-funnel hook in the Sarah set.**

### Script (24.4s · 6 visuals)

**Visual 1 (0–3s)** — Tablet propped on kitchen counter showing **Sarah Mitchell's L-forearm post-operative X-ray** (titanium plates + six screws clearly visible on both forearm bones). Hand holding a printed settlement offer letter dated May 7, 2026, "**SETTLEMENT OFFER: $18,000** / Final offer. Please respond by May 21, 2026" from Midland Casualty Insurance. Photos of rear-end crash damage (sedan back-ends crushed) on the counter beside the tablet.

**VO + caption:** *"They offered eighteen thousand."*

**Visual 2 (3–6s)** — Empty operating room, single overhead surgical light on, surgical equipment + anesthesia cart in background, blue surgical drape on the table. Cool blue-green clinical lighting.

**VO + caption:** *"After surgery."*

**Visual 3 (6–10s)** — Sarah at her mailbox outside her home (AZ desert single-family residential, daylight). Arm brace on L forearm. Holding a *Valley Regional Medical Center Patient Billing Statement* — line items visible (Emergency Dept Services, Operating Room, Anesthesia, Surgical Hardware, Inpatient Room, Surgeon Professional Fee, etc.). Bottom of statement reads **"PATIENT BALANCE DUE: $74,839.00"**.

**VO + caption:** *"The hospital bill was $74,000."*

**Visual 4 (10–14s)** — Two letters side-by-side on a wooden table. Left: Midland Casualty offer letter — *"SETTLEMENT OFFER: $18,000."* Right: Valley Regional bill — *"PATIENT BALANCE DUE: $74,839.00."* Sarah's hands visible with the arm brace, comparing the two documents.

**VO + caption:** *"The math doesn't add up."*

**Visual 5 (14–17s)** — Empty stainless-steel kitchen trash can against a brown wall, dim ambient light. The trash can is open, lined, ready.

**VO + caption:** *"You don't have to take it."*

**Visual 6 (17–24.4s)** — Over-shoulder: hand holding iPhone showing Fair Case intake quiz (FAIR CASE logo, "What type of motor vehicle accident were you in?" question, 6 options, Step 1 of 7 progress bar, WCTL sponsorship disclaimer at form bottom).

**VO + caption:** *"No fee unless we win."*

### Visual direction
**Cinematic Documentary** — every prop is real-document calligraphy (X-ray screen, settlement letter, hospital bill, side-by-side comparison, trash can). The escalation goes **proof of injury → proof of treatment → proof of cost → proof of mismatch → permission to refuse → action.** Empty trash can is the metaphor pivot — it visualizes the physical *throwing-away-the-offer* action without showing it. Then the quiz frame is the alternative.

### Rationale
**Strongest late-funnel hook in the Sarah set.** Numerical mismatch ($18K offer vs $74K bill) is the entire scroll-stop — the math hits even sound-off. Reaches Sarah-type viewers who *already have an offer* and suspect it's low but don't know they can walk away. The empty-trash-can metaphor + "You don't have to take it" is the psychological permission slip — telling the viewer they can refuse without saying it directly. Then the quiz delivers the alternative.

This is the **offer-validation concept**, complementary to the S1 trucking primary which reaches active-victim audiences. S2 is the brutal-specificity end of the Sarah audience funnel.

### Compliance
- $18,000 settlement offer + $74,839 hospital bill = framed as **Sarah's documented case example, not WCTL case results**
- "Midland Casualty Insurance" + "Valley Regional Medical Center" = generic fictitious carrier + hospital names; no real-party reference
- "Final offer. Please respond by May 21, 2026" prop language = standard adjuster-letter format, not a defamation claim
- AZ ER 7.1 clean — "No fee unless we win" is the AZ-permissible contingency-fee disclosure
- Meta SAC: third-person; "You don't have to take it" = generic-class statement about offer rejection, not personal-attribute assertion
- AI-generated medical X-ray + bill / letter props are illustrative; no real patient data or facility identification
