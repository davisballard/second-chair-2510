## Concept Hypothesis

Jason watches concrete-finishing tutorials on YouTube (per `Jason/01` §7 — "moderate; concrete-finishing tutorials, F-250 maintenance videos"). The trade-tutorial format — workbench POV, instructor's calloused hands handling tools, step-by-step Sharpie annotations on paper — is a register he *trusts the way he trusts a spec sheet*. Apply that exact register to *how a personal injury case actually works*, and the lawyer-relationship converts from "advocate" to "specialist" without any rhetorical lift. The form itself does the reframe.

The role-inversion lever (per `Jason/02` Loss 3 + Shotton's bias remediation in `Jason/05` §6.2): Jason has spent twenty-two years teaching Hector and Marco at this workbench. The format flips — for once, *he* is the apprentice in his own didactic register.

## Hook

> **"Here's how a personal injury case works — the way it gets explained on a jobsite."**

**Source:** `Video_Hook_Templates.md` How-To/Listicle category — *"I am going to teach you how to identify a good [thing] to a bad [thing]"* (line 168) compressed with `Hooks_And_Angles.md` "Insider Secret" angle. McCabe's tightening drops the first-person, drops "you," lands the jobsite-as-explanation register. Third-person; clears AZ Bar ER 7.3.

**Why it lands:** "How a [X] actually works" is a register Jason searches YouTube *for*. The hook arrives in the wrapper of his own learning behavior. The qualifier "the way it gets explained on a jobsite" is the register-bend — it's the *promise* of the tutorial format and Jason recognizes the promise on contact.

## Register-Bend

YouTube trade-tutorial channel format. Specifically the Mike Day / Concrete Pump School / Concrete Network grammar:

- Static workbench POV camera (overhead or 3/4 to the bench surface)
- Calloused hands enter frame, handle paper + tools didactically
- Sharpie annotations on the paper, made on-camera
- Per-step chapter cards in monospace ("CHAPTER 1 // ...") — the YouTube-tutorial title-card register
- Plain VO — instructor cadence, no advocacy lift, no music
- Slow zooms that move at *instructional* pace, not commercial pace

## Why-This-For-Jason

The tutorial format is a register Jason actively chooses to spend time in. Per `Jason/01` §7: he watches concrete tutorials AND F-250 maintenance videos. The format announces itself as instructional — which means the ad gets received as *information* rather than *pitch*. The role-inversion (Jason teaches Hector → someone teaches Jason) lands sideways and quiet. He sees his own workbench, his own Sharpie discipline (`Jason/01` §3 — "everything labeled with painter's tape"), and his own format applied to material he didn't realize he could learn. The recognition is multi-layered.

## Why-Not-Corny

The corny version is a stagey actor pretending to be a tradesman explaining law. **Avoided by:**

- Actual production rigor: real workbench (matches `Jason/01` §9 hero set), real concrete-trade ephemera in frame (a folded structural drawing, a Sharpie that's been used before, a pencil with bite marks), real plumb-bob-precision in the camera grammar.
- Overhead static shots of paper laid out like a spec sheet — not a presenter "speaking to camera."
- Instructional zoom cadence — slow, didactic, NOT commercial.
- Title-card chapter markers in Söhne Mono monospace, not advertising fonts.
- VO is plain. No music. No theatrical pause.
- Jason character does not appear above the wrist (per SC no-on-camera-dialogue rule). Only his calloused right hand.

The form doing the work is the principle — same as Concept 01, different register.

## Visual Treatment Direction (5 frames, 15s)

**Frame 1 — Title card (0–3s).** Workbench POV, slightly overhead, looking down at the bench surface. On the bench: a folded structural drawing partially open, a pencil, a Sharpie cap-on, three envelopes neatly stacked. **No hands yet.** Title-card chapter overlay top-third in Söhne Mono 13pt monospace small-caps:

> *CHAPTER 1*
> *HOW A PI CASE ACTUALLY WORKS*
> *// JOBSITE EDITION*

VO begins: *"Here's how a personal injury case works — the way it gets explained on a jobsite."*

**Frame 2 — Step 1, the hospital bill (3–7s).** Calloused right hand (Jason character-lock — wedding band, no long sleeve covering the watch) enters from frame-right, picks up envelope #1 (Banner Gateway letterhead), sets it back down on the bench. Hand picks up the Sharpie, removes the cap with a thumb-flick (didactic), writes diagonally across the envelope: **"RECOVERABLE — they don't pay this."** Sharpie cap goes back on. Hand exits.

VO over: *"Step one. The hospital bill is not what you owe."*

Per-step chapter card top-third: *"STEP 1 // THE BILL ≠ THE DEBT."*

**Frame 3 — Step 2, the lowball offer (7–10s).** Same workbench. Hand re-enters, picks up envelope #2 (the at-fault-driver's insurance offer letter), sets it next to envelope #1. Sharpie writes diagonally: **"FIRST OFFER — not the offer."** Cap on. Exit.

VO: *"Step two. The first offer is the cheapest the math gets — not the math."*

Per-step chapter card: *"STEP 2 // THE FIRST OFFER IS A QUESTION."*

**Frame 4 — Step 3, workers' comp parallel (10–13s).** Hand re-enters, picks up envelope #3 (workers'-comp dispute letter), sets it next to envelopes #1 + #2 — but offset slightly, parallel not in sequence. Sharpie: **"PARALLEL — runs alongside, not against."**

VO: *"Step three. Workers' comp dragging? It runs alongside a PI case, not against it."*

Per-step chapter card: *"STEP 3 // WORKERS' COMP // PARALLEL LINE."*

**Frame 5 — Resolution (13–15s).** Wide pull-back to the full workbench: three envelopes lined up with their Sharpie annotations visible, the Sharpie cap back on, the structural drawing folded shut. The composition reads as a finished spec sheet. Kinetic text resolves bottom-third in Tiempos Headline 64pt Cream over Warm Charcoal scrim:

> *Find out what the math actually says.*

End-card (final 1.5s): WCTL + Fair Case torch + Torch Amber CTA chip + AZ Bar disclaimer.

## Production Spec

- **Duration:** 15s
- **Aspect:** 9:16 only (the workbench-POV register is portrait-native; 1:1 crops poorly).
- **Stills:** 5 Higgsfield reference images. Frames 2–4 are continuity-locked — same workbench, same lighting, same Sharpie. Iterative flow: lock frame 1 first as the bench-state ref, forward through 2/3/4, frame 5 is the resolution wide.
- **Motion:** Kling 2.6 — the on-camera Sharpie-writing is the load-bearing motion. Each step is one continuous gesture (cap off → annotation → cap on). Prompt budget under 2500 chars per `feedback_kling_26_prompt_char_limit.md`. The handwriting on the envelope is the most failure-prone shot in the concept; budget two re-rolls per step in the Kling pass.
- **VO:** ElevenLabs TTS, Bill voice primary. Instructor cadence — plain, didactic, slightly slower than typical Bill VO so the per-step chapter cards land in sync.
- **Music:** None. Ambient shop tone only — distant compressor, very faint.
- **Foley:** Sharpie cap thumb-flick (frames 2/3/4 — distinct, present), envelope-on-bench (each pickup), Sharpie writing (the audible draw of pen on paper), cap-on click. Silence between Foley moments.
- **Estimated batch cost:** ~$18 (more Higgsfield iterations needed for the workbench continuity lock; Kling has the most failure-prone shots).
