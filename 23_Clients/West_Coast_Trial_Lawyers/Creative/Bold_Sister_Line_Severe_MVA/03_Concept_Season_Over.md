## Concept Hypothesis

Jason was 20–25 years old during the PS2 sports-game era (2001–2005). He played Madden with his crew on Sunday nights. He watched NFL on FOX every Sunday in his early twenties. The cel-shaded polygonal cutscene + broadcast-chyron grammar of that era is HIS adolescent/early-adult cultural register — *the* register, not *a* register. The unexpected pairing of "PS2-era sports-game cinematic" with "personal injury settlement math" is the scroll-stop mechanism — a trades-male sees this in his feed and his thumb stops because pattern-recognition runs through six layers of *wait, what is this* before he disengages.

The hook is the *visual register itself*. Overlay copy IS the hook — broadcast-chyron content lands the SMP without a spoken line.

The pivot — the GAME OVER → live-action cut at frame 4 — is the load-bearing structural moment. Without it the spot reads as flippant. *With* it the playful register pivots into substantive content and Jason reads the whole piece as deliberate.

## Register-Bend

PS2-era (2001–2005) cel-shaded 3D sports-game cinematic cutscene + broadcast-chyron grammar.

**Visual specifics:**
- Low-poly polygonal 3D rendering (period-accurate polygon counts — visibly faceted geometry, not modern smooth shading)
- Period-accurate texture compression (low-res baked textures, simplified shadow maps)
- Hard-edged cel-shading (period-accurate shader, not modern Borderlands-style)
- Camera grammar from the PS2-era cutscene playbook — slightly stiff, slow pans, fixed-ratio zooms, no modern motion-blur
- Broadcast-chyron typography — period-accurate compressed Helvetica Bold or DIN, hard-edged not anti-aliased, white-on-translucent-blue
- "GAME OVER" frame in period-accurate pixelated sans-serif

**The register is generic to the era.** Madden 02, NCAA Football 2003, NFL 2K3, ESPN NFL 2K5 all share these conventions. McCabe explicitly does not invoke any specific game/franchise.

- Actual rigor in the polygonal rendering. Period-accurate polygon counts, period-accurate texture compression, period-accurate shader. NOT a "stylized" or "vibey" PS2-look — the *actual* PS2 look.
- The chyron content is *unflinchingly real*. - No music, no swelling soundtrack, no "epic" cue. Period-accurate ambient cutscene audio only (faint synth pad, room hum).
