## Concept Hypothesis

Per `Jason/01` §7: "Instagram (rarely posts, follows: tools, classic trucks, two AZ surf-fishing accounts, Tyler's football team's account, his cousin Pete)." Classic-truck content is in his daily feed. The Bring-A-Trailer / classic-truck-IG auction-listing aesthetic — utilitarian listing typography, photo-grid layout, lot-detail captions written in a specific terse register — is what his algorithm already serves him.

Bend the format: instead of a 1995 Square Body Chevy auction listing, write the listing for *the year a foreman lost*. The wrapper is what he scrolls past every morning on the recliner. The content cuts.

## Hook

The hook is the LOT TITLE, not a spoken line:

> **"LOT 2026-04: 2019 F-250 SUPER DUTY — SITTING IN THE DRIVEWAY SINCE APRIL."**

**Source:** `Hooks_And_Angles.md` Pattern Interrupt category (lines 28–39) + `Video_Hook_Templates.md` Audience Engagement structure. The format-bend IS the hook — the listing-title register applied to a personal injury narrative. The hook lives in the visual register, not in copy a presenter speaks.

**Why it lands:** Auction-listing titles compress story into a single line in a way Jason's eye is trained to read fast. He reads the title and his algorithm-trained pattern-recognition cycles through "is this a truck I want?" before pivoting to "wait — what kind of listing is this?" That cycle is the scroll-stop.

## Register-Bend

Bring-A-Trailer / classic-truck Instagram auction-listing aesthetic, rendered as a **single composite IG graphic-design listing post** — the way real classic-truck IG accounts post when they want all listing info in the image (so caption can be different). Specifically the BAT/Hemmings/Bring-A-Trailer-IG graphic-design common register:

- 4:5 portrait composite — BAT 3/4 hero photograph in the upper half, solid Warm Charcoal panel with full listing copy in the lower half, clean horizontal seam at the 50% line
- Lot-detail typography in utilitarian listing format — Inter Tight Bold for the title-block headline + Inter Tight Medium small-caps tracked for key-value rows + Tiempos italic for the closing action line
- All listing data baked in: lot #, model name, owner location, hours, last use, condition, owner profile, reason for listing, out of work, asking price (with the substantiated parenthetical), fair market value
- The format-bend completes inside the single image — Jason's eye lands on the truck photo, drops to the listing block, reads "ASKING: $38,000," processes "REASON / OWNER / OUT OF WORK 5+ MOS," realizes the lot is the year. ~2–4 seconds of feed-scroll comprehension.
- Massimo gates final typography selection — auction-listing is utilitarian not advertorial; Inter Tight Bold + Inter Tight Medium small-caps + Tiempos italic for the close.

## Why-This-For-Jason

This is the format Jason actively follows on his phone. Per `Jason/01` §7: classic trucks is in his Instagram diet. The format-bend lives in his actual feed environment. The bait of recognition is platform-native — Jason's thumb stops because the algorithm has trained him to stop on listings.

The role-inversion lever is sideways: the "lot" is not the truck, it's the year. The truck is the *photographic subject* but the LISTING is for the foreman's twelve months. Per `Jason/02` Loss 6 (the $38K verdict) — "ASKING: $38,000 (PER INSURANCE COMPANY ASSESSMENT)" turns the lowball into the literal asking price the system put on the year. Then "FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD" inverts it.

## Why-Not-Corny

The corny version is a winking parody — Forced Wry Auction Listing with a heart-string twist. **Avoided by:**

- ACTUAL rigor in the listing format. Specs, miles, owner profile, asking price, condition notes, all written like a real BAT listing — utilitarian register, no editorial voice.
- Photos shot in the auction-listing photographic register — clean, well-lit, descriptive, NOT cinematic.
- Listing typography is compressed sans-serif, NOT advertising fonts.
- The role-inversion hook (the year-as-lot) lands sideways, not stated. McCabe's listing copy never says "this listing is actually about a person." The format does the conversion.
- No music, no swell. Soft slide-advance audio only.

## Hook Overlay Copy + CTA

All copy is baked INTO the single composite image. There is no kinetic text, no CTA chip graphic, no end-card frame. The image IS the ad.

- **Title block + key-value rows:** Cream `#FFF7F0` Inter Tight Bold (headline) + Inter Tight Medium small-caps (labels) on Warm Charcoal `#1C1917` panel. The "$38,000" value and the "FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD" closing row are the load-bearing legibility locks.
- **Closing action line:** "*Fair market value? Find out. →*" in Tiempos italic Torch Amber `#C8821A` at the bottom of the panel. Per `feedback_static_ad_cta_discipline.md`: type only, NO in-image button graphic.
- **Meta's Learn More button:** rendered by Meta UI below the image in feed; carries the click. WCTL form lives downstream on the landing page.
- **Caption copy (Meta caption field, runs below the image in feed UI):** terse BAT-listing register, supplements but does not duplicate the in-image listing block. Sample: *"22-year AZ concrete foreman. T-boned by an at-fault driver on US-60. Insurance offered $38,000 for the year he can't work. Fair market value: TBD."*

## Production Spec

- **Duration:** static (no duration — single image)
- **Aspect:** 4:5 portrait (1080×1350) — IG feed-native, NOT 9:16 (Reels) and NOT 1:1
- **Surface:** Meta feed (IG + Facebook) static image ad placement. NOT Reels.
- **Stills:** ONE Higgsfield composite image — photo + listing-block panel + closing action line, all baked in one render.
- **Fallback path:** if ChatGPT Image 2 cannot render the listing-block typography legibly in one pass, generate the photo only and composite the listing block in CapCut / Figma using locked Inter Tight Bold + Inter Tight Medium + Tiempos italic system fonts. Per `feedback_branding_last_pass.md`, graphic-design typography lives in CapCut anyway — bake-in is preferred only if it lands cleanly.
- **Motion:** none.
- **VO:** none.
- **Music:** none.
- **Foley:** none.
- **Iterative flow per `feedback_higgsfield_workflow.md`:** 6 candidates per first batch; lock the winner; capture seed; reject with rejection notes. If first batch fails legibility, second batch with reinforced typography prompt; if still failing, switch to fallback path.
- **Estimated batch cost:** ~$3–6 across iterations to lock (cheapest concept in the set by a margin — one render vs the prior six-still spec).

## Voice Test (Ferrier audit)

1. **Recognized, not pitied?** ✅ The listing format is utilitarian — no advocacy lift, no rescue language. The format treats the "lot" with auction-style respect; the dignity is in the precision of the specs.
2. **Send to Pete with no caption?** ✅ Pete watches classic-truck IG too. He'd send it to Mike (Jason's brother) with no caption. The format reads as content; the message lands sideways.
3. **Role inversion without naming it?** ✅ The reader's expectation as the carousel advances is "this is a truck listing." That expectation gets quietly inverted across slides — the lot is the year, not the truck. The inversion is structural; never spoken; lands on slide 5 ("ASKING $38,000 PER INSURANCE COMPANY ASSESSMENT") and resolves on slide 6 ("FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD"). Format does the work.

**Score: 3/3. Ships.**
