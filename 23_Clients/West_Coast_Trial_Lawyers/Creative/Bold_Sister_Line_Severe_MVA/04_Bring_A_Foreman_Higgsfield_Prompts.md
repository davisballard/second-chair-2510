## Why This Is One Image, Not a Reel

The Reels-as-carousel framing was killed in production (Davis 2026-04-29). The format-bend mechanism — auction-listing register applied to a foreman's stolen year — REQUIRES the wrapper and the click target to land in the same feed-scroll moment. A linear video buries the lead four slides deep and forces forced-linear pacing on a register that's natively user-controlled.

The fix: deliver the entire format-bend in **one feed-native image**, the way real classic-truck IG listing accounts actually post. Jason's eye lands on the truck → drops to the title block ("ONE YEAR OF A 45-YEAR-OLD AZ FOREMAN") and processes the metaphor immediately — *the lot is the year, the photo is the trope* — then scans the listing rows that confirm it (trade tenure, role, crash, current status, at-fault insurance offer, fair market value: TBD), then hits the Torch Amber closing call telling him what to do about it. ~2–4 seconds of feed-scroll comprehension.

The auction-listing FORMAT does the creative work. The CONTENT is explicit: this is a listing for a year, not for a truck. The format-bend is the wrapper, not the punchline.

## Aesthetic Discipline

Same auction-listing photographic grammar as the original spec — the photographic register is load-bearing. Get the photo wrong and the whole image reads as a cheesy real estate flyer.

| Dimension | Auction-Listing Register (this concept) | NOT this (Elevated Documentary) |
|---|---|---|
| Light | Even descriptive natural daylight, slightly warm | Single motivated source, deep falloff |
| DOF | Deep at f/8, sharp foreground-to-background | Shallow at f/2.8, eyes sharp / ears soft |
| Lens | 35mm, descriptive | 50mm, intimate |
| Color | Honest neutral, slightly warm, no grading | Restrained Vision3, slightly desat |
| Composition | Conventional 3/4 BAT angle, truck dominant | Rule-of-thirds, asymmetric, off-camera eye |
| Subject treatment | The OBJECT is the subject; description is the payload | The PERSON is the subject; emotion is the payload |
| Photographer reference | Bring-A-Trailer, Hemmings, classic-truck IG | Soth, Kander, PTA *Phantom Thread* |

**The image must read as: "A real classic-truck listing post that someone made into an Instagram graphic with the listing details overlaid on the photo."** That's the whole register. Get this right and the format-bend works in feed without a single word of advocacy.

## Composition Architecture

A single composite image. One Higgsfield generation produces the entire ad.

**Aspect ratio:** **4:5 portrait** (1080×1350, IG feed-native). NOT 9:16 (that's Reels — wrong surface). NOT 1:1 (less feed real estate).

**Vertical real-estate budget:**

- **Top ~50%:** F-250 hero photograph — the truck dominant in the frame, BAT 3/4 angle, sky and AZ context above.
- **Bottom ~50%:** solid Warm Charcoal `#1C1917` listing-block panel, full width edge-to-edge, with the entire listing in baked compressed-sans typography in Cream `#FFF7F0`.

The two regions meet at a clean horizontal seam at the 50% line — the Warm Charcoal panel sits on top of the photo as a graphic-design overlay, the way IG listing-graphic posts work. The truck above; the listing below. Like one continuous BAT post.

## The Listing Block (the entire format-bend lives here)

Three typographic tiers on the Warm Charcoal panel. All Cream `#FFF7F0` EXCEPT the closing Tiempos italic line (Torch Amber `#C8821A`). Inter Tight Bold (or similar tight bold compressed sans-serif) for headline-tier; Inter Tight Medium small-caps tracked for label/value rows; Tiempos italic for the closing Torch Amber resolve.

**Tier 1 — TITLE BLOCK** *(largest type — the lot title is the listing's commitment: this lot is a year, not a truck)*:
```
LOT 2026-04
ONE YEAR OF A 45-YEAR-OLD AZ FOREMAN
OWNER: HARDIN CONCRETE, APACHE JUNCTION
```
The lot title "ONE YEAR OF A 45-YEAR-OLD AZ FOREMAN" is the largest type on the entire image (~46–52pt-equivalent at 1080×1350). The lot # above it is small-caps tracked Cream (~14pt). The owner line below is small-caps tracked Cream (~14pt) — naming Hardin Concrete ties the title to the truck-door decal in the photograph above. The "45-YEAR-OLD" disambiguates Jason's age from his trade tenure (no "22-YR" abbreviation collisions).

**Tier 2 — KEY-VALUE LISTING DETAILS** *(medium type, key-value rows, monospace-aligned columns — facts about the lot, every one of them about the man not the truck)*:
```
TRADE TENURE:                   22 YEARS
ROLE:                           CONCRETE FOREMAN
CRASH:                          T-BONE COLLISION, US-60
CURRENT STATUS:                 OUT OF WORK 5+ MONTHS

AT-FAULT INSURANCE OFFER:       $38,000
WORKERS' COMP:                  DISPUTED

FAIR MARKET VALUE:              TBD
```
Rows are key-value pairs: label small-caps tracked Cream Inter Tight Medium left-aligned + value bold Cream Inter Tight Bold right-aligned (or tab-aligned in a clean column). Approximately ~22pt-equivalent. The "AT-FAULT INSURANCE OFFER: $38,000" row is set off with a hairline Cream rule above and below it (1px, 60–70% opacity) — the way a real BAT listing emphasizes the asking price; here, that BAT convention is repurposed to spotlight the adjuster's number, not a truck price. The "FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD" row gets a hairline rule above it as the closing line of the listing — this is the format-bend's question to the reader.

**Critical:** "AT-FAULT INSURANCE OFFER" attaches the $38,000 explicitly to the at-fault driver's insurance adjuster — NOT to the truck. A 2019 F-250 Super Duty in clean condition would actually sell at $42–55K used, so any framing that reads "$38K asking price for the truck" collapses the format-bend (Jason reads it as a deal, not a lowball). The "AT-FAULT INSURANCE OFFER" label is load-bearing for the concept's logic AND for ER 7.1 substantiation.

**Tier 3 — CLOSING ACTION LINES** *(panel bottom — two lines, format-bend resolve + click clarifier)*:
```
Hurt in a crash on the job? Find out what your year is worth. →
Free case review for AZ trade workers.
```
- **Line 1:** Tiempos italic Torch Amber `#C8821A` (~18pt-equivalent). The format-bend resolve — "your year" picks up "FAIR MARKET VALUE: TBD" from the listing block above, addresses the reader directly, and makes the click destination unambiguous.
- **Line 2:** Cream `#FFF7F0` Inter Tight Medium (~14pt-equivalent), smaller than line 1, sits directly below it. The click clarifier — explicitly tells the reader the click goes to a free case review.

Per `feedback_static_ad_cta_discipline.md`: NO in-image CTA button graphic — Meta's system Learn More button below the image carries the click. The Torch Amber line is the ONLY non-Cream-on-Charcoal type in the listing block.

## The Photo (top 50% of frame)

A 2019 Ford F-250 Super Duty, plain white, parked in the gravel driveway of an Apache Junction stucco-and-terracotta ranch home. Hardin Concrete decal on the driver-side door (plain sans-serif, generic phone number under the company name). Washed and clean — sun catching the chrome grille, white paint freshly cleaned. Open garage door behind, keys on a hook just inside. Two saguaros, a small mesquite, Superstition Mountains faintly in the deep background, clear AZ blue sky.

BAT/Hemmings 3/4-front photographic grammar — slight elevation above the front bumper line, looking down the passenger side, three-quarter angle showing both the front grille and the full passenger-side profile. Truck centered in frame. f/8 deep focus throughout — every detail of the truck sharp from grille to rear bumper, the house behind in legible focus, the saguaros sharp. Honest color, slightly warm, NO grading. Even descriptive late-morning AZ daylight.

The truck DOMINATES the upper 50% of the 4:5 frame. The cab top at approximately 5–10% from the top edge of the image; the lower edge of the wheels at approximately 47–49% (just above the seam where the listing-block panel begins). The truck pushes DOWN into the seam — there is NO empty foreground gap between the wheels and the panel.

## Run Protocol

**Aspect ratio:** confirm 4:5 portrait in Higgsfield UI (1080×1350 or equivalent). If 4:5 unavailable, generate at 9:16 and crop in CapCut to 4:5 — but 4:5 native is preferred.

**Candidates per batch:** 6 in the first batch. Generation will produce variation in:
- Truck composition / framing tightness
- Listing block typography legibility (the load-bearing variable)
- Seam cleanness between photo and panel
- AZ context details (saguaros, mountains, garage door)

**Seed:** do not fix on first batch. Lock the winner's seed for any follow-up regen.

**Lock criteria (in order of importance):**
1. Listing block typography is fully legible — at minimum the lot title "ONE YEAR OF A 45-YEAR-OLD AZ FOREMAN" reads cleanly, the "AT-FAULT INSURANCE OFFER: $38,000" row reads cleanly, key-value labels and values readable, both closing action lines readable.
2. The label "AT-FAULT INSURANCE OFFER" specifically renders cleanly — the format-bend logic depends on the dollar figure being attributed to the adjuster, not to the truck. If the label is illegible or wrong, regenerate.
3. Auction-listing photographic register holds — deep focus, even descriptive light, conventional 3/4 angle, NOT cinematic.
4. Truck dominates the upper half — no large foreground gap between wheels and the panel seam.
5. Seam between photo and panel is clean — no bleed, no awkward gradient.
6. Closing Torch Amber Tiempos italic line is the ONLY non-Cream type and reads cleanly; closing line 2 (Cream Inter Tight Medium) sits directly below it and reads cleanly.
7. No brand infringement on the listing or the truck.

**If the listing copy renders garbled or partially:** ChatGPT Image 2 may struggle with this density of type in one pass. Fallback path:
- Generate the photo only (no listing block). Lock photo. Then composite the listing-block panel as a graphic-design layer in CapCut / Figma / Photoshop using locked Inter Tight Bold + Inter Tight Medium + Tiempos italic system fonts.
- This fallback is acceptable per `feedback_branding_last_pass.md` — graphic-design-system typography lives in CapCut anyway. The bake-in is preferred ONLY if it lands cleanly in the model.

**Lock procedure:**
1. Generate 6 candidates.
2. Apply lock criteria in order. Reject any that fail criterion 1 (legibility) or 2 (photographic register).
3. Pick winner. Save to `Creative/Bold_Sister_Line_Severe_MVA/Renders/04_Bring_A_Foreman/static_ad_locked_v1.png`.
4. Capture seed for the lock record.
5. If no candidate clears: switch to fallback (photo-only generation + CapCut typography composite).

**After lock:** write lock record at `Creative/Bold_Sister_Line_Severe_MVA/Renders/04_Bring_A_Foreman/04_Bring_A_Foreman_lock_record.md`. Hand off to CapCut only for any final color-grading or for the typography-composite fallback path.
