# Concept 01 — Job Site Documentary
## Mike Rowe / *Dirty Jobs* register applied to a man who can't pour anymore

**Prepared:** 2026-04-29
**Owner:** Hegarty (concept) · McCabe (copy) · Giuseppe (downstream production)
**Persona:** Jason Reardon (`Audience/Deep_Personas/Jason/`)
**Format:** 15s short-form video, 9:16 + 1:1
**Pairs with:** existing Cinematic Still (warm retargeting cohort)

---

## Concept Hypothesis

Jason listens to Mike Rowe's podcasts. *Dirty Jobs*' grammar — handheld doc-camera, plain-language voiceover that respects the work, the camera as a witness rather than a sales pitch — is a register Jason *willingly consumes*. Land the SMP inside that register and the ad arrives in a familiar wrapper. The reframe of "lawyer = specialist" (per Shotton's bias remediation in `Jason/02` §6) lands without any rhetorical lifting because the form *itself* is competence-respecting.

## Hook

> **"Twenty years on construction sites taught me one thing about how insurance companies handle a pelvic fracture."**

**Source:** `Video_Hook_Templates.md` Insider Secret/Authority — *"What years working with [target audience] taught me about [pain point]"* (line 81), tightened to construction-trade specificity. McCabe's compression — drops the pronoun ambiguity, fronts the timeline, lands the medical fact.

**Why it lands:** Authority-from-tenure is the trades male's native trust signal. Twenty years is a number Jason runs his own life by (he's been doing this for twenty-two). "Pelvic fracture" is medically specific — clears the soft-tissue self-identifier filter cleanly. Third-person throughout — clears AZ Bar ER 7.3.

## Register-Bend

Mike Rowe / *Dirty Jobs* documentary segment. Specifically the *Dirty Jobs* episode-opening grammar: handheld doc-cam establishing on a real working environment, VO over real ambient sound, the camera as a third party observing competence. Then the *but-here's-what-happens-after* cut — the same grammar applied to absence.

**Cultural anchor in `Jason/01` §7:** "Watches Mike Rowe podcasts (Tyler sends him clips)." This is a register Jason's algorithm already serves him. The bait of recognition is in the *form* before the content lands.

## Why-This-For-Jason

Mike Rowe respects the work. The Job Site Documentary register respects the worker. Jason recognizes the respect and the recognition is the role-inversion lever — for a man whose entire competence has been temporarily relocated to a recliner, being seen as a *worker* rather than an *injured person* is the SMP delivered visually. Per `Jason/02` Loss 3 (Crew Authority) — the documentary form treats him as still on the job even though the cut tells you he isn't.

## Why-Not-Corny

The corny version is a faux-documentary about "Real Arizona Heroes" with swelling music and a hero shot at sunset. **Avoided by:**

- Actual *Dirty Jobs* production rigor (handheld GoPro doc-cam, Vision3 grain, no music, no swell).
- VO that's content-fact, not emotional appeal — Mike Rowe-style plain-language explanation.
- No "you've been through enough"-class register failures (per `Jason/04` §5).
- No swelling score; ambient room tone only until the kinetic-text CTA.
- Hook is informational ("years on construction sites taught me X") not appeal ("you deserve...").

## Visual Treatment Direction (5 frames, 15s)

**Frame 1 — Establishing (0–3s).** Handheld doc-cam at first light on an active concrete pour. Crew on the slab, a foreman (NOT Jason — generic AZ-trades crew, character-locked separately) walking the perimeter with a steel trowel. Apache Junction / East Valley exterior — Superstition Mountains in the background, golden-hour sun low to camera-right. Vision3 grain visible. 35mm prime equivalent. **No music.** Ambient — slab-grinder distant, a guy calling out to another guy, gravel underfoot.

VO begins over frame 1: *"Twenty years on construction sites taught me one thing about how insurance companies handle a pelvic fracture..."*

**Frame 2 — The empty version (3–7s).** Cut to an opposite jobsite — empty. The framework half-set, the trailer parked, no crew. The truck (a 2019 F-250, character-locked Hardin Concrete decals) sits in a different driveway, motionless. Same handheld doc-cam grammar, same Vision3 grade, same time of day — but the absence is the subject.

VO continues: *"They handle it the way you'd expect a stranger to. Fast, low, and final."*

**Frame 3 — The bills (7–11s).** Tight overhead on a kitchen counter. The stack of envelopes — Banner Gateway letterhead clearly visible on top, then a credit-card statement, then an insurance-company response envelope. A calloused right hand (Jason character-lock — wedding band, no long sleeve covering the watch) reaches into frame and rests on the counter edge, motionless. **He's not reaching for them.**

VO continues: *"Foreman's been out four months. The math the adjuster ran isn't the math his family runs."*

**Frame 4 — The shop calendar (11–13s).** Tight on the paper desk-pad calendar pinned to the workbench (per `Jason/01` §9). Frozen on the first week of the month. A pencil rests on the calendar where Jason left it. Dust on the pencil. Static shot, no motion.

**Frame 5 — Resolution (13–15s).** Wide of the F-250 in the driveway, washed, motionless. Keys visible on the hook just inside the open garage door. Kinetic text resolves over the lower third in Tiempos Headline 64pt Cream over a 60% Warm Charcoal scrim:

> *Find out what the law actually says.*

End-card (final 1.5s): WCTL logo, Fair Case torch + Torch Amber CTA chip, AZ Bar disclaimer in 9pt.

- **AZ Bar ER 7.5 (firm naming):** WCTL logo + AZ Bar # in end-card disclosures. Standard.
- **Copyright fence:** Mike Rowe register is *aesthetic*. McCabe's VO does not invoke "Mike Rowe," "*Dirty Jobs*," "Discovery Channel," or any branded reference. The Vision3-grain handheld doc-cam grammar is a generic documentary register pre-dating *Dirty Jobs*. **Clears.**
- **No on-camera dialogue:** VO only. Subject (foreman extra in frame 1, Jason's hand in frame 3) does not speak. **Clears `Style_Sheets/Elevated_Documentary.md` §0.2.**
- **No crash footage / no hospital scenes / no surgery aestheticization.** None present. **Clears.**
