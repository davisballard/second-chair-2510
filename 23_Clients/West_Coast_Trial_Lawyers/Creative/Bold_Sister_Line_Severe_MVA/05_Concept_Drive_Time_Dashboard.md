## Concept Hypothesis

Per `Jason/01` §7: *"KMLE 107.9 (country) in the truck most mornings."* This was the radio station Jason listened to every working morning of his life from 5:30 AM to 6:45 AM in his F-250 for twenty-two years. He knows the morning-DJ patter cadence at the level of muscle memory.

Bend the format: hold a static dashboard shot of the parked truck and let the morning patter narrate his case. The truck doesn't move. The radio still works. The world still narrates. He just isn't in motion anymore. The role-inversion lands sideways — he used to be the listener; now he's the subject the show is talking about. Without naming it.

This is the most format-experimental of the five concepts: audio-led short-form on a sound-OFF-default platform. Indigo benchmarks the sound-on rate; if it's <40% over 14 days, the unit retires. The captioned DJ patter does load-bearing work even at sound-off.

**Source:** `Video_Hook_Templates.md` Audience Engagement (lines 232–243) — the *"This is your friendly reminder that..."* register adapted into authentic morning-DJ patter cadence. Plain-language, slight overlap between DJs, content-fact register. No advocacy lift. No "you" in the patter.

**Why it lands:** Cadence is muscle memory for Jason. The two-DJ format with "yeah-yeah" interjections is how country morning shows have sounded for the entirety of his career. Hearing his own life narrated in that exact cadence is the deepest scroll-stop possible — the audio bypasses the ad-skepticism filter because it arrives in a non-ad wrapper.

## Register-Bend

KMLE 107.9 morning-DJ patter format. Specifically the country-radio morning-show convention:

- Two-DJ format (one male lead, one slightly younger male sidekick) — the Phoenix country-radio standard
- Slight conversational overlap, "yeah-yeah" interjections, the ad-bumper inflection
- AZ Spanish-speaking influence in Mesa register where appropriate (the Mesa morning-radio sound)
- Plain-language content delivery — country-radio DJs talk *to* the audience like they're in the truck
- No fake folksiness; the cadence is conversational, slightly tired, present-tense

**Visual register: parked-truck dashboard.** Static interior shot of the F-250 — keys in the ignition, radio dial lit, dashboard reading 5:50 AM (the time Jason used to leave for work, per `Jason/01` §6). Light streams in from outside (golden-hour AZ pre-dawn, matching his commute window). The shot HOLDS for ~14 seconds with two micro-motion edits only. The truck does not move.

## Why-This-For-Jason

The audio register is in Jason's auditory muscle memory. He has heard country-radio morning patter every working morning of his life from 5:30 AM to 6:45 AM in his F-250 for twenty-two years. The cadence is internal architecture — when he hears it on his phone in the recliner, his body remembers.

Per `Jason/02` Loss 2 (the body that worked) — the truck not moving is the visualization of his own paused life. The radio still works. The world still narrates. He doesn't.

Per `Jason/02` Loss 3 (Crew Authority) — the DJ talking *about* him in third-person is the inversion of who he used to be. He was the man other people called. Now he's the subject the morning show is reporting on.

The role inversion lands sideways. He used to be the listener; now he's the subject. Never spoken in the patter; always present in the format.

## Why-Not-Corny

The corny version is two stagey DJ characters with fake country accents over a generic American-flag-truck-at-sunset visual. **Avoided by:**

- Casting/voice-direction discipline in ElevenLabs TTS — actual morning-DJ patter cadence (slight overlap, "yeah-yeah" interjections, ad-bumper inflection). Bill voice (per `project_sc_vo_is_elevenlabs.md`) is DJ A; second TTS voice cast freshly for DJ B. McCabe directs the read with reference clips from real Phoenix country morning shows (NOT to copy them; to calibrate the cadence).
- AZ Spanish-speaking influence subtle, not theatrical (Mesa morning-radio sound, not "country" parody).
- Visual is utilitarian — parked dashboard, no flag, no sunset glow, no slow-motion cowboy hand. Period-accurate truck-cab interior in honest pre-dawn light per `Style_Sheets/Elevated_Documentary.md` §5 motivated single-source.
- DJ patter is content-fact, not emotional appeal. "Day a hundred and forty out of work" is a number; "thirty-eight grand to make it go away" is a fact; "the law doesn't think thirty-eight grand is the number" is a soft procedural claim, not a promise.
- No music. Period-accurate KMLE-style station bumper synthesized as generic country-radio jingle (NOT KMLE asset) at start; otherwise truck-cab ambient + DJ patter only.

## Sound-Off Risk Mitigation (Indigo's gate)

Reels are sound-OFF by default. Audio-led short-form is structurally fragile in this format. Three mitigations baked into the concept:

1. **Captions are exhaustive.** CapCut auto-captions render the full DJ patter; sound-off readers get every line.
2. **The visual stillness alone is intriguing.** A parked truck dashboard at 5:50 AM with light streaming through is a documentary-style composition that earns sound-on curiosity from the trades-male audience — the visual code reads as "something specific to a guy who drives a work truck" before any audio plays.
3. **Kinetic CTA is sound-off-readable.** The final 1.5s resolves into Tiempos Headline kinetic text that completes the message even if no audio played.

**Indigo benchmark:** Track sound-on rate over 14 days. If <40%, the unit retires. If ≥40%, the audio-led format extends into Phase 2 with additional concepts.
