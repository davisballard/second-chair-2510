## §1 — THE FRAMEWORK BEING APPLIED

The review measures every unit against four lenses, in order:

### Lens 1 — The two principles from the SC Designed Overlay System

1. **Overlays earn their place.** Default state is zero overlays. An overlay ships only when it serves comprehension, credibility, or conversion for *this specific spot*.
2. **Design-first, motion-when-it-serves.** Most overlays should be static. Motion is one tool — used only when it adds narrative meaning, never as decoration.

### Lens 2 — The 4-job time budget

| Time | Job | What it looks like in WCTL Elevated Documentary |
|------|-----|--------------------------------------------------|
| 0.0–0.5s | Comprehension lock | Hook text overlay (3-7 words) + scene framing that filters in the qualified audience |
| 0.5–1.5s | Credibility signal | Elevated Documentary register itself (Mindhunter / Villeneuve / Coens light + composition) carries the credibility |
| 1.5–8s | The case | The narrative arc — Ty's empty parking spot, Sarah's boardroom contrast, Diana's bills accumulating |
| 8–12s | The call to act | Single CTA on Fair Case Torch Amber button, diegetic finish, no CTA slab |

### Lens 3 — Fair Case three-tier message architecture cross-referenced against WCTL personas

| Fair Case Tier | Days post-incident | WCTL units that hit this tier |
|---------------|--------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Tier 1 — Active Decision Window** (Days 0-30) | First 30 days post-crash, adjuster's call just landed | S1 primary, S2 primary, S5 primary, M1 primary, M3 primary |
| **Tier 2 — Accumulation Phase** (Months 1-6) | Bills arriving, first offer made, second-guessing | S1-V3 ("first offer rarely fair"), M2 primary, M2-V1, M2-V2, S5-V3 ("$18K offer / $6K ER") |
| **Tier 3 — Statute of Limitations** (Months 6-SOL) | Hasn't acted, may think it's too late | None directly — opportunity for a Phase 2 add |

The 24 units skew heavily Tier 1 / Tier 2. Tier 3 isn't covered yet. **Note for Phase 2 planning** (out of scope for this review): consider 1-2 Tier 3 variants once Phase 1 data lands.

### Lens 4 — Standing five-voice production room

Per the just-locked memory `feedback_sc_production_room.md`:

## §8 — COMPLIANCE NOTE: THE $1.7B HERO NUMBER UNDER FAIR CASE

**Saul Goodman's call.** Davis confirmed the `$1.7 billion recovered` figure is factually accurate for WCTL (their lifetime recovery, public on their website / press materials). The compliance question is: **how does Fair Case advertise that number when WCTL is the law firm but Fair Case is the brand consumers see?**

Three options analyzed:

| Option | Language | Compliance posture | Strategic risk |
|--------|----------|-------------------|----------------|
| **A. Direct attribution** | *"$1.7 billion recovered by West Coast Trial Lawyers."* | Cleanest factual — WCTL named, no ambiguity. ER 7.1 / SB-37 clean. | **Conflicts with Fair Case lead-gen positioning.** Consumer doesn't know who WCTL is; introduces the law-firm name pre-quiz; muddles the funnel (consumer thinks they're going to WCTL's site, not Fair Case). |
| **B. Network attribution** | *"$1.7 billion recovered by trial lawyers in our network."* | Technically true (WCTL is in the network). ER 7.1 clean if "our network" is defensible. Requires Fair Case to be able to demonstrate that the number comes from network-firm work in the discovery if challenged. | Mid-risk — "our network" is unusual framing but defensible. Fair Case has multiple firms in network long-term, but currently WCTL is the first/only. |
| **C. Generic factual** | *"Trial lawyers in our network have recovered $1.7 billion in case value."* | Most Fair Case-aligned. Retains the factual anchor. ER 7.1 clean. | Lowest strategic risk. Slightly less punchy than Option B. |

**Saul's recommendation: Option B — Network attribution.** Reasoning:
1. Strongest punch of the three options
2. Defensible factual basis (WCTL recovered the $1.7B; WCTL is in Fair Case's network; therefore network firms have recovered $1.7B — true statement)
3. Future-proof: as Fair Case adds firms, the same line works without needing a new number
4. Fair Case-friendly: doesn't introduce WCTL by name pre-quiz; preserves the lead-funnel architecture

**Adopted language (locks for HN-1.7B unit):**
> **Hero text:** *$1.7 BILLION* (Tiempos Headline, white on Roman Navy field, generous tracking)
> **Subhead:** *Recovered by trial lawyers in our network.* (Caslon Pro, Torch Amber accent — small)
> **Source attribution (small text bottom):** *Lifetime case recovery, network-attorney data, 2026.* (IBM Plex Mono 8pt, Muted Navy Light)

Full unit spec in `New_Unit_Briefs.md` §7.
