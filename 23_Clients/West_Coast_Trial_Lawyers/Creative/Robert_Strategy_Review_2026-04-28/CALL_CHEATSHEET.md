## THE 14-DAY USER JOURNEY — how a target user gets hit

This is the heart of the call. **Walk Robert through one realistic Sarah-archetype encounter day-by-day** so he can picture what we're actually delivering — not "ads," a *sequence*.

### The setup

A Sarah-archetype target user: 38, Trauma ICU charge nurse at Flagstaff Medical Center, recent crash on I-17 by a Freightliner. She's on Meta daily — Instagram in the morning, Facebook in the evening, Reels passively in bed. She doesn't yet know she's being targeted by the campaign.

### Day 1–3 — Post-crash, in pain, processing

She's in a hospital bed, then home. Pain meds. Phone is the lifeline — checking Instagram for normalcy.

- **Touch 1: Reels 9:16 cinematic primary** (Format #1). Evening passive Reels session. She sees the kitchen scene — another ICU nurse, the brace on the same arm, the I-17 voiceover. *Recognition.* "Wait. That's literally me." She doesn't click. She doesn't engage. But the algorithm noticed she watched it past the first 2 seconds.
- *Cumulative exposures so far: 1*

### Day 3–5 — Paperwork starts arriving, lunch-break processing

She's seeing the medical bills. Insurance company calls. She's googling things she's never had to google.

- **Touch 2: Feed 4:5 cinematic photographic still** (Format #3). Lunch-break feed scroll. A still frame of the I-17 stretch at dusk, brace visible, no caption-headline. *Familiarity.* She pauses. "I keep seeing this." Doesn't click, but the brain has now bookmarked the visual.
- **Touch 3: Reels 9:16 motion-still** (Format #4). Late-night insomnia scroll. The same composition as touch 2, but with subtle camera push and a hint of motion. Reads as a different ad to her — but the visual cue is doing recognition work. *"They're not stopping. Maybe I should look."*
- *Cumulative exposures: 3, across 2 formats and 3 different placements/moments*

### Day 5–7 — Financial reality lands, looking around

The bills keep coming. She's lost income. Marcus (husband) is asking what they're going to do. She's on Facebook in the evening because Marcus is asleep.

- **Touch 4: UGC-Native Reels 9:16** (Format #2 — S1's V3 dual-register variant). Phone-handheld kitchen-table shot, another nurse on a video-call to a friend, talking through what happened. *Trust shift.* "This isn't an ad. This is a person." She doesn't yet know it's the same brand. The algorithm is still expanding her exposure.
- *Cumulative exposures: 4, across 3 formats*

### Day 7–10 — Actively researching, comparing

She's googling personal-injury lawyers in Flagstaff. She's on her work computer reading Yelp reviews of firms. She's in the consideration phase.

- **Touch 5: Feed 1:1 DR copy-led print still** (Format #5). Lunch-break feed. *"Hit by a Freightliner on I-17?"* in McCabe's editorial register, Fair Case mark, "Free case review. No fee unless we win." *Comprehension.* "Yes. That's exactly what happened. There's a quiz. Let me look."
- **Touch 6: Feed 4:5 designed-static HN-1.7B** (Format #6). Evening feed. "$1.7B recovered." Massimo's grid, restrained type. *Authority.* "Wait — these guys are serious."
- *Cumulative exposures: 6, across 4 formats*

### Day 10–14 — Decision window

She's mentally ready. She just needs the right moment.

- **Touch 7: Feed 1:1 designed-static EX-Settlement-Gap** (Format #6). Morning commute scroll. Comparative chart shows what people leave on the table without representation. Not a scare tactic — a quiet exhibit. *Urgency without manipulation.* "I can't afford to leave this on the table."
- **Touch 8: Reels 9:16 cinematic primary, second viewing** (Format #1, repeated). Evening passive Reels. Different mood now — she's no longer just recognizing herself, she's *deciding*. The voiceover lands differently the second time.
- **Touch 9 (the conversion): Feed 1:1 DR copy-led, retargeted variant** (Format #5). She clicks. Lands on the quiz. Step 3 confirms non-soft-tissue. Contact form. TrustedForm consent. **Delivered to WCTL intake.**
- *Total: 9 touches over 14 days, across 5 distinct formats, 3 placements (Reels / Stories / Feed), and 4 day-parts (morning commute / lunch / evening / late-night)*

### What this journey is doing that single-ad campaigns can't

| Effect | Mechanism |
| --- | --- |
| **Cumulative recognition** | Same person, different formats, different moods, different times of day. By touch 4 she *knows* the brand even if she hasn't named it. |
| **Frequency-cap evasion** | Meta caps roughly 7 exposures/user/week to a single creative before fatigue kicks in. Multi-format means we can deliver 9+ effective touches inside the cap. |
| **Placement-inventory coverage** | Reels, Stories, Feed 1:1, Feed 4:5 — each format lives where it lives. Multi-format = multi-placement = full inventory access. |
| **Day-part / mood-state matching** | Morning-commute Reels feels different than late-night insomnia scroll than lunch-break feed pause. Different formats *land* in different mood-states. |
| **Mental availability + activation, layered (Binet/Sharp)** | Cinematic primaries build mental availability. Designed-statics build brand-trust. DR print stills convert. The funnel works because no single format tries to do all three jobs. |

## PHASE 1 → PHASE 2 → PHASE 3 — why the lineup looks expensive

This is the part Robert most needs to be able to repeat back to his team:

> ***"The long expensive ads are doing hypothesis work, not optimization work. Once we know which doors open in Phase 1, Phase 2 scales those winners with simpler print stills and pre-written DR variants — production-ready, sitting in the Copy_Treatments docs. Complex ads bought us the learning. Simple ads scale the winners."***

| Phase | Weeks | What runs | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Phase 1** | 1–2 | 12 units (6 cinematic primaries + 2 designed-static brand units + 4 DR copy-led print stills) | Validate four-persona NST hypothesis — which behavioral levers actually filter at ≥15% NST pass rate |
| **Phase 2** | 3–5 | Winners + ~20 McCabe-pre-written DR variants + persona-specific Hero Numbers (HN-$1M Sarah, HN-263 Laura, HN-47% Ty, HN-$33.7K Diana) | Scale validated winners with cheaper-to-produce ammo (~10× lower per-unit production cost) |
| **Phase 3** | 5+ | Top 3–5 performers, 1 new variant per validated persona per 2-week window | Winners-only refresh, taper to budget cap |

**Day 14 hypothesis gate:**
- **<10% NST pass rate** → kill the unit, no Phase 2 expansion in that lane
- **10–15%** → revise creative + quiz Step 3, re-test
- **≥15%** → pass, Phase 2 expansion authorized

## ANSWER-ANYTHING WINDOW — likely Robert questions, prepped

| Likely question | Short answer |
| --- | --- |
| **"Why so many formats per persona?"** | Multi-format = multi-placement coverage + frequency-cap evasion + mood-state matching. One ad can't do recognition + trust + activation. We split the jobs across formats. |
| **"Why static-only for Laura and Ty M3?"** | Stillness is the subject for place-loss and presumption-of-fault. Deliberate test of feed-static vs. Reels-video on those audiences. If they win, Phase 2 rolls static variants across other personas. |
| **"Why UGC for Diana but not the others?"** | Diana's audience pattern-recognizes polished PI creative and scrolls. UGC slips through. Sarah/Ty/Laura need cinematic dignity — UGC would undercut them. Register matched to behavioral lever. |
| **"Why no crash footage anywhere?"** | SB-37 attack surface + SC brand standard + Saul compliance gate. The empty parking spot, the empty crosswalk, the brace alone — *absence is the creative*. Hegarty's Absence visual doctrine for Ty applies more broadly. |
| **"How do we know Phase 2 is cheaper?"** | DR copy-led variants and Hero Numbers are produced in Higgsfield + ChatGPT Image 2 in 45 min apiece. Cinematic primaries take days. Production cost ratio is ~10:1. |
| **"What if a unit dies at Day 14?"** | Hot-swap from refresh pool — 6 units already built and ready. No production lag. The architecture has insurance built in. |
| **"What's the Hero Number doing that the persona ads aren't?"** | Persona ads do recognition. Hero Numbers do brand-trust authority. The user who's been hit by 3-4 persona ads needs to know they're talking to a $1.7B firm before they convert. |
| **"Can we add a fifth or sixth persona later?"** | Phase 2/3 question, not Phase 1. Adding personas now fragments the learning — Meta needs ~50 optimization events per ad set per week. Once Phase 1 hypothesis validates, we can expand. |
