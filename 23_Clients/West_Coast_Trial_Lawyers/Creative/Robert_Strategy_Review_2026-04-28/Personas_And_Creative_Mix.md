# WCTL Arizona — Personas & Creative Mix

## The Four Personas

We've built creative around four specific archetypes — chosen to cover the addressable non-soft-tissue accident market in Arizona without overlapping behavioral territory.

### Sarah Mitchell — Trucking
38, Trauma ICU charge nurse at Flagstaff Medical Center. Hit by a Freightliner on I-17 during her regular outreach drive. Compound left humerus fracture. *The clinical professional made the patient.*

### Laura Henderson — Pedestrian
42, elementary school librarian, Gilbert. Hit at the Gilbert & Warner crosswalk by a right-turning driver in dawn light. Right tibial plateau fracture, Grade 2 concussion. *Fifteen years of walking the same neighborhood — broken.*

### Diana Nguyen — Severe MVA
42, Senior Office Manager in Chandler, household's stable-income anchor. Rear-ended on I-10 by an F-250 driver looking at his phone. C5-C6 disc herniation, Grade 2 concussion. *The household operating system that quietly stops functioning.*

### Ty Rivera — Motorcycle
32, HVAC technician in Chandler, Yamaha R6 supersport rider. Left-turn driver didn't see him at Southern & Dobson, 4:45 PM full sun. Femur fracture, surgical nail with 6 screws. *Did everything right. Still got blamed.*

## How a User Gets Hit

Within the 14-day learning window, a target user encounters approximately **8–10 ad touches across 4–5 different formats**, varying by placement and time of day:

- **Reels (morning + evening):** cinematic video or motion-still — full-screen vertical
- **Feed (lunch break):** cinematic still or DR print still — passive scroll pause
- **Reels (late night):** UGC variant — feels like a person, not an ad
- **Feed (decision window):** Hero Number + comparative exhibit — brand authority signal
- **Conversion:** DR copy-led print still with the matched persona hook

Multi-format coverage delivers what no single ad can: **recognition → familiarity → trust → comprehension → authority → action**, layered across two weeks.
