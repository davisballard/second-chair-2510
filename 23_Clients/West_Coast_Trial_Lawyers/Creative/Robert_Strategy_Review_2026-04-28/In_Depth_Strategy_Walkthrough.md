### Format 2 — UGC-Native Video (15s, vertical 9:16)

**What it is:**
A 15-second vertical, deliberately *unpolished* phone-handheld iPhone aesthetic. Flat phone color (no cinematic LUT). Natural daylight through a kitchen window or a domestic interior. Slightly shaky framing. Reads as authentic — *doesn't read as advertising*.

**Used by:**
- **S5 Diana — all 6 units, native UGC** (Diana's the only persona where UGC is the primary register, not a variant)
- S1 Sarah V3 — dual-register variant (cinematic primary + UGC variant)
- M2 Ty V1 — dual-register variant

**Composition rules:**
- Phone-handheld physical framing — slight wobble, not aggressive shake
- Flat color, no cinematic grade
- Natural daylight, kitchen-table or domestic setting
- Framing acknowledges the phone — sometimes the talker is mid-sentence, sometimes off-center

**Placement:**
Reels 9:16 + Stories — same surfaces as cinematic primary, but reads completely differently in the same scroll.

**Job:**
**Pattern break.** The user has been pattern-trained by years of I-10 billboard PI advertising to scroll past polished lawyer creative. UGC bypasses that filter because it doesn't *look* like a lawyer ad. By the time she registers it as advertising, she's already engaged with the content.

**Why Diana gets UGC native (not variant):**
Diana's audience (form-fillers, working moms, household-OS frame) is the most pattern-trained against polished PI creative. Polish reads as "they're trying to sell me something." UGC reads as "this is a person." The kitchen-table + falling-envelopes + interrupted-family-system story *is* the UGC register — Goffman role-performance grounded in domestic specificity.

**Why Sarah/Laura/Ty don't get UGC primary:**
They need cinematic dignity. Sarah is a clinical professional — UGC would undercut her authority register. Laura is contemplative place-loss — UGC would feel false-casual. Ty is rider-identity with Absence visual doctrine — UGC would dilute the visual world. UGC stays as a *variant* for them, not a primary.

**Production cost:**
Medium. Lower than cinematic primary (less grading, less compositional precision), higher than print stills.

### Format 3 — Cinematic Photographic Still (1:1, 4:5)

**What it is:**
A single-frame still, photographic quality. Reference register: Alec Soth, Nadav Kander — quiet compositional rigor. Stillness is the subject. Generated in ChatGPT Image 2 within Higgsfield iterative flow.

**Used by:**
- **S2 Laura — 6 stills (static-only discipline, no video for Laura at all)**
- **M3 Ty — 4 stills (static-only discipline for the Injustice frame)**

**Composition rules:**
- Single-frame, no motion
- Photographic quality, Soth/Kander register — natural light, contemplative
- For Laura: the empty crosswalk at Gilbert & Warner, dusk light + cyan fading sky + amber streetlight, Laura at the curb looking at the painted lines. The *empty* crosswalk markings are the co-star.
- For Ty M3: the empty driver's-side mirror reflecting an empty intersection. Ty is *not visible in the reflection* — that absence is the argument.
- Side-specific continuity props locked: Laura — cane LEFT hand, ACE wrap RIGHT ankle, wedding band LEFT hand, kitchen scar LEFT forearm. Ty — supersport visual world, Alpinestars leathers, Yamaha R6.

**Placement:**
Feed 1:1 / 4:5. Lives in the passive feed scroll, not in Reels. Doesn't compete with motion content.

**Job:**
**Pause-the-scroll moment.** Quiet, contemplative, non-aggressive. The user's eye lingers because there's no motion demanding attention, just composition holding it.

**Why static-only for Laura and Ty M3:**
Place-loss (Laura) and presumption-of-fault (Ty M3) both read better in stillness than motion. A video of Laura *not* walking would dilute the absence. A video of Ty's empty mirror would lose the argument the moment the camera moved. **Stillness is the subject.**

**The deliberate test:**
Static-only discipline on these two units is a controlled test of feed-static vs. Reels-video performance on those audiences. If they win at the Day 14 NST gate, Phase 2 rolls static variants across the other personas. If they lose, the result validates video-heavy composition for the broader funnel. Either way, it's a load-bearing data point.

**Hegarty's read on this** — the zag inside the zag: *"The PI firm that ships a still where everyone else ships motion is the firm that's thinking, not following format default."*

**Production cost:**
Low. Higgsfield + ChatGPT Image 2 iterative flow — ~45 minutes per render, several refinement passes.

### Format 4 — Animated Still / Motion-Still (9:16)

**What it is:**
A 9:16 Reels-eligible motion extension of a static — minimal movement (subtle camera push, parallax on layered elements, light shift, environmental motion like a curtain or breath). ~80% still, 20% motion. Built by extending a Format #3 cinematic still with Kling motion.

**Used by:**
- Extracted from Laura's cinematic stills to extend into Reels inventory
- Extracted from Ty M3's mirror composition for the same purpose
- Available as motion-still extensions for any Format #1 cinematic primary if Reels rotation needs more variety

**Composition rules:**
- Motion is *supportive*, not the subject — the still composition is still doing the work
- Camera moves slow and patient (motivated lively camera movement default per the motion-prompt-defaults rule, but at the *minimal* end of the spectrum)
- No subject motion that contradicts the stillness register

**Placement:**
Reels 9:16. The bridge that gets a static-only persona onto Reels surfaces.

**Job:**
**Bridges static-only units into Reels inventory** without producing a new full-motion video. Adds a different *moment* in the user's session — Reels feels active, but the motion-still pulls back into contemplation. From the user's point of view it reads as a different ad than the static she saw in feed yesterday, even though the composition is the same.

**Why this format exists:**
Reels traffic is too large to skip. If we didn't have motion-still extensions for Laura and M3 Ty, they'd be invisible on Reels. With motion-stills, they get the Reels touchpoint without breaking the static-as-subject discipline.

**Production cost:**
Very low — animates an already-built still in 5–15 minutes per unit.

- **Touch 2:** Feed 4:5 — Format #3 (cinematic photographic still). Lunch-break feed scroll. A still of the I-17 stretch at dusk, brace visible, no caption-headline. *Familiarity.* She pauses. "I keep seeing this." Doesn't click, but the brain has now bookmarked the visual.
- **Touch 3:** Reels 9:16 — Format #4 (motion-still). Late-night insomnia scroll. The same composition as touch 2, but with subtle camera push. Reads as a different ad to her — but the visual cue is doing recognition work. *"They're not stopping. Maybe I should look."*

**Day 5–7 — Financial reality lands, looking around**

Bills keep coming. Lost income. Marcus (husband) is asking what they're going to do. She's on Facebook in the evening because Marcus is asleep.

- **Touch 4:** Reels 9:16 — Format #2 (S1's V3 UGC variant). Phone-handheld kitchen-table shot, another nurse on a video-call talking through what happened. *Trust shift.* "This isn't an ad. This is a person." She doesn't yet know it's the same brand.

*Cumulative: 4 touches, across 4 formats.*

**Day 7–10 — Actively researching, comparing**

She's googling personal-injury lawyers in Flagstaff. Reading Yelp reviews. Consideration phase.

- **Touch 5:** Feed 1:1 — Format #5 (DR copy-led print still). Lunch-break feed. *"Hit by a Freightliner on I-17?"* in McCabe's editorial register, Fair Case mark, action line. *Comprehension.* "Yes. That's exactly what happened. There's a quiz. Let me look."
- **Touch 6:** Feed 4:5 — Format #6 (HN-1.7B designed-static). Evening feed. "$1.7B recovered." Massimo's grid, restrained type. *Authority.* "Wait — these guys are serious."

*Cumulative: 6 touches, across 5 formats.*

**Day 10–14 — Decision window**

Mentally ready. Just needs the right moment.

- **Touch 7:** Feed 1:1 — Format #6 (EX-Settlement-Gap exhibit). Morning commute scroll. Comparative chart — what people leave on the table without representation. Not a scare tactic. Quiet exhibit. *Urgency without manipulation.* "I can't afford to leave this on the table."

**Total: 9 touches over 14 days, across 5 distinct formats, 3 placements (Reels / Stories / Feed), and 4 day-parts (morning commute / lunch / evening / late-night).**

### What the 14-day journey is doing that single-ad campaigns can't

| Effect | Mechanism |
| --- | --- |
| **Cumulative recognition** | Same person, different formats, different moods, different times of day. By touch 4 she *knows* the brand even if she hasn't named it. |
| **Frequency-cap evasion** | Meta caps roughly 7 exposures/user/week to a single creative before fatigue kicks in. Multi-format means we deliver 9+ effective touches inside the cap by varying which creative she's seeing. |
| **Placement-inventory coverage** | Reels, Stories, Feed 1:1, Feed 4:5 — each format lives where it lives. Multi-format = full inventory access. Single-format campaigns leave half of Meta's surfaces empty. |
| **Day-part / mood-state matching** | Morning-commute Reels feels different than late-night insomnia scroll than lunch-break feed pause. Different formats *land* in different mood-states. |
| **Mental availability + activation, layered (Sharp + Binet)** | Cinematic primaries build mental availability. Designed-statics build brand-trust authority. DR print stills convert. The funnel works because no single format tries to do all three jobs. |
| **Algorithmic learning quality** | Meta's algorithm learns *who* engages with *which* format. By the end of Phase 1, we know not just "Sarah's audience exists" but "Sarah's audience converts on UGC variant V3 in Reels at 9 PM on weekdays." That signal compounds into Phase 2 efficiency. |

| --- | --- |

15% gate calibrated down from initial 40% target after Indigo's signal-volume math (recalibrated 2026-04-22 — original rates compounded to a 7× overstatement vs. the 0.95% implied by the 21-lead contractual target).
