## Why Arizona — The Data Case

Robert told us Arizona and Washington are both "better markets" than California right now. We went deep on the numbers and Arizona is the clear winner for a $10K pilot.

### The Core Comparison

| Metric | Arizona (Statewide) | Washington (Seattle DMA) |
|--------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| Total crashes/year | 122,247 | ~42,000 |
| Total injuries/year | 54,000+ | ~13,000 |
| Est. non-soft-tissue incidents/month | **925–1,233** | 325–430 |
| Cumulative unrepresented pipeline | **2,775–7,400** | 970–2,590 |
| Capture rate needed for 21 leads | **0.3–0.8%** | 0.8–2.2% |
| WCTL office status | Established, staffed | New lease, building out |
| Motorcycle volume/month | 264 crashes | 125–167 |
| Seasonal consistency | Year-round | Winter dip (motorcycle/cycling drops) |
| PI statute of limitations | 2 years | 3 years |

### What the Numbers Mean

Arizona produces **~60% more non-soft-tissue incidents per month** than the Seattle DMA. But the real advantage is the cumulative pipeline — the pool of unrepresented people who had a qualifying accident in the past 6-12 months and haven't retained an attorney.

Our ads don't target "people who had an accident this week." They run broadly, and people who've had a qualifying accident self-select by engaging. At any given time in Arizona, there are an estimated **2,775–7,400 unrepresented people** with non-soft-tissue injuries in the pipeline. Needing ~21 delivered leads from that pool is a **0.3–0.8% capture rate** — very comfortable for paid social performance.

In Seattle, that same math requires a 0.8–2.2% capture rate. Achievable, but less margin for error — especially on a $10K test where the first 25% of spend goes to pixel learning.

Additionally, the pool isn't static — **925–1,233 new non-soft-tissue incidents happen in Arizona every month**. Over the 4-6 week campaign window, the pipeline constantly refreshes with new potential leads.

### Why "All of Arizona" and Not Just Phoenix

Arizona has two DMAs: Phoenix (62% of state population) and Tucson (15-17%). On Meta, we target statewide and the algorithm naturally concentrates spend where engagement is highest — which will be Phoenix. But including Tucson is free volume: lower competition, lower CPMs, and WCTL services both from the same Arizona office. No reason not to capture it.

### Arizona's Category Richness

The types of non-soft-tissue cases Arizona produces align perfectly with what WCTL takes:

| Category | AZ Monthly Volume | Why It Matters |
|----------|------------------|----------------|
| Moderate-to-severe MVA | ~400–600 | Largest pool — broken bones, herniated discs, concussions, surgeries |

### Why $475/Lead — Not $315

The $315 price point in our standard pricing table is built for a **blended PI lead mix** — one where 60-70% of delivered leads are soft tissue cases with $20K median settlements and $6,600 in expected attorney fees. That price reflects the cost to acquire a lead from the full PI population.

**This is a fundamentally different product.** Non-soft-tissue-only leads cost more to acquire and are worth dramatically more to the firm:

**Why it costs us more:**
- We're fishing in a pool that's 30-40% of all injury crashes, not 100% — smaller targetable audience = higher CPMs to find them
- The funnel has aggressive severity filtering — we pay for every click, but a significant portion of quiz-starters get disqualified at the injury severity step because they ARE soft tissue. Those clicks cost us money but don't produce a delivered lead.
- Fewer delivered leads per dollar of ad spend = higher effective acquisition cost on our end

**Why the lead is worth more to WCTL:**
- Minimum median settlement jumps from $20K (soft tissue floor) to $75K (moderate MVA floor) — **3.75x higher**
- Expected attorney fee floor jumps from $6,600 to $25,000 — **3.75x higher**
- Zero wasted intake time on leads WCTL would reject — every lead is a genuine opportunity
- Robert himself said WCTL's case selectivity *"can justify higher CPAs"* — this is that higher CPA, and the math supports it

**The pricing math:**

| Metric | Standard Blended PI ($315) | Non-Soft-Tissue Only ($475) |
|--------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| Avg expected attorney fee per lead | ~$12,000–$15,000 | ~$30,000 |
| Lead value to firm | Mixed (60-70% are cases WCTL rejects) | Every lead is a case WCTL would take |
| Cost to acquire | Standard | ~1.5-2x (smaller audience, funnel filtering) |
| **Price per lead** | **$315** | **$475** |
| **Price as % of expected fee** | **2.1-2.6%** | **1.6%** |

At $475/lead, WCTL is actually paying a **lower percentage of expected case value** than they would at $315 for blended leads. The premium is real in absolute dollars but it's a better deal in relative terms — they're paying less per dollar of projected pipeline value.

## How Leads Move Through Our System

### The Funnel

```
User sees ad (Meta / TikTok)
        ↓
Lands on quiz-style funnel
        ↓
Step 1: Were you in an accident? (Yes/No)
Step 2: When did the accident happen? (Recency filter)
Step 3: Were you at fault? (Not-at-fault filter)
Step 4: What type of accident? (MVA, trucking, motorcycle, pedestrian, rideshare, other)
Step 5: Describe your injuries (severity qualifier — filters out soft tissue)
Step 6: Have you seen a doctor / been to the hospital? (treatment status)
Step 7: Do you already have an attorney? (representation filter)
Step 8: Contact information + TCPA consent
        ↓
Verification & scoring pipeline
        ↓
Delivered to WCTL
```

### Qualification Criteria (What Gets Through)

A lead must meet ALL of the following to be delivered:

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Accident type | Motor vehicle, trucking, motorcycle, pedestrian, bicycle, rideshare, DUI (victim) |
| Fault status | Not at fault (or shared fault where lead is not primary) |
| Injury severity | Non-soft-tissue — must indicate broken bones, surgery, hospitalization, herniated disc, TBI, concussion, lacerations, or comparable |
| Recency | Accident occurred within statute of limitations (2 years for AZ) |
| Representation | Does not currently have an attorney |
| Location | Arizona resident or accident occurred in Arizona |
| Contact validity | Verified phone + email |
| TCPA consent | 1-to-1 consent naming West Coast Trial Lawyers |
| Bot/fraud check | Passed automated fraud detection |

### Verification Pipeline

Before a lead reaches WCTL, it passes through:

1. **TCPA 1-to-1 consent** — The consent form explicitly names West Coast Trial Lawyers. Not a generic "you consent to be contacted by a lawyer." WCTL's name is on it.
2. **Bot and fraud filter** — Automated detection for fake submissions, VPN masking, duplicate entries, and bot patterns
3. **Non-litigator scrub** — API check against known litigator databases to flag potential abuse
4. **Lead scoring** — Every lead receives a Fit Score and Quality Score based on injury type, case type, treatment status, recency, fault position, and contact quality
5. **Lead grading** — Composite score maps to a letter grade (A/B/C/D) that determines sign probability and Projected Attorney Fee
6. **Territory matching** — Confirmed Arizona geography before routing

## Refund Policy

We believe in clean accountability. If a lead doesn't meet our stated qualification criteria, it shouldn't count against WCTL's budget.

### Refundable Leads

A lead qualifies for a full credit (applied to additional leads, not cash refund) if it meets ANY of the following:

| Condition | How We Verify |
|-----------|--------------|
| **At-fault lead** — Lead is determined to be the at-fault party | Police report or intake discovery reveals primary fault |
| **Soft tissue only** — Lead's injuries are exclusively soft tissue despite passing our funnel filter | WCTL intake confirms no qualifying injuries beyond minor soft tissue |
| **Duplicate** — Same person delivered twice within the same campaign | System match on phone/email/name |
| **Fake / bot** — Fabricated contact information or automated submission that bypassed fraud filter | Non-working phone, bounced email, or clear fabrication |
| **Outside geography** — Lead's accident did not occur in Arizona and lead does not reside in Arizona | Intake discovery confirms out-of-territory |
| **Already represented** — Lead already has an attorney despite indicating otherwise in the funnel | Intake discovery confirms existing representation |
| **Outside statute** — Accident occurred more than 2 years ago (AZ statute of limitations) | Date confirmed outside window |

### Non-Refundable Leads

Leads are NOT refundable for:

- WCTL's intake team was unable to reach the lead (contact was valid at time of delivery)
- Lead decided not to proceed after initial contact with WCTL
- Lead signed with a different firm after being delivered to WCTL
- Case value is lower than WCTL expected (but injuries are non-soft-tissue)
- Lead doesn't respond within WCTL's follow-up window (7-day cycle) but contact info was valid

### How It Works

Robert flags the lead with a reason. We review within 48 hours. If it meets the criteria above, we issue a credit that adds an additional lead to the batch at no charge. Simple, clean, no fighting.

Robert said on the call that he's not trigger-happy with refund requests — *"the only time I'll ever request or ask for one is if it's legitimate."* We respect that. This policy is designed for the same standard: legitimate misses get made right, and everything else is part of the normal conversion funnel.
