## The Strategy in One Paragraph

Six format types each fill a different scroll-mood-state job (cinematic recognition, UGC pattern-interrupt, designed-static authority, motion-still bridging, DR copy-led conversion, designed-static brand units). One Advantage+ ad set, broad AZ-statewide, Special Ad Category Social Issues — Meta's algorithm distributes across placements. The creative IS the targeting, since SAC blocks demo targeting. Phase 1 adds a sixth-pattern Anchor split-test: ~40% of units carry a persistent Fair Case-branded bottom-third overlay (FB-Feed-native), ~60% stay Clean (IG-Reels-native). Week-1 placement-segmented metrics validate. (See 02_Strategy_Brief, 03_Media_Mix, 04_Creative_Slate for the substance.)

## What We Refuse

| | |
|---|---|
| ❌ Shock-factor / fear-based imagery | ❌ Settlement-promise hero claims |
| ❌ "You deserve more" urgency | ❌ Fake awards / fake stats |
| ❌ Generic templates with city names swapped | ❌ Templates without market specificity |
| ❌ Soft-tissue lead delivery | ❌ AI slop (waxy skin, uncanny faces, HDR over-sharpening) |

**Note on AI crash footage in AZ:** Arizona doesn't have SB-37, so AI-generated crash imagery is permissible here when it's high-craft and non-shock-factor. The discipline against "AI crash slop" (low-quality scary footage designed to terrify) still holds — but well-directed crash sequences that match SC's "indistinguishable from real footage" standard are in-bounds for the AZ pilot. CA + multi-state Phase 2 may require revisiting per state-specific rules.
