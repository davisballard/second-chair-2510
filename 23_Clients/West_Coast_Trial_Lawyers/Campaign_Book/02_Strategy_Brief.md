## Single-Minded Proposition

> **For Arizonans seriously injured in a crash that wasn't their fault, Fair Case connects you to West Coast Trial Lawyers — the firm that wins seven-figure verdicts for people like you, with no fee unless we win.**

One sentence. One promise. One implied social proof. One risk-reversal. Everything below operationalizes this.

## The Living / Looking / Buying Journey for PI

Most lead-gen vendors run flat funnels — one creative, one CTA, one moment. PI doesn't work like that because the audience isn't in-market until they are. Julian's framework: campaigns are **loops, not funnels.** Brand-building runs continuously; performance lights up at the moment of need.

| Phase | Who they are | What they're doing | Our job | When this phase happens |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Living** | Pre-incident — not in market | Scrolling FB/IG normally | We don't reach them — too expensive at $7,750 to brand-build broadly. Phase 2 may add awareness layer if budget supports. | n/a in Phase 1 |
| **Looking — Active Decision Window** | Post-accident, Day 0-30 | Researching options, comparing firms, may have already received insurance lowball | **Be findable + credible + low-friction** — quiz-funnel ad with the right hook + Fair Case authority signal | Phase 1 PRIMARY focus |
| **Looking — Accumulation Phase** | Post-accident, Month 1-6 | Bills arriving, second-guessing the first offer, treatment ongoing | **Trigger the "I should call a lawyer" decision** — financial-distress framing, "What the crash took is still being taken" | Phase 1 secondary focus (M2 Ty Loss, S5 Diana variants) |
| **Buying** | Ready to engage, filling the form | Submitting the quiz, talking to WCTL intake | **Don't break the experience** — quiz friction low, TCPA clean, immediate confirmation | Quiz funnel + WCTL handoff |

**Phase 1 lives mostly in "Looking — Active Decision Window" with Accumulation Phase as the secondary territory.** That's where our $7,750 buys the most lead volume.

## How Robert Should Read This

The single most important sentence: **"The creative is the targeting."** Special Ad Category locks demo targeting; the only lever we have is creative diversity to self-qualify the right viewer. That's why six formats exist, why each persona has a different emotional hook, why we're testing the Anchor as a sixth pattern. Every variant in the slate is doing a job no other variant does.

If Robert asks "why don't we just run one strong cinematic ad?" — the answer: because one creative covers one mood-state, and the AZ non-soft-tissue addressable market lives across at least four mood-states (active-decision FB scroll, accumulation-phase FB scroll, UGC-pattern-interrupt IG Reels, motorcycle-identity IG Reels). One creative leaves three quarters of the addressable market unreached.
