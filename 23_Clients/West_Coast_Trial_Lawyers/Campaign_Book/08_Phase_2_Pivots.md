## Pivot 1 — Spanish ad set (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Research finding:** AZ has 31% Hispanic Maricopa share, 1.41M Hispanic residents in Phoenix, ZIP 85033 Maryvale >80% Latino. Vendor consensus across PI marketing literature: don't translate, **transcreate** — separate Spanish-only ad sets, not bilingual ads. PI + workers' comp are highest Hispanic-targeting ROI verticals.

**Phase 1 must produce:**
- Baseline English CPL (already tracked)
- Hispanic-share lead delivery from quiz responses — does the funnel attract Spanish-speakers despite English-only creative? Track via quiz language preference / surname / ZIP cluster
- Maryvale + South Phoenix ZIP-level lead concentration data (geo concentration in Hispanic-dense ZIPs)

**Decision criteria:**
- **GO Phase 2 Spanish ad set if:** Phase 1 Hispanic-share lead delivery <15% (vs 31% TAM share). Indicates current English creative is not capturing the Hispanic audience and dedicated Spanish creative is leverage.
- **PARK if:** Phase 1 Hispanic-share lead delivery is 25-40%. English creative is somehow capturing Hispanic audience (possible but unusual). Test bilingual variant in Phase 2 instead of dedicated ad set.
- **NEVER GO if:** Phase 1 lead total is below target (<15 leads). Spanish ad set is leverage when English funnel is healthy; not a fix for a broken funnel.

**Phase 2 spec if approved:**
- Dedicated Spanish-only ad set, parallel to English ad set
- 25-30% of Phase 2 spend allocated
- Transcreated (not translated) variants — family/multi-generational framing, "su caso" as family protection, not individual-rights framing
- Hispanic attorneys / staff in creative if WCTL has any
- Phoenix / Maryvale / South Phoenix / Mesa / Glendale geo concentration

**Owner:** Indigo + Spanish-language copywriter (Phase 2 prep). Davis sources copywriter via referral if proceeding.

## Pivot 2 — LP+quiz vs Instant Form A/B test

**Research finding:** Meta Instant Form delivers 30-70% lower CPL but materially lower lead quality. PI vendor consensus (Grow Law, CasePeer, INSIDEA) recommends LP+quiz over Instant Form because PI case value ($5K-$50K+ in fee per signed case) makes lead-quality differential matter more than raw volume. Jon Loomer's testing confirmed Instant Form leads measurably worse-quality at intake.

**Phase 1 must produce:**
- LP+quiz CPL benchmark (already tracked — expected $337-$517 band)
- Signed-case rate from LP+quiz leads (intake conversion to retained client)
- Cost per signed case = CPL ÷ signed-case rate

**Decision criteria:**
- Phase 2 adds an Instant Form ad set running parallel to LP+quiz with matched creatives.
- **GO Instant Form scale if:** Instant Form signed-case rate ≥ 50% of LP+quiz signed-case rate AT 50%+ lower CPL. Instant Form wins on net cost-per-signed-case.
- **LP+quiz holds primary if:** Instant Form signed-case rate <50% of LP+quiz. Quality gap exceeds cost gap.
- **Hybrid if:** mixed signal — Instant Form for top-of-funnel volume + LP+quiz for premium pipeline. Allocate 70/30 LP+quiz / Instant Form in Phase 2.

**Owner:** Simon (media buying) + Robert / WCTL intake (signed-case rate data).

## Pivot 4 — Live-transfer escalation tier

**Research finding:** Live-transfer leads at $300-$1,500 vs web-form at $150-$300, but signed-case rates 3-5× higher. Auto-accident live transfers commonly $300-$1,500. Industry standard 3-tier: Instant Form / web form / live transfer at roughly 1× : 3× : 5-10× cost differentials.

**Phase 1 must produce:**
- Signed-case rate from web-form leads (baseline)
- WCTL intake bottleneck data: average first-call delay, number of leads dropped due to delayed contact

**Decision criteria:**
- **ADD live-transfer tier if:** WCTL signed-case rate from web-form is <20% AND intake first-call delay >2 hours. Live-transfer pre-screens callers and routes hot leads to attorneys in real time. Pays back when intake bottleneck is the conversion limiter.
- **DON'T ADD if:** Web-form signed-case rate >35%. Pipeline is healthy at lower cost; live-transfer would be over-investment.
- **DON'T ADD if:** WCTL doesn't have intake-team capacity for live-transfer (it's an operational requirement, not just a media decision).

**Phase 2 spec if approved:**
- Salesforce integration with live-transfer vendor (Quote Wizard, On Point Legal Leads, Legal Brand Marketing)
- Allocate 15-20% of Phase 2 budget to live-transfer initially; scale based on signed-case rate

**Owner:** Robert + WCTL intake operations. Agency facilitates Salesforce integration if approved.

## Pivot 5 — TCPA April 2025 compliance hygiene (PRE-PHASE-2 TASK)

**Research finding:**
- April 2025: opt-out window narrowed from 30 days to **10 business days**
- Jan 2025: One-to-one consent rule was vacated by 11th Circuit (*Insurance Marketing Coalition v. FCC*) and FCC repealed it — but voluntary compliance recommended for litigation defense
- Penalty exposure: $500-$1,500 per non-compliant message

**Phase 1 must produce:**
- Existing consent block audit (current language)
- STOP-handling response time baseline (how fast does WCTL CRM honor opt-outs?)

**Decision criteria:** This is hygiene, not a decision. Phase 2 launch requires:
- Consent block updated to current Meta + FCC standards (5 elements: entity ID + message description + frequency disclosure + opt-out mechanism + standard rate disclaimer + affirmative consumer action)
- Seller-specific consent (name "Fair Case" AND WCTL specifically — voluntary post-vacatur but litigation-defensive)
- CRM auto-honors STOP within 10 business days
- Quarterly TCPA training for intake team

**Owner:** Saul (compliance) + WCTL counsel. Pre-Phase 2 sign-off required.

## Pivot 6 — Anchor split-test outcome → Phase 2 standard or retirement

**Research finding:** No public PI benchmark validates persistent designed bottom-third overlay. Practitioner consensus (Hott / Denney / Common Thread Collective) runs against persistent designed overlays — they argue branded panels from t=0 telegraph "ad" and likely reduce hook rate. Already pressure-tested 2026-05-01 PM, Anchor build cut from 13 to 4 with Day 5 hook-rate gate.

**Phase 1 must produce:** Day 5 hook-rate gate result on the 4 Anchor units (per `06_Variant_Slate_Plan.md` revised plan).

**Decision criteria (already locked):**
- **SCALE Anchor if:** Anchor hook rate within 15% of matched Clean (or higher) on FB Feed. Build remaining ~9 Anchor composites in Week 2 production cycle.
- **KILL Anchor if:** Anchor hook rate drops >15% with no CPL offset. Pause the 4 launched units; freed impression share goes to Clean cohort.
- **MIXED — keep winning pairs:** within 15% on some pairs, >15% drop on others. Diagnose by copy register, kill losing pairs, do not scale.

**Phase 2 contingencies:**
- **If Anchor wins overall:** Phase 2 mandates Anchor as FB Feed standard for all FB-Feed-native cinematic units. Maintains the proprietary register experiment.
- **If Anchor loses overall:** Phase 2 retires the pattern entirely. Reallocates production hours to talking-head + Spanish ad set work.
- **If mixed:** Phase 2 keeps winning copy registers (Promise / Specificity / Speed / Reassurance) on FB Feed only; doesn't scale, doesn't retire.

## Cross-cutting Phase 2 considerations

These are not standalone pivots but inform Phase 2 thinking:

1. **CPL up ~20% YoY across Meta lead campaigns.** Phase 2 budget projections should not assume 2025 CPL bands. Add 15-20% buffer to expected CPL ranges.

2. **Andromeda dedup kills "1 image / 6 headlines" testing.** Phase 2 hook variant production needs **visual diversity**, not just copy diversity. Plan for distinct Higgsfield generations per hook variant, not text overlays on the same base image.

3. **Foxwell lean-budget cadence: 1-3 concepts/month NOT 1/week.** Already aligned with Phase 1 cadence. Affirms Phase 2 can scale spend without scaling concept-production proportionally — focus on iteration on Phase 1 winners.

4. **Reels structurally lower hook rate baseline.** Phase 2 Reels-specific creative should expect 5-10pp lower hook rate baseline. Don't compare blended Reels-Feed numbers when evaluating creative performance.

5. **Mass tort consideration.** Mass Tort Ad Agency manages $250M+ Meta spend across 600+ firms. If WCTL ever expands to plaintiff recruitment for specific torts, that's a separate playbook with its own SAC framing. Not a current Phase 2 priority but flagged.
