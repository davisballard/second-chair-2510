- **Visible to you** — every ad, every placement, in your dashboard
- **Approvable by you** — optional pre-launch approval workflow, on a standing basis
- **SB-37 compliant** — no prohibited content, mandatory disclosures, full documentability

No black box. You see what runs before it runs.

### 2. Territory Exclusivity

When Tofer holds a Market, no competing PI firm can purchase leads from Second Chair in that market. Not "exclusive leads" — exclusive territory. Your competitor in LA can't buy from us while you hold LA.

### 3. Projected Attorney Fee — Day-One Value Reporting

Every lead we deliver has a dollar-denominated projected value attached before any case has signed. This is built from our Fit Score model:

```
Projected Attorney Fee = Expected Fee by Case Type × Sign Probability by Lead Grade
Projected ROAS = Σ(Projected Attorney Fee) ÷ Total Lead Spend
```

No vendor in this space projects per-lead value like this. Most report CPL (what you paid for a phone number) or CPSC 6-12 months after cases close. Projected Attorney Fee shows you the value of your pipeline on day one.

This is a number you can track in your dashboards from the first lead delivered.

### 4. Market-Specific Creative

We don't swap in a city name on a national template. We go deep.

For Los Angeles, that means: Westside rideshare density, South Bay working-class commute patterns, Valley suburban family MVA scenarios, Inland Empire I-10/I-15 trucking corridors. For any Market we work in, we research the local landscape — commute corridors, industry patterns, demographic composition, language needs — and build creative that reflects the real community, not a generic template swapped with a city name.

### 5. Creative Production

We use AI-assisted production tools to create ad imagery and video that is indistinguishable from real commercial and UGC footage. We color grade it, match real camera styles (both commercial-grade and iPhone/UGC with natural imperfections), and deploy highly personalized creative for different audience segments.

This lets us split test at a pace and level of personalization traditional production can't touch — while keeping everything looking like real footage, not AI slop. We run all scripts and visual creative through an AI compliance screening system before presenting anything or running it live.

Every piece of creative is empathy-driven. We show people we understand their lives and their situation. No scare tactics, no crash footage, no settlement promises. No IP infringement — no branded characters, no copyrighted material.

## How Leads Are Qualified

Every lead that reaches your intake team has passed through this:

1. **Accident verification** — Were they in an accident? When? (We prioritize leads within 18 months)
2. **Fault filter** — Not at fault, or shared fault where they're not primary
3. **Accident type** — MVA, trucking, motorcycle, pedestrian, cyclist, rideshare, DUI victim
4. **Injury assessment** — severity screening (configurable: all-PI or non-soft-tissue-only)
5. **No existing attorney** — Already represented = disqualified
6. **Contact verification** — Valid phone + email confirmed via verification APIs
7. **TCPA consent** — 1-to-1 consent that explicitly names your firm. TrustedForm certificate on every lead.
8. **Bot/fraud filter** — 5-signal automated detection (honeypot fields, submission speed, user agent, browser headers, referrer validation)
9. **Duplicate suppression** — phone/email match within 24-hour rolling window, auto-blocked
10. **Rate-limited spam** — 3 submissions max per IP per 60-minute window

Full audit trail stored for every verification step.

## Lead Scoring — Fit Score Model

Every lead is scored by our dual-scoring engine:

**Fit Score** — Measures case viability: injury severity, case type, medical treatment status, fault position, recency, geographic match.

**Quality Score** — Measures lead reliability: consent strength, contact validity, engagement pattern, fraud signals.

Combined into a composite **Grade:**

| Grade | Composite Fit Score | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | 85+ | High-value case indicators, strong contact, high sign probability |
| **B** | 65–84 | Solid case, verified contact, moderate-to-good sign probability |
| **C** | 45–64 | Lower-value indicators or weaker contact; still qualified |
| **D** | Below 45 | Minimum-qualifying; lower sign probability |

Every lead you receive includes its grade, Fit Score, and Projected Attorney Fee. Your intake team can prioritize accordingly — work the A's first, batch the C's for later.

## Disqualified Lead Program

Our funnel filters aggressively — which means some leads that don't pass our screening may still be viable for a firm that evaluates them differently. Cases where someone answers "at fault" but was lane-splitting (legal in CA), or where there's no medical treatment yet but the accident is recent — these get filtered out of the qualified pipeline, but they may still have value.

We're open to routing disqualified leads to your team on a **pay-per-sign basis** — you receive the DQ leads, work them through your intake process, and only pay for the ones that convert to signed cases within an agreed retention window. This means nothing goes to waste — your intake team gets additional pipeline at zero upfront cost, and we both benefit when those cases convert.

Details on DQ routing, retention window, and pricing to be finalized as part of the service agreement.

## SB-37 Compliance (California)

Tofer operates entirely within California. Under SB-37 (effective January 1, 2025), the firm that benefits from a lead gen ad — not just the firm that produced it — can be held liable for non-compliant content. Statutory damages: $5,000 to $100,000 per violation, with private right of action.

Every Second Chair creative placed in California is built to SB-37 standard:

- No crash footage or graphic accident imagery
- No specific settlement amount promises
- No "you deserve more" or "get more than you settled for" framing
- Mandatory disclosures on every placement
- Full documentability — you can produce a complete record of every ad that ran under your firm's name at any point

Your current vendors may be running SB-37-compliant creative. The question we'd ask is: can you pull up everything that ran under Tofer's name this week? If you can, great. If you can't, that's the gap we close.
