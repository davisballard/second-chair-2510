## The Wedge — Why Tofer ≠ WCTL

**WCTL was a brand-exposure play.** Neama's TV personality → his brand is the firm's #1 asset → bad vendor creative = SB-37 exposure + reputation damage. Robert was a defensive junior protecting vendor relationships.

**Tofer is an operator play.** No TV personality. No Neama-equivalent brand face. Alex Tofer is a respected trial lawyer but not a media figure. So the *brand-exposure-via-vendor-creative* wedge lands softer here.

**What replaces it: Measurement.** Carlos lives in Looker dashboards. He measures contract conversions, lead quality, referral performance, intake team productivity. **No vendor in his current stack gives him day-one projected value per lead.** Projected Attorney Fee → Projected ROAS is the number he's been hungry for without knowing it existed.

### The wedge stack (in order of priority)

1. **PRIMARY — Projected Attorney Fee → Projected ROAS (measurement).** Carlos gets a dollar-denominated projection per lead on day one. No CPL guesses, no 6-month CPSC lag. Plugs into his Looker dashboard. Alex's TradeStation credibility sells the math rigor.

2. **SECONDARY — Creative visibility + SB-37.** Still applies — all 4 offices are CA. But use it as a *"you already know this from your Legal Soft Solution days"* lever — not a *"let me educate you"* reveal. Assume he's already seen vendor black boxes.

3. **TERTIARY — Territory exclusivity + intake-feed quality.** Scored A/B/C/D leads → his team works fewer Ds, more As, sign rate goes up per lead touched. This helps his team's productivity numbers without replacing them.
