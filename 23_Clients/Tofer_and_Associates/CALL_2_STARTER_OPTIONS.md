**How to pitch it:**

> *"If it were me, I'd start with SF and the Inland Empire. Both are big enough to get the pixel past learning phase at 100 leads each. You test two very different markets — Bay Area and Inland Empire — and your Upland office is right there for Riverside cases. $72K total, 200 leads, and you have real comparison data in about 6 weeks."*

## If They Push for LA

> *"LA is our highest-value California market, and we want to make sure we get it right. Our recommendation is to prove the model in a market like SF or Riverside first — both are big enough to get clean data — and then bring LA in as Phase 2 once we both know the campaigns are dialed in. That way your LA launch benefits from everything we learn in the first 100."*

## If They Push for Non-Soft-Tissue

> *"We can definitely do non-soft-tissue — the pricing doc we sent has the full breakdown. My recommendation for the starter is all injury types first. It gives the pixel more signal to optimize faster, gives your intake team full volume, and gives us real CPSC data across the full case mix. Once that's dialed in, we can layer non-soft-tissue campaigns on top or switch over. Phase 2 conversation."*

## If They Want Less Than 100

> *"The first 30-50 leads are the algorithm's learning phase — costs are higher, targeting is least precise. At 50 leads, you'd evaluate us mostly on pre-optimization data. At 100, you see the full arc — learning, stabilization, and early optimization. 100 is the number where you get to see what the campaigns actually produce."*
