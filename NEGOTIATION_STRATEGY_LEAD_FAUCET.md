## The Negotiation Framework: Chris Voss + Jeremy Miner (NEPQ)

You'll be using two complementary frameworks:

### Chris Voss: Tactical Empathy
- **Mirroring**: Repeat last 3 words to encourage them to expand
- **Labeling**: Name their emotions/thoughts to defuse them
- **Calibrated Questions**: "How/What" questions that make them solve YOUR problem
- **Accusation Audit**: List all their objections BEFORE they say them
- **"No"-Oriented Questions**: Make them feel safe by letting them say "No"
- **Strategic Silence**: After asking a question, SHUT UP. Let them fill the void.

### Jeremy Miner: NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning)

**Tonality** is critical in NEPQ:
- **Down tone (↘️)**: Statements, building trust, showing confidence
- **Up tone (↗️)**: Curious questions, genuine interest, non-threatening
- **Down-up tone (↘️↗️)**: Transitioning from statement to curious question

**NEPQ Question Sequences:**

1. **Problem Admission Questions ("Feel" Questions)**
   - "How does it feel when..." ↗️
   - "How concerned are you about..." ↗️
   - Get them to acknowledge the problem emotionally

2. **Solution Awareness Questions**
   - "What if there was a way to..." ↘️↗️
   - Paint the future state

3. **Consequence Questions**
   - "What happens if we don't solve this?" ↗️
   - Make the cost of inaction clear

4. **Future Pacing**
   - "Imagine if..." ↘️
   - Help them visualize the positive outcome

5. **Commitment Scale (1-10)**
   - "On a scale of 1-10, how important is it to solve this?" ↗️
   - Anything below 8-9 means dig deeper

6. **Ownership Language**
   - "It seems like..." ↘️
   - "It sounds like..." ↘️
   - Makes them own the conclusion
