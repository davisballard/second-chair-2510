# Fair Case — B2C Consumer Acquisition Strategy

**Version:** 1.0 — March 2026  
**Status:** Initial framework. This is an ad-driven business. Organic strategy is supplementary.

---

## Strategic Reality

Fair Case is not a brand-building exercise. It is a paid acquisition machine with a brand face.

The funnel is:
**Ad → Landing page → Quiz → Lead → Law firm**

Every strategic decision — social presence, content, page warmup, follower count — exists to support paid ad performance, not to build an audience. The Instagram page is a trust signal, not a media channel.

This distinction matters. Resources should not be allocated as if organic reach were the goal. Organic reach is a byproduct at best. The goal is: when someone clicks a Fair Case ad and checks the Instagram page, they see a real brand.

---

## Business Model

Fair Case generates revenue by producing qualified personal injury leads and delivering them exclusively to law firm partners through Second Chair's platform.

**Revenue per lead:** Based on firm contracts (not disclosed here)  
**Lead quality floor:** Only MVA cases (car, truck, motorcycle, rideshare, pedestrian)  
**Lead qualification:** 6-question quiz at getafaircase.com + TCPA consent  
**Lead exclusivity:** One firm receives each lead — no sharing  

The consumer never pays anything. Attorneys work on contingency. Fair Case never takes a fee from the claimant.

## Acquisition Channel Strategy

### Primary: Meta Ads (Instagram + Facebook)

Meta is the primary acquisition channel. Fair Case runs under Special Ad Category (Housing, Employment, or Credit is not applicable — Legal is the category) which limits targeting.

**What we can target:**
- Geographic radius (down to 5–15 miles)
- Age 18+
- Language

**What we cannot target:**
- Injury-related interests
- Health/medical interests
- Lookalike audiences built on injury data

**Solution:** Creative does the targeting. The first 3 seconds call out the specific avatar. The algorithm delivers to people who respond. Meta's system self-optimizes toward converters once the Pixel has data.

**Objectives in order of priority:**
1. Lead generation (primary — quiz form submissions)
2. Conversion (secondary — landing page traffic → form)
3. Video views (warmup — building Pixel data before lead gen campaigns launch)

### Secondary: Google Search

High-intent traffic. People searching "car accident lawyer," "do I have a case," "what to do after car accident." Google campaigns are managed separately from Instagram warmup. SEO is a long-term play.

### Tertiary: TikTok

Competitors are underrepresented on TikTok. Opportunity for UGC-style content, humor, educational content. Not a priority in the first phase — activate after Meta campaigns are stable.

## Funnel Architecture

```
AWARENESS
↓ Meta ad (video, static, carousel) — calls out MVA victims
↓ Instagram or Facebook feed / Stories / Reels

LANDING PAGE
↓ getafaircase.com — conversion-optimized, mobile-first (75% of traffic is mobile)
↓ Headline angles tested: emotional (victim framing), value-focused (settlement range),
  adversarial urgency (insurance company framing)

QUIZ
↓ 6-question qualification sequence
↓ Settlement range estimator shown as conversion device
↓ Contact step: name, phone, email

LEAD CAPTURE
↓ TCPA consent + TrustedForm certificate
↓ UTM parameters, session data, behavioral signals captured

PIPELINE
↓ Second Chair validation, scoring, compliance checks
↓ Lead graded A/B/C/D

DELIVERY
↓ Exclusive delivery to one law firm partner
```

## Audience

### Primary: Car Accident Victims

- **Age:** 25–65
- **Timeframe:** 30–90 days post-accident (sweet spot)
- **Mindset:** Stressed, overwhelmed, dealing with insurance, unsure of rights
- **Pain:** Insurance lowballing / ghosting them, medical bills piling up
- **Hook:** "Insurance companies have lawyers. Shouldn't you?"

### Secondary: Motorcycle Accident Victims

- **Age:** 25–55
- **Mindset:** Often more severely injured, feel bias works against them
- **Hook:** Rider rights, serious injury, other driver at fault

### Tertiary: Truck Accident Victims

- **Complexity:** Multiple liable parties (driver, company, manufacturer)
- **Hook:** Level the playing field against corporate lawyers

### Tertiary: Rideshare Accident Victims

- **Age:** 21–45 (heavy rideshare user demographic)
- **Hook:** "You don't know who's responsible. We do."

## Messaging Framework

| Situation | Primary Message | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Right after the accident | "Don't say anything to insurance yet" | Time is critical |
| Insurance contacted them | "They're not on your side" | Get a second opinion |
| Unsure if they have a case | "Most accident victims don't know they qualify" | Takes 2 minutes to find out |
| Worried about cost | "No upfront cost. Attorneys work on contingency." | You pay nothing unless you win |
| Already got an offer | "The first offer is almost never the best offer" | Know what your case is really worth |

## Phase Plan

### Phase 1 — Page Launch & Warmup (Weeks 1–2, now)

- Instagram page created, profile fully set up
- 14 posts published over 14 days (content calendar: `02_Social/CONTENT_CALENDAR.md`)
- Followers acquired to ~1,000–2,500 via paid service (strategy: `02_Social/INSTAGRAM_WARMUP_PLAN.md`)
- No ads running yet — page building trust signals

### Phase 2 — Ad Launch (Week 3+)

- Meta lead gen campaigns launch targeting Colorado initially
- Test 2–3 creative hooks simultaneously (emotional, value, adversarial)
- Monitor CPL and lead quality score distribution
- Pixel builds data on converters

### Phase 3 — Optimization (Month 2+)

- Kill underperforming creatives, double down on winners
- Expand geographic targeting (CA, NY, FL, TX)
- Introduce retargeting for landing page visitors who didn't complete quiz
- Introduce video view warmup campaigns before lead gen campaigns in new geos

### Phase 4 — Scale (Month 3+)

- TikTok campaigns launch in parallel
- Test UGC-style video ads
- Build lookalike audiences from converters (within Special Ad Category constraints)
- Consider YouTube pre-roll for high-intent search terms

## What This Is Not

- Fair Case does not publish organic content to drive traffic. Posts exist for trust, not reach.
- Fair Case does not build a community or run a newsletter.
- Fair Case does not pursue influencer partnerships (for now).
- Fair Case does not sell directly to consumers — the product is attorney representation via referral.
