# Fair Case — Instagram Warmup Content Calendar

**Purpose:** 14 posts over 14 days to make the page look credible before ads launch.  
**Goal:** Not organic reach. Visual density + brand legitimacy for ad-driven profile visitors.  
**Format:** Post one per day at a consistent time (7–9 PM local performs best for PI audience).  

---

## Messaging Framework — How These Posts Connect to the Paid Strategy

These 14 posts are not designed for organic reach — they are trust infrastructure for people who click through from a paid ad and land on the Fair Case profile. That visitor has already been self-selected by the ad. They are likely in one of the three timing tiers. What they see on the profile should reinforce the message that brought them there.

**Tier 1 (Days 0–30) posts — the undecided claimant:**  
Days 1, 2, 4, 6, 7, 8, 11, 14  
These speak to someone still in the window. The insurance company has called. Nothing has been signed. The message: information before action.

**Tier 2 (1–6 months) posts — the accumulating situation:**  
Days 3, 9, 10  
These speak to someone who's been sitting on a situation. Bills arriving. Uncertainty about whether it's too late. The message: some still have options.

**Universal / Trust-building:**  
Days 5, 12, 13  
These speak to any claimant at any stage — social proof, myth-busting, brand definition.

**Note on copy register:** Organic posts on a brand's own Instagram page operate under different rules than Meta paid ads. The "YOU" ban applies specifically to Meta ad targeting — the Personal Attributes restriction that prevents ads from implying the viewer has an injury or condition. Organic post copy on the brand's own page can use "you" more freely (the viewer is on Fair Case's page by choice; there's no ad targeting assertion). However, any copy that gets repurposed into a paid ad must be rewritten in third-person. See `COPY_VOICE_GUIDE.md` for the full rules.

## Format Guide

| Format | What It Is |
|---|---|
| **Static** | Single image — clean graphic, stat, quote, or visual |
| **Carousel** | 2–6 slides — for lists, steps, myth/fact, or educational content |
| **Reel** | Short video — 15–30 seconds, vertical. Can be a text-on-screen edit or talking-head style |

All content: design on-brand (consistent color palette, typography, Fair Case logo). No stock photos of courtrooms, gavels, or legal pads. Prefer clean, human, real.

## The 14-Day Calendar

---

### Day 1 — Brand Introduction
**Format:** Static  
**Concept:** Who Fair Case is. Clear value statement. First impression.  
**Headline:** *"You deserve to know what your case is worth."*  
**Caption direction:** Keep it short and direct. Introduce the brand. No legalese. End with: *"Follow for what every accident victim should know."*  
**Visual:** Clean branded graphic — Fair Case logo, headline text, simple background  
**Tone:** Confident, plain-spoken, on the claimant's side  

---

### Day 2 — Educational Carousel: 3 Mistakes After an Accident
**Format:** Carousel (4 slides)  
**Concept:** Immediately establish that Fair Case knows things the claimant doesn't.  
**Slide 1 (Cover):** *"3 mistakes accident victims make that cost them money"*  
**Slide 2:** Mistake 1 — *"Accepting the first settlement offer"* — Insurance companies start low. The first offer is rarely the best offer.  
**Slide 3:** Mistake 2 — *"Giving a recorded statement to insurance"* — You're not required to. It can be used against you.  
**Slide 4:** Mistake 3 — *"Waiting too long to act"* — Most states have a 2-3 year statute of limitations, but cases get weaker over time. Act while evidence is fresh.  
**Caption:** *"Mistake #1 is the most expensive one. Save this post before your next conversation with an insurance adjuster."*  
**Hashtags:** None, or 1–2 max. The goal is not discovery — it's credibility.  

---

### Day 3 — Stat Graphic: Settlement Averages
**Format:** Static  
**Concept:** Anchor the claimant on what cases are actually worth. Creates urgency to find out their number.  
**Headline:** *"The average car accident settlement is $20,000–$25,000. Serious injury cases can reach six figures."*  
**Subtext:** *"Most people have no idea. Their insurance company does."*  
**Caption:** *"What's your case worth? Most accident victims never find out — because they settle for less than they deserve. We help you know the number before you sign anything."*  
**Visual:** Clean data-forward graphic. Bold numbers, simple layout. No charts needed.  

---

### Day 4 — Know Your Rights Carousel: Insurance Lowball Tactics
**Format:** Carousel (5 slides)  
**Concept:** Position Fair Case as the claimant's advocate against a powerful adversary.  
**Slide 1:** *"Insurance companies have a playbook. Here's what's in it."*  
**Slide 2:** Tactic 1 — *"Quick low offer"* — Before you know your medical costs, they offer a settlement to close the case cheap.  
**Slide 3:** Tactic 2 — *"Delay and frustrate"* — The longer they wait, the more desperate you get.  
**Slide 4:** Tactic 3 — *"Minimizing your injury"* — They'll question whether you were really hurt that badly.  
**Slide 5:** *"They have lawyers. You should too."*  
**Caption:** *"You're not paranoid. This is how it works. Knowing their playbook is the first step to not falling for it."*  

---

### Day 5 — Testimonial-Style Quote Card
**Format:** Static  
**Concept:** Social proof — real or composite. A voice that sounds like the claimant.  
**Quote:** *"I didn't think I had a case. Turns out the insurance company's offer was less than a third of what I was owed."*  
**Attribution:** — *Sarah T., car accident victim, Colorado* (composite or real — keep state-specific if possible)  
**Caption:** *"The number the insurance company gives you is not the number you're entitled to. Find out the difference."*  
**Visual:** Clean quote card — name, location, subtle Fair Case branding. No stock photo needed.  
**⚠️ Compliance note:** At launch, Fair Case has no real claimant testimonials. Composite voices are acceptable on organic posts as long as they are not represented as real client testimonials. If used as composite, add a small-text clarifier: "Illustrative. Individual experiences vary." Do NOT use actor testimonials in paid ads, ever. When real testimonials exist (Phase 2), update this post with actual client quotes (with written consent).

### Day 13 — Myth vs. Fact: "The Accident Was Partly My Fault"

**Caption:** *"One of the most common reasons people don't pursue a case — and one of the most common misconceptions. You may have more options than you think."*

## Post-Launch Cadence (Week 3+)

Once ads launch, the content calendar shifts from daily to sustainable:

**Week 3+: 3 posts per week**

Rotate content types:
- 1 educational carousel per week (new topics: time limits by state, what to document after an accident, rideshare accident rights, etc.)
- 1 social proof or stat post per week
- 1 Reel per week (or a static if Reel production isn't ready)

**Stories:** 2–3x/week. Repurpose post content — add a poll, a swipe-up to bio link, or a countdown if a campaign is running.

**Chopping from winning ads:** When a paid creative wins, adapt the concept for organic:
- Winning ad hook → lead the carousel
- Winning video cut → post as a Reel
- Winning stat from ad copy → stat graphic post

One winning ad concept generates 3–5 organic posts. Minimal additional production.

## Content Compliance Notes

All posts must comply with Fair Case's disclosure library and Colorado state bar advertising rules.

**Organic post copy rules (brand's own Instagram page):**
- Do not promise specific outcomes or specific dollar amounts
- Do not use the word "guarantee" in any form
- Stats used (settlement averages, 3.5x multiplier, $33,700 gap) must be sourced — small-text source credit on the graphic is sufficient
- "No upfront cost" is accurate and permissible for contingency representation
- Testimonial-style content must be clearly individual experience, not a promise — at launch, use composite voices with "Individual experiences vary" clarifier
- "You" is acceptable on organic posts on the brand's own page (no Meta ad targeting assertion) — use naturally

**If repurposing organic copy into paid ads:**
- Rewrite all "you" + injury/condition language to third person first (see `COPY_VOICE_GUIDE.md`)
- Run all paid copy through the banned phrases checklist before publishing
- Add required ad disclosures to landing pages (see `DISCLOSURE_REQUIREMENTS.md`)
- Any settlement figures must carry: "Settlements in similar cases have ranged from... Past results do not guarantee future outcomes."

**Per-post compliance flags:**
- Day 3 (Settlement Averages): The headline figures ($20K–$25K average, six figures for serious injury) must be sourced. Add source note in small text on graphic.
- Day 5 (Testimonial): See compliance note on the post itself — composite voices only at launch.
- Day 9 (3.5x stat): Cite Insurance Research Council directly on the graphic.
- Day 13 (Colorado comparative negligence): The <50% fault threshold is correct for Colorado modified comparative negligence. Update this fact when expanding to new states.
