## The Core Tension

Fair Case B2C voice must be:
- **Human and direct** — not clinical, not corporate, not lawyerly
- **Compliant** — Meta's Special Ad Category rules make "you" radioactive in the wrong context
- **Specific** — vague copy doesn't self-select; specific copy finds the 1% who need it

These are not in conflict if the rules are understood. Specificity and compliance are complementary — both require being precise, not broad.

## The "YOU" Ban — Critical Compliance Rule

**THE GOLDEN RULE:** In paid Meta advertising, the word "you" paired with injury/condition language gets ads rejected or accounts restricted.

Meta Advertising Standards (Personal Attributes rule): Cannot assert or imply personal attributes.

| BANNED (Second Person + Condition) | APPROVED (Third Person) |
|---|---|
| "Are you injured?" | "Accident victims may be entitled to compensation" |
| "Were you in a crash?" | "Car accident cases are being accepted" |
| "Were you hurt at work?" | "Workplace injury claims are on the rise" |
| "You could get $$$" | "Settlements in similar cases have reached $X" |
| "You may be owed money" | "Compensation may be available for qualifying cases" |
| "Don't wait!" | "Statutes of limitations apply to all cases" |

**The logic:** Second person = "I know something about you" = Personal Attribute = Ban  
Third person = "This exists in the world" = Observation = Safe

**When "you" IS allowed:**  
"You" can appear in contexts that don't assert personal attributes about injury or condition:
- "You can call anytime" (process, not condition) ✓
- "Connect with an attorney who can help you" (neutral help) ✓
- "Get the information you need" (neutral) ✓

**Quick check before publishing any ad copy:**
1. "You" + injury/pain/condition → REJECT
2. "You" + deserve/owed/entitled → REJECT
3. "You" + were/are + [medical condition] → REJECT
4. "You" + neutral action (call, learn, connect) → OK

## The Empathy Structure (Third Person, Compliant)

Third-person copy can be deeply empathetic. The goal is to "enter the conversation already taking place in the claimant's mind" without triggering personal attribute flags.

**3-Part Empathy Structure:**

1. **Acknowledge their situation (third person)**  
   "Accident victims often feel overwhelmed by medical bills and insurance calls."

2. **Validate their feelings (third person)**  
   "It's natural to feel frustrated when someone else's mistake changed your life."

3. **Offer hope (third person)**  
   "Compensation may be available for those who qualify."

**Empathy compliance examples:**

| BANNED (Second Person) | APPROVED (Third Person + Empathy) |
|---|---|
| "Are you overwhelmed after your accident?" | "Accident victims often feel overwhelmed. They don't have to face it alone." |
| "You deserve better than this." | "Those injured through no fault of their own deserve answers." |
| "We know you're hurting." | "Recovery is hard. We help lighten the burden." |
| "Don't let them take advantage of you." | "Insurance companies often try to settle for less than cases are worth." |

**Entering the conversation — what claimants are thinking at each stage:**

| Stage | Their Mental Conversation | Third-Person Copy That Joins It |
|---|---|---|
| Shock | "What do I do now?" | "After an accident, the first 48 hours matter most. Here's what to know." |
| Anger | "This wasn't my fault." | "When someone else's negligence causes harm, there are legal options." |
| Overwhelm | "I can't handle one more thing." | "We handle the insurance companies. Accident victims focus on healing." |
| Decision | "Who can I trust?" | "One call. Ten minutes. Free. No commitment." |

## Approved Phrases (Fair Case B2C)

### Opening Lines (Third Person, Compliant)
- "Accident victims may be entitled to compensation"
- "Those injured in [type] accidents may have legal options"
- "Individuals who have experienced [situation] may qualify"
- "Car accident cases are being accepted"
- "Legal options may be available for those who qualify"

### Claims Language (Qualified)
- "Settlements in similar cases have ranged from $X to $Y depending on case specifics"
- "Compensation may be available for qualifying cases"
- "Past results do not guarantee future outcomes"
- "Each case is evaluated individually"
- "The average accident victim leaves $33,700 on the table by not consulting an attorney" (documented fact — use it)
- "68% of accident victims accept the first offer without consulting an attorney" (documented fact — use it)

### Process Language
- "Connect with an experienced attorney"
- "Speak with a legal professional at no upfront cost"
- "Find out if a case qualifies"
- "One call. Ten minutes. Free."
- "Get a free case evaluation"
- "No upfront cost to speak with an attorney"
- "Attorneys only get paid if you win"

### Urgency Language (Compliant)
- "Statutes of limitations apply to all cases"
- "Time limits may affect legal options"
- "In [state], you have [X] years from the date of your accident"
- "Evidence degrades over time — earlier review produces better outcomes"

## Banned Phrases (The Blacklist)

These will get ads rejected or accounts restricted. No exceptions.

| BANNED PHRASE | WHY |
|---|---|
| "Are you injured?" | Personal attribute |
| "Were you hurt?" | Personal attribute |
| "Were you in a crash?" | Personal attribute |
| "You deserve compensation" | Guarantee + personal attribute |
| "You could get $$$" | Promise / guarantee |
| "You may be owed money" | Personal attribute + guarantee |
| "Call now before it's too late" | False urgency — Meta flags this |
| "Don't wait!" | False urgency |
| "100% free" | Nothing is 100% free |
| "Guaranteed settlement" | No outcome can be guaranteed |
| "We'll get you paid" | Promise |
| "Get $100,000+" | Promise of specific outcome |
| "We fight for victims like you" | "Like you" = personal attribute |
| "Don't be a victim twice" | Exploitative |
| "In pain?" | Personal attribute |
| "We recommend the best attorneys" | Lead gen cannot "recommend" |
| "sue" / "lawsuit" / "litigation" | Identity barrier activation — use "case" / "options" |
| "make millions" | Guarantee |
| "you could be owed THOUSANDS" | Caps + guarantee |

**Pattern detection before publishing:**
1. "You" + injury/pain/condition → REJECT
2. "You" + deserve/owed/entitled → REJECT
3. Any specific dollar guarantee → REJECT (unless "similar cases have ranged from...")
4. Urgency without a legal basis → REJECT
5. "Recommend" or "best" → REJECT
6. Health condition questions → REJECT

## 12 Behavioral Triggers (Reference Summary)

Full detail: `13_Brand_Strategy/03_Voice/Behavioral_Copy_Triggers.md`

| # | Trigger | Fair Case Application |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Loss Aversion** | "$33,700 left on the table." Frame what they're losing, not what they'd gain |
| 2 | **Scarcity / Urgency** | Real deadlines only — SOL, evidence degradation. Never manufactured |
| 3 | **Social Proof** | "68% of accident victims accept the first offer. Find out if you're in the other 32%." |
| 4 | **Authority** | "The insurance company's adjuster is trained for this. You deserve information too." |
| 5 | **Cognitive Ease** | Max 15 words per hook sentence. Active voice. One idea per sentence. |
| 6 | **Curiosity Gap** | "There's a reason the adjuster calls you within hours. And it's not to help you." |
| 7 | **Anchoring** | "First offer: $2,500. Similar cases: $47,300–$85,000. What's the gap worth knowing?" |
| 8 | **Commitment** | Progressive quiz: micro-agreements build investment. Never ask for big commitments first. |
| 9 | **Emotion / Affect** | Match emotional state by tier. Tier 1: clarifying. Tier 2: validating. Tier 3: informing. |
| 10 | **Identity** | "People who get a fair case don't accept the first offer without knowing what they have." |
| 11 | **Trust** | "Free. No obligation. No commitment. One call." Safety signals reduce perceived risk. |
| 12 | **Time / Delay** | Frame benefits as happening now. "Find out in 60 seconds" beats "eventually get compensated." |

## Quiz Funnel Copy

### Question Sequence (Commitment Ladder)

- Q1: "Was the accident in the last [X] years?" (micro-agreement)
- Q2: "What type of accident?" (specificity builds investment)
- Q3: "Have you already spoken with insurance?" (situational)
- Q4: "What's your zip code?" (localization)

### Loading Screen (Anticipation)

- "Reviewing your case details..."
- "Checking applicable deadlines for your state..."
- "Confirming legal options in your area..."

### Result Screen + Lead Capture (Earned Access)

- "Based on what you've shared — here's what's next."
- "Legal options may be available in your situation. Last step: confirm your details."
- "You're almost there. Where should we send your results?"

## Tone Principles (B2C Voice Summary)

1. **One specific fact. One minimum-barrier action.** Not a sales pitch. Not a story.
2. **Never manufacture urgency.** Real deadlines only, stated precisely.
3. **Write to agency, not vulnerability.** "Know what you have before you decide" — not "don't let them take advantage."
4. **Tier-specific always.** Tier 1 copy is wrong for Tier 2.
5. **Short sentences.** Max 15 words in hooks. Active voice.
6. **Specificity is targeting.** The more specific and true, the stronger the self-selection.
