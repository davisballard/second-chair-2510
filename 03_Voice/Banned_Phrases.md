# Banned Phrases (The Blacklist)

**Last Updated:** March 2026
**Sources:** Platform compliance (Saul.md Section 8) + Brand Architecture §6 (Brand Voice)

> Two categories of banned phrases:
> 1. **Platform compliance bans** — will get ads rejected or accounts restricted
> 2. **Brand voice bans** — violate Second Chair's brand character and non-negotiables

---

## Instant Rejection Phrases

| ❌ BANNED PHRASE | WHY | ✅ USE INSTEAD |
|------------------|-----|----------------|
| "Are you injured?" | Personal attribute — implies you know something about them | "Accident victims may be entitled to compensation" |
| "Were you hurt?" | Personal attribute | "Those injured in accidents may have legal options" |
| "Were you in a crash?" | Personal attribute | "Car accident cases are being accepted" |
| "You deserve compensation" | Guarantee + personal attribute (double whammy) | "Compensation may be available for qualifying cases" |
| "You could get $$$" | Promise/guarantee | "Settlements in similar cases have reached $X" |
| "You may be owed money" | Personal attribute + guarantee | "Compensation may be available for qualifying cases" |
| "Hurt at work?" | Personal attribute | "Workplace injury claims are on the rise" |
| "Call now before it's too late" | False urgency — Meta thinks you're scamming | "Statutes of limitations apply to all cases" |
| "Don't wait!" | False urgency | "Time limits may affect legal options" |
| "100% free" | Nothing is 100% free — they know it | "No upfront cost to speak with an attorney" |
| "Exposed to [toxic substance]?" | Personal health attribute | "Individuals exposed to [substance] may have legal options" |
| "Were you diagnosed with...?" | Personal health attribute | "Those diagnosed with [condition] may have legal options" |

## Guarantee Language (Banned)

| ❌ BANNED | WHY |
|-----------|-----|
| "Get $100,000+" | Promise of specific outcome |
| "Guaranteed settlement" | No outcome can be guaranteed |
| "You WILL receive..." | Promise |
| "We'll get you paid" | Promise |
| "Maximum compensation guaranteed" | Double guarantee |

> **Saul says:** "'Get $100,000+' is a promise. Promises get you banned AND sued. 'Settlements in similar cases have ranged from $X to $Y depending on case specifics.' That's a fact. Facts are bulletproof."

---

## Urgency Language (Banned or Risky)

| ❌ BANNED/RISKY | ✅ COMPLIANT ALTERNATIVE |
|-----------------|--------------------------|
| "Call NOW!" | "Connect with an attorney" |
| "Don't wait!" | "Statutes of limitations apply" |
| "Before it's too late!" | "Time limits may affect legal options" |
| "Limited time!" | "Legal deadlines vary by state" |
| "Act fast!" | "Learn about your legal options" |
| "Last chance!" | [Remove — no compliant equivalent] |

---

## Exploitative Language (Banned)

| ❌ BANNED | WHY |
|-----------|-----|
| "We know you're suffering" | Exploitative + personal attribute |
| "In pain?" | Personal attribute |
| "Struggling after your accident?" | Personal attribute |
| "We fight for victims like you" | "Like you" = personal attribute |
| "Don't be a victim twice" | Exploitative |

---

## Recommendation Language (Banned for Lead Gen)

| ❌ BANNED | WHY |
|-----------|-----|
| "We recommend the best attorneys" | Lead gen cannot "recommend" — that's referral service territory |
| "The best lawyers for your case" | Same issue |
| "We'll match you with the perfect firm" | "Perfect" = guarantee |

**We are a lead aggregator, not a referral service.** We connect, we don't recommend.

✅ Use instead: "Connect with experienced attorneys"

---

## Pattern Recognition

Before approving copy, check for these patterns:

1. **"You" + injury/pain/condition** → REJECT
2. **"You" + deserve/owed/entitled** → REJECT
3. **Any specific dollar guarantee** → REJECT (unless qualified with "similar cases have...")
4. **Urgency without legal basis** → REJECT
5. **"Recommend" or "best"** → REJECT
6. **Health condition questions** → REJECT

---

## Saul's Final Word

> **Saul says:** "See the pattern? Remove 'you.' Add qualifiers. Turn accusations into observations. It's lawyer-speak, yes. But it's lawyer-speak that keeps the lights on."

## Brand Voice Banned Phrases

> **Source:** Brand Architecture §6 — Tone of Voice. These are not platform compliance issues — they are brand character violations. Using these phrases makes Second Chair sound like every other vendor in the category.

### B2B — Never Say These to Attorneys

| ❌ BANNED PHRASE | WHY |
|---|---|
| "We're committed to..." | Values performance — shows nothing |
| "We're dedicated to..." | Same empty commitment language |
| "We're passionate about..." | Performative enthusiasm |
| "Best-in-class" | Category noise — every vendor says it |
| "Industry-leading" | Unverifiable, generic |
| "Cutting-edge" | Tech startup jargon — wrong register |
| "Innovative" | Overused, means nothing |
| "Solution" (standalone noun) | Corporate jargon |
| "Ecosystem" | Meaningless abstraction |
| "Synergy" | The word that kills credibility |
| "Leverage" (as a verb) | Consultant-speak |
| "Game-changing" | Hyperbole |
| "Revolutionary" | Nobody believes it |
| "Transformative" | Empty promise |
| "We get results" | Without immediately specifying what results, for whom, and how |
| "Would you like to see how we can help?" | Questions position SC as seeking permission — state, don't ask |
| "The best" | Superlative without proof |
| "Guaranteed results" | Violates non-negotiable #8 |
| "Unlimited quality" | Contradicts honest-in-the-specific character |
| "Act now — limited markets available!" | Scarcity mechanics violate Respectful of Intelligence trait |
| "The Creative Advantage" | Retired positioning phrase (pre-strategy V1) |
| "Better Creative. Better Cases." | Retired tagline (pre-strategy V1) |
| "Geo-exclusive demand infrastructure" | Retired language (pre-strategy V1) |
| "We send cases, not contacts" | Retired contrast line (pre-strategy V1) |

### B2C — Never Say These to Claimants

| ❌ BANNED PHRASE | WHY |
|---|---|
| "You could be owed THOUSANDS" | Caps + implied amount = lowest form of PI advertising |
| "Don't let them get away with it" | Antagonism without substance |
| "You may have a case!" | May + exclamation point = manipulation |
| "Call now before it's too late!" | Manufactured urgency (unless genuine SOL deadline stated specifically) |
| "We'll fight for you!" | Promise the attorney makes, not the advertising |
| Any unsubstantiated dollar amount | Can't guarantee for specific situations |
| "Claim Justice Now" | Retired consumer brand CTA (pre-strategy V1). Consumer brand is now **Fair Case**. |
| "Claim Now" / "ClaimNow" | Retired CTA variant |
| Fear-based hooks | Violates non-negotiable #7 — never uses fear, artificial urgency, or manufactured scarcity |
| "You deserve..." | Positions advertising as validator of the claimant's worth — patronizing |
| "We know what you're going through" | Presumptuous — the advertising doesn't know their specific situation |
| Crash footage / injury imagery | Exploits vulnerability for attention |

### General — Never Use in Any Context

| ❌ BANNED | WHY |
|---|---|
| "92% acceptance rate" | Fabricated pre-launch statistic — violates non-negotiable #8 |
| "47 territories locked" | Fabricated pre-launch statistic |
| "$400/lead" | Old pricing model — not part of locked strategy |
| "$1,500+" | Old pricing model |
| "$1,600 per signed case" | Fabricated pre-launch statistic |
| "Fight for justice at scale" | Retired mission statement |
| "Protector" / "Protector/Guide" | Retired brand voice archetype. Use: Expert not Arrogant, Honest in the Specific, Respectful of Intelligence, Weighted not Heavy |
