# Second Chair — Crypto / Web3 Website Inspiration
*Tech-forward, innovation-signaling sites to reference for the “high tech” layer*

**Purpose:** Curated list of crypto, Web3, and fintech company websites with strong, institutional-grade design. Use for aesthetic inspiration when making Second Chair feel tech-forward and innovation-aligned without abandoning the prestige legal foundation.

**Synthesis rule:** Borrow **structure, clarity, and confidence** — not neon, not “crypto bro” maximalism. The best of these sites look like serious financial/infrastructure companies that happen to be in crypto. That’s the register Second Chair wants for its tech layer.

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES (from this set)

1. **Trust first.** Palette and tone read as serious finance/infrastructure. No neon, no “wagmi” in hero.
2. **One idea per section.** Hero = one value prop. Sections = one use case or audience per block.
3. **Typography carries authority.** Large, clear type; hierarchy through scale and weight, not only color.
4. **White space as structure.** These sites feel uncluttered. Second Chair’s cream + restraint already aligns; add tech-forward **clarity** and **modular layout** from these.
5. **Navigation that makes sense.** Products, Solutions, Resources, Company — or similar. No mystery meat nav.
6. **Data/analytics presence.** Where they show charts or metrics, they’re simple and readable. Ties into your Exhibit convention and data viz standards.

---

## WHAT NOT TO BORROW

- **Neon gradients and “crypto purple”** — undermines trust; conflicts with Second Chair’s warm, prestige palette.
- **Token logos and price tickers** as hero content — not relevant to B2B PI lead gen.
- **Meme tone or slang** — “based,” “degens,” etc. Keep voice professional.
- **Overly dense, “terminal” aesthetics** — you already have a more refined intelligence layer (IBM, McKinsey, Tufte). Don’t replace it with a fake dev-console look.
