## THE PSYCHOLOGY BEHIND THE ORDER

| Principle | Source | Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Foot-in-the-Door** | Cialdini, Freedman & Fraser | Start with easiest question (everyone says yes) |
| **Sunk Cost Fallacy** | Kahneman & Tversky | Each answer increases commitment to finish |
| **Sensitivity Gradient** | Luke Wroblewski | Low → High sensitivity questions in order |
| **Disqualifier Last** | Lead Gen Playbook | Maximum investment before potential rejection |
| **Loss Aversion** | Prospect Theory | Loading screen creates fear of losing the "result" |
| **Value Exchange Frame** | Form Design Mastery | Contact info framed as receiving, not giving |
| **Goal Gradient Effect** | Hull, 1932 | People accelerate as they approach the goal |

## COMPARISON: CURRENT VS. OPTIMAL

### Current California Flow (5 Questions)
1. Injury (Yes/No) — Direct qualifier
2. Timing (4 options)
3. Fault (3 options)
4. Medical care (3 options)
5. Attorney (3 options)

### Current Nevada Flow (6 Questions)
1. Engagement ("What are you hoping to figure out?") — Soft entry
2. Injury (Yes/No)
3. Timing (4 options)
4. Fault (3 options)
5. Medical care (3 options)
6. Attorney (3 options)

### Optimal Flow (6 Questions)
1. **Easy Win** — Soft entry + qualification combined
2. **Recency** — Low sensitivity, filters SOL
3. **Injury** — Medium sensitivity, validates pain
4. **Medical Care** — Case strength indicator
5. **Fault** — Higher sensitivity, asked after investment
6. **Attorney** — Disqualifier at maximum sunk cost

## THE OPTIMAL 6-QUESTION FLOW

---

### QUESTION 1: The Easy Win (Engagement + Soft Qualifier)

**Primary Option:**

> **"Were you recently in a car accident in [State]?"**
> 
> - ✅ Yes, within the last 2 years
> - ❌ No

**Why This Works:**
- Everyone in your target audience clicks "Yes"
- Combines engagement (easy entry) with qualification (recency)
- No wrong answer anxiety — just "yes" or "no"
- State mention creates local relevance (Cocktail Party Effect)
- Binary choice = fastest possible completion

**Alternative Option (softer, more empathetic):**

> **"Are you trying to figure out your next steps after a car accident?"**
> 
> - ✅ Yes, I need guidance
> - ❌ No, I'm all set

**When to Use Alternative:**
- If testing shows high bounce on primary option
- For warmer audiences (retargeting)
- For states with softer tone preferences

**Progress Bar:** 1 of 6 filled

**Micro-copy after selection:** None needed — auto-advance

---

### QUESTION 2: The Recency Check (Low Sensitivity)

> **"When did your accident happen?"**
> 
> - Within the last 30 days
> - Within the last 3 months
> - Within the last year
> - Over a year ago

**Why This Position:**
- Time-based question = low emotional load
- Doesn't ask about fault or injuries yet (saves sensitive questions for later)
- Filters out SOL-expired cases early
- Easy to answer (they know approximately when it happened)

**Progress Bar:** 2 of 6 filled

**Micro-copy after selection:** "Great — keep going!"

**Conditional Logic:**
- "Over a year ago" → Consider soft warning about statute of limitations, but continue flow

---

### QUESTION 3: The Injury Check (Medium Sensitivity)

> **"Have you experienced any pain or discomfort since the accident?"**
> 
> - ✅ Yes, I've had pain or symptoms
> - ⏳ Not yet, but I'm concerned
> - ❌ No, I feel fine

**Why This Position:**
- Personal but expected question (they know you need to ask)
- 3 options captures the "worried but not sure" segment
- Validates their pain without requiring specific details yet
- Follows recency naturally (when → what happened)

**Progress Bar:** 3 of 6 filled (halfway celebration)

**Micro-copy after "Yes":** "We're sorry to hear that. Let's see how we can help."

**Micro-copy after "Not yet, but I'm concerned":** "That's valid — symptoms often appear days or weeks later."

**Conditional Logic:**
- "No, I feel fine" → Soft disqualification or property-damage-only path

---

### QUESTION 4: The Medical Care Check (Medium-High Sensitivity)

> **"Have you seen a doctor since the accident?"**
> 
> - ✅ Yes, I've received medical care
> - ⏳ Not yet, but I'm planning to
> - ❌ No, I haven't

**Why This Position:**
- Follows naturally from injury question (pain → treatment)
- Indicates case strength for intake team
- "Planning to" option keeps undecided leads in funnel
- Sets up expectation that medical care matters

**Progress Bar:** 4 of 6 filled

**Micro-copy after "Yes":** "Good — medical records strengthen your case."

**Conditional Content (if "No, I haven't"):**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                              │
│  ⚠️ Quick note: Medical records strengthen claims           │
│                                                              │
│  Accident injuries often appear days or weeks later.        │
│  Seeing a doctor protects both your health AND your case.   │
│                                                              │
│  [I'll consider it — Continue →]                            │
│  [I understand — Continue anyway]                           │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### QUESTION 5: The Fault Check (Higher Sensitivity — With Reassurance)

> **"Who was at fault in the accident?"**
> 
> - The other driver was at fault
> - I may have been partly at fault
> - I'm not sure yet

**Why This Position:**
- Most sensitive qualifying question = asked after significant investment
- 4 questions of sunk cost before this
- "Partly at fault" wording acknowledges reality without hard disqualification
- "I'm not sure" captures uncertain leads who may still qualify

**Progress Bar:** 5 of 6 filled — "Almost there!"

**Conditional Content (if "I'm not sure" or "Partly at fault"):**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                              │
│  🤔 Not sure who was at fault? That's completely normal.    │
│                                                              │
│  Many accidents involve shared circumstances — and          │
│  [State]'s "comparative negligence" law means you may       │
│  still qualify for compensation.                            │
│                                                              │
│  ✓ Police reports often determine fault differently         │
│    than you'd expect                                        │
│                                                              │
│  ✓ Insurance companies WANT you to doubt yourself           │
│                                                              │
│  ✓ Even partial fault can still mean money owed to you      │
│                                                              │
│  [Continue →]                                                │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why This Reassurance Matters (from Audience Psychology Insights):**
> "You're wondering if it was your fault. They're counting on that doubt."

---

### QUESTION 6: The Attorney Disqualifier (Maximum Sunk Cost)

> **"Do you currently have an attorney for this accident?"**
> 
> - ❌ No, I don't have one yet
> - 🔍 No, but I'm looking for one
> - ✅ Yes, I already have an attorney

**Why LAST:**
- 5 questions of investment before asking this disqualifier
- If they've come this far, they're highly committed
- "Looking for one" is your highest-intent segment — flag these leads
- Placing disqualifiers early = wasted sunk cost psychology

**Progress Bar:** 6 of 6 filled — "Last question!"

**Micro-copy after "No, I don't have one yet":** "Perfect — we can help."

**Micro-copy after "Looking for one":** "You're in the right place."

**Conditional Content (if "Yes, I already have"):**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                              │
│  👍 Great — you're already represented                      │
│                                                              │
│  Since you have an attorney, we can't assist with          │
│  this specific case.                                        │
│                                                              │
│  But if you ever need a second opinion, or have             │
│  questions about the process, we're here.                   │
│                                                              │
│  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐   │
│  │ 📧 Want our free accident recovery guide?           │   │
│  │    [your@email.com] [Send It →]                     │   │
│  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘   │
│                                                              │
│  [No thanks — exit]                                         │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why Soft Exit:**
- Captures email for future nurture
- Respectful exit builds brand goodwill
- Reduces junk leads in intake queue
- They may refer others or return for future cases

## THE LOADING SCREEN (Critical Psychological Moment)

**Duration:** 4-6 seconds (enough to build anticipation, not enough to abandon)

**Optimal Design:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                              │
│     ⚖️ Analyzing Your [State] Case...                       │
│                                                              │
│     ████████████████████████████░░░░░░░░  78%               │
│                                                              │
│     ✓ Reviewing your accident details                       │
│     ✓ Checking [State] statute of limitations               │
│     ✓ Comparing to similar settlements                      │
│     → Estimating your potential compensation...             │
│                                                              │
│   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐  │
│   │ 💰 Similar [State] cases have recovered             │  │
│   │    an average of $47,500                            │  │
│   └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘  │
│                                                              │
│         "This only takes a few more seconds..."             │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Elements:**

| Element | Purpose | Psychology |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Progress bar (animated)** | Shows tangible progress | Goal Gradient Effect |
| **Checkmarks appearing** | Creates completion satisfaction | Achievement reward |
| **State name** | Reinforces local relevance | Cocktail Party Effect |
| **Social proof stat** | Anchors expectation, builds desire | Anchoring + Social Proof |
| **"Few more seconds"** | Reduces abandonment anxiety | Expectation setting |

**Animation Sequence:**
1. 0-1s: "Reviewing your accident details" → ✓
2. 1-2s: "Checking [State] statute of limitations" → ✓
3. 2-4s: "Comparing to similar settlements" → ✓
4. 4-5s: "Estimating your potential compensation" → ✓
5. 5-6s: Transition to lead capture form

**Optimal Design:**

```
│                                                              │
│  Where should we send your free case estimate?              │
│                                                              │
│  Full Name                                                   │
│  [_______________________________________________]          │
│                                                              │
│  Phone Number                                                │
│  [_______________________________________________]          │
│  📞 A specialist will call once to discuss your case        │
│                                                              │
│  Email Address                                               │
│  [_______________________________________________]          │
│  📧 We'll send your case details here                       │
│                                                              │
│  ☐ I agree to be contacted about my case                    │
│     🔒 100% private. Never shared.                          │
│                                                              │
│  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐│
│  │          GET MY FREE ESTIMATE →                          ││
│  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘│
│                                                              │
│  ⚖️ [Law Firm Name] | ★★★★★ Trusted by 10,000+ clients     │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Key Elements:**

| Element | Current | Optimal | Why |
|---------|---------|---------|-----|
| **Fields** | 4 (First, Last, Email, Phone) | 3 (Full Name, Phone, Email) | Less friction |
| **Field order** | Name → Email → Phone | Name → Phone → Email | Phone is primary contact method |
| **Micro-copy** | None | Under each field | Explains why you need it, reduces anxiety |
| **Consent** | Long legalese | Short + trust signal | Less intimidating |
| **CTA** | "Show My Claim Estimate" | "GET MY FREE ESTIMATE →" | Action-oriented, specific |
| **Trust signals** | Footer only | Adjacent to CTA | Address anxiety at peak moment |

**Mobile-Specific Requirements:**

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Field height** | Minimum 48px |
| **Font size** | Minimum 16px (prevents iOS zoom) |
| **Phone keyboard** | `type="tel"` |
| **Email keyboard** | `type="email"` |
| **Autocomplete** | `autocomplete="name"`, `email`, `tel` |
| **CTA position** | Sticky at bottom of viewport |

## WHAT CHANGED FROM CURRENT FLOWS

| Element | Current (CA/NV) | Optimal | Expected Impact |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-----------------|
| **Q1** | Direct injury question | Softer "were you in an accident" | +5-10% step 1 completion |
| **Question order** | Fault in middle | Fault near end | +3-5% on fault step |
| **"Not sure" handling** | No reassurance | Conditional content | +5-10% on that step |
| **"Has attorney" handling** | Proceeds to form | Soft exit with email capture | Reduces junk leads |
| **Loading screen** | Basic text | Progress bar + social proof | +3-5% to form start |
| **Form fields** | 4 fields | 3 fields + micro-copy | +15-25% form completion |
| **Trust signals** | Footer only | Header + near CTA | -15% bounce rate |
| **Progress micro-copy** | None | Encouraging phrases | +3-5% overall completion |
