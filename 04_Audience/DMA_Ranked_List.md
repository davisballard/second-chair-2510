## Tier 1: Premium Markets (MOI 7.0+)

**3 DMAs | ~39M population | Avg price: $435 | Year 1 launch markets**

These are the only DMAs where media costs, legal complexity, and market size combine to justify $400+ lead pricing. All three have existing team relationships (Sasha in NYC, Davis/Alex in LA). Miami is the stretch — no local team, but highest PI volume per capita in the US.

- **NYC** — Strictest no-fault screening in the US (NY Insurance Law §5102(d)), but screened leads are case-ready. $460 is capped; MOI could justify higher. Cross-state compliance (NY/NJ/CT).
- **LA** — Largest PI DMA nationally. SB37 tone restrictions in CA. 85K–100K annual PI filings. CPC $250–$500+.
- **Miami** — FL no-fault + HB 837 (2023) changed the landscape. Highest uninsured rate (20.6%) among top DMAs. 70%+ Hispanic market. Bilingual capability is table stakes.

## Tier 2: Core Markets (MOI 5.0–6.9)

**27 DMAs | ~105M population | Avg price: $345 | Year 1 — active targeting alongside Tier 1**

Tier 2 is not an expansion play — it's Year 1 alongside Tier 1. Three DMAs alone can't generate enough revenue to sustain the business. Tier 1 + Tier 2 combined = 30 DMAs covering ~144M people at a weighted avg ~$395/lead. That's the Year 1 addressable market.

**No-fault premium DMAs in this tier:**
- **Detroit** (6.8) — MI's strict no-fault system (serious impairment threshold) pushes this above most Tier 2 markets despite declining population.
- **West Palm Beach** (6.3), **Pensacola** (5.2), **Lakeland** (5.1) — FL no-fault gives even mid-size FL DMAs Tier 2 pricing power.
- **Grand Rapids** (5.2) — MI no-fault makes this small market price like a much larger one.
- **Buffalo** (5.5), **Rochester** (5.1) — NY no-fault screening costs are baked in.

**Large metro DMAs:**
- **Chicago** (6.9) — Cook County "nuclear verdicts." 3rd largest US market.
- **Dallas/Houston** (6.2/6.3) — Best margin state (TX). No no-fault, moderate CPC, high volume. Trucking corridors.
- **Washington DC** (6.2) — Large DMA but contributory negligence in MD/VA/DC limits viability. High case values offset.
- **Atlanta** (6.1) — Alex DuBelko's relationships. Anti-running legislation favors compliant lead gen.
- **Minneapolis** (5.6) — MN no-fault ($4K threshold) + large market.
- **Nashville** (5.3) — Growing market. 1-year SOL (shortest in US) adds urgency.
- **Las Vegas** (5.2) — Extreme tourism/transient X-factor drives up waste. Strategic hold per team decision.

## Tier 3: Growth Markets (MOI 4.0–4.9)

**41 DMAs | ~85M population | Avg price: $300 | Year 2–3 expansion**

Wide geographic coverage. Mix of large metros with unfavorable legal systems (contributory negligence states), mid-size markets in favorable states, and smaller markets with no-fault premiums.

**Notable dynamics:**
- **Charlotte/Raleigh-Durham** (4.8/4.6) — Large NC metros score lower than their size suggests because NC contributory negligence (plaintiff at ANY fault = zero recovery) kills lead viability.
- **Phoenix** (4.9) — AZ pure comparative negligence is favorable, but ABS law (PE-owned law firms) creates different competitive dynamics.
- **Denver** (4.9) — Existing SC operations in CO. Modified 50% bar.
- **Honolulu** (4.9) — HI no-fault system boosts small market. Tourism/military X-factors.
- **FL micro-DMAs** — Gainesville (4.3) and Tallahassee (4.6) punch above their size due to FL no-fault.
- **MI micro-DMAs** — Flint (4.9) and Lansing (4.7) same story with MI no-fault.
- **PA DMAs** — Harrisburg (4.8) and Wilkes-Barre (4.3) benefit from PA choice no-fault system.
- **Birmingham** (4.5) — AL contributory negligence + high uninsured (18%) is a tough combination.
- **New Orleans** (4.3) — LA pure comparative is favorable but 1-year SOL (shortest nationally tied with TN) compresses the lead window.
- **St. Louis/Kansas City** (4.3/4.1) — MO pure comparative negligence is favorable. Cross-state DMAs (MO/IL and MO/KS).

## Tier 4: Opportunistic Markets (MOI 2.9–3.9)

**50 DMAs | ~55M population | Avg price: $265 | Year 3+ fill**

Long-tail markets. Lower media costs and competition mean decent margins even at $245–$280 lead prices. Most are viable but low-volume — expect 15–40 leads/month per DMA. Best pursued as bundle deals (multi-DMA clients in the same state) or when a firm reaches out proactively.

**State clusters worth noting:**
- **TX small markets** (McAllen, El Paso, Waco, Beaumont, Corpus Christi, Tyler, Lubbock) — 7 DMAs. TX at-fault system + moderate uninsured. Good candidates for TX statewide bundle deals.
- **OH mid-markets** (Dayton, Toledo, Youngstown) — 3 DMAs. Modified 51% bar. Bundle with Cleveland/Columbus/Cincinnati from Tier 3.
- **NC/VA/SC contributory/modified markets** — Multiple DMAs dragged down by unfavorable legal systems despite reasonable populations.
- **CA Central Valley** (Fresno, Bakersfield, Santa Barbara) — Lower competition than coastal CA but same favorable pure comparative system. High uninsured rate (20.4%).

## Below the Line: Excluded DMAs

**84 DMAs excluded.** These markets collectively represent ~12% of the US population (~40M people).

### Why They're Excluded

At sub-250K DMA population:
1. **Search volume is too thin.** Fewer than 50–100 monthly searches for PI keywords. Paid campaigns can't exit the learning phase on Meta or Google.
2. **Lead volume can't sustain a client.** Even at aggressive conversion rates, a 150K-population DMA generates maybe 5–8 leads/month. A firm paying $250/lead for 6 leads ($1,500/mo) won't see enough value to stay.
3. **B2B buyer pool is too small.** Under 10 PI firms in the market. One rejected pitch and you're done.
4. **Operational overhead isn't worth it.** Each DMA requires campaign setup, creative, landing pages, compliance review. Marginal ROI at <$2K/month revenue.

### Excluded by Category

**States with ALL DMAs excluded (every DMA sub-250K):**
Wyoming (1 DMA), Montana (3 DMAs), South Dakota (2 DMAs), North Dakota (1 of 2 — Fargo on watchlist), Alaska (1 DMA)

**Micro-DMAs in otherwise-covered states (examples):**
- CA: Chico–Redding, Eureka, Palm Springs (covered by Riverside DMA), Yuma
- TX: Amarillo, Odessa–Midland, Abilene, San Angelo, Laredo
- FL: Panama City (watchlist), Ft. Myers–Naples overlap (covered by Cape Coral DMA)
- NY: Binghamton, Elmira, Utica, Watertown
- OH: Lima, Zanesville, Wheeling (WV overlap)
- IL: Quincy, Harrisburg (IL)
- GA: Albany GA, Valdosta
- PA: Johnstown–Altoona
- NC: Wilmington
- VA: Charlottesville, Harrisonburg
- TN: Jackson TN

**US Territories excluded:**
San Juan PR (different legal system), Guam

### Revenue Impact of Exclusion

If Second Chair captured 100% of these 84 excluded DMAs at floor pricing ($175–$210/lead, 10 leads/month each): ~$150K–$175K/month. That's less than a single Tier 1 DMA client generates. The juice isn't worth the squeeze.

## Methodology Note

All MOI scores use the 6-dimension weighted framework documented in `DMA_Baseline_Pricing_Framework.md`:

| Dimension | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Competition & Media Cost | 25% |
| Legal Viability Rate | 25% |
| Market Size | 15% |
| Case Value Competition | 15% |
| Uninsured Motorist Rate | 10% |
| Market Quality X-Factors | 10% |

**Price formula:** `Price = $175 + ($36.11 × (MOI − 1.0))`, rounded to nearest $5.
**Price floor:** $175/lead. **Price ceiling:** $500/lead ($525 NYC only).
**Margin target:** 55%+ gross margin at all price points.

**Scoring basis:**
- **34 DMAs** (marked with \*) have established scores with full sub-dimension breakdowns in the Baseline Pricing Framework. These scores are canonical.
- **87 DMAs** are estimated using the same rubrics, applying state-level legal data (fault system, uninsured rates, SOL) uniformly within each state, and scaling competition/media cost from regional CPC benchmarks (Northeast $468, West $402, Southeast $389, South $382, Midwest $314). These scores should be refined with market-specific data as Second Chair enters each DMA.

**Data sources:** Nielsen DMA rankings (2024–2025), IRC uninsured motorist data (2023), NHTSA CrashStats (2023), Google Ads CPC benchmarks (SpyFu/SEMrush 2024–2025), state statutes (current as of March 2026). Full source list in `DMA_Baseline_Pricing_Framework.md`.
