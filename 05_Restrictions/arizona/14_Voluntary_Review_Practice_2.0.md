# AZ Bar Practice 2.0 Voluntary Review

> **Saul:** "Practice 2.0 is the AZ Bar's confidential, free advisory department. They'll review your ad creative before publication. It's not a safe harbor — but it's strong mitigating evidence if a complaint ever lands. For a high-volume operation like SC/WCTL, proactive submission is cheap insurance."

---

## 1. WHAT PRACTICE 2.0 IS

**Practice 2.0** is the State Bar of Arizona's confidential, free practice-management and advisory department, available as a benefit of bar membership. It is the successor to / consolidation of what was historically the Ethics Department's advisory function (the State Bar reorganized advisory ethics work into Practice 2.0 in the past few years, fully absorbing the Ethics Hotline function).

## 4. PRACTICE 2.0 vs. LAWYER REGULATION OFFICE (LRO)

These are **separate departments by design**:

| | Practice 2.0 | Lawyer Regulation Office (LRO) |
|---|---|---|
| Function | Advisory, confidential, prospective | Enforcement, public-facing once formal complaint issues, retrospective |
| Output | Guidance, suggestions | Investigations, sanctions |
| Records | No file opened, no record kept that can be used against you | Records confidential through screening; public after formal complaint |
| Posture | Help you stay compliant | Adjudicate alleged misconduct |

**Critical:** Calling Practice 2.0 does NOT trigger an LRO investigation. They're administratively separate.

---

## 5. SAFE HARBOR? — NOT EXACTLY

**Practice 2.0 review does NOT create a formal safe harbor.** It provides **meaningful evidentiary value**:

- A documented good-faith pre-publication review by Bar staff is **strong evidence of non-willful intent**
- Reasonable reliance on Bar-counsel advice is **mitigating in a disciplinary proceeding**
- ABA *Standards for Imposing Lawyer Sanctions* and ER 8.3 (the AZ analogue) treat good-faith reliance as a sanctions-phase mitigating factor

It is **advisory comfort, not legal immunity.**

For a high-volume lead-gen operation, **proactive submission is cheap insurance.** Specifically worth submitting:

## 8. RECENT CHANGES (PRACTICE 2.0 RESTRUCTURING)

The State Bar reorganized advisory/ethics functions in recent years:
- The Ethics Department's traditional opinion-writing role moved to a different posture
- Written formal Ethics Opinions are rarer
- Most guidance now flows through Practice 2.0 advisories and ER best-practice publications on azbar.org
- The August 2024 AZ Supreme Court rule changes primarily addressed the disciplinary side, not Practice 2.0 directly

This means Practice 2.0 is **the primary advisory channel** today, replacing the older formal-opinion-writing model.
