## 3. AZ-PERMITTED REGISTERS

### Tone Positioning (AZ)

- **Fighter / advocate** positioning permitted — more direct than CA or CO
- **Professional but assertive**, not passive
- **Urgency language** can reference the 2-year statute of limitations directly
- **Anti-insurance messaging** permitted at moderate levels
- **Empathy-driven** is preferred (matches SC AI creative approach), but not required

### Avoid (AZ)

- **Unsupported superlatives** ("the best," "the most," "#1," "leading," "top-rated") — AZ views these as potentially deceptive under ER 7.1
- **Promises of specific amounts** without disclaimer + substantiation
- **Implied case-evaluation by Second Chair** ("we'll find the best lawyer for you")
- **Implied government affiliation** ("AZ Crash Authority," "Official Injury Hotline")
- **Gratuitous gore / shock imagery**

## 4. CRASH FOOTAGE RULES (AZ-Specific)

Arizona has **no SB37 analogue**. Crash footage is permitted, with these guardrails:

| Use | Status |
|---|---|
| News-style B-roll of generic crash scenes | :white_check_mark: Permitted |
| Stylized AI-generated crash recreation (matching SC AI creative stack) | :white_check_mark: Permitted with "Dramatization" disclosure |
| Real client crash footage with consent + redaction | :white_check_mark: Permitted with disclosure |
| Stock crash footage | :white_check_mark: Permitted |
| Graphic injury close-ups (blood, broken bones, distressed faces) | :x: Platform policy violations (Meta Personal Attributes) — also CFA/ER 7.1 issues if sensationalized |
| Footage of identifiable real victims without consent | :x: Privacy violation + potentially defamatory |

(See [`../06_Tribal_Jurisdiction.md`](06_Tribal_Jurisdiction.md) — note: tribal-land crash footage may have additional sensitivities.)

## 5. SETTLEMENT-AMOUNT MESSAGING

AZ permits specific dollar amounts with substantiation + disclaimer. The standard structure:

```
[$X.XM settlement] — [case-type description], [year]*

*Past results do not guarantee future outcomes. Each case is unique
and depends on its own facts and circumstances. Results may vary.
```

Examples (compliant):
- "$8.4M settlement — Truck crash, 2024*"
- "$4.1M settlement — Pedestrian motor vehicle, 2023*"
- "$2.3M settlement — Multi-vehicle crash, 2024*"

(All claims must trace to records in the substantiation file. See [`../13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md`](13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md).)

Aggregate claims:
- "$1.7B+ recovered for clients*"
- "97% of qualifying cases reach a settlement or verdict*"

## 6. URGENCY FRAMING (AZ-Specific Hooks)

AZ's 2-year statute of limitations and pure comparative fault rule unlock urgency hooks that aren't ER 7.1-defensible in other states:

| Hook | Why it works in AZ |
|---|---|
| "2 years to file under Arizona law" | Cites § 12-542 — factually accurate |
| "Don't lose your right to compensation — Arizona's deadline is 2 years" | True; just cite the statute |
| "Even partial fault doesn't end your case in Arizona" | Cites § 12-2505 — factually accurate (pure comparative fault) |
| "If your accident was within the last 24 months, you may still have a case" | True; works with form's date-routing |
| "Insurance companies want to settle fast — for less than your case is worth" | Industry-true, ER 7.1-defensible (anti-insurance, moderate) |

## 7. AI-GENERATED CREATIVE — AZ POSTURE

Per the SC AI creative stack (ChatGPT Image 2.0 stills via Higgsfield, Kling/Seedance video), AZ has no specific rule against AI-generated imagery. The standards that apply:

- **ER 7.1**: AI-generated imagery cannot be misleading. If a still depicts a "client," disclose it's a dramatization or use clearly stylized aesthetics.
- **Meta platform policy**: AI lip-sync deepfakes of real public figures are banned. Avoid.
- **AI testimonials**: Cannot use AI-generated "testimonials" claiming specific case results — those imply real client outcomes the AI didn't actually achieve.
- **Stylized recreation** (the "Elevated Documentary" register WCTL is using) is fine, with the dramatization disclosure.

> **Saul:** "AI imagery is fine here. The 'indistinguishable from real' aesthetic is fine — that's an ER 7.1 question only if you're making a factual claim with the image (like 'this is a real client'). For ambient creative — light, mood, scene — go nuts. Just don't put words in a fake person's mouth and call it a testimonial."

## 8. THE PLAY (Creative Latitude)

**Do (AZ):**
- Push harder than CA register: fighter positioning, anti-insurance, direct urgency
- Use crash footage / wreckage imagery with context (not gratuitous)
- Cite specific dollar amounts with disclaimer and substantiation
- Cite the 2-year SOL and pure comparative fault rule directly
- Use stylized/AI imagery freely, with dramatization disclosure where appropriate

**Don't (AZ):**
- Don't use unsupported superlatives ("best," "top," "#1")
- Don't promise specific outcomes or amounts without context
- Don't use graphic gore or sensationalized injury imagery (platform policy + ER 7.1)
- Don't run AI lip-sync deepfakes (Meta ban)
- Don't run AI-generated "testimonials" claiming results
