# Insurance and Coverage (Arizona)

> **Saul:** "Arizona is at-fault, no PIP, 25/50/15 minimums. Most catastrophic-injury cases against minimum-limits drivers cap at $25K from the tortfeasor — the rest comes from UM/UIM, MedPay, and stacking. Lead-gen ad copy that ignores UM/UIM education is leaving money on the table for claimants and the firm. Educate, don't promise."

---

## 1. ARIZONA IS AT-FAULT / TORT — NOT NO-FAULT

Arizona is a **traditional tort liability ("at-fault") state**. There is **no PIP mandate**. The injured party recovers from:
1. The at-fault driver's liability insurer
2. The injured's own UM/UIM coverage (if applicable)
3. The injured's own MedPay coverage (if elected)
4. The injured's health insurance
5. Out of pocket

**Implication:** AZ PI cases are about establishing fault and quantifying damages — the standard tort framework. Subrogation and lien resolution complicate net recovery.

---

## 2. MANDATORY MINIMUMS — 25 / 50 / 15

Effective **July 1, 2020**, raised from prior 15/30/10. Current as of 2026:

| Coverage | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Bodily injury per person | **$25,000** |
| Bodily injury per accident (all persons combined) | **$50,000** |
| Property damage per accident | **$15,000** |

Source: [AZ DIFI Automobile Insurance page](https://difi.az.gov/consumer/automobile-insurance).

**The hard ceiling problem:** A serious-injury case against a minimum-limits driver caps at $25K/person from the tortfeasor. UIM is often the difference between a viable case and a non-case.

---

## 3. UM / UIM UNDER A.R.S. § 20-259.01

### Mandatory Offer, Optional Purchase
Every insurer writing AZ auto liability must **offer in writing** UM and UIM coverage in limits **not less than the bodily-injury liability limits** in the policy. The named insured may decline or accept lesser amounts. **Rejection must be documented.**

### Declarations Page as Final Expression (Post-2019 Amendment)
"The policy declarations page must be sent to the named insured, constitutes the final expression of the named insured's decision to purchase or reject" UM/UIM. No separate offer form is required if the insured purchases UM/UIM at full liability limits.

### Anti-Stacking (Subsection H)
"If multiple policies or coverages purchased by one insured on different vehicles apply to an accident or claim, the insurer may limit the coverage so that only one policy or coverage, selected by the insured, shall be applicable to any one accident."

**AZ permits insurers to write anti-stacking clauses for same-insured multi-vehicle policies.** Inter-policy stacking across separate insureds (household members) remains a fact-specific question.

### Subrogation (Subsection I)
UM-paying insurer subrogates against the uninsured tortfeasor.

### MedPay Liens (Subsection J)
Insurers may establish liens on MedPay payments exceeding $5,000 with formal recording/notice requirements.

### Commercial Vehicle Exception (Subsection C)
Insurers may decline to offer UM/UIM on public conveyances, rentals, or property-transport vehicles. Multi-vehicle policies may require uniform UM/UIM limits.

---

## 4. MEDPAY — OPTIONAL, NO STATUTORY MANDATE

Standard limits available are typically:
- $1,000
- $2,000
- $5,000
- $10,000
- $25,000

Covers reasonable medical expenses for the named insured and passengers regardless of fault.

**Subrogation:** Generally **NOT** available against MedPay in AZ (Title 20 limits MedPay subrogation in many contexts).

**Health insurance coordination:** AZ permits **stacking of MedPay with health insurance recovery** in many scenarios.

## 5. BAD-FAITH FRAMEWORK

### Common Law (Robust in AZ)
- ***Noble v. Nat'l Am. Life Ins. Co.***, 128 Ariz. 188 (1981) — established the first-party bad-faith tort
- ***Rawlings v. Apodaca***, 151 Ariz. 149 (1986) — defined the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing as actionable in tort in the special insurance contractual relationship; foundational case
- ***Zilisch v. State Farm Mut. Auto. Ins. Co.***, 196 Ariz. 234 (2000) — clarified the modern standard:

> "In defending against a fairly debatable claim, an insurer must exercise reasonable care and good faith. [The insurer must] immediately conduct an adequate investigation, act reasonably in evaluating the claim, and act promptly in paying a legitimate claim."

**Fair debatability is necessary but not sufficient** to defeat a bad-faith claim — the insurer's conduct in investigation and evaluation is independently scrutinized.

### Test
- **Objective component** — was the insurer's investigation/evaluation/processing unreasonable?
- **Subjective component** — did the insurer know it was acting unreasonably or act with reckless disregard such that knowledge can be imputed?

### Statutory Layer
**A.R.S. § 20-461 (Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act):** Codifies prompt-acknowledgment, prompt-investigation, prompt-affirmation/denial, and good-faith effectuation of fair settlement where liability is reasonably clear.

**Critical:** § 20-461 expressly creates **NO private right of action** — it is solely an administrative remedy enforceable by the AZ DIFI Director. § 20-461 standards may, however, **inform the reasonableness analysis in a common-law bad-faith case**.

### Workers' Comp Bad Faith
**A.R.S. § 23-930** is the parallel workers' comp bad-faith statute (separate regime).

## 6. AD COPY IMPLICATIONS

### Compliant copy that leverages AZ insurance regime
- "Don't accept the insurance company's first offer — you may be entitled to more"
- "Even if the at-fault driver had only minimum coverage, you may have UIM that can pay"
- "Insurance companies want to settle fast — for less than your case is worth"
- "Arizona requires insurers to deal with you in good faith. When they don't, that's a separate claim"

### Non-compliant
- "Guaranteed payout from your insurance" (false)
- "We always recover the policy limits" (false / impossible standard)
- "The insurance company is required to pay you $X" (oversimplifies)

### Educational angle that's underutilized
**UM/UIM education** — many AZ consumers don't realize:
1. Their own UM/UIM is often the **primary recovery source** when the tortfeasor is uninsured/underinsured
2. Stacking might be available across household policies (fact-specific)
3. UM/UIM rejection requires documented written rejection; if the rejection isn't properly documented, default UM/UIM coverage may be in force

This is high-leverage educational content for top-of-funnel ads — explains "why call a lawyer" without overpromising.

## 7. PRACTICAL CASE-VALUE ANCHORING

For "what your case may be worth" messaging, anchor case value to realistic recovery sources:

| Source | Typical ceiling/floor |
|---|---|
| At-fault driver's BI liability limits | Often the $25K state minimum ceiling |
| Plaintiff's own UIM coverage | Up to declared UIM limits (often 25K, 50K, 100K, 250K) |
| MedPay (if elected) | $1K-$25K |
| Umbrella policies | Variable (often $1M-$5M) |
| Health insurance (with subrogation) | Variable |
| Bad-faith damages (if applicable) | Above policy limits in egregious cases |

**Case-value messaging in ads:** Don't promise specific amounts. Use ranges with disclaimers, or focus on the categories of recovery rather than dollar figures.

---

## 8. A.R.S. § 20-1632 — REPLACEMENT PARTS DISCLOSURE (Tangential)

Governs aftermarket / non-OEM crash-repair parts disclosure obligations for auto insurers and repair shops. Limited PI relevance — mostly property-damage / first-party physical-damage coverage. Can come up in diminished-value or repair-quality disputes but not central to bodily-injury PI ad copy.

---

## 9. THE PLAY (Insurance Specific)

**Do:**
- Use AZ insurance education as a copy hook (UM/UIM, MedPay, stacking, bad-faith leverage)
- Anchor case-value messaging to the realistic recovery stack, not just the at-fault driver's limits
- Maintain awareness of the Rawlings/Zilisch bad-faith leverage in case selection
- Track UM/UIM rejection-document defects as a potential coverage-expansion angle

**Don't:**
- Don't promise specific recovery amounts
- Don't overstate the at-fault driver's coverage as a guarantee
- Don't rely on § 20-461 as an independent cause of action (no private right)
- Don't treat AZ as a no-fault state — it isn't
