# Lead Generator vs. Lawyer Referral Service — Arizona Application

> **Saul:** "This is the single most important classification question in the entire program. If we're a lead generator, we're cool. If we're an unregistered lawyer referral service, we're operating illegally. The line is whether we *select* or just *connect*. Don't get cute with the copy."

---

## 1. THE STAKES

In most states (CA, TX, FL, NY), operating an unregistered lawyer referral service can:
- Void the contract between SC and the law firm
- Subject SC to civil penalties
- Subject participating attorneys to disciplinary action
- Disgorge fees received

**In Arizona, the stakes are different but not gone.** Arizona doesn't have a CA-style BPC § 6155 LRS certification regime. But ER 7.1 (lead-gen rules) and ER 7.5 (trade name) still classify the operation, and if SC is *de facto* operating as a referral service while marketing as a lead generator, the AZ Bar can take action — and the Consumer Fraud Act can be invoked by claimants who feel they were misled about what kind of service they were using.

---

## 2. THE TEST: SELECT vs. CONNECT

| Language / Behavior | Classification |
|---|---|
| "Connect with a participating attorney" | Lead Generator :white_check_mark: |
| "We'll match you with the best lawyer for your case" | Referral Service :x: |
| "Get a free case evaluation from a local lawyer" | Lead Generator :white_check_mark: |
| "Our team reviews your case to find the right attorney" | Referral Service :x: |
| "Speak with a lawyer in our network" | Lead Generator :white_check_mark: |
| "We recommend the attorney best suited to your needs" | Referral Service :x: |
| "Submit your information and our intake team will route you" | :warning: Borderline — "route" is fine, but "intake team" implies analysis |
| "Your information will be shared with WCTL, a participating law firm" | Lead Generator :white_check_mark: |

**The test:** Does the service imply *selection* or just *connection*?

---

## 3. ARIZONA'S SPECIFIC FRAMEWORK

### ER 7.1 — Lead Generator Payment Rules (consolidated from former ER 7.2)

A lawyer **may** pay others for generating client leads. The lead generator must:
- **Not** recommend the lawyer
- **Not** state, imply, or create a reasonable impression that:
  - It is recommending the lawyer
  - The referral is being made without payment from the lawyer
  - It has analyzed the person's legal problems to determine which lawyer should receive the referral
- Comply with ER 7.1 (truthful, not misleading)

### ER 5.4 — Eliminated (Big AZ Advantage)

Arizona eliminated ER 5.4 effective January 1, 2021. **Fee-sharing between lawyers and non-lawyers is permitted.** This is unique to Arizona among major US legal markets and is a structural advantage for the lead-gen model.

(See [`02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md`](02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md) for the full ABS framework and what it means for SC's contracting structure.)

### Arizona Ethics Opinion 04-02 — Referral Fees (pre-2021, but still informative)

Established that AZ permits referral fees with disclosure to the client and consent. Post-2021 with ER 5.4 eliminated, the referral-fee landscape is even more permissive.

---

## 4. SECOND CHAIR'S STATED POSITION

Second Chair operates as a **lead generator**, NOT a lawyer referral service. This classification holds because:

1. SC does **not** recommend WCTL specifically — it routes leads to the contracted firm based on geography (state-level routing) and case-type (auto vs. medmal vs. premises), not based on case-merit analysis or attorney-client fit
2. SC does **not** state, imply, or create the impression it has analyzed the person's legal problem to determine which lawyer should receive the referral
3. All SC communications comply with ER 7.1 (truthful, not misleading)
4. WCTL is identified as the responsible attorney/firm in all SC advertising
5. The fee structure between SC and WCTL is consistent with AZ rules (fee-sharing now permitted under post-2021 framework)

## 5. COPY DISCIPLINE — WHAT TO USE / AVOID

### Compliant Lead-Gen Copy (AZ)

| Phrase | Use case |
|---|---|
| "Connect with a participating attorney" | Generic call-to-action |
| "Get a free consultation from a local lawyer" | CTA on landing page |
| "Your information will be shared with West Coast Trial Lawyers" | TCPA consent / disclosure |
| "Speak with an attorney about your case" | CTA, neutral |
| "Submit your information to be contacted by a participating law firm" | Form submit button |
| "Free case review by a participating attorney" | Headline / hook |

### Non-Compliant Referral-Service Copy (AZ)

| Phrase | Why it crosses the line |
|---|---|
| "We'll find the best attorney for your case" | Implies selection based on case |
| "Our experts review your accident to match you with the right lawyer" | Implies case analysis + matching |
| "Based on your injuries, we recommend..." | Affirmative recommendation |
| "We screen attorneys to find the right fit" | Implies selection |
| "Our intake team will analyze your case before connecting you" | Implies legal analysis (ER 7.3 + LRS issues) |
| "Top-rated AZ injury lawyer assigned to your case" | Implies selection + unsupported superlative |

## 6. THE FORM ARCHITECTURE THAT KEEPS US LEAD-GEN

The Fair Case quiz has been engineered to operate as a **mechanical, non-discretionary lead-gen funnel**, not a case-evaluation referral service:

1. Claimant clicks ad → lands on Fair Case
2. Quiz asks **demographic + factual** questions (state, accident date, accident type, injury severity, insurance status) — *not* legal-analysis questions
3. Form filters claimants who fall outside SOL or other gates (mechanical date-routing, not legal judgment)
4. WCTL is named to the claimant **before** form submission (TCPA + ER 7.5)
5. On submit, lead is routed to WCTL — not "matched" or "recommended"
6. WCTL's intake team handles the actual case evaluation

The entire architecture is designed so that **no one at Second Chair is making legal-analysis decisions about which firm is "best" for a given case**. SC just runs the funnel that passes leads to WCTL (the only firm contracted for AZ traffic).

> **Saul:** "The 'mechanical, non-discretionary' framing is the magic. It's not just for the AZ Bar — it's also why CA's BPC § 6155 doesn't bite us if we ever expanded to CA traffic. The form makes routing decisions based on the answers; humans don't pick attorneys based on case fit. Keep it that way."

---

## 7. WHAT BREAKS THE LEAD-GEN CLASSIFICATION

If SC ever did any of the following, it would risk being reclassified as an unregistered LRS:

- Adding a question like "What kind of attorney are you looking for?" and routing based on the answer (selection logic)
- Multi-firm panels in AZ where SC routes to one firm vs. another based on case characteristics
- Marketing claims like "We screen attorneys" or "We pick the right firm for your case"
- Charging the firm a percentage of recovery (fee-sharing is fine in AZ post-2021, but percentage-of-outcome can flip the classification under federal/cross-state analysis)
- Holding out as a "lawyer matching service" or "find a lawyer" platform

**Current state: SC is single-firm-per-state (WCTL for AZ), routes based on mechanical state-and-case-type filters, charges a per-lead fee. Solidly in lead-gen territory.**

---

## 8. THE PLAY

**Do:**
- Keep all copy in the "connect / route" register
- Maintain single-firm routing per state (WCTL = AZ)
- Use mechanical filters (date, geography, case type) — never legal-analysis questions
- Identify WCTL as the named recipient in TCPA consent and disclosures

**Don't:**
- Don't add "we recommend" / "we match" / "best fit for you" language
- Don't add legal-analysis questions to the form
- Don't run multi-firm panels in AZ unless explicitly architected
- Don't shift to percentage-of-recovery fee structure without legal review
