# Recent AZ Disciplinary Cases on Lawyer Advertising (2020-2026)

> **Saul:** "AZ doesn't pile on advertising discipline cases the way Florida or NY does. Most AZ enforcement is trust-account violations, client abandonment, and fraud — not ads. The new active zone is the ABS regime, where the Bar has supervisory authority by virtue of the licensing scheme. Read the cases that *are* there. They tell you exactly where the live tripwires are."

---

## 1. ENFORCEMENT POSTURE (2020-2026)

The AZ Lawyer Regulation system runs through the **Office of the Presiding Disciplinary Judge (PDJ)** at azcourts.gov, with formal sanctions (reprimand, suspension, disbarment) issued by the PDJ or AZ Supreme Court.

**Public-record decisions:**
- AZ Courts: [azcourts.gov/attorneydiscipline](https://www.azcourts.gov/attorneydiscipline)
- AZ Bar Formal Decisions: azbar.org formal decisions index
- Disciplinary Cases Matrix: [azcourts.gov/attorneydiscipline/DisciplinaryCasesMatrix](https://www.azcourts.gov/attorneydiscipline/DisciplinaryCasesMatrix)

**Key observation:** Over 2022-2026, public AZ disciplinary orders specifically targeting (a) social-media testimonial discipline, (b) influencer/affiliate fee-splitting outside the ABS context, or (c) PI-specific advertising discipline against traditional (non-ABS) personal-injury firms are **rare**. This is consistent with the historical AZ Bar pattern: post-Bates AZ has been **less aggressive** than Florida, NY, or NJ in policing PI advertising content.

The active enforcement zone is the **ABS regime**, where the Bar has more affirmative supervisory authority by virtue of the licensing scheme itself.

## 2. NOTABLE CASE — IN RE LEGAL HELP PARTNERS PLLC, PDJ-2024-9035 (2024)

**The first major Arizona ABS advertising-discipline case.** Highly relevant precedent for any operation involving lead-gen, co-counsel arrangements, or multi-firm handoffs.

### Background
Legal Help Partners was an Arizona-licensed Alternative Business Structure (ABS license granted **August 25, 2021**) operating a lead-generation / co-counsel model.

### Findings
The PDJ found the firm engaged in **negligent advertising practices**:
1. **TV and web ads conflated multiple corporate names** — confusion about which entity was actually representing the consumer
2. **Failed to clearly disclose to consumers the co-counsel arrangement** under which their cases would actually be handled by separate firms
3. **Failed to make required division of responsibility disclosures** in client engagement agreements

### Rules Implicated
- **ER 7.1** (false/misleading communications)
- **ER 7.5** (firm names/letterheads)
- **Rule 1.5(e)** (fee splitting / division of responsibility)
- **ABS-specific obligations** under ACJA § 7-209

### Sanction
- **Reprimand with probation**
- Payment of disciplinary costs within 30 days

### Direct Relevance to SC/WCTL Lead-Gen Operations

The lessons published in the AZ State Bar ABS Compliance Counsel Guide (issued in the wake of Legal Help Partners) explicitly warn against:

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| National/multi-state advertising that misleads AZ consumers about firm identity or location of lawyers | Disclose WCTL's licensed jurisdictions on every Fair Case landing page; identify AZ-admitted attorneys for AZ traffic |
| Inaccurate or incomplete advertising disclosures | Maintain "clear and conspicuous" standard on all disclosures (ER 7.1) |
| Failure to disclose co-counsel/handoff arrangements before retention | The Fair Case form names WCTL pre-submission; the engagement letter discloses the SC compensation arrangement under ER 1.5(f) |

> **Saul:** "Legal Help Partners is the cautionary tale. They had the ABS license — that wasn't the problem. The problem was their advertising created confusion about who was actually representing the client, and the engagement docs didn't make the co-counsel structure clear. The lesson: name names, name responsibility, name jurisdictions. Don't let a claimant submit without knowing exactly what entity they're connecting with."

## 3. AZ ABS DISCIPLINARY UNIVERSE (2021-2026)

As of the State Bar's 2024 ABS Compliance Counsel Guide, **two ABS entities** have been subject to public discipline (Legal Help Partners is one). The AZ Supreme Court amended ABS rules (ACJA § 7-209) **effective August 29, 2024**, tightening disclosure and oversight requirements following these cases.

The 2024 amendments primarily addressed:
- Sharper disclosure requirements about non-lawyer ownership in advertising
- Tighter compliance-lawyer supervision standards
- Annual renewal documentation requirements

## 4. OLDER PRECEDENT (Doctrinally Important)

### In re a Member of the State Bar of Arizona (2010)

A hearing officer found a lawyer **negligently violated ER 7.1** by writing and using a materially misleading television advertisement in 2007 about DUI defense services and a new DUI law. Sanction was tailored to the negligence finding (not knowing).

**Why it matters:** Foundational AZ case for the **negligent vs. knowing** mens rea distinction in advertising discipline. Negligent violations get lighter sanctions; knowing violations get heavier ones. Documentation of good-faith compliance review (e.g., Practice 2.0 submissions) is mitigating evidence at the sanctions phase even if the underlying conduct is found violative.

## 5. WHAT'S NOT IN THE PUBLIC RECORD

The published AZ PDJ orders during 2022-2026 do **not** show:

| Topic | Status |
|---|---|
| Discipline against a traditional non-ABS PI firm for misleading advertising | None located |
| Discipline against social-media influencer marketing arrangements | None located |
| Discipline for affiliate-link fee-splits outside the ABS context | None located |
| Discipline for AI-generated testimonials or deepfakes | None located |
| Discipline for chatbot-driven intake creating ER 1.18 problems | None located |

This is **suggestive of low enforcement activity in these areas, not active permission**. The AZ Bar's complaint-driven posture means enforcement requires a complaint to land, an investigation to be opened, and a probable-cause finding. Many advertising-related complaints likely resolve at the LRO level (informal disposition or dismissal) without producing a public order.

## 6. ENFORCEMENT-PATTERN-DERIVED OPERATIONAL GUIDANCE

From the public record (Legal Help Partners + the 2010 case + the absence of other public ad-discipline orders):

### Live tripwires
- **Specific factual claims without documentable foundation** ("Top-rated," "Best in AZ," "Award-winning") — ER 7.1 backstop
- **Trade names that confuse consumers about who is providing services** — ER 7.5 + Legal Help Partners precedent
- **Co-counsel / handoff arrangements without disclosure to client** — Rule 1.5(e) + Legal Help Partners precedent
- **Inadequate disclosures (size, color, placement)** — ER 7.1 "clear and conspicuous" standard

### Less-active enforcement zones
- Aggressive copy tone, fighter positioning (constitutionally protected per Bates)
- Crash footage with context (no SB37 analogue in AZ)
- Settlement amounts with disclaimers (permitted per ER 7.1)
- Specialization language used truthfully (ER 7.1 post-2021)

## 7. MITIGATION ARCHITECTURE

### Document everything
The AZ Bar's *Standards for Imposing Lawyer Sanctions* (per ABA Standards) recognize good-faith compliance documentation as mitigating. SC/WCTL should maintain:
- **Substantiation file** — every factual claim backed by data (see [`../13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md`](../13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md))
- **Practice 2.0 review log** — every voluntary review request, with date and reviewer (see [`../14_Voluntary_Review_Practice_2.0.md`](../14_Voluntary_Review_Practice_2.0.md))
- **Engagement letter archive** — every executed letter retained
- **Disclosure architecture documentation** — design rationale for the form and landing page disclosure structure

### When a complaint hits
- Notify defense counsel immediately (reciprocal discipline risk for CA-licensed WCTL attorneys)
- Pull substantiation file within 24 hours
- Respond within 14 days as demanded by the LRO
- Document everything; do not destroy or alter records
- See [`../15_Disciplinary_Process_and_Sanctions.md`](../15_Disciplinary_Process_and_Sanctions.md) for the full process flow

---

## 8. THE PLAY (Recent Discipline Specific)

**Do:**
- Treat Legal Help Partners as the cautionary tale — clear firm-identity disclosure, clear co-counsel disclosure, clear consent
- Maintain documentation that supports good-faith compliance (Practice 2.0 reviews, substantiation files, engagement letters)
- Watch for new AZ discipline orders quarterly; update this file as new precedent emerges

**Don't:**
- Don't assume the absence of public ad-discipline orders means no enforcement is happening — many resolve informally below the public-order threshold
- Don't ignore the 2024 ABS amendments if SC/WCTL's structure ever evolves into an ABS arrangement
- Don't underestimate the reciprocal-discipline risk for WCTL's CA-licensed attorneys
