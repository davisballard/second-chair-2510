# Bilingual / Spanish-Language Compliance (Arizona)

> **Saul:** "Phoenix is 42% Hispanic. Tucson is 36-43%. Nogales is 93% — and 90% of Nogales households are Spanish-only. Running an English-only Fair Case buy in Arizona means leaving a third of the state — and the dominant share of border communities — outside the funnel. Spanish-language is a *first-class* peer to English here, not a translated afterthought. Same disclosure rigor applies."

---

## 1. ARIZONA-SPECIFIC FRAMEWORK

**Arizona has no statute or Rule of Professional Conduct that specifically governs Spanish-language legal advertising.** ER 7.1 (no false or misleading communications), ER 7.3 (solicitation), and ER 7.5 (trade names) apply uniformly regardless of language.

However, ER 7.1's prohibition on "false or misleading" communications applies to **the actual message received by the consumer** — meaning a Spanish-language ad must not be misleading **in Spanish**.

If the English version contains a disclaimer (e.g., "results may vary," "no fee unless we win," "attorney advertising"), the **Spanish version must contain a faithfully translated disclaimer**. Omitting the disclaimer in translation, or translating it in a way that softens it, is itself a potential ER 7.1 violation.

## 2. FEDERAL FRAMEWORKS THAT MAY APPLY

### FTC 16 C.F.R. § 14.9
When advertising or sales material is in a language other than English, **required disclosures must appear in the same language as the principal advertising**. This is FTC enforcement guidance focused on consumer-protection statutes the FTC administers, but courts and bar counsel cite it as a general "best practice" benchmark.

### CFPB / Regulation F
Does not mandate Spanish disclosures for legal services (legal advertising is largely outside CFPB jurisdiction), but the CFPB's Limited English Proficiency (LEP) guidance is influential. Where a firm advertises **in Spanish**, the CFPB position is that consumer-facing disclosures (including the limits of services offered in Spanish) should be in Spanish.

### TILA / Regulation Z
Applies to credit advertising. If a PI firm advertises a **medical-funding referral or litigation-finance product** in Spanish, TILA Spanish-disclosure obligations apply. (FTC has settled multiple matters requiring Spanish-language TILA disclosures in Spanish-language advertising.)

### FDCPA
No FDCPA implications for legal services advertising (FDCPA covers debt collection).

---

## 3. AZ CONSUMER FRAUD ACT (A.R.S. § 44-1521) — APPLIES TO SPANISH ADS

The Arizona Consumer Fraud Act applies to ads "made or disseminated in this state in any manner" — a Spanish-language ad to AZ consumers is squarely within the statute.

**A Spanish-language ad whose English-version disclosures are dropped, mistranslated, or buried likely satisfies the statutory definition of "concealment or omission of material fact"** — exposing both Second Chair and WCTL to CFA private actions or AG enforcement.

(See [`03_Statutes/44-1521_Consumer_Fraud_Act.md`](03_Statutes/44-1521_Consumer_Fraud_Act.md) for the full CFA framework.)

## 4. BEST-PRACTICE DISCLOSURE APPROACH

### Mirrored Bilingual Disclosures
Every landing page, every quiz step, and every confirmation screen has Spanish disclosures matching the English ones. **Use professional translation, not machine translation alone** — *traducción profesional*, with a translator's certification on file.

### Same Prominence
Spanish disclosures must be **at least as prominent** as the Spanish-language headline/CTA — same font weight, same size, same color contrast. If the English version meets ER 7.1 "clear and conspicuous," the Spanish version must too.

### Identify Responsible Attorney/Firm in Spanish
ER 7.1 requires the firm/attorney name and contact information. Carry this through to the Spanish creative — name WCTL conspicuously in Spanish too.

### "Attorney Advertising" / "Publicidad Legal"
Mark clearly. Best-practice (not strictly required by AZ).

### "Not a law firm" / "No es un bufete de abogados"
Translate the ER 7.5 trade-name disclosure. Fair Case must be disclosed as a non-law-firm entity in Spanish too.

### No-Fee Disclaimer
"Sin cargos a menos que ganemos su caso" — "no fees unless we win your case" — must be paired with the same qualifying language used in English (case-cost-recovery exclusions, etc.).

### Past-Results Disclaimer
"Los resultados anteriores no garantizan resultados similares. Cada caso es único y depende de sus propios hechos y circunstancias."

### AI-Generated Imagery Disclosure
"Dramatización" — same standard as English creative.

## 8. AZ HISPANIC DEMOGRAPHIC CONTEXT

| Geography | Hispanic/Latino % |
|---|---|
| Arizona statewide | ~31.4% (2024 ACS) |
| Phoenix (city) | ~42% — largest racial/ethnic group |
| Tucson (city) | ~36-43% (estimates vary by data source) |
| Nogales | ~93% Hispanic; ~90% Spanish-only households; ~41% foreign-born |
| Douglas | ~82% |
| Yuma | ~61% |
| San Luis, Somerton | Approaching Nogales-level Spanish dominance |

**Operational implication:** Spanish-language Fair Case creative is not optional for Arizona. A pure-English buy under-serves roughly 30% of the state and 40%+ of metro Phoenix and Tucson, and the dominant share in border communities. **Build the Spanish funnel as a first-class peer to the English funnel**, not a translated afterthought, with parity in disclosure rigor.

---

## 9. THE PLAY (Spanish-Language Specific)

**Do:**
- Build Spanish-language Fair Case creative as a peer to English, not a translation afterthought
- Use professional translation with a translator's certification on file
- Match disclosure prominence (size, color, contrast) on Spanish creative to English
- Translate ER 1.18 disclaimer, TCPA consent, ER 7.5 trade-name disclosure faithfully
- Test Spanish-language landing pages with native speakers before launch

**Don't:**
- Don't rely on machine translation for disclosures
- Don't translate disclaimers in ways that soften legal qualifications (e.g., omitting "results may vary" framing)
- Don't run an English-only buy in Arizona — leaves ~30% of the addressable market untouched
- Don't run Spanish creative with English-only landing pages
