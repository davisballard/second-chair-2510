# ER 7.3 — Solicitation of Clients
## Arizona Rules of Professional Conduct

> **Saul:** "Here's where people get confused. The red ink, double-size font stuff — that's for direct mail and email solicitation, when you're targeting a specific person you know has a legal need. Your Meta ads? Those are general advertising under ER 7.1. Different rules. Don't paste 'NOTICE: THIS IS AN ADVERTISEMENT!' in red across your Instagram creative. That's not what the rule says."

---

## 1. THE RULE

### Prohibited
- Live person-to-person solicitation for pecuniary gain (in-person, live telephone, real-time electronic contact)
- Soliciting persons known to be in need of legal services when the lawyer's primary motivation is pecuniary gain
- Continuing to solicit after the prospective client has made known a desire not to be solicited

### Permitted
- Written, recorded, or electronic communications (including direct mail and email)
- Digital advertising and lead generation (not considered live solicitation)
- Contact with other lawyers, close personal friends, family members, and persons who routinely use the type of legal services offered

---

## 2. THE CRITICAL DISTINCTION FOR DIGITAL LEAD-GEN

**General digital advertising ≠ solicitation under ER 7.3.**

A Meta/Instagram/TikTok/Google ad placed to a broad audience, that a claimant chooses to click and respond to, is classified as **advertising** under ER 7.1 — not solicitation under ER 7.3. The red-ink and double-size-font rules in ER 7.3 apply to **targeted written communications to specific known individuals** (direct mail, email).

| Channel | Rule that applies |
|---|---|
| Meta / Instagram / TikTok ads (general audience) | ER 7.1 (Advertising) |
| Google search ads (broad keywords) | ER 7.1 (Advertising) |
| YouTube ads (general audience) | ER 7.1 (Advertising) |
| Direct mail to known accident victims | ER 7.3 (Solicitation) — red ink + double-size required |
| Email to known accident victims | ER 7.3 (Solicitation) — "Advertising Material" required |
| Cold-call to identified accident victim | ER 7.3 — **Prohibited** (live solicitation for pecuniary gain) |
| Live chat that proactively messages claimants | ER 7.3 — **Prohibited if uninitiated by claimant** |
| Quiz form that claimant initiates by clicking ad | ER 7.1 (claimant-initiated, not solicitation) |

---

## 3. INBOUND FORMS ARE NOT SOLICITATION

A claimant who sees a Fair Case ad, clicks through, fills out the quiz, and submits is **requesting a case evaluation** — they're initiating contact. The Second Chair → WCTL handoff that follows is responding to that initiated request, not soliciting.

**This is the structural exemption that makes the entire lead-gen funnel ER 7.3-compliant.**

For the exemption to hold:
- Ad creative must be informational, not personally targeted to identifiable injured persons
- Form submission must be claimant-initiated (the claimant must affirmatively submit)
- Follow-up contact must be within the scope of consent given on the form
- TCPA consent must be specific and current

---

## 4. WHAT WOULD VIOLATE ER 7.3

| Conduct | Why it violates |
|---|---|
| Scraping AZ police crash reports and texting/calling identified victims | Live solicitation of known accident victims |
| Buying a lead list of injured persons and cold-calling | Live solicitation; victims are "known to be in need" |
| Live-chat widget that proactively messages users without their initiation | Uninitiated real-time electronic contact |
| Continuing to text/call after a claimant has said "stop" | ER 7.3(c) — continuing after refusal |
| Hiring "runners" or "cappers" to approach hospital ER patients | Classic ambulance-chasing; per se ER 7.3 violation; criminal under some statutes |
| Targeting a Meta ad to "people who recently filed an insurance claim" using PII match | Crossing from general advertising into targeted solicitation; high-risk |

> **Saul:** "The hospital-ER thing isn't just an ethics violation, it's a felony in some states. Don't even joke about it. We're a digital lead-gen operation — claimants come to us, not the other way."

## 5. WRITTEN SOLICITATION RULES (If We Ever Did Direct Mail / Email)

If Second Chair ever expanded into direct mail or targeted email to identified prospective clients (we don't, currently), ER 7.3 imposes:

**Physical mail:**
- Envelope and first page must include in **red ink**: `NOTICE: THIS IS AN ADVERTISEMENT!`
- Must be in **all capital letters** in type size at least **double** the largest type size in the body

**Email:**
- Subject line must contain "Advertising Material"
- "Advertising Material" must appear at beginning and end of email
- In a font **twice the size** of the body text
- Must contain accurate transmission and routing information
- Must not contain false or misleading information

**Note:** Second Chair's current operation is broad-audience digital advertising only. The above is reference information in case the model expands.

---

## 6. THE PLAY (ER 7.3 Specific)

**Do:**
- Run general-audience digital ads under ER 7.1
- Make sure all follow-up contact is within scope of TCPA consent given on the form
- Honor opt-outs immediately and document them
- Treat the form submission as the **initiation of contact** — every downstream communication is a response to that initiation, not a solicitation

**Don't:**
- Don't slap "NOTICE: THIS IS AN ADVERTISEMENT!" in red on Meta creative — that's the wrong rule and looks amateur
- Don't run live-chat widgets that send the first message
- Don't scrape police reports or buy lists of identified victims
- Don't target ads using PII-matched audiences of known accident victims
- Don't keep contacting after a claimant says stop
