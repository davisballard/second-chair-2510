# ER 5.5 — Unauthorized Practice of Law / Multijurisdictional Practice

> **Saul:** "WCTL has out-of-state office operations *handled* for Arizona — Davis confirmed it. AZ-admitted attorneys staff the AZ office, AZ work is done by AZ lawyers, the structure is solid. This file is the reference, not the alarm bell. Read it to understand the framework, not because there's a fire."

---

## 1. THE RULE

**Citation:** Ariz. R. Sup. Ct. 42, ER 5.5 — "Unauthorized Practice of Law; Multijurisdictional Practice of Law."

### Core Prohibitions (ER 5.5(a)-(b))

A lawyer not admitted in Arizona shall not:
- "Establish an office or other systematic and continuous presence in Arizona for the practice of law," OR
- "Hold out to the public or otherwise represent that the lawyer is admitted to practice law in Arizona."

A lawyer admitted in another jurisdiction may engage in AZ practice **only** in the temporary-practice safe harbors under ER 5.5(c) or under one of the limited-admission categories under Sup. Ct. Rule 38.

---

## 2. SAFE HARBORS (ER 5.5(c)-(d))

### ER 5.5(c) — Temporary Practice
An out-of-state lawyer in good standing may practice on a temporary basis in AZ if practice is:
1. **Associated with an AZ lawyer** who actively participates;
2. **Related to a pending or potential AZ tribunal** where pro hac vice authorization is sought or reasonably anticipated;
3. **Related to ADR** arising out of practice in the home jurisdiction; OR
4. **Reasonably related to the lawyer's home-jurisdiction practice.**

### ER 5.5(d) — Federal/Foreign Law Only
A non-AZ lawyer may provide AZ-located services that "exclusively involve federal law, the law of another jurisdiction, or tribal law."

### Limited-Admission Categories (Sup. Ct. Rule 38)
- **Rule 38(b)** — Foreign Legal Consultant (registration only, scope-limited)
- **Rule 38(d)** — Limited Practice for Indigent / Approved Legal Services Organizations
- **Rule 38(f)** — Approved Legal Services Organizations (out-of-state lawyer must be admitted/active ≥2 years, must remain active in at least one home jurisdiction)
- **Rule 38(h)** — Admission on Motion (reciprocity-style admission for out-of-state lawyers meeting AZ requirements)
- **Rule 38(i)** — In-House Counsel registration

**Important:** AZ does **not** admit California attorneys on motion. California is **not** on Arizona's reciprocal-admission list. CA attorneys seeking AZ practice must take the AZ Bar exam or use a limited-admission category.

## 3. PRO HAC VICE — Sup. Ct. Rule 39

**Note:** Pro hac vice in Arizona state court is **Rule 39**, not Rule 41. Rule 41 was the older numbering; consolidated and renumbered effective May 1, 2020.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | Active member in good standing of another U.S. or foreign jurisdiction |
| Application | Notarized application + original certificate(s) of good standing + motion to associate AZ local counsel + proposed order |
| Fee | $505 to State Bar |
| Approval | By trial judicial officer |
| Disqualifying factors | Applicant must NOT (i) reside in AZ, (ii) be regularly employed in AZ, OR (iii) be regularly engaged in the practice of law in AZ |
| Processing | ~2-3 weeks |

### Federal Court — D. Ariz. LRCiv 83.1
- **Pro hac vice (LRCiv 83.1(b)(2))**: by written application
- Same disqualifying factors as state court (no AZ residency, no regular AZ employment, no regular AZ practice)
- Fee: $100
- ECF registration required

## 4. "OPERATING AN OFFICE" / "PRINCIPAL OFFICE"

**There is no separate AZ "registration" for an out-of-state firm's office distinct from licensure of its individual lawyers.** The compliant operating model is built **at the lawyer level**:

- Any lawyer working **in or from** an AZ-physical office on AZ-law matters must be AZ-admitted (full admission, admission-on-motion under Rule 38(h), or a recognized limited-admission category)
- An out-of-state lawyer **physically present** in an AZ office cannot maintain a "systematic and continuous presence for the practice of law" unless practice is restricted to (a) home-jurisdiction law, (b) federal law, (c) tribal law, or (d) qualifying temporary/safe-harbor work
- Per AZ State Bar UPL Op. 10-02, a non-AZ lawyer who *resides* in AZ cannot establish an "office of record" in AZ even when sharing space with an AZ-admitted lawyer, and cannot practice their home-state's law from that office on a systematic basis
- Firm-name and letterhead rules (former ER 7.5(b), now consolidated into ER 7.1 post-2021) require disclosure of jurisdictional limitations of attorneys not licensed in AZ

## 5. THE COMPLIANT STRUCTURE FOR A CA-HQ FIRM WITH AN AZ OFFICE

This is the framework that WCTL has built. Davis confirmed it is in place and "handled":

1. **Staff the AZ office with at least one fully AZ-Bar-admitted attorney** who is the responsible attorney of record on every AZ matter and supervises any AZ work product (ER 5.1)
2. **AZ-admitted attorney handles all client communications** that constitute AZ-law legal advice or representation. CA-only attorneys do not give AZ-law advice from the AZ office on a systematic basis
3. **Firm letterhead/website lists jurisdictional admissions** for each attorney (ER 7.1 transparency requirements; satisfies former ER 7.5(b))
4. **CA-only partners visiting AZ** rely on ER 5.5(c) temporary-practice safe harbors only for incidental, time-limited work — not as the operating model
5. **Litigation appearances:** AZ-admitted attorney files appearance; out-of-state partners enter pro hac vice under Rule 39 (state) or LRCiv 83.1(b)(2) (federal D. Ariz.)
6. **Firm trade name in AZ** complies with ER 7.1 (no false/misleading firm name; jurisdictional limitations disclosed)
7. **No offering of "AZ-law" services** to leads/prospects unless an AZ-admitted lawyer is the actual provider. Lead-intake routes AZ matters to the AZ-admitted attorney before legal advice attaches

## 6. CALIFORNIA AB 931 — CROSS-BORDER NOTE

**Effective January 1, 2026** (sunset January 1, 2030), California AB 931 prohibits California lawyers from sharing fees with an out-of-state ABS. This is documented in [`ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md`](ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md). Relevant here because:

- WCTL is California-headquartered
- Some WCTL attorneys are CA-only
- AZ work is done through AZ-admitted attorneys at the AZ office (per the structure above)
- AZ fees flow to AZ attorneys; CA AB 931 does not bite this structure because the fee flow does not depend on CA-attorney compensation

The rule is documented for completeness. Per Davis's confirmation, this is handled.

## 7. RECIPROCAL DISCIPLINE

A disciplinary order issued by the AZ Supreme Court will trigger reciprocal proceedings against the same attorney in their home state. For WCTL CA-licensed attorneys:
- AZ discipline → reported to State Bar of California under Cal. Rules of Court 9.20, B&P Code § 6049.1
- Likely produces identical or proportionate California discipline

This is a real risk vector for any cross-border lead-gen operation: a single AZ Bar complaint against the AZ-admitted partner can ripple through the CA license too. Mitigation is straightforward — keep substantiation files current, route AZ complaints to defense counsel immediately, document Practice 2.0 review where applicable.

## 8. THE PLAY (ER 5.5 Specific)

**Do (already in place per Davis):**
- Maintain AZ-admitted attorneys staffing WCTL's AZ office
- Route AZ-law work to AZ-admitted attorneys
- Disclose jurisdictional admissions on letterhead/website
- File pro hac vice for visiting CA-only partners on AZ litigation under Sup. Ct. Rule 39

**Don't:**
- Don't have CA-only attorneys give AZ-law advice from the AZ office on a systematic basis
- Don't "hold out" CA-only attorneys as AZ-admitted on Fair Case landing pages or ad copy
- Don't structure fees so CA AB 931 catches the WCTL side
- Don't let a CA-only attorney be the named contact for AZ matters
