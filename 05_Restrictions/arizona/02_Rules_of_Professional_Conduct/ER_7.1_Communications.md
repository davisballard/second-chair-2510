# ER 7.1 — Communications Concerning a Lawyer's Services
## Arizona Rules of Professional Conduct

> **Saul:** "ER 7.1 is the master rule. Everything else in the 7-series is downstream of it. The standard is simple — don't be false, don't be misleading. The way you actually fail it is sneakier: omitting a fact that makes the rest of the message read wrong, or building a copy line that creates an unjustified expectation. Substantiation is the antidote. Every claim has paper behind it."

---

## 1. THE RULE

### Core Prohibition
A lawyer shall not make a false or misleading communication about the lawyer or the lawyer's services. A lawyer shall not knowingly permit another person to make such communications on the lawyer's behalf.

### What Makes a Communication "False or Misleading"
- Contains a material misrepresentation of fact or law
- Omits a fact necessary to make the statement as a whole not materially misleading
- Creates unjustified expectations about results the lawyer can achieve
- States or implies the lawyer can achieve results by means that violate the Rules of Professional Conduct or other law
- Compares the lawyer's services with other lawyers' services without factual substantiation

### Required Disclosures (consolidated from former ER 7.2 in 2021)
- [ ] Name and contact information of at least one lawyer or law firm responsible for the content
- [ ] Contact information must include at least one of: office address, website, telephone number, or email address
- [ ] All disclosures must be **clear and conspicuous** — same size, color, duration, cadence, contrast, location, and audibility as the primary content, such that an ordinary person would easily read, hear, notice, and understand them

---

## 2. WHAT 2021 CONSOLIDATION CHANGED

Effective January 1, 2021, Arizona consolidated former ER 7.2 (Advertising) and ER 7.4 (Communication of Fields of Practice and Specialization) **into ER 7.1**. The Master Rule now carries all three substantive bodies. What stayed standalone: ER 7.3 (Solicitation) and ER 7.5 (Firm Names).

**What survived from former ER 7.2:**
- Advertising via written, recorded, or electronic communication is permitted
- Public media including TV, radio, print, internet, social media (Meta, Instagram, TikTok)
- Payment for the reasonable cost of advertisements is permitted
- Lead generator payment rules (see "Lead Generator Payment Rules" below)
- Arizona does **not** mandate retention of advertising copies (unlike FL, TX, NV)

**What survived from former ER 7.4:**
- Lawyer may communicate fields of practice
- Lawyer may say they "specialize" if they actually have expertise or limit practice (2021 change — see Specialization Language below)
- "Certified specialist" reserved for AZ Board of Legal Specialization or ABA-accredited certifications

---

## 3. SPECIALIZATION LANGUAGE (Post-2021)

Arizona is one of the few states that explicitly permits "specialize" language without certification, *if true*.

| Phrasing | Status |
|---|---|
| "Specialize in personal injury" | :white_check_mark: Permitted if practice is genuinely focused there |
| "Certified specialist in personal injury" | :x: Reserved for AZ Bd. of Legal Specialization or ABA-accredited certified attorneys |
| "Focuses on" / "experienced in" / "practice limited to" / "concentrates in" | :white_check_mark: Permitted, lower-risk phrasings |
| "Expert in" / "leading authority" | :warning: Risky — implies certification or unjustified superiority |

> **Saul:** "Use 'focuses on' or 'experienced in' for WCTL ad copy and you're golden. Save 'specialize' for cases where the firm genuinely runs that vertical exclusively. And never put 'certified specialist' on anything unless you've got the certificate framed on the wall."

## 4. PAST RESULTS

| Rule | Application |
|---|---|
| Past results may be referenced | :white_check_mark: Permitted |
| Disclaimer required? | :warning: Recommended but not technically mandated under AZ rules — best practice to include |
| Implied guarantee? | :x: Forbidden |
| Client consent required for case-identifying references? | :white_check_mark: Yes, before identifying a former client by name or distinctive facts |

**Standard past-results disclaimer language:**
```
Past results do not guarantee future outcomes. Each case is unique
and depends on its own facts and circumstances. Results may vary.
```

## 5. FEE INFORMATION

If fees are mentioned in any advertisement or landing page, ER 7.1 requires:
- Specify whether contingency or hourly
- For contingency fees: disclose whether the fee is calculated **before or after expenses**
- Disclose the client's potential portion of any costs

(See [`ER_1.5_Fees_and_Contingency.md`](ER_1.5_Fees_and_Contingency.md) for the full fee-agreement requirements.)

## 6. LEAD GENERATOR PAYMENT RULES (Critical for Second Chair)

A lawyer **may** pay others for generating client leads (e.g., internet-based lead-gen). The lead generator's communications must:

- Comply with ER 7.1 (no false or misleading content)
- **Not** recommend the lawyer — must present options or be neutral
- **Not** state, imply, or create a reasonable impression that:
  - It is recommending the lawyer
  - The referral is being made without payment from the lawyer
  - It has analyzed the person's legal problems to determine which lawyer should receive the referral

Any payment must be consistent with ER 1.5(e) (Fees) and the advertising rules.

> **Saul:** "Second Chair pays for ads, captures leads, and routes them to WCTL. That's lead generation. The line you can't cross: pretending Second Chair *evaluated* the case and *picked* WCTL as the right firm. We're a connector, not a matchmaker. The copy reflects that — 'connect with' / 'request a consultation from' / 'speak with a participating attorney.' Never 'we recommend' or 'we'll match you with the best.'"

(See [`11_Lead_Generator_vs_LRS_Arizona.md`](../11_Lead_Generator_vs_LRS_Arizona.md) for the full LRS-vs-lead-generator analysis.)

## 7. SUBSTANTIATION REQUIREMENT

ER 7.1's "false or misleading" standard is enforced through **substantiation**. Every factual claim in WCTL/Second Chair advertising must be backed by data the firm could produce within 14 days of an AZ Bar inquiry.

| Claim type | Substantiation required |
|---|---|
| Aggregate recovery ("$1.7 billion settled") | Internal records of historical settlements/verdicts, summed |
| Case-outcome rate ("97% of qualifying cases resolve favorably") | Closed-case data with definition of "favorable" |
| Specific case results ($X.M settlement) | Court records, settlement documents, redacted summaries |
| Review aggregates ("4.9/5 from 254 reviews") | Source platform (Google, Yelp, Avvo) screenshots and dates |
| "Years of experience" | Bar admission dates |

(See [`13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md`](../13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md) for the binder format.)

## 8. COMPLIANT vs. NON-COMPLIANT COPY

### Compliant (ER 7.1-safe)

| Copy line | Why compliant |
|---|---|
| "$1.7B+ recovered for clients" (with disclaimer + substantiation) | Factual; backed by records |
| "97% of qualifying cases reach a settlement or verdict" (with disclaimer) | Factual; backed by data |
| "Connect with a participating attorney" | Neutral, no recommendation |
| "Focuses on personal injury and motor vehicle crashes" | Truthful practice description |
| "2-year deadline to file under Arizona law (A.R.S. § 12-542)" | Factually accurate statutory reference |

### Non-Compliant (ER 7.1-violating)

| Copy line | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| "Best personal injury attorney in Arizona" | Subjective superlative, unsubstantiable |
| "$500,000 average settlement" without disclaimer | Implied guarantee, misleading |
| "We never lose" / "Guaranteed result" | Impossible standard |
| "We'll find the right lawyer for your case" | Implies analysis → triggers LRS classification + ER 7.1 deception |
| "Top-rated #1 firm" without certification basis | Unsubstantiated comparison |

## 9. ENFORCEMENT REALITY

- **Complaint-driven** — AZ State Bar does not pre-screen advertising. No filing requirement.
- Voluntary review available through the **Practice 2.0 Department**. (See [`14_Voluntary_Review_Practice_2.0.md`](../14_Voluntary_Review_Practice_2.0.md).)
- Disciplinary actions specifically for advertising are **rare** — most enforcement is for misrepresentation or fraud, trust account violations, and client abandonment.
- However: a complaint that lands triggers a 14-day response window. Substantiation file must be ready.

> **Saul:** "Arizona is the cleaner state to operate in if you've got TCPA-2025 dialed in and a substantiation binder ready. Bar isn't aggressive on lead-gen ads. But complaint-driven means *one* unhappy claimant who can't reach WCTL after submitting a quiz can light a fuse. Keep the paperwork ready and the copy honest."

---

## 10. THE PLAY (ER 7.1 Specific)

**Do:**
- Substantiate every factual claim in a tracked binder
- Use "focuses on" / "experienced in" for practice-area copy
- Include past-results disclaimers even though not technically required
- Identify WCTL as the responsible attorney/firm in every ad
- Specify contingency fee structure if fees are mentioned

**Don't:**
- Use unsupported superlatives ("best," "top," "#1")
- Imply Second Chair analyzed the case to pick WCTL
- Omit disclaimers next to results claims
- Use "certified specialist" without actual certification
- Run any claim that the firm couldn't back in a 14-day response
