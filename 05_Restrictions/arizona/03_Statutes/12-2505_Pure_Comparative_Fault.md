# A.R.S. § 12-2505 — Pure Comparative Fault

> **Saul:** "Pure comparative fault is a gift for ad copy. In a modified comparative fault state, you can't recover if you're 50%+ at fault. In Arizona? Even at 99% fault, you technically have a claim. That's a powerful message — 'You may still have a case' — and it's *true*. Just don't promise the moon."

---

## 1. THE STATUTE

A.R.S. § 12-2505 codifies Arizona's **pure comparative fault** rule:

> "The defense of contributory negligence or of assumption of the risk is in all cases a question of fact and shall, at all times, be left to the jury. **The defense of contributory negligence or of assumption of the risk shall not bar a plaintiff's recovery; rather, the trier of fact shall apportion fault and reduce the plaintiff's recovery in proportion to the plaintiff's contributory negligence.**"

**Translation:** Arizona doesn't have a 50% bar (modified comparative fault) or a 51% bar. A plaintiff who is 99% at fault for their own injuries can still recover 1% of their damages from a defendant who was 1% at fault.

---

## 2. THE THREE COMPARATIVE-FAULT REGIMES — WHERE AZ FITS

| Regime | States (examples) | Plaintiff recovers if % at fault is... |
|---|---|---|
| **Pure comparative fault** (AZ) | AZ, CA, FL, KY, LA, MS, NM, NY, RI, WA | Any percent under 100 (recovery proportionally reduced) |
| **Modified comparative — 50% bar** | AR, CO, GA, KS, ME, NE, ND, TN, UT | Less than 50% |
| **Modified comparative — 51% bar** | CT, DE, HI, IL, IN, IA, MA, MI, MN, NV, NH, NJ, OH, OK, OR, PA, SC, TX, VT, WI, WV, WY | 50% or less |
| **Pure contributory negligence** (1% bar) | AL, MD, NC, VA, DC | 0% only — any contribution bars recovery |

**Arizona is one of only 13 pure-comparative-fault states.** This is a structural advantage for plaintiff-side PI marketing in AZ.

---

## 3. THE COPY ANGLE

The pure-comparative-fault rule unlocks copy that wouldn't be ER 7.1-defensible in a modified-fault state:

### Compliant (under AZ pure comparative fault)

| Copy line | Why it's true and ER 7.1-safe |
|---|---|
| "Even if you were partially at fault, you may still have a case under Arizona law" | Factually accurate per § 12-2505 |
| "Don't let the insurance company tell you it was your fault — Arizona law protects you" | Urgency + accurate; insurance often overstates fault to deny |
| "Partial fault doesn't end your case in Arizona" | True; recovery is reduced, not barred |
| "Arizona is one of the few states where partial fault doesn't bar recovery" | True (12 other states have the same rule) |
| "If you were even 1% in the right, you may have a claim" | Mathematically accurate under pure comparative |

### What NOT to say

| Copy line | Why it's a problem |
|---|---|
| "Not at fault? Call us." | Implies fault is a prerequisite, which AZ doesn't require — leaves money on the table and is misleading by omission |
| "If you weren't at fault, you have a case" | Same problem — implies the converse is also true |
| "Innocent victims call now" | Implies "innocence" is a gating condition under AZ law; it isn't |
| "We win even when it's your fault!" | Overstates; recovery is *reduced* by fault, and a 99%-at-fault case is often economically not worth pursuing |

> **Saul:** "The trick is framing partial fault as a *non-disqualifier*, not promising a win. 'You may still have a case' is the magic phrase — it's true under § 12-2505, and it doesn't promise an outcome. Pair it with the substantiation file and you're bulletproof on ER 7.1."

---

## 4. THE NAMED-DEFENDANT WRINKLE

A.R.S. § 12-2506 — separate but related — abolished joint and several liability in most cases. Each defendant is liable only for their proportionate share of fault (with narrow exceptions: hazardous waste, intentional torts, agency relationships).

**Practical implication:** If WCTL takes a case where the at-fault defendant has only a 30% fault share, the defendant pays only 30% of damages. That's why "named non-party at fault" practice is critical in AZ — defendants will name everyone they can think of (the city for road conditions, the bar for over-serving, the manufacturer for defects) to dilute their share.

For ad copy: don't promise a recovery amount. The proportionate-share rule means a $1M damages case can collapse to $300K if fault is fragmented.

---

## 5. INTERACTION WITH UM/UIM

Arizona's pure comparative fault rule applies in UM/UIM (Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist) coverage too. If an injured AZ plaintiff is 40% at fault and the at-fault driver is uninsured, the plaintiff's UM claim against their own insurer is reduced by 40%.
