# A.R.S. § 12-821.01 — Government Claim Notice (180 Days)

> **Saul:** "Read this twice. Government cases don't run on the regular 2-year clock — they have a **180-day notice requirement** AND a **1-year SOL** (not 2). Miss either deadline, the case dies. The intake form has to flag any vehicle, employee, or condition that hints government, because the malpractice exposure on missing the 180-day notice is the kind of thing that ends a firm."

---

## 1. THE STATUTE

A.R.S. § 12-821.01 establishes the **notice of claim** requirement for actions against AZ public entities, public schools, and public employees.

### Subsection A — The 180-Day Notice
Persons with claims against a public entity, public school, or public employee shall file claims with the person authorized to accept service under the AZ Rules of Civil Procedure **within 180 days after the cause of action accrues**.

The notice must contain:
1. **Facts sufficient** to permit the public entity/school/employee to understand the basis on which liability is claimed
2. **A specific amount** for which the claim can be settled (the "sum certain")
3. **The facts supporting that amount**

### Subsection B — Accrual
A cause of action accrues "when the damaged party realizes he or she has been damaged and knows or reasonably should know the cause, source, act, event, instrumentality or condition that caused or contributed to the damage."

(Same discovery-rule jurisprudence as § 12-542 — but the 180-day clock runs from accrual, not from incident date.)

### Subsection C — Tolling
ADR / administrative-claims exhaustion tolls accrual until final decision date.

### Subsection D — Disability Tolling
Minors / insane / incompetent persons may file within 180 days after disability ceases.

### Subsection E — Deemed Denial
Claim is **deemed denied 60 days after filing** unless earlier written denial.

### Subsection H — Eminent Domain Excluded
Just-compensation claims (eminent domain) excluded from this regime.

---

## 2. THE 1-YEAR SOL TRAP — A.R.S. § 12-821

**Critical:** § 12-821 (the related statute) imposes a **1-year statute of limitations** on the underlying suit against a public entity — **NOT the standard 2-year SOL under § 12-542**.

The full timeline for a government-defendant case:

```
Day 0:    Cause of action accrues (date of injury or discovery)
Day 1-180: Window to file notice of claim
Day 180:  HARD DEADLINE — notice must be filed
Day 240:  Notice deemed denied (if no earlier written denial)
Day 365:  HARD DEADLINE — suit must be filed (1-year SOL under § 12-821)
```

Compare to a private-defendant case:

```
Day 0:    Cause of action accrues
Day 730:  HARD DEADLINE — suit must be filed (2-year SOL under § 12-542)
```

**Government cases run on roughly half the clock. Notice + suit must both be timely.**

---

## 3. STRICT COMPLIANCE STANDARD

AZ courts have repeatedly enforced **strict compliance** with the 180-day notice requirement. A defective notice (missing sum certain, missing supporting facts) is treated as **no notice**. Courts have rejected "substantial compliance" arguments for missing core elements.

**Practical implication:** PI lawyers in AZ keep notice-of-claim templates ready to deploy within days of intake on any government case. Backdating or post-hoc amendment of a defective notice generally won't save the claim.

---

## 4. WHAT TRIGGERS THE 180-DAY RULE

The notice requirement attaches to claims against:
- **State of Arizona** (any state agency, ASU, UA, NAU, AZ DPS, ADOT, AZ DCS, etc.)
- **Counties** (Maricopa, Pima, Yavapai, Coconino, etc.)
- **Cities and towns** (Phoenix, Tucson, Mesa, Scottsdale, etc.)
- **School districts** (Mesa Unified, Phoenix Union, TUSD, etc.)
- **Public charter schools** (depends on governance structure)
- **Special districts** (water, fire, irrigation, transit)
- **Public employees** acting within scope of employment

## 5. PI INTAKE — WHAT TO FLAG

The Fair Case quiz should flag any of the following for **government-vehicle** or **government-employee** triage:

| Indicator | Likely public entity |
|---|---|
| Vehicle was a school bus | School district |
| Vehicle was a city bus, Sun Tran, Valley Metro | City / regional transit authority |
| Vehicle was a police cruiser, sheriff vehicle | City / county / state DPS |
| Vehicle was a fire truck or ambulance (public) | City / fire district |
| Vehicle was a state DOT truck (orange/state-decal) | ADOT |
| Other driver was on duty as a government employee | Public entity (employer) |
| Crash occurred on construction zone managed by government | Public entity (depending on contractor allocation) |
| Slip-fall on government property (city sidewalk, school grounds, etc.) | Public entity |
| Defective road condition (potholes, signage, design) | Government with design/maintenance authority |
| ASU/UA/NAU shuttle, university vehicle | State university (state entity) |
| Tribal vehicle, tribal employee, tribal police | NOT § 12-821.01 — see § 6 below |

**On any flag:** route immediately for notice-of-claim drafting and 180-day countdown.

---

## 6. TRIBAL ENTITIES — DIFFERENT REGIME

Crashes involving tribal-government employees (Navajo Nation police, Salt River Pima-Maricopa transit, Gila River casino shuttle, Tohono O'odham public works, etc.) generally do **NOT** fall under § 12-821.01.

Possible alternative regimes:
- **Tribal sovereign immunity** + tribal court jurisdiction → check the specific tribe's tort claims act
- **Federal Tort Claims Act (FTCA)** if the tribal employee was operating under a Self-Determination Act § 638 contract (notably IHS providers and many tribal police) → 2-year administrative-claim window to HHS Office of General Counsel or relevant federal agency

**Misclassifying tribal cases as state-government cases is a known trap.** Tribal cases need their own analysis.

(See [`../06_Tribal_Jurisdiction.md`](../06_Tribal_Jurisdiction.md) for full tribal jurisdiction framework.)

## 7. ADVERTISING / LEAD-GEN IMPLICATIONS

### Time-urgency framing — accurate AND useful
"Don't wait — Arizona requires action within 6 months for crashes involving government vehicles." — **factually accurate**, ER 7.1-defensible, distinguishes government cases from regular 2-year SOL cases.

### Form question architecture
The Fair Case quiz should include a question like:
- "Was any vehicle involved a government vehicle (police, school bus, city bus, etc.)?"
  - Yes → priority routing flag for WCTL intake
  - Unsure → priority routing flag for WCTL intake
  - No → standard routing

The "Unsure" branch is important — many claimants don't know if a vehicle was government-owned, so the question should accept ambiguity and let WCTL's intake team make the call.

### Slip-fall localization
For premises liability cases, the form should ask whether the location was government property (sidewalk, school, government building). This triggers government-claim analysis.

---

## 8. THE PLAY (§ 12-821.01 Specific)

**Do:**
- Flag any government-vehicle, government-employee, or government-property indicator at intake for priority routing
- Use accurate 180-day urgency in ad copy for government-case-relevant verticals
- Train WCTL intake on the 180-day notice + 1-year SOL combined deadline
- Maintain notice-of-claim templates ready to deploy within days of intake
- Distinguish tribal entities from state/local government — different regime

**Don't:**
- Don't run any government-vehicle case on the standard 2-year clock — it's 1 year
- Don't accept "substantial compliance" framing on a defective notice
- Don't treat tribal entities as covered by § 12-821.01 (they're not)
- Don't delay notice while investigating; the 180-day clock doesn't pause
