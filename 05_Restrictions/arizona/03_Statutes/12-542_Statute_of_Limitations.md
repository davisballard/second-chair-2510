# A.R.S. § 12-542 — Statute of Limitations (Personal Injury)

> **Saul:** "Two years. From the date of injury, or — if it's a delayed-discovery case — from the date a reasonable person would have known. The form should filter out anyone outside that window with a graceful soft-decline. Don't waste their time, don't waste WCTL's intake."

---

## 1. THE STATUTE

A.R.S. § 12-542 establishes a **two-year statute of limitations** for personal injury actions, including:

- Bodily injury claims (motor vehicle, slip-and-fall, premises liability)
- Wrongful death (separate provision under A.R.S. § 12-542(2), also two years)
- Products liability injury (separate two-year provision under § 12-551)
- Trespass to person or property
- Conversion

**The clock starts:** on the date the cause of action **accrues**, which is generally the date of injury — except where the **discovery rule** applies (latent injuries, fraud-concealed injuries, medical malpractice with delayed-discovery facts).

---

## 2. EXCEPTIONS / TOLLING

| Circumstance | Effect on SOL |
|---|---|
| Plaintiff is a minor at time of injury | SOL tolled until plaintiff turns 18; full 2 years available after (A.R.S. § 12-502) |
| Plaintiff has a mental incapacity at time of injury | SOL tolled until incapacity removed (§ 12-502) |
| Defendant is absent from state or evading service | SOL tolled during absence (§ 12-501) |
| Discovery rule applies (latent injury, fraud) | SOL runs from date plaintiff "knew or should have known" of injury and its cause |
| Action against a public entity / public employee | **180-day claim notice required first** (see [`12-821-01_Government_Claim_Notice.md`](12-821-01_Government_Claim_Notice.md)); separate 1-year SOL on the action itself |
| Wrongful death | 2 years from date of death (not date of underlying injury) |
| Medical malpractice | 2 years; discovery rule applies (§ 12-542 + case law); preliminary expert affidavit required (see [`12-2603_MedMal_Affidavit.md`](12-2603_MedMal_Affidavit.md)) |
| Asbestos / latent disease | Discovery rule, generally 2 years from diagnosis |

## 3. COMPARATIVE CONTEXT

| State | PI SOL | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Arizona** | **2 years** (12-542) | Discovery rule available |
| California | 2 years (CCP § 335.1) | Discovery rule available |
| Texas | 2 years (CPRC § 16.003) | Discovery rule limited |
| Florida | 2 years (post-2023; was 4 years pre-Tort Reform Act of 2023) | Major change in 2023 |
| Nevada | 2 years (NRS 11.190(4)(e)) | Discovery rule available |
| Colorado | 2 years (CRS § 13-80-102); 3 years for motor vehicle (CRS § 13-80-101) | Auto carve-out |
| Washington | 3 years (RCW 4.16.080) | Longest of west-coast peers |

## 4. APPLICATION TO FAIR CASE QUIZ FUNNEL

The Fair Case quiz includes a **date-of-incident question** (typically Q2 or Q3) that branches:

| Date of incident | Routing |
|---|---|
| Within last 24 months | :white_check_mark: Pass to next question (in-window) |
| 22–24 months ago | :warning: Soft warning, urgency framing ("Arizona requires action within 2 years") |
| Older than 24 months | :x: Soft-decline branch ("Unfortunately, Arizona's 2-year deadline has passed for most claims. If your case involves a delayed-discovery situation, you may still have options — please contact us directly.") |

The soft-decline branch is important under ER 7.1: it doesn't *guarantee* the case is dead (discovery rule, tolling can apply), but it filters out 98%+ of out-of-window claimants without making a false statement.

## 5. ADVERTISING IMPLICATIONS

- "Arizona's 2-year deadline" / "You have 2 years to file under Arizona law" — **factually accurate**, ER 7.1-compliant
- "Don't wait — Arizona law gives you 24 months" — **acceptable urgency framing**
- "If your accident was within the last 2 years, you may still have a case" — **ER 7.1-compliant**
- "Time is running out — call now" — **acceptable** if not coupled with implied promises
- "Don't lose your right to compensation" — **acceptable** as urgency, but pair with truthful framing
- Avoid: implying that exactly 2 years is always the right answer when discovery rule, government-entity claims, or tolling could change it

> **Saul:** "Two-year SOL is the cleanest, most universally true urgency hook in PI advertising. Use it. Just don't say it like the 2-year is some company policy — it's the statute. A.R.S. 12-542. Cite if you want to flex."
