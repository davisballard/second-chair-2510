# A.R.S. § 12-2603 — Medical Malpractice Preliminary Expert Affidavit

> **Saul:** "Med-mal in Arizona has its own gate: a preliminary expert affidavit. No expert, no case. The affidavit costs $2K-$10K just to obtain. Most med-mal leads don't survive the affidavit gate, so case selection has to front-load the expert question. Lead-gen ad copy that promises 'free med-mal evaluation' is fine — just understand that 'evaluation' means 'we'll figure out fast whether an expert will sign'."

---

## 1. THE STATUTE

A.R.S. § 12-2603 imposes a preliminary expert opinion affidavit requirement on medical malpractice claims.

### Subsection A — Initial Certification
When a med-mal claim is filed, the claimant (or party designating a non-party at fault) must certify in a written statement filed and served with the **complaint** (or pleading raising the claim) **whether or not expert opinion testimony is necessary** to prove the standard of care or liability.

### Subsection B — Affidavit Timing
If expert testimony is certified as necessary, the preliminary expert opinion affidavit must be served:
- **With the initial Rule 26.1 disclosures** (which under AZ Rule 26.1 are due 40 days after defendant's answer), OR
- For a non-party at fault designation, **within 60 days after filing the designation**

### Subsection B — Required Content
The affidavit must state:
1. **Expert's qualifications** to opine on the relevant standard of care
2. **Factual basis** for each claim against each health-care professional
3. **Specific acts, errors, or omissions** the expert considers a violation of the applicable standard of care
4. **The manner** in which those violations caused or contributed to the claimed damages

### Subsections D-E — Disputes
Defendants may move challenging a "no expert needed" certification; the court resolves and stays other proceedings pending its ruling.

### Subsection F — Failure Consequence
Court "shall dismiss the claim against the health care professional or designated non-party at fault **without prejudice**" if claimant fails to file/serve a required affidavit.

**Critical:** Dismissal *without prejudice* matters because the **2-year SOL likely keeps running** — refiling is often time-barred. A dismissal without prejudice is functionally a dismissal with prejudice if the SOL has expired.

### Subsection G — Cure Window
Court must allow reasonable time to **cure** an insufficient affidavit before dismissing. Insufficient ≠ missing.

---

## 2. WHAT THIS MEANS FOR PI LEAD-GEN

### Med-mal cases are different from other PI verticals
- Auto crashes, slip-falls, premises liability — most don't require an upfront expert affidavit
- Med-mal — **always requires expert affidavit unless the case fits a "common knowledge" exception** (sponge left in patient, wrong-limb surgery, etc.)
- Common-knowledge exceptions still require the certification to be filed; the affidavit may not be required, but the certification is

### Economic implications
- Expert affidavits cost **$2K-$10K+** to obtain (board-matched physician, written opinion)
- Many cases that look like good med-mal leads can't get expert support
- WCTL intake must front-load the expert question: is this a case where a physician will sign an affidavit?

### Constitutionality
The Arizona Supreme Court has upheld § 12-2603 against constitutional challenge (right to access courts, separation of powers).

---

## 3. PRE-SUIT DEMAND STRATEGY

The affidavit is **not** required pre-suit — it's required after suit is filed (with disclosures, not the complaint itself). However:

- Pre-suit demand letters do not legally need the affidavit attached
- BUT credible PI shops will already have an expert lined up before sending demand because:
  - Defense carriers won't take demands seriously without expert support
  - The case will fail at disclosure if no expert can be retained
  - The expert's view shapes settlement valuation

**Best practice:** retain expert (at least informally) before sending the demand letter; convert to formal affidavit when suit is filed.

## 4. AD COPY IMPLICATIONS

### Compliant
- "Injured by medical malpractice? We'll evaluate your case." — true; "evaluation" includes the affidavit/expert assessment
- "Free consultation for medical malpractice claims" — true; consultation is free
- "Medical malpractice cases require strict deadlines and expert review under Arizona law" — accurate, ER 7.1-compliant urgency

### Problematic
- "Win your medical malpractice case — guaranteed!" — misleading, ER 7.1 violation
- "We take every medical malpractice case" — misleading; med-mal case selection is necessarily heavy
- "No upfront cost — even for medical malpractice" — technically true (contingency fee), but misleading if claimant interprets as "no costs to me ever" (case costs are typically deducted from any recovery)

### What to disclose
- Med-mal cases are subject to expert-affidavit requirements that may filter many claims
- Case investigation includes consultation with medical experts
- 2-year SOL applies; discovery rule may extend in delayed-diagnosis cases

## 5. INTAKE QUESTIONS (FAIR CASE FUNNEL)

For med-mal-flagged cases, additional intake questions:

| Question | Why |
|---|---|
| Where did the medical care occur (hospital, clinic, doctor's office)? | Identify defendant entity for conflict check + venue |
| Who was the treating provider (specialty, name if known)? | Identify defendant + match to expert specialty |
| What was the medical issue / procedure? | Determine case type (surgical error, misdiagnosis, medication, birth injury) |
| Did you obtain medical records? | Records availability gates expert review |
| Approximate date of incident? | SOL filtering (2-year + discovery rule) |

## 6. INTERACTION WITH OTHER STATUTES

| Statute | Interaction |
|---|---|
| A.R.S. § 12-542 (2-year SOL) | Applies to med-mal; discovery rule available |
| A.R.S. § 12-821.01 (180-day gov notice) | Triggered if defendant is public hospital, university medical center, county health system, etc. — adds 180-day notice + 1-year SOL on top of expert-affidavit requirement |
| ER 1.5(c) (contingency fee writing) | Applies; signed retainer at engagement before case investigation costs |
| ER 7.1 (truthful advertising) | Applies; ad copy must not promise med-mal recovery |

---

## 7. THE PLAY (§ 12-2603 Specific)

**Do:**
- Treat med-mal as a separate intake vertical with extra screening questions
- Maintain a panel of board-matched expert physicians for affidavit purposes
- Filter cases based on records availability and expert appetite before significant investment
- Be straightforward in ad copy: "evaluation" means an honest screen, not a guaranteed acceptance

**Don't:**
- Don't promise med-mal recovery in ad copy
- Don't take a med-mal case without an expert path identified
- Don't assume the 2-year SOL is the only deadline (discovery rule and government-defendant overlay can change it)
- Don't accept "no expert needed" certification unless a clear common-knowledge exception applies
