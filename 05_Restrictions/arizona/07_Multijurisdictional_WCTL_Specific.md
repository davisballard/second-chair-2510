## 3. WHAT THIS MEANS FOR FAIR CASE OPERATIONS

### Lead routing
AZ-targeted Fair Case ads → quiz submission → Second Chair routes lead to WCTL → **WCTL's AZ-admitted attorney(s)** handle the conflict check, the prospective-client consultation (ER 1.18), the engagement letter (ER 1.5(c)), and the AZ-law representation.

### Attorney identification
Fair Case landing pages name WCTL as the receiving firm. The TCPA consent block (under FCC 2025 one-to-one consent) names WCTL specifically. The engagement letter identifies the WCTL AZ-admitted attorney as the responsible attorney of record.

### Disclosure architecture
Per ER 7.1 (consolidated post-2021), the firm's licensed jurisdictions are disclosed. For AZ-targeted creative, the relevant disclosure is: WCTL is licensed to practice in Arizona (and other states); the AZ-admitted attorney is the responsible attorney of record.

## 4. CALIFORNIA AB 931 — DOCUMENTED, NOT TRIGGERED

**Effective January 1, 2026** (sunset January 1, 2030), California AB 931 prohibits California lawyers from sharing fees with an out-of-state ABS.

**Why this is documented but not triggered for SC/WCTL:**

| Element | Status for SC/WCTL |
|---|---|
| Is Second Chair an ABS? | **No** — SC is a non-law-firm advertising vendor; no AZ ABS license held |
| Does the SC↔WCTL fee structure share fees with an out-of-state ABS? | **No** — there's no ABS in the structure |
| Are CA-licensed WCTL attorneys compensated from AZ-fee flow? | **AZ fees flow to AZ-admitted attorneys** at WCTL's AZ office; CA-attorney compensation is separate |
| Does the structure create AB 931 exposure? | **No** |

The rule is documented in [`02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md`](02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md) for completeness and for future reference if SC ever expands to ABS structures or other state-fee-sharing models.

## 5. RECIPROCAL DISCIPLINE — REAL RISK VECTOR

A disciplinary order issued by the AZ Supreme Court will trigger reciprocal proceedings against the same attorney in their home state.

For WCTL CA-licensed attorneys (those appearing pro hac vice in AZ or otherwise touching AZ matters):
- AZ discipline → reported to State Bar of California under Cal. Rules of Court 9.20, B&P Code § 6049.1
- Likely produces identical or proportionate California discipline

**Mitigation:**
- Substantiation file maintained current (see [`13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md`](13_Substantiation_File_Spec.md))
- Any AZ Bar inquiry routed to defense counsel immediately
- Practice 2.0 review log documented (see [`14_Voluntary_Review_Practice_2.0.md`](14_Voluntary_Review_Practice_2.0.md))
- Engagement letter compliance enforced (ER 1.5(c) writing requirement)

## 6. WHAT WOULD BREAK THE STRUCTURE

If any of the following changed, the multijurisdictional posture would need re-evaluation:

| Change | Risk created |
|---|---|
| AZ-admitted attorney leaves WCTL's AZ office without replacement | ER 5.5 violation: out-of-state firm with AZ office but no AZ counsel |
| CA-only partners begin systematically practicing AZ law from AZ office | ER 5.5 + UPL Op. 10-02 violation |
| Fair Case landing page identifies a CA-only attorney as the AZ responsible attorney | ER 7.1 + 7.5 problem |
| WCTL applies for AZ ABS license with non-lawyer (CA) compensation tied to AZ fees | Potential AB 931 trigger if CA attorneys are fee-share parties |
| Engagement letter omits ER 1.5(f) Second Chair compensation disclosure | Engagement defect; ER 1.5 violation |
| AZ-targeted ads run without WCTL's AZ attorney named as responsible attorney | ER 7.1 disclosure violation |

These are the pressure points to monitor.
