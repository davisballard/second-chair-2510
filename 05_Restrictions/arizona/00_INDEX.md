## TL;DR (READ THIS FIRST)

**Arizona is structurally favorable for SC/WCTL operations.** Key advantages:
- **ER 5.4 eliminated** (Jan 1, 2021) — fee-sharing with non-lawyers explicitly permitted
- **Pure comparative fault** — even 99%-at-fault plaintiffs can recover
- **Bates v. State Bar of Arizona (1977)** — constitutional protection for truthful lawyer advertising originated here; Bar enforcement posture has remained light
- **No SB37 analogue** — crash footage, settlement amounts, fighter positioning all permitted with disclaimers
- **No state TCPA layer** — federal FCC 2025 rule is the binding consent standard
- **WCTL multijurisdictional structure handled** — AZ-admitted attorneys staff the AZ office

**Live risks:**
- ER 7.1 substantiation (every factual claim → backing record within 14 days)
- ER 7.5 trade-name confusion (Fair Case must be clearly disclosed as non-firm)
- ER 1.18 prospective-client duties (every form submission attaches at WCTL)
- Government-defendant cases (180-day notice + 1-year SOL, not 2-year)
- Tribal-land cases (different jurisdictional regime; 30-90 day notice on tribal entity defendants)
- Reciprocal discipline (AZ Bar order against CA-licensed WCTL attorney → CA discipline)

**Canonical substantiation figure:** **$1.7 billion** aggregate WCTL settlements.
