# Overview and Risk Posture (Arizona)

> **Saul:** "If California is the red light and Nevada is the green light, Arizona is the green arrow. You've got a protected left turn — just don't run the oncoming traffic. Aggressive, truthful PI advertising is on the strongest constitutional footing in Arizona of any state in the country. Bates happened here. ER 5.4 was eliminated here. The Bar is complaint-driven, not aggressive. Just keep the substantiation file ready and the disclosures clear."

## 2. WHY ARIZONA IS UNIQUE

### Bates Heritage
Arizona is the home of *Bates v. State Bar of Arizona*, 433 U.S. 350 (1977) — the SCOTUS case that established attorney advertising as constitutionally protected commercial speech. The AZ Bar's posture has been less aggressive than other states partly because its own ER was the rule struck down. (See [`04_Case_Law/Bates_v_State_Bar_1977.md`](04_Case_Law/Bates_v_State_Bar_1977.md).)

### ER 5.4 Eliminated (First in the Country)
Effective January 1, 2021, Arizona eliminated ER 5.4 — the prohibition on (a) sharing legal fees with non-lawyers, (b) forming partnerships with non-lawyers if any of the activities consist of the practice of law, and (c) permitting non-lawyer ownership of a law firm. **Arizona is the first U.S. state to fully repeal ER 5.4.** (See [`02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md`](02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_5.4_Elimination_and_ABS.md).)

### Pure Comparative Fault
A.R.S. § 12-2505 establishes pure comparative fault — a plaintiff 99% at fault still recovers 1% of damages. Most states are modified comparative (50% or 51% bar) or pure contributory (1% bar). AZ is one of only 13 pure-comparative-fault states. (See [`03_Statutes/12-2505_Pure_Comparative_Fault.md`](03_Statutes/12-2505_Pure_Comparative_Fault.md).)

### Specialization Language Permitted
Post-2021, AZ explicitly permits "specialize in personal injury" if the practice is genuinely focused there. Most states require formal certification before the word "specialize" can be used. (See [`02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_7.1_Communications.md`](02_Rules_of_Professional_Conduct/ER_7.1_Communications.md).)

## 3. RISK POSTURE FOR SECOND CHAIR / WCTL

### Structural advantages (compared to operating in CA, TX, FL, NY)
- Lead-gen fee structure is **explicitly lawful** — no ER 5.4 worry
- Trade name "Fair Case" is permitted with clear disclosures (no Texas-style mandatory format)
- Aggressive copy register is constitutionally protected
- Crash footage allowed with context (no SB37 analogue)
- Settlement amounts permitted with disclaimers
- Pure comparative fault unlocks "even partial fault doesn't end your case" copy

### Live risks
- **ER 7.1 substantiation** — every factual claim ($1.7B settled, 97% settlement rate, 4.9-star reviews, table amounts) must be backed by records the firm could produce within 14 days of an AZ Bar inquiry
- **ER 7.5 trade-name confusion** — Fair Case must be clearly disclosed as a non-law-firm operated by Second Chair, with WCTL named separately on the form
- **ER 1.18 prospective-client attachment** — every form submission attaches ER 1.18 duties at WCTL; conflict-checks required before substantive consultation
- **Government-defendant cases** — 180-day notice + 1-year SOL combined deadline, not the standard 2-year clock
- **Tribal-land cases** — different jurisdictional regime; intake should flag for triage
- **Reciprocal discipline** — an AZ Bar order against a CA-licensed WCTL attorney triggers proportionate California discipline

### Risks to watch but currently mitigated
- **Multijurisdictional / out-of-state firm operations (ER 5.5)** — WCTL has out-of-state offices handled per Davis (AZ-admitted attorneys staff the AZ office). (See [`07_Multijurisdictional_WCTL_Specific.md`](07_Multijurisdictional_WCTL_Specific.md).)
- **California AB 931** (effective Jan 1, 2026) — prohibits CA lawyers from sharing fees with out-of-state ABS. Not a live risk for SC/WCTL because the structure routes AZ fees to AZ-admitted attorneys, not through an ABS that would catch CA-licensed attorneys' compensation.
