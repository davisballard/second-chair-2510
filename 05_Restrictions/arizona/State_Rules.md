## What Changed

The previous single-file structure covered ER 7.1/7.3/7.5, comparative fault, SOL, Consumer Fraud Act, market data, disclosures, and a checklist. The new multi-file structure preserves all of that and adds:

- **ER 5.4 elimination + full ABS framework** (R-20-0034, ACJA § 7-209, ~151 licensed entities)
- **ER 5.5 multijurisdictional** (out-of-state firm operations; pro hac vice Sup. Ct. Rule 39)
- **ER 1.18 prospective-client analysis** (when form submission attaches duties; conflict-check architecture)
- **ER 1.6 confidentiality** (with AZ-specific tech-competence duty)
- **ER 1.5 fees** (contingency writing requirement; ER 1.5(e)/(f) post-2021)
- **Tribal jurisdiction** (22 tribes; *Strate* framework; sovereign immunity)
- **AZ insurance regime** (25/50/15 minimums; UM/UIM; bad-faith framework)
- **Government claim notice** (180-day rule + 1-year SOL trap)
- **Med-mal expert affidavit** (12-2603)
- **Bates v. State Bar of Arizona full case treatment**
- **Recent discipline survey** (Legal Help Partners PDJ-2024-9035)
- **Substantiation file spec** (canonical $1.7B figure)
- **Practice 2.0 voluntary review** (602-340-7332)
- **Full disciplinary process** (intake → LRO → ADPCC → PDJ → Commission → Supreme Court; sanctions ladder; reciprocal discipline)

This redirect file may be removed once any external references have been updated.
