## 1. WHY ARIZONA IS THE GREEN ARROW

If California is the red light and Nevada is the green light, **Arizona is the green arrow**. Aggressive, truthful PI advertising has the strongest constitutional and regulatory footing in Arizona of any state in the country.

**The four structural advantages:**

1. **Bates v. State Bar of Arizona (1977)** happened here. The AZ Supreme Court was directly reversed. The AZ Bar's posture has remained complaint-driven and light-handed ever since.

2. **ER 5.4 was eliminated** January 1, 2021 — Arizona is the first U.S. state to fully repeal the fee-sharing prohibition. Lead-gen vendor relationships are explicitly lawful. Non-lawyer ownership of law firms is licensable through ABS. **Arizona is the deregulated frontier of legal-services structure.**

3. **Pure comparative fault** (A.R.S. § 12-2505) — even 99%-at-fault plaintiffs can recover. This unlocks "even partial fault doesn't end your case" copy that wouldn't be ER 7.1-defensible in modified-fault states.

4. **No SB37 analogue.** Crash footage, settlement amounts, fighter positioning all permitted with disclaimers. Aggressive register is fine. Sensationalism isn't — different thing.

## 2. THE PLAY (TL;DR)

### Do (constantly)
- **Substantiate every factual claim.** Build the substantiation file. Use $1.7B as the canonical aggregate. Refresh quarterly.
- **Use "focuses on" / "experienced in"** for practice-area copy. Use "specialize" if it's actually true. Skip "certified specialist" unless the certificate is on the wall.
- **Pair every results claim with the past-results disclaimer.**
- **Identify WCTL by name** in every Fair Case ad and form. Name the responsible AZ-admitted attorney where appropriate.
- **Maintain the "Fair Case is operated by Second Chair. Not a law firm."** disclosure in clear-and-conspicuous prominence — same size, color, contrast as the body text.
- **Run the pre-launch checklist** ([`16_Pre_Launch_Compliance_Checklist.md`](16_Pre_Launch_Compliance_Checklist.md)) before every new AZ campaign.
- **Submit landing-page templates to Practice 2.0** for voluntary review. Document the review log. It's mitigation evidence.
- **Triage tribal-land cases** at intake (where did the crash happen?) and government-vehicle cases (180-day notice trap).
- **Build Spanish-language creative as a first-class peer** — Phoenix is 42% Hispanic, Nogales is 93%.

### Don't (ever)
- **Don't use unsupported superlatives** ("best," "top," "#1," "leading"). AZ views these as potentially deceptive under ER 7.1.
- **Don't use selection language** ("we'll match you" / "best fit for you" / "we recommend"). That flips Second Chair into unregistered LRS territory.
- **Don't run AI lip-sync deepfakes** (Meta ban). Don't run AI-generated "testimonials" claiming results.
- **Don't have CA-only WCTL attorneys give AZ-law advice** from the AZ office on a systematic basis.
- **Don't run a government-vehicle case on the standard 2-year clock** — it's 1 year + 180-day notice.
- **Don't slap "NOTICE: THIS IS AN ADVERTISEMENT!"** on Meta creative. That's the wrong rule (ER 7.3 direct-mail format), and it looks amateur.
- **Don't promise specific recovery amounts** in ad copy. Use ranges with disclaimers, or focus on categories of recovery.

## 4. THE LIVE TRIPWIRES

### ER 7.1 substantiation
Every factual claim must have backing. **$1.7B canonical.** Quarterly refresh. 14-day response capability.

### ER 7.5 trade-name confusion
Fair Case can't read like a law firm to a reasonable claimant. Footer disclosure + WCTL named on form before submission. Don't write "Fair Case lawyers."

### ER 1.18 prospective-client attachment
Every form submission creates ER 1.18 duties at WCTL. Run conflict checks before substantive consultation. Don't ask the form for case strategy or settlement views — keep it to identifiers + claim category.

### ER 1.5 contingency writing
Engagement letter at engagement, signed. AZ COA has held: no writing, no fee. Bake the ER 1.5(f) Second Chair disclosure into WCTL's standard letter.

### Government-defendant trap
180-day notice + 1-year SOL. The 2-year clock does NOT apply.

### Tribal-land cases
*Strate* governs state-highway-on-tribal-land jurisdiction (state court has jurisdiction). Anything with an Indian party or tribal entity needs separate analysis. Sovereign immunity, 30-90 day tribal notice, FTCA where applicable.

### Reciprocal discipline
AZ Bar order against a CA-licensed WCTL attorney → CA proportionate discipline. Substantiation file + Practice 2.0 review log + defense-counsel-on-deck are the mitigation stack.

## 5. THE DISCIPLINE-RESPONSE MUSCLE

When (not if) a complaint hits:

1. **Hour 0:** Notify WCTL compliance lawyer immediately
2. **Hour 24:** Engage defense counsel (CA + AZ for cross-border attorneys); pull substantiation file; pull Practice 2.0 review log
3. **Day 12:** Response drafted, defense-counsel reviewed
4. **Day 14:** Submitted before deadline
5. **Throughout:** Document everything; do not communicate with complainant outside formal channels; do not alter records

This muscle gets exercised on ad-creative claims that haven't been substantiated, on disclosure-architecture failures, on inadvertent ER 7.1 misleading-omission issues. Most cases die at intake or LRO screening. The substantiation file plus Practice 2.0 review log is what gets cases dismissed early.

## 6. SAUL'S BOTTOM LINE

> Arizona is the cleanest US state to operate a personal-injury lead-gen funnel of any size. The structural advantages — Bates heritage, ER 5.4 elimination, pure comparative fault, complaint-driven enforcement, no SB37 — combine to give SC/WCTL the strongest legal footing of any state.
>
> The cost of those advantages: maintain the substantiation file, keep the disclosures clear-and-conspicuous, route AZ-law work to AZ-admitted attorneys, run the pre-launch checklist before every campaign, and submit creative to Practice 2.0 when something material changes.
>
> Do those things, and you can run AZ at scale, with confidence, and with less compliance friction than any other state.
>
> Don't do those things, and the same green-arrow latitude becomes a clear runway for an embarrassing complaint to reach a Disciplinary Commission and ripple into California through reciprocal discipline. You don't want that. Nobody wants that.
