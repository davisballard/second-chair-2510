### Section 5: Qualification Standards

**Headline:**
```
What "Qualified" Actually Means
```

**Subheadline:**
```
We don't just ask if they were injured. We verify case viability using the same criteria your intake team uses.
```

**7-Question Intake Script:**

1. **Were you injured in the accident?**
   - Gate question (no injury = no case)

2. **Did you see a doctor or get medical care?**
   - Treatment verification (ER, hospital, urgent care, doctor)

3. **When did the crash happen?**
   - Statute of limitations check (varies by state)

4. **Who was at fault?**
   - Liability screening (other driver, shared, unclear)

5. **Is the other driver insured?**
   - Recovery screening (insurance company, UM coverage)

6. **Can you briefly describe what happened?**
   - Narrative check (red flags, injury mechanism)

7. **Do you already have an attorney?**
   - Disqualifier

**Plus State-Specific Screening:**

- **No-fault states (FL, NY, MI, MA):** Tort threshold verification (permanent injury, fracture, hospitalization, $X medical bills)
- **Choice no-fault states (PA, NJ, KY):** Tort election status (full vs. limited tort)
- **Contributory negligence states (DC, MD, VA, NC, AL):** Zero-fault verification

**Quality target:** >85% of qualified leads accepted by firm as viable cases

### Section 9: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I start with standard tier and upgrade later?**

A: Absolutely. Most firms start with standard tier to test lead quality, then add premium tier for a portion of their leads once they see conversion rates. Premium tier is an optional upgrade, not a requirement.

---

**Q: How do you bill for premium tier?**

A: Base lead package is billed upfront (1st of month). Premium surcharges are billed weekly based on actual premium lead delivery. Example: If you order 100 leads and 20 go premium in Week 1, we invoice you for 20 × $1,200 = $24,000 on the following Monday (due net-7).

---

**Q: What if the lead doesn't answer when you call?**

A: If the lead doesn't answer, we don't bill the premium surcharge. You only pay for premium tier when we successfully complete the qualification call. No call completion = no premium billing.

---

**Q: What if the firm is unavailable for live transfer?**

A: We still bill the premium surcharge (pay-per-transfer model) because we qualified the lead. You'll receive detailed intake notes, call recording, and an urgent priority flag. Your team calls back within 5 minutes with a warm introduction. Industry data shows this still converts at 25-35% (better than standard web leads at 10-15%).

---

**Q: Do you guarantee conversion rates?**

A: We don't guarantee specific conversion rates (we can't control your intake team's closing ability), but we do provide weekly reporting so you can track performance by tier. Industry benchmarks are 10-15% for web leads, 15-25% for AI-qualified, and 40-60% for live transfers. If your conversion rates are significantly below benchmarks, we'll work with you to troubleshoot (lead quality, intake script, response time, etc.).

---

**Q: Can I get 24/7 human live transfers?**

A: Our default availability is M-F 9am-6pm PT. For firms with 24/7 intake capability (like Jacoby & Meyers), we can discuss custom availability hours. Note: 24/7 human availability may require higher premium or dedicated intake specialist (we'll discuss economics on discovery call).

---

**Q: How is this different from Settlement Allies or other CPA vendors?**

A: Settlement Allies charges $2,500-$6,500 per signed case with review-before-pay. You take zero conversion risk, but you pay 2-3x per case and wait 30-60 days for billing. Our premium tier charges $1,500 average per lead with 40-60% conversion = $2,500-$3,750 per signed case. You take conversion risk, but you get faster cash flow, Market exclusivity, and full control over which cases you sign. Which model is better depends on your priorities (risk vs. cash flow).

---

**Q: What states do you cover?**

A: All 50 states. Pricing varies by Market. Use our pricing calculator to see your market's base rate.

---

**Q: What happens if lead quality is poor?**

A: We track qualification accuracy (% of SC-qualified leads that your firm accepts as viable cases). Target is >85%. If you're rejecting >20% of qualified leads, we review call recordings together to identify the issue (qualification script adherence, state-specific criteria, etc.). We stand behind our qualification standards.

## Design Notes

### Visual Style

**Brand:**
- Second Chair brand colors (from brand guidelines)
- Clean, professional, legal-industry appropriate
- Not "flashy" — trustworthy before impressive

**Typography:**
- Headings: Bold, clear hierarchy
- Body: Readable, scannable
- Tables: Clean, easy to compare

**Icons:**
- Simple line icons (not cartoonish)
- Consistent style throughout
- Use sparingly (not decorative, functional only)

**Images:**
- Professional stock photos (if any)
- Prefer diagrams/flowcharts over photos
- Charts/graphs for data visualization

---

### Responsive Design

**Desktop:**
- Full comparison tables
- Side-by-side tier cards
- Interactive calculator prominent

**Tablet:**
- Stacked tiers (still readable)
- Simplified tables (scrollable)
- Calculator still functional

**Mobile:**
- One tier at a time (swipeable?)
- Simplified tables (tap to expand)
- Calculator simplified (fewer fields)
