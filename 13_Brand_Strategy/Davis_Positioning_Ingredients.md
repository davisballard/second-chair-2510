## Ingredient 1: Deep Craft Is What Produces Consistency

When you work on big brands and big campaigns, you go deep. You do the work to find the truth — on the customer, the category, the competition, the company. That depth is what Second Chair brings. This is not a shallow operation.

That depth is what produces ads that:
- **See people as they actually are** — not as targets to manipulate, but as specific human beings in a specific situation
- **Emotionally prime them** — not trick them. The claimant who comes through a Second Chair funnel is excited for the lawyer they're about to speak to. They're informed. They're a good fit. They arrived ready.
- **Are sustainable long-term** — not built on tricks that expire, legal loopholes that close, or tactics that the platforms ban. The advertising gets better over time because the knowledge compounds. We're always building new ways to reach people, better ways, and that never ends. That's the craft.
- **Work with compliance, not against it** — Fortune 500 brands, financial brands, healthcare. We know what legally and ethically acceptable advertising looks like under scrutiny. We know these lawyers' worlds.

The competitor's leads have been tricked into showing up. Second Chair's leads have been persuaded — properly, honestly, with depth — into showing up. That difference is everything in the first intake call.

## Ingredient 2: The AI System — A Council, Not a Tool

This is not "we submit things to Claude or ChatGPT." That's what the competition does and that's why it's shallow. The competition dips into AI as a giant pool of generic information. Second Chair built something categorically different.

The system is a roundtable of distinct perspectives — all valid, all coming from different angles:
- Byron Sharp / advertising science: what actually builds memory and drives category entry
- Julian Cole / strategic execution and comms planning: how strategy becomes real decisions
- Behavioral scientists: what actually changes behavior, what creates action
- Brand strategists: what makes a brand coherent and ownable over time

Second Chair uses it to think like many different people simultaneously — all the influences, all the disciplines, all the angles — and then makes decisions from the full picture, not a single biased perspective. It removes the one-lens problem that every lead gen vendor has. They see the problem through one angle (media buying, or tech, or sales). Second Chair sees it through all of them at once.

**Why this has to be mentioned in the pitch:** Attorneys are going to wonder if Second Chair is just another tech-forward startup that uses AI like everyone else claims to. The answer is: the logos check the box, but the real story is that this is how Second Chair thinks — with a breadth of perspective that would cost a company millions in agency and consulting fees to replicate, and that is directed by founders with the actual expertise to evaluate what each perspective is saying. That's the distinction. That's not something the competition can replicate by subscribing to Claude.

**The future-moat argument:** They will never run out of approaches because they're not using tricks. The system gets smarter, the council gets better inputs as the market knowledge compounds, and the advertising keeps improving. That's consistency. That's what attorneys can't get from vendors chasing the next loophole.

**The creative production dimension:** The AI system also enables making exactly the right ad for exactly the right person in exactly the right market at exactly the right time — at a production scale that would be prohibitively expensive with traditional methods. Phoenix gets ads with Phoenix landmarks, Phoenix streets, Phoenix specifics. Miami gets different ads. Chicago gets different ads. That's the targeting — not the platform's demographic tools, but the creative itself. And the quality of that production is indistinguishable from real craft. That last part requires someone who knows what real craft looks like to direct it. The council is that direction system.

## Ingredient 6: Media — Meta (FB and IG) Is 95–100% of B2C Spend

The primary channels are Facebook and Instagram. These are the two Meta channels that carry the spend. Other channels within the Meta network (Audience Network, Messenger placements, etc.) are way less — more internal/operational, not the focus.

Google Search is not in the plan right now — too expensive, harder to manage on a campaign-to-campaign basis. Long-tail keyword content is a long-term consideration, not current.

**What attorneys need to know about this:** Nothing. They don't care how Second Chair reaches claimants. They care that the claimants arrive persuaded, not misled, top-tier quality, and not garbage fed through to get paid on volume. The channel is an operational detail. The outcome is what matters.

The mechanism in Meta is creative self-selection: the ad is specific and honest enough that the person who had an accident recognizes themselves in it and stops scrolling. Everyone else scrolls. That's the targeting — the specificity of the creative is the precision instrument, not platform demographic tools (which are locked under Special Ad Categories anyway).

**The craft challenge this creates — and why it's a moat:** On Meta, an ad is also seen by the 99% of people who didn't have an accident. The ad has to be specific and honest enough to stop the 1% — while not irritating the 99% who scroll past. That is a very specific creative problem that requires real craft to solve. The execution style — cinematic, phone-shot aesthetic, emotionally real, invoking motion without manipulating — has to be executed at a level that makes it look genuine. If you try to fake the phone-shot aesthetic with AI and don't know what you're doing, it looks wrong immediately. If you try cinematic without real craft behind it, it looks like every other embarrassing PI ad. The advertising quality that solves the 99%/1% problem is the same quality that demonstrates to an attorney — the moment they see the ad — that this is not what they've seen before. The creative is its own proof of claim.

**The visual and emotional direction of the ads:** Cinematic, emotionally real, specific to the person and the market. Not all the same format — various executions. What they share: they invoke motion and emotional response without manufacturing fear or greed. That is the non-manipulative distinction. You can make someone feel seen without making them scared. You can make the case that fighting back is worth it without promising a number. That's the craft. That takes experience. The competition can't do it because they only know how to get people scared and greedy — and that's the only emotional lever they've ever pulled.

## Ingredient 7: CPSC — We Only Win When You Win

Cost Per Signed Case is the success metric. Second Chair's measurement of success is not lead volume, click-through rate, or form submissions — it is signed cases. This matters because it structurally aligns Second Chair's success with the attorney's success at the outcome level, not the activity level.

This is skin in the game. It's the structural proof that Second Chair believes in what it claims. If the leads don't convert to signed cases, the model doesn't work. That's not a pricing model quirk — it's a statement about what Second Chair is optimizing for and who it's accountable to.

**In the attorney pitch:** CPSC doesn't need to be explained at length. The framing is simple: "We measure ourselves by the same metric you use to measure your own practice." That's the closer. It makes the alignment real and self-evident in a way that no amount of talking about lead quality can achieve.

## Ingredient 9: What Doesn't Work in the Pitch

- **"Leads from advertising you'd show your clients"** — attorneys don't care about showing ads to their clients. The consumer brand (Fair Case) is in the fine print. It's not on their brand. So this argument is weaker than if Second Chair were doing their personal advertising. Drop it as a lead.
- **"Show you the advertising before you commit"** — this is a nice proof move but it's putting the cart before the horse. The bigger story is: the whole system produces consistent, quality, emotionally ready leads. The advertising is part of that system. Show it as evidence, not as the pitch.
- **"Tech-forward and AI"** alone — too generic. Every SaaS company says this in 2026. It has to be in there as a checkbox, but the bigger story is the expertise-directed system built on proven frameworks. That's the distinction.
