## Section 1: The Founding Insight

**What was seen, not what was researched.**

The founding insight did not come from the category analysis. It came in two stages — and the second stage is the one that matters.

Stage one was professional: the advertising in this space is terrible. Non-compliant, untrustworthy, embarrassingly bad creative. A trained advertising strategist can look at what the PI lead gen category is producing and know immediately that none of it was made by anyone who understands what advertising is supposed to do. That was the initial pull — the professional recognition of a gap anyone with Davis's background would have noticed.

Stage two was the founding moment: reading the personal stories of PI attorneys during the first round of deep research. Davis came in with the same inherited bias most Americans carry — the ambulance chaser frame. It is not an accident that frame exists. Insurance corporations have spent billions lobbying it into the culture, deliberately convincing the people these attorneys fight for that the fighters are the villains. It is one of the most sustained and effective corporate propaganda campaigns in American history. And it worked on someone whose whole life has been built around seeing through that kind of manipulation.

What the research revealed: PI attorneys are therapists. They are fighters. They get dismissed at Thanksgiving as pariahs while being the only people standing between their clients and a system that is deliberately designed to crush those clients. Insurance companies collect premiums for a person's entire life, then lowball them the second they actually need it — and spend billions more convincing those same people that fighting back makes them the problem.

PI law is one of the few systems where a regular person can actually fight back. And the advertising apparatus that is supposed to help them access that system is just another form of the same manipulation.

That is when Second Chair became inevitable — not as a market opportunity, but as a moral one. When the gap between what advertising could be and what the PI lead gen category was actually doing became personal, not professional.

**The specific observation that someone else would have walked past:** The person who designed the advertising for Vacation, Disney, and Google is the same person who can see, in a 30-second PI television spot, exactly what creative decisions were made and why — and know, with certainty, that none of those decisions were made by someone who understood the human being they were trying to reach. The category doesn't know what it's missing. It has never had anyone with that lens inside it.

## Section 3: The Product Capability — Made Specific

**What Alex has actually built, as of March 2026. Not what he could build — what is already built.**

The Second Chair platform is a two-sided lead generation and delivery system built specifically for personal injury law. It owns the full chain from the moment an injured person clicks an ad to the moment a qualified lead lands in a firm's intake queue. Most lead gen businesses stop at form submission. This one does not.

**The Consumer Intake Funnel (Fair Case / getafaircase.com):**
A guided 6-question quiz that collects structured case data before anyone picks up the phone. By the time a lead reaches a firm, the firm already knows injury type, accident recency, treatment status, fault position, and severity. The intake work is partially done on arrival. Most competitor funnels are 3-field forms. Every submission also captures TCPA consent with timestamp, a TrustedForm certificate URL, UTM parameters, session metadata, and behavioral signals including bot detection. This is not commodity intake infrastructure. This is purpose-built for a category where consent documentation is the difference between a defensible lead and a TCPA liability.

**The Pipeline — Sequential, Non-Bypassable:**
Every submission runs through: validation and sanitization → rate limiting and deduplication → Cloudflare Turnstile bot challenge → internal suppression check → heuristic bot detection → external compliance checks (DNC, litigator, TrustedForm) → dual scoring → routing → delivery. No lead skips any stage. The pipeline was designed with the same architectural discipline Alex applied at TradeStation: if a failure mode exists, it is caught before it reaches the end user.

**The Dual Scoring Engine — CPSC Made Product:**
Every lead receives two independent scores and a composite letter grade (A/B/C/D).

- Fit Score (0–100): Injury severity (40 pts), recency (20 pts), medical treatment (15 pts), fault position (10 pts), case type (15 pts). Hard adjustments: −40 points if already represented.
- Quality Score (0–100): Time on page (25 pts), behavioral integrity (25 pts), attribution clarity (20 pts), session metadata completeness (15 pts), TCPA consent quality (15 pts). Hard adjustments: −25 for missing consent; individual bot signals carry additional deductions.
- Composite grade: (Fit × 60%) + (Quality × 40%). Scoring weights are stored in the database and adjustable via admin UI in under 30 seconds without a code deployment.

This is the product proof of CPSC alignment. The grade travels with the lead into the firm portal. The attorney sees it immediately. They are not trusting a vendor's claim — they are reading a score derived from a verifiable methodology.

**Compliance Infrastructure — FINRA/SEC Grade Applied to PI:**
Alex built a 0-to-1 mobile trading app to FINRA/SEC compliance standards at TradeStation. That discipline is now applied to PI advertising compliance:

- TrustedForm (ActiveProspect): Certify runs on every lead. Verify runs on every lead to be routed. Retain preserves the certificate for long-term legal defense. In the event of a TCPA dispute, the firm has a timestamped, court-admissible consent record. Most lead vendors cannot provide this. Most shared marketplaces do not generate certificates at the lead level at all.
- DNC and Litigator Screening (dnc.com): Phone numbers screened against federal and state DNC registries and against known TCPA plaintiff numbers. Litigator hits suppress delivery entirely. Fail-closed policy.
- Twilio Lookup: Line type and reassigned number risk on every accepted lead.
- Full audit trail: Every lead carries a verification_status field plus a full lead_verifications log recording every provider call, its result, latency, reason code, and timestamp. Nothing is a black box.

**The Firm Portal:**
The attorney-facing interface gives PI firms real-time visibility into exactly what Second Chair knows. Every lead surfaces two attorney-visible scores: a **Conversion Score** (predicts likelihood to sign — weighted toward accident recency, active treatment status, and no current representation) and a **Case Value Score** (predicts attorney fee revenue potential — weighted toward injury severity, liability clarity, and insurance adequacy). These two scores combine into a four-level priority routing decision: Priority 1 (high conversion + high case value — immediate call, senior intake); Priority 2 (high conversion + lower case value — fast routing, standard intake); Priority 3 (lower conversion + high case value — VIP flag, extra follow-up warranted); Priority 4 (standard queue). A critical rule: a Priority 3 lead — an older submission with a serious spinal or TBI injury — should never be treated as low-priority simply because the accident was months ago. The product makes that distinction visible so the attorney allocates effort correctly. Full case summary before first contact. A live lead age counter — green, yellow, red — making speed-to-contact urgency visible the moment a lead arrives. Speed-to-contact is the single largest predictor of conversion in PI intake. One-click status updates. Browser push notifications for new leads. Delivery configurable via portal, email, or webhook — leads flow into whatever system the firm already uses (Clio Grow, Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack).

**The Competitive Distinction in Plain Language:**
Most shared lead marketplaces sell the same lead to multiple firms simultaneously, capture a 3-field form with no case qualification, have no scoring, have no compliance audit trail, and deliver via email to whoever paid most. Second Chair delivers one firm per lead, pre-qualified case data, a letter grade, a TrustedForm certificate, DNC and litigator screening, a real-time portal, and a full audit trail. The platform was built to make compliance a feature, not a checkbox.

**Tech Stack:**
SvelteKit (consumer funnel) + Next.js 14 App Router with TypeScript (pipeline, admin, firm portal) + Supabase with Row-Level Security + Upstash QStash (async queue, 5-retry delivery) + Resend + Vercel + Cloudflare Turnstile + ActiveProspect TrustedForm + Twilio Lookup. Modular from day one — the infrastructure designed to serve PI MVA is the same infrastructure that scales to mass tort, SSD, and worker's comp without a full rebuild.

## Section 8: The Honest Limitations

**What Second Chair does not do yet, stated plainly.**

**Geography:** Not yet in every market. The market-by-market depth required to do hyper-targeted local advertising correctly — using landmarks, visual references, and linguistic specificity appropriate to each DMA — takes time. The model is proven by depth in select markets first, then expanded. Overpromising geographic reach before the research depth is there would violate the core honest advertising claim.

**The post-accident timing window:** Second Chair cannot guarantee reaching claimants within any specific timeframe after an accident — and does not claim to. The first 72 hours is not a targetable paid social window: on any given day in an average metro, there are 40–50 newly injured people, and no platform can surgically identify them. What Second Chair does is reach claimants as quickly as possible via Meta (Facebook and Instagram — 95–100% of B2C ad spend), structured across three timing tiers (Days 0–30, 1–6 months, 6+ months). The mechanism is creative self-selection: the specificity and honesty of the ad causes the person in each tier to self-identify and stop scrolling. Google Search is not in the current plan — cost-prohibitive at this stage; a long-term consideration as the business scales. The goal is always to reach claimants before they have accepted the insurance adjuster's offer — not to manufacture a timing guarantee that cannot be operationally fulfilled.

**Case types:** Currently focused on PI MVA (motor vehicle accidents). Mass tort, SSD, worker's comp, and other high-CPL legal verticals are the expansion thesis — but they are a thesis, not a live product. The platform infrastructure is modular and designed for this expansion from day one. The advertising methodology translates. But the market-specific research and creative validation is not yet done for non-MVA verticals.

**Volume:** Second Chair cannot serve unlimited firms at launch. The CPSC model requires sufficient case flow per firm to produce statistically meaningful CPSC data. An undercapitalized media buy does not produce enough leads per attorney to make the model legible. Sasha knows this: he will not sell a firm a volume of leads that will overwhelm their intake or that won't produce enough data to demonstrate CPSC performance. The model requires minimum viable scale per account.

**Alex's personal input:** Alex's emotional input questions and the Founders Together document have not yet been completed. The product layer is fully documented. The personal conviction behind Alex's participation in this company — why specifically he is building this here — is known directionally but not yet on record with the specificity of Davis's and Sasha's inputs. This will be completed after the creative brief is locked and Alex has reviewed the full strategy from the product side.
