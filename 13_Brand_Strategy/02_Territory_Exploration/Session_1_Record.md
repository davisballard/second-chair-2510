## Session 1 Findings

### Territories A, B, and C — Reassigned as Infrastructure

A, B, and C do not survive as standalone primary territories. However:

- **Territory A** (Case Quality Engine) → proof infrastructure within D/E: dual scoring, CPSC measurement, routing intelligence are evidence that the honest advertising territory is architecturally enforced
- **Territory B** (Aligned by Architecture) → structural commercial argument: the CPSC model makes honesty architecturally necessary, not aspirationally chosen; the CPSC sales opener belongs in the B2B Sales Language Guide regardless of which territory wins
- **Territory C** (Before They Decide) → B2C creative execution standard: the visual language (decision moment, not accident), the tone ("before you decide"), and the proof mechanic ($33,700 data point) become the B2C creative brief inputs regardless of which territory wins

### The Core Debate for Session 2

Two territories pass all five filters: **D** and **E**.

**D** is specific, structural, and immediately credible. "The leads PI attorneys buy should be as honest as the cases they take." It gives Sasha the best cold-call opener, Davis the clearest creative brief, and attorneys the most specific resolution to the ambulance chaser wound. It closes deals.

**E** is the highest-purpose, highest-risk territory. It names the contradiction the plaintiff bar lives inside and resolves it. It produces advertising attorneys share unprompted. It builds a company attorneys advocate for. But it cannot open a cold call, and it requires the listener to feel the weight of the broken connection before they can feel the value of the honest one.

**The hypothesis entering Session 2:**
D and E may not be competing territories. D is the ground-level expression — what Second Chair does. E is the purpose that explains why D exists. The winning brand strategy may be D as the primary commercial territory with E as the purpose layer that gives D its emotional weight and long-term brand-building power.

**D closes deals. E builds a company.**
