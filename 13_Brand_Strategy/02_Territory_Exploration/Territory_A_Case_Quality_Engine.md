# Territory A — The Case Quality Engine

**Session:** 1 — Territory Exploration

## Territory Name
The Case Quality Engine

## Single-Minded Proposition
*Second Chair delivers cases, not leads — built end-to-end from the advertising creative through the intake infrastructure, measured by whether they sign.*

## What This Territory Claims
The PI lead gen category sells a product (leads) at the point of delivery and measures success there. Second Chair sells a system whose output is a signed case — honest advertising that creates an informed claimant, an intake pipeline that qualifies and scores them, and a delivery mechanism that routes the right contact to the right attorney at the right time. Every component is calibrated to the same end point: cost-per-signed-case.

## Proof Points
- Dual scoring engine — Conversion Score + Case Value Score — produces attorney-visible intelligence no competitor provides
- TrustedForm full stack (certify + verify + retain): every lead arrives with a court-admissible consent record and verifiable advertising origin
- Advertising creative built to produce informed claimants, not manipulated ones — the honesty of the ad is what makes the case quality possible
- CPSC as the native success metric — not CPL, not leads delivered, not volume

## What This Territory Sounds Like
- "Other vendors measure success when the lead arrives. We measure it when the case signs."
- "Everything in the pipeline — the ad, the intake, the score, the routing — exists to produce one thing: a signed case."
- "We don't get paid for leads. We get paid for cases. The system is built around that difference."

## What This Territory Looks Like
Systems diagrams executed with the precision of financial product documentation — the visual language of infrastructure, not hustle. Clean, hierarchical, institutional. An attorney looking at the visual language should feel: *this was built by someone who takes compliance seriously.* Not a startup pitch aesthetic — the visual grammar of institutional credibility with a product system at its center. The pipeline is visible; the attorney can see how each step produces the next.

## Who This Territory Speaks To
Jennifer Walsh — the established builder (3–10 attorney PI firm managing partner) who has been burned by every vendor's volume claims and wants to evaluate by the metric she actually uses: cases signed per dollar spent. Also: the marketing-director-tier attorney who can evaluate a product system and compare it to what they currently use.

## Research Alignment
- Consumer Synthesis Tension 1: Certainty vs. Unpredictable Delivery
- Category Consumer Synthesis Tension 5: The Category's Price vs. The Attorney's Metric (CPL vs. CPSC)
- Three Cs Master Synthesis: proof requirements by claim
- Founding Truth Brief Section 3: platform intelligence and product capability

## Risk Factors
- Sounds like SaaS, not a brand. "Case Quality Engine" is a product descriptor, not an emotional territory.
- The identity wound (ambulance chaser wound) is not addressed. This territory speaks to the rational attorney but doesn't resolve the professional shame the research identified as the deepest driver.
- A well-resourced competitor could build a similar system within 18 months. The territory is not permanently exclusive.
- "Better system" is claimable by anyone with enough capital to build one.
