# Territory C — Before They Decide
## Session 1 Territory Document

**Session:** 1 — Territory Exploration
**Status:** Generated — to be evaluated in Session 2

## Territory Name
Before They Decide

## Single-Minded Proposition
*The most important moment in a personal injury case isn't when they call a lawyer. It's the moment before — when they're deciding whether to.*

## What This Territory Claims
The insurance adjuster calls within 72 hours of an accident. They establish the frame. 68% of claimants accept the first offer without ever consulting an attorney — losing an average of $33,700. The frame is the case. Second Chair's advertising reaches claimants before that frame becomes permanent — with honest information, a specific competing perspective, and a minimum viable action that costs nothing and takes ten minutes. The claimant who acts on a Second Chair ad arrives with a different first frame. That difference is everything in the intake call.

## Proof Points
- The $33,700 average settlement gap between claimants who consulted an attorney vs. those who didn't
- 68% of claimants accept the first offer without consulting anyone
- Creative self-selection on Meta: the ad is specific and honest enough to stop the right person mid-scroll — without irritating the 99% who didn't have an accident
- Speed-to-lead: sub-60-second routing from form submission to attorney contact — the product is fast enough to honor the time sensitivity the territory claims
- Three-tier system: the advertising structure that creates the competing frame at each decision stage (Days 0–30, 1–6 months, 6+ months to SOL)

## What This Territory Sounds Like

**B2C:**
- *"Before you decide anything, make one call. Ten minutes. Free. Here's what most people don't know."*
- *"In California, accident victims who talked to a lawyer before accepting averaged $47,300 vs. $13,600 without one. Same injuries. One call."*
- *"The adjuster called yesterday. Before you answer, here's what you should know."*

**B2B:**
- *"Before your client talks to their adjuster, they should have talked to you. Second Chair puts you in the conversation first."*
- *"The case is decided before the client calls. We reach them before they decide."*

**Brand:**
- *"Before you sign anything."*

## What This Territory Looks Like
The decision moment, not the accident. Ordinary light. A hand on a phone. Someone in a kitchen or a parked car, on the verge of making a choice they can't take back. The visual language of quiet consequence — not emergency, not drama, not scales of justice. The specific, ordinary, consequential moment before a major decision. The visual distinction: every other PI ad shows the crash or shows a lawyer. This territory shows neither. It shows the human being at the inflection point — the moment before. That visual shift is itself the distinctive signal.

## Who This Territory Speaks To
Both audiences simultaneously, from different angles.

**B2C:** Maria — the working parent who hasn't yet accepted or declined. She's in the window. She received the adjuster's call. She's not sure she has a case. She's not sure she wants "to sue." She wants to know what she actually has before she decides.

**B2B:** The attorney who has heard a client say "I already accepted — I didn't know I could fight it." The one who has watched people sign before they ever called. This territory speaks to the attorney's professional frustration with a system that routes people away from them before they even know help was available.

## Research Alignment
- Consumer Synthesis Tension 4: Timing vs. Default — the adjuster calls first; 68% sign the first offer
- Claimant Decision Architecture research: the pre-decision window as the highest-leverage intervention point
- The $33,700 settlement gap: the specific, verifiable proof that the decision moment matters
- Consumer Synthesis B2C Brief: "Before you say yes to anything, take 10 minutes"

## Risk Factors
- **Critical operational note:** The 72-hour window is not operationally targetable via paid social — Special Ad Category restrictions eliminate demographic/behavioral targeting, and daily newly-injured claimant volume in any metro is too small for CPM-based reach. This territory's name ("Before They Decide") must not imply timing precision Second Chair cannot deliver. The real claim is reaching them with honest information while their decision is still open — not "within 72 hours."
- If framed as "we reach them first," competitors could claim the same thing. The specific, defensible claim is: "we reach them with honest information before the dishonest frame calcifies" — which is harder to replicate but requires more words to explain.
- The territory requires the $33,700 data point to land as proof — that data point is industry-sourced, not Second Chair's own. Attorneys who are familiar with it might find it less compelling as a proof mechanism.

## Hegarty's Test
Strong on B2C execution. The ad from this territory — the quiet decision moment, the specific data point, the minimum viable action — would be shown by an attorney to another attorney as "this is what PI advertising should look like." It passes if the execution is precise. The territory's visual direction (the moment before, not the accident) is inherently distinctive against category norms.

## Roma's Test
Moderate as a cold-call opener. "We reach your potential clients before they decide — honestly, with information that makes them more likely to call you" requires more setup than Territory B's CPSC opener. Stronger as a second or third point in a conversation once the attorney is already engaged.

---

## Session 2 Recommendation
Territory C should not compete against D as a primary brand territory. Its value is specific and strong: the B2C creative execution direction. The visual language (the decision moment, not the accident), the tone ("before you decide"), and the proof mechanic (the $33,700 data point) should become the B2C creative brief inputs regardless of which territory wins. The territory's insight — reaching people honestly before the exploitative frame calcifies — belongs inside D as a proof of what honest advertising actually does in the claimant's life.
