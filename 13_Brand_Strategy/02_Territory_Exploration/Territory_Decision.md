## The Three Candidates Debated

### Candidate 1 — Territory D SMP, refined
*"The leads PI attorneys buy should be as honestly won as the cases they take."*

**McCabe's verdict:** The engine. "Won" carries: earned through genuine advocacy, not manufactured. The attorney wins cases. Second Chair wins leads the same way — through craft and genuine persuasion. Correct word, correct structure, correct rhythm. Locked.

### Candidate 2 — Outcome-led
*"The leads PI attorneys buy should arrive as ready as the cases deserve."*

**McCabe's verdict:** Retired. "As the cases deserve" is semantically muddled. Cases don't deserve readiness — attorneys do. Claimants do. The parallel fails under pressure.

### Candidate 3 — Two-sentence structure
*"PI attorneys only take cases they believe in. Their leads should come from advertising that earns the same belief."*

**McCabe's verdict:** Retired. Two sentences trying to do the job of one. The parallel is there but the architecture is flabby. Does not have SMP discipline.

## What the Roundtable Locked

### The Verbal System

| Element | Line | Job |
|---|---|---|
| **SMP** | *"The leads PI attorneys buy should be as honestly won as the cases they take."* | The strategic claim. The commercial and ethical argument in one sentence. |
| **Purpose sub-line** | *"Second Chair exists so the attorney who needs clients and the client who needs an attorney can finally find each other honestly."* | The emotional foundation. Why D exists. E's layer. Not the SMP — the reason the SMP is a conviction rather than a product claim. |
| **Brand statement** | *"The plaintiff bar fights for injured people. Second Chair makes sure they find each other."* | The simplest external expression. Usable as a one-line identity statement. |
| **Campaign line** | *"You've never settled a case you weren't proud of. But are you settling for leads?"* | The B2B emotional hook. Identity-consistent reasoning. Attorney-facing. Shares without being asked. |
| **Sales room opener** | *"Before I explain what we do, I want to show you the advertising we run to generate our leads."* | Roma's primary cold-call move. No claim made before the proof is shown. |
| **CPSC transparency line** | *"We give you the metric that actually tells you if it's working — not CPL, which tells you nothing about case value."* | Replaces all "we only succeed when you succeed" language. |
| **B2C invitation** | *"Before you decide anything, make one call. Ten minutes. Free. Here's what you should know."* | Honest, minimum-barrier B2C action. No manufactured urgency. |

### Visual Direction: Cinematic

Locked. Not naturalistic. Not AI-obvious. Cinematic: intentionally produced, emotionally evocative, honest in emotional logic rather than surface texture. The craft is visible in the quality. The care is visible in the precision. The claimant's world elevated to the emotional register of the decision being made, not the accident that preceded it.

### What Was Removed

| Item | Why |
|---|---|
| Ambulance chaser wound as primary emotional driver | The primary lever is commercial (honestly won → higher conversion). Identity resolution is a byproduct, not the hook. |
| $33,700 settlement gap | Claimant-side data. The attorney's frame is case acquisition economics, not aggregate claimant outcomes. |
| "Business model cannot be profitable through dishonesty" | Replaced by CPSC transparency framing. Less adversarial, more useful. |
| "Showing advertising to clients/peers" framing | Reframed: the proof mechanism is showing advertising before commitment, in the sales context. Not about what the attorney shows to third parties. |
| Naturalistic/real-faces visual direction | Retired in favor of cinematic. |

### CPSC Framing — Corrected for All Future Documents

Second Chair is **paid per lead.** CPSC is not the payment model — it is the transparency and relationship health metric. The correct framing throughout all strategy and execution documents:

> *"We report on cost-per-signed-case because that's the metric that tells you whether the relationship is working — not CPL, which tells you nothing about case value."*

Every instance of "our success depends on your success" or "we only get paid when you sign a case" or "business model cannot be profitable through dishonesty" is retired and replaced with the CPSC transparency framing above.

## The Claim Chain — Why "Honestly Won" is Commercial, Not Just Ethical

This is the central insight that makes the territory defensible beyond ethics:

**Honestly won → accurate expectations → emotionally ready to engage → converts at higher rate → ethical choice = commercial choice**

The attorney who wants the highest-converting lead and the attorney who wants the most ethically sourced lead are asking for the same thing. Second Chair is the first company that recognized this and built for it. This is the claim chain that must live in all strategy documents, pitches, and execution briefs.

---

## Why This Territory and Not the Others

**A and B** are not territories — they are proof mechanisms within this territory. The case quality system, the dual scoring engine, the CPSC metric: these are the evidence that "honestly won" is architecturally enforced, not aspirationally stated.

**C** (Before They Decide) is not a territory — it is the B2C creative execution direction. The visual language (the decision moment, the claimant in their world, before they've acted) and the tone (honest, minimum-barrier action, no manufactured urgency) become the B2C creative brief inputs.

**D and E** were never competing. D is the ground-level expression of what Second Chair delivers. E is the purpose that explains why D needs to exist. The fusion (Honestly Won) holds both — D's specificity and immediate commercial credibility, E's emotional depth and conviction.
