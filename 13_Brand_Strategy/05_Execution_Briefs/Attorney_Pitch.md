**Standard Web Lead** — Pre-qualified web form submission. You call them. Converts at 10-15%.

**Better intake calls.**
Standard tier: The claimant who arrives through Second Chair was honestly persuaded — given accurate information about their situation and a genuine reason to call. They arrive with the right expectations. The intake call doesn't start with damage control.

Premium tiers: The claimant has already been qualified before you ever speak with them. You're receiving a warm transfer or calling back with detailed intel about injury type, liability, medical treatment, and case viability.

**The metric that tells you if it's working.**
We report on your cost-per-signed-case — not CPL. CPL tells you what you paid to talk to someone. CPSC tells you what you paid to sign them. That's the number that tells you whether lead gen is contributing to your practice or costing it.

- Standard leads: $315 (Denver) at 12% conversion = $2,625 CPSC
- Human Live Transfer: $1,515 at 50% conversion = $3,030 CPSC

You pay 15% more per signed case but need 75% fewer leads and your intake team saves 6+ hours per week.

**A compliance record on every lead.**
TrustedForm full stack on every lead — court-admissible consent documentation and advertising origin record. You know where every lead came from and you can prove it. Premium tier calls are recorded with two-party consent compliance.

**No shared leads.**
The lead generated for your firm is delivered to your firm. Not re-routed to another attorney in your market. Premium tier live transfers are exclusive to your Market.

**2. What arrives is qualified, scored, and compliant.**

Every lead that enters Second Chair's system is processed through:

- **TrustedForm full stack (certify + verify + retain):** Every lead arrives with a court-admissible consent record and documented advertising origin. You know exactly where each lead came from and you can prove it.
- **Fail-closed DNC and litigator screening:** Leads that fail DNC or litigator list screening are not delivered. This is architectural — the system doesn't forward non-qualifying leads.
- **Dual scoring engine:** Every lead is scored on two dimensions before delivery:
  - **Conversion Score** — likelihood of reaching and signing, based on contact information quality, response speed, and behavioral signals
  - **Case Value Score** — case type, injury severity indicators, and case-complexity signals

The two scores produce a priority routing matrix — you know which leads to call first before you pick up the phone. Not volume. Intelligence about the volume.

### The Model

**Four things. How Second Chair works.**

1. **Paid per lead.** Second Chair earns a fixed fee per delivered lead. The attorney's payment is not tied to case outcomes. What is tied to outcomes is how Second Chair measures the relationship's success — CPSC — which means the incentive to deliver quality leads is structural.

2. **CPSC as the shared metric.** Second Chair reports cost-per-signed-case because that's the number that tells both parties whether the relationship is working. If CPSC is below the attorney's acceptable threshold after 90 days, the math doesn't work — and Second Chair will say so.

3. **Month-to-month.** No contracts. No setup fees. No lock-in. The business model assumes Second Chair must earn the relationship continuously. If the quality drops, the attorney leaves. That's the incentive structure.

4. **No shared leads.** Every lead generated for a Second Chair attorney partner is exclusive to that attorney in that market. It is not re-routed, re-sold, or shared.
