Vesting of Membership Units

Notwithstanding the ownership interests set forth above, each Member’s Membership Units shall vest in accordance with the following schedule:

a.) Vesting Commencement Date. For each Member, the “Vesting Commencement Date” shall be the Effective Date of this Agreement.

b.) Vesting Schedule. Each Member’s one hundred (100) Membership Units shall vest as follows:

  1. One-Year Cliff. No Units shall vest until the first anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date. On the first anniversary, twenty-five (25) Units (25%) shall vest in a lump sum.

  2. Monthly Vesting Thereafter. The remaining seventy-five (75) Units shall vest in equal monthly installments over the subsequent thirty-six (36) months, such that each Member’s Units shall be one hundred percent (100%) vested on the fourth anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date.

c.) Continued Meaningful Participation. Vesting is contingent upon the Member’s continued meaningful participation in the Company. “Meaningful participation” means actively and substantively contributing to the Company’s operations, goals, and objectives. Mere passive retention of Member status without substantive contribution shall not constitute continued participation and may, upon unanimous determination by the other Members, trigger a cessation of further vesting.

d.) Treatment of Unvested Units on Departure.

  1. Voluntary Withdrawal or Cessation of Participation. If a Member voluntarily withdraws, ceases meaningful participation, or otherwise departs from the Company before full vesting, all unvested Units shall be immediately forfeited and returned to the Company at no cost. The departing Member shall retain only the vested portion of their Units.

  2. Involuntary Removal for Cause. If a Member is involuntarily removed for cause under Section 19, all unvested Units shall be immediately forfeited.

  3. Death or Permanent Disability. In the event of a Member’s death or permanent disability, vesting shall accelerate by twelve (12) months from the date of death or determination of permanent disability. The Member (or their estate or designated beneficiary) shall retain all vested Units as so accelerated; any remaining unvested Units shall be forfeited.

e.) Treatment of Forfeited Units. Forfeited Units shall be returned to the Company. The remaining Members may, by unanimous vote, (a) reallocate such Units pro-rata among the remaining Members, (b) reserve such Units for future issuance, or (c) cancel such Units and reduce the total number of outstanding Membership Units accordingly.

f.) Rights During Vesting Period. During the vesting period, each Member shall retain full voting rights and full economic rights (including distributions) on all of their allocated Units, provided the Member continues meaningful participation. The vesting schedule governs only the right to retain Units upon departure from the Company; it does not restrict voting or economic participation during active involvement.

g.) Acceleration on Sale of Company. In the event of a sale of all or substantially all of the Company’s assets, a merger, or a change of control in which the acquiring party does not assume the Members’ continued roles on substantially similar terms, vesting shall accelerate by twelve (12) additional months for each Member who continues meaningful participation through the closing of such transaction. If a Member’s role is terminated by the acquirer without cause within twelve (12) months following such transaction, full acceleration to one hundred percent (100%) vested shall apply.

3. VOTING REQUIREMENTS

a.) Business Decisions. Business decisions related to the Company’s activities, finances, and management shall be made by majority decision by the Members.

b.) Amending this Agreement. Any amendments to this Agreement shall be made by unanimous decision by the Members.

c.) Adding New Members. Adding new Members to this Company shall be made by unanimous decision by the Members.

d.) Withdrawing Members. To allow Members to withdraw shall be made by unanimous decision by the Members.

e.) Dissolving the Company. To dissolve the Company and cease business activities, this shall be made by unanimous decision by the Members.

4. VOTING RIGHTS

When votes are cast by Members as required under Section 3, or any decision regarding the Company, each Member’s vote shall be counted equally, regardless of their ownership of the Company.

5. MANAGEMENT

Any and all decisions of the Company shall be made by the Members.

6. MEMBERS’ AUTHORITY

The authority to make decisions that can potentially bind the Company, legally or financially, may be exercised by any individual Member for commitments of three thousand dollars ($3,000) or less. Any commitment, contract, loan, or other obligation that binds the Company legally or financially for more than three thousand dollars ($3,000) shall require the unanimous written consent of all Members. For purposes of this Section, a series of related transactions shall be aggregated and treated as a single commitment.

7. DISTRIBUTIONS

a.) Tax Distributions. Within thirty (30) days after the end of each fiscal quarter, the Company shall distribute to each Member an amount equal to such Member’s allocable share of the Company’s taxable income for the period, multiplied by a presumed combined federal and state tax rate of forty-two percent (42%). Tax Distributions are mandatory and shall not require a vote of the Members. Tax Distributions shall be made only to the extent the Company has available cash and may be delayed or reduced pro-rata among the Members if cash is insufficient, with any shortfall to be paid as soon as cash is available.

b.) Additional Distributions. Any distribution in excess of the mandatory Tax Distributions described in Section 7(a) shall require the unanimous written consent of all Members.

c.) Pro-Rata. All distributions of any kind shall be made to the Members in proportion to their ownership interests in the Company. No Member shall receive a distribution that is disproportionate to their ownership interest without the unanimous written consent of all Members.

d.) Adjustment for Actual Tax Liability. The Members may, by unanimous written consent, adjust the presumed tax rate set forth in Section 7(a) to reflect material changes in applicable federal or California tax law, provided such adjustment shall apply prospectively only.

8. MEETINGS

Company meetings shall be held, with all Members expected to be present, only when requested or called upon by the Members.

9. PROFITS AND LOSSES

The allocation of profits and losses of the Company shall be shared equally among Members, in ratio to their ownership of the Company.

10. COMPETITION

A Member’s ability to compete in the same or similar business as the Company shall be permitted and allowed, provided that such competition does not violate the confidentiality and non-solicitation obligations set forth in Section 25(p) of this Agreement. This Section is intended to comply with California Business and Professions Code Section 16600, which generally voids covenants restraining lawful trade or employment.

11. CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

No initial Capital Contributions shall be made at the time of this Agreement by any of the Members.

12. TIME COMMITMENTS

No Member is required to devote time to the Company as part of their ownership.

13. ANNUAL REPORTS

The Company must provide the following to each of the Members: tax reports (K-1 forms and annual tax returns), profit and loss (summary), income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement.

14. SELLING OF NON-MEMBERS

Not allowed to sell to non-Members. No Member is permitted to assign, transfer, sell, pledge, or otherwise dispose of their ownership interest in the Company to non-Members, whether voluntarily or involuntarily, without all the other Members’ prior unanimous written consent.

15. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

If a Member decides to sell, assign, transfer, or otherwise dispose of their ownership interest in the Company, the Company or its Members must be given the same rights to purchase under the same terms or better.

16. LIABILITY

If any Member and any authorized person acting under the management of the Company, or under the capacity of any officer, director, stockholder, partner, member, affiliate, employee, agent, or representative of the Company or any of the Members, shall not be liable to the Company or to any of the Members for any loss, damage, or claim incurred because of any action taken or omitted to be taken by such Covered Person in their respective capacity, so long as such action or omission does not constitute fraud or willful misconduct. This Agreement is not intended to, and shall not, create or impose any fiduciary duty on any Covered Person. Each Member and the Company hereby waive any fiduciary duties that, absent such waiver, may be implied by Governing Law, and in doing so, acknowledge and agree that the duties and obligations of each Covered Person to each other and the Company are only as expressly outlined in this Agreement. The provisions of this Agreement, to the extent that they restrict the duties and liabilities of a Covered Person otherwise existing at or law or in equity, are agreed by each Member to replace such other duties and liabilities.

17. INDEMNIFICATION

The Company will indemnify any person who is or was a defendant, or is threatened to be made a defendant, in a pending or completed action, suit, or proceeding (excluding actions by or in the right of the Company) due to their role as a Member, employee, or agent of the Company, or their service at the Company’s request. This indemnification covers expenses (including attorney’s fees), judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement that are actually and reasonably incurred in connection with such action, suit, or proceeding, provided that the Member determines the individual’s actions were in, or not opposed to, the best interest of the Company. For any criminal action or proceeding, the individual must have had no reasonable cause to believe their conduct was unlawful. The termination of any action, suit, or proceeding by judgment, order, settlement, conviction, or plea of no contest does not by itself create a presumption regarding whether the person acted in good faith or in a manner they reasonably believed to be in the best interest of the Company, or, in the case of a criminal action or proceeding, whether they had reasonable cause to believe their conduct was lawful.

18. CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

The Members may make an initial Capital Contribution, although there is no obligation to make such a contribution unless otherwise mentioned. If a Capital Contribution is made, it shall not receive interest based on its cash amount or non-cash value. Any Capital Contribution made shall be reflected in the capital accounts. Return of any Capital Contributions, if made, shall be at the Members’ discretion. It is understood by all Members that Capital Contributions made are not loans to the Company.

a.) Additional Contributions. Capital Contributions may be added from time to time in accordance with the needs of the Company. If an additional Capital Contribution is required, and a Member is unwilling or unable to meet the additional contribution requirement within the period granted to all other Members, the remaining Members may contribute proportionately to their existing Capital Contributions to resolve the amount in default. In such case, the allocation of profits, losses, and distribution of assets shall be adjusted accordingly to compensate for the Member’s deficiency. Any Capital Contribution made by a Member more than the amounts provided in this Agreement, specifically cash payments, shall be deemed a debt due the Company rather than an increase in the Capital Contributions from a specific Member. This liability shall be repaid with interest at such rates that are voted upon by the remaining Members. This liability shall not entitle the lending Member to any increased share of the Company’s profits nor a greater voting power. Repayment of such Company debts shall take precedence over any other future payments to the Members.

b.) Interest. No interest or borrowing fees shall be charged with the Capital Contributions made by Members.

c.) Withdrawal. No Member shall be allowed to withdraw any portion of their Capital Contributions without the unanimous written consent of the other Members.

19. INVOLUNTARY WITHDRAWAL

A Member may be involuntarily removed from the Company only for one or more of the following objective grounds:

a.) Death. The death of the Member.

b.) Permanent Disability. A permanent physical or mental disability, as documented by two (2) independent licensed physicians, that prevents the Member from engaging in any meaningful participation in the Company for a continuous period of at least twelve (12) months.

c.) Criminal Conviction. Conviction of, or a plea of guilty or no contest to, (i) any felony, or (ii) any crime involving fraud, dishonesty, theft, embezzlement, or moral turpitude.

d.) Material Breach. A material breach of this Agreement that remains uncured for thirty (30) days following written notice of such breach delivered by any other Member to the breaching Member specifying the breach with reasonable particularity.

e.) Judicial Determination. A final, non-appealable judicial determination by a court of competent jurisdiction that the Member has (i) engaged in wrongful conduct materially and adversely affecting the Company’s business; (ii) willfully and materially breached a duty owed to the Company or its Members; or (iii) engaged in conduct related to the Company’s business that makes it not reasonably practicable to continue business operations with such Member.

f.) Voting on Removal. A decision to involuntarily remove a Member shall be made by the unanimous vote of all Members other than the Member proposed to be removed (the “Remaining Members”). The Member proposed to be removed shall not vote on, nor be counted toward any quorum for, the decision regarding their own removal. Written notice of the proposed removal, including the specific grounds therefor, shall be delivered to the affected Member not less than fourteen (14) days prior to the vote, and the affected Member shall have a reasonable opportunity to respond in writing before the vote is taken.

g.) Effect of Involuntary Removal. The involuntary withdrawal of a Member does not affect the continuation of the Company. The departing Member’s Units shall be treated in accordance with Section 2 (Vesting of Membership Units), Section 21 (Dissociation), and Section 23 (Equal Financial Interest) of this Agreement.

20. WITHDRAWING MEMBERS

A Member can withdraw from the Company if permitted under the voting requirements. If a Member decides to withdraw, they must provide written notice to the Company, which shall be voted upon at the next meeting. If a request to withdraw from the Company is voted on and accepted, it must be processed and finalized within a reasonable time period. After the withdrawing Member’s departure, the remaining Members can decide whether or not to dissolve the Company.

21. DISSOCIATION

When a Member withdraws from the Company, voluntarily or involuntarily, and the remaining Members decide to purchase the withdrawing Member’s ownership interest, they must provide written notice of their decision. This notice, detailing the purchase price and the payment method and schedule, should be given to the withdrawing Member or their legal representative within a reasonable time after the change in circumstances is known. The buyout amount of the Member’s interests will be determined according to Section 23 of this Agreement. The remaining Members shall have the right to seek damages from the departing Member if the withdrawal occurred due to malicious or criminal actions, a breach of fiduciary duties, or any conduct likely to harm the Company or its reputation in the public domain. A withdrawing Member is only liable for Company obligations incurred during their time as a Member. After withdrawal, the Company will handle all legal notices to protect the departing Member from future liabilities. If the remaining Members buy out the departing Member’s ownership interest, the full payment shall be made within 90 days of withdrawal, without interest. The Company retains exclusive rights to all trade names, firm names, and related brand and model names, regardless of the Member’s name on the application or renewal certificates.

22. DISSOLUTION

At the Member’s decision, the affairs of the Company may be wound down and dissolved. Upon the dissolution of the Company, it shall immediately commence to wind down its affairs and for the Member to liquidate, sell, or transfer the business operations and assets of the Company. If debts are owed at the time of dissolution, the Company must pay creditors first before distributing cash, assets, and/or initial capital to the Member or any other economic interests.

23. EQUAL FINANCIAL INTEREST

In the event of a dissociation or dissolution, each Member shall retain an equal financial interest in the Company in accordance with their ownership interest. Without a written agreement establishing a value, the Company’s value shall be assessed based on the fair market value of all Company assets minus liabilities while following generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP). An independent accounting firm, mutually agreed upon by all Members, shall conduct such appraisal. An appraiser shall be appointed within a reasonable period following the dissociation or dissolution. The appraisal’s results will be binding on all Members. This section ensures the Company’s survival despite any Member’s dissociation. No value should be assigned to goodwill, trade names, patents, or other intangible assets unless these assets were recorded in the Company’s records before any such valuation.

24. SEVERABILITY

If any provision of this Agreement shall be declared to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, such provision shall survive to the extent it is not so declared, and the validity, legality, and enforceability of the other provisions hereof shall not in any way be affected or impaired thereby unless such action would substantially impair the benefits to any party of the remaining provisions of this Agreement.

25. MISCELLANEOUS

a.) Amendments. Any amendment to this Agreement must be agreed to in accordance with the Voting Requirements in Section 3(b). After a successful vote, the approving Members must sign the amendment and attach it to this Agreement.

b.) Audit Rights. Each Member shall have the right, at their own expense, to audit the financials of the Company. Such audits shall be conducted during normal business hours and with reasonable notice. During the audit, the Company shall make available all necessary books, records, and documents relevant to the Company’s financial condition, including but not limited to the general ledger, spreadsheets, bank statements, tax returns, and any related financial documents.

c.) Binding Arrangement. This Agreement and the terms and conditions contained herein apply to and are binding upon each Member’s successors, assigns, executors, administrators, beneficiaries, and representatives.

d.) Business Days. Refers to the standard five days of the work week, Monday through Friday, excluding federally recognized public holidays.

e.) Business Decisions. As mentioned in Section 3(a), refers to a wide range of decisions to be made on behalf of the Company, including but not limited to day-to-day activities, entering into agreements with third parties, lending money, obtaining loans, buying or selling assets, and any other act that directly or indirectly affects the Company’s operations, assets, and liabilities.

f.) Forbidden Acts. Members are strictly forbidden from any act that:

  1. Is in contravention of this Agreement;

  2. Permits, intentionally or unintentionally, the assignment of express, implied, or apparent authority to a third party that is not a Member;

  3. Would make it impossible to carry on the ordinary business of the Company;

  4. Will have the right or authority to bind or obligate the Company to any extent with regard to any matter outside the intended purpose of the Company;

  5. Confesses a judgment against the Company; or

  6. Violates any of the forbidden acts in this section of which shall be deemed an involuntary withdrawal and may be treated accordingly by the remaining Members.

g.) Fiscal Year-End. Shall be the last day of the month mentioned in Section 1(e).

h.) Force Majeure. A Member shall be free of liability to the Company where the Member is prevented from executing their obligations under this Agreement in whole or in part due to force majeure, also known as acts of God, which include but are not limited to floods, fires, and other natural disasters. In addition, this includes human-created acts such as terrorism, war, or other types of attacks which are unforeseen or out of the control of the Company or its Members. A Member must communicate the circumstance of any such event mentioned in this section to the other Members and to take all appropriate action to satisfy the Company’s duties and obligations and mitigate the effects of said event.

i.) Goodwill. The Company’s goodwill shall be assessed at an amount to be determined by appraisal using generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP).

j.) Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the statutory laws, regulations, and rules of the State of Formation without giving effect to any choice or conflict of law provision or rule, whether of the State of Formation or any other jurisdiction.

  1. Precedence of Agreement. In the event a provision of this Agreement shall differ from what is written under Governing Law, this Agreement shall take precedence to the extent it is legal.

k.) Liability Insurance. The Company is allowed to acquire insurance on behalf of any Member, employee, agent, or other person engaged in the Company’s business interests against any liability asserted against them or incurred by them while acting in good faith on behalf of the Company.

l.) Life Insurance. The Company shall have the right to acquire life insurance on the lives of any or all of the Members whenever it is deemed necessary. Each Member shall cooperate fully with the Company in obtaining any such policies.

m.) Management. The Company’s business and affairs shall be managed, operated, and controlled by or under the selection made in Section 4. Such management selection shall have full authority to take necessary actions to achieve the Company’s objectives and legally bind the Company into certain agreements and contracts. No other individual or entity has the authority to act on behalf of the Company unless specific written authority has otherwise been granted.

  1. Manager’s Removal. If the Company is managed by a Manager, the Members can remove the Manager at any time by vote in accordance with Section 3(a).

  2. Manager’s Compensation. If compensation is paid to a Manager who is making decisions on behalf of the Company, the payment made by the Company to the Manager is for their services performed. The compensation amount can be changed at any time, whether or not it is amended in this Agreement.

n.) Meetings. For any issue or subject matter regarding the Company, a meeting may be requested and scheduled by any Member subject to the notice requirements set forth below.

  1. Notice and Agenda. No action taken at any meeting shall be binding unless all Members received not less than seventy-two (72) hours’ written notice of the meeting, including an agenda of items to be voted upon. Written notice may be delivered by email, text message, or other reliable electronic means. The notice requirement may be waived only by the unanimous written consent of all Members. If any Member cannot attend the scheduled meeting, they may submit their vote in writing prior to the meeting, or the meeting shall be rescheduled if a majority of Members so requests.

  2. Meeting Minutes. Notes on the topics covered in a meeting shall be made available to each Member after the meeting. The minutes shall include the topics of discussion, the recording of votes, agreements made, and any consensus of decisions made among the Members.

  3. Voting. Any decision related to the Company’s business affairs may be voted upon at a duly noticed meeting. Any Member may request a vote on any matter by including it on the meeting agenda.

  4. Quorum. A quorum for conducting business at a meeting shall consist of no fewer than two (2) Members representing at least fifty percent (50%) of the Company’s ownership.

  5. Means of Participation. Meetings may be conducted in person, by video conference, by telephone, or by any other means enabling all participants to communicate with one another in real time. Participation by such means constitutes presence in person at the meeting.

  6. Action by Written Consent in Lieu of Meeting. Any action that may be taken at a meeting of the Members may instead be taken without a meeting if a written consent (including email, text message, or other recorded electronic communication) setting forth the action to be taken is signed or affirmatively acknowledged by all Members entitled to vote on such action. Such written consent shall have the same force and effect as a unanimous vote at a duly noticed meeting.

o.) Members. A Member mentioned herein is a legal and registered owner of the Company in accordance with any conditions, stipulations, or obligations outlined in this Agreement.

p.) Confidentiality and Non-Solicitation. Each Member shall be subject to the following protections upon the Company:

  1. Confidentiality. During membership and at all times thereafter, each Member shall hold in strict confidence and shall not, directly or indirectly, use, disclose, or permit the disclosure of any of the Company’s trade secrets, confidential information, proprietary processes, client lists, client contact information, pricing strategies, marketing strategies, financial information, business plans, or any other non-public information acquired in connection with the Company. This obligation shall survive indefinitely with respect to trade secrets and for a period of five (5) years with respect to other confidential information following the termination of membership.

  2. Non-Solicitation of Clients. During membership in the Company and for a period of two (2) years following the termination of membership, sale of the Company, or upon its dissolution, a Member shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, divert, induce, or attempt to solicit, divert, or induce any client, customer, or active prospect of the Company with whom the Member had material contact or about whom the Member acquired confidential information during their membership, for the purpose of providing services that are the same as or substantially similar to those offered by the Company. This restriction is intended to protect the Company’s trade secrets and confidential information and shall be construed and enforced to the maximum extent permitted under California law.

  3. No Restriction on Lawful Competition. For the avoidance of doubt, and in accordance with California Business and Professions Code Section 16600, nothing in this Agreement shall prohibit a former Member from engaging in any lawful profession, trade, or business of their choosing following their departure from the Company, subject only to the confidentiality and non-solicitation obligations set forth above.

q.) Officers. Individuals who may or may not be members shall be elected and appointed by vote as part of the general decisions of the Company. Officers may be changed, re-appointed, or vacated at any time.

r.) Ownership of Company Property. Ownership of all Company property shall be titled in the Company’s name, not any individual Members.

s.) Purpose. The business purpose is written in this Agreement for documentation reasons only. The Company is entitled to perform, engage, or be associated with any legal business purpose under the law.

t.) Record-Keeping. The Company shall always maintain the following records for access to any of the Members:

  1. Financial Status. Information related to the Company’s business status and financial condition.

  2. Tax Records. A copy of Federal, State, County, and any other tax records, filings, statements, and receipts for a period as required per Internal Revenue Service rules.

  3. Members’ Details. The details of each Member, including but not limited to their contact details, ownership interest, capital contributions, loans, distributions, and the date of becoming a Member.

  4. Company Records. The Company shall retain all its agreements, such as this Agreement, and with any third parties. In addition, any formation documents such as articles of organization or any documents filed with the State or Federal authorities must be kept in a safe and accessible location available to any of the Members.

  5. Right to Access. Each Member shall have the right to access a copy of any of the Company records mentioned herein provided that reasonable time has been given to produce such request.

u.) Registered Agent. The registered agent and office of the Company shall be the same as recorded and filed with the Secretary of State. Such details were recorded and filed with the Secretary of State on the formation documents or when filing an annual report.

  1. Service of Process. The registered agent and office of the Company shall serve as the process of service. As required by Governing Law, the registered agent shall meet the requirements of being open to accept notices.

v.) Right of First Refusal.

  1. First Priority. The Company shall have first priority to accept the sale terms of a separate offer for the purchase of Company ownership within 15 business days from receipt of notice.

  2. Second Priority. The Members shall collectively have a second priority in accepting the sale terms of a separate offer to purchase Company ownership. Each Member shall have a period of 5 business days from receipt of notice to accept, with priority given to Members with the largest allocation of ownership.

  3. Same Terms. If the Company or any Member agrees to the terms of the first right of refusal, they shall be obligated to purchase such Company ownership under the same terms as the separate offer unless otherwise agreed upon by the selling Member.

  4. Minimum Timeframe. Regardless of the terms mentioned in any separate offer, a purchaser acting on their right of first refusal shall have a minimum of 120 days to close and complete the sale.

w.) Secretary of State. Refers to the office or department where the Company is registered in the State of Governing Law. The term “Secretary of State” is a general term, whether or not such office exists with such title in the State of Governing Law or if the Secretary of State’s office is responsible for the formation of business entities.

x.) Time. Time is of the essence in this Agreement. Any delay in the performance of obligations shall constitute a material breach.

y.) Time Commitments. If any Member is required to commit time to the Company, it shall be dedicated to its business affairs as determined by the Members. If a Member that is beholden to this Time Commitment requirement does not fulfill their performance for the full term mentioned, their ownership under this Agreement can be subject to forfeiture.

This Agreement contains the entire agreement between the Members with all negotiations and understandings having been included. Any statements or representations that may have been made by any Member during the negotiation stages of this Agreement, may in any way be inconsistent with this final written Agreement. All such statements have no force or effect with regard to this Agreement. Only the written terms of this Agreement shall bind the Members.
