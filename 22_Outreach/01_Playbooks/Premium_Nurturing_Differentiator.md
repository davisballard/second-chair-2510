# Premium Nurturing as Differentiator — Universal

> **Type:** Strategic positioning reference — universal across prospects
> **Status:** INTERNAL ONLY — sales strategy
> **When to apply:** NOT Call 1. This is a Call 2+ / Phase 2 expansion conversation.

---

## The Two Tiers

**Standard tier (Call 1 focus):**
- Pre-qualified exclusive web leads via portal/email/webhook
- Client's intake team calls them
- Price: per-Market via MOI (see `../../07_Research/02_Sales_Intelligence/Market_Analysis/Market_Baseline_Pricing_Framework.md`)
- Expected conversion: 10-15% (industry standard for exclusive web leads; higher for firms with strong intake)

**Premium tier (Phase 2 option):**
- We call leads within 90 seconds of form submission
- Run a 7-question intake qualification script (injury, treatment, liability, insurance, statute of limitations, etc.)
- State-specific screening
- Live transfer to client intake — or deliver with detailed intake notes if their team is unavailable
- Price: standard rate + $1,200/lead premium
- Expected conversion: 40-60% industry standard; 60%+ for firms with extended intake hours

---

## When Premium Tier Fits

Evaluate against the prospect's operation:

- **Scale** — large intake infrastructure (staff count, hours)
- **Market volatility** — top-tier markets where fast follow-up is existential
- **Brand positioning** — firms whose reputation is "serious lawyer, not ambulance chaser" benefit from a controlled-script first touch
- **Sign rate economics** — $1,510/lead at 50% conversion = signed case at $3,020 vs. $320/lead at 12% = $2,667 standard. Premium isn't cheaper per-case but produces meaningfully higher case volume at same daily delivery with less intake team load
- **Compliance layer** — we control the script/tone on the live transfer. Additional SB-37 compliance safeguard.

---

## What Premium Tier Is NOT

- **Not a guarantee.** See `Negotiation_Playbook.md` → Guarantee Reframe.
- **Not a concession.** We don't offer premium if the prospect pushes on price. Discount logic is CA lockdown only.
- **Not a Call 1 sell.** Call 1 is discovery + SB-37 wedge + arming the contact. Premium isn't in the Call 1 deck.
- **Not a replacement for standard.** Most clients start standard and add premium selectively once the model proves out.

## How to Introduce Premium (If It Comes Up On Call 1)

### If they ask "how do we get higher-converting leads?"
> *"There are two levers. One is exclusivity — which you'd already get from us. The other is qualification depth. Standard tier gives you exclusive, scored web leads — typically 10-15% convert. We also have a premium tier where we pick up the phone within 90 seconds, run a full intake script, and transfer live. Live transfers convert at 40-60% — and for a firm with your scale, probably higher."*
>
> *"I don't think you should start with premium. Standard proves the model with less capital at risk. But it's worth knowing the lever exists — worth discussing in a second conversation, once you've seen standard work."*

### If they ask "can your team call the leads for us?"
> *"Yes — that's our premium tier. Call within 90 seconds, full qualification script, live transfer. Meaningful upgrade — higher sign rate, lower intake load, better brand safety because we control the initial intro. Costs $1,200 per lead on top of the standard rate. Not right for every client, but right for some."*

### If they ask about pay-per-signed-case / CPA models (Settlement Allies, etc.)
> *"Pay-per-signed-case sounds cleaner on paper, but the trade-offs are: the vendor controls which leads count, you wait 30-60 days for cash flow, and there's no territory exclusivity. We've looked at it — the economics don't favor strong firms. If you want higher conversion, our premium tier delivers live transfers at competitive cost per signed case, with weekly billing and full exclusivity. Same outcome, cleaner structure."*

## Phase 2 / Call 2 Framing

**When to introduce:**
- After standard tier delivers first 100 leads in at least 2 Markets
- After the client has real conversion data to compare standard against
- In response to "how do we scale this up?"

**How to position:**
> *"Standard has proven out. Phase 2 could look two ways. Double down on standard — more Markets, more leads, ride the optimization curve. Or add premium to your top 2-3 Markets and see what happens when live transfers hit [top market]. Intake spends less time on cold callbacks, sign rate jumps, and the SB-37 footprint gets tighter because we control the initial conversation script end-to-end."*

**Test structure options:**
- **A** — All standard. Blended × 100 leads × N Markets.
- **B** — Standard in most, premium in 1 Market (typically top). Direct sign-rate comparison in 30-45 days.
- **C** — Premium in top 2-3 only. The "intake force multiplier" play.

---

## Summary

- **Call 1:** Do not lead with premium. Focus is Socratic discovery + SB-37 + arming the contact.
- **Call 2+ or when prospect asks:** Introduce premium as Phase 2 expansion, not a Phase 1 start.
- **Positioning:** Higher sign rate, lower intake load, tighter brand safety.
- **Not:** Guarantees, CPA models, or risk-transfer structures. Premium is a quality upgrade, not a risk reallocation.
