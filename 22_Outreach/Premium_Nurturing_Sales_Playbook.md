## Overview

This playbook provides sales positioning, scripts, objection handling, and collateral for selling Second Chair's premium lead nurturing service. Use this to introduce premium nurturing in discovery calls, proposals, and ongoing client conversations.

**Key positioning:**
- Premium nurturing is an **upgrade option**, not a requirement
- Lead with **conversion rate math**, not just price
- Position as **time savings + quality improvement**, not "more expensive leads"
- Emphasize **integrated service** (one vendor vs. three)

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script

### When to Introduce Premium Nurturing

**Introduce premium tier when:**
- ✓ Prospect mentions slow intake response times
- ✓ Prospect asks about conversion rates
- ✓ Prospect has 24/7 intake capability (Jacoby & Meyers)
- ✓ Prospect is in premium market (NYC, LA, Chicago, Miami)
- ✓ Prospect mentions using call centers or AI tools separately
- ✓ Prospect complains about low conversion on current leads

**Don't force it if:**
- ✗ Prospect is focused only on base pricing (let them sign for standard, upgrade later)
- ✗ Prospect is price-shopping aggressively (wrong client)
- ✗ Prospect is solo practitioner with small budget

---

### Discovery Questions to Surface Need

**Question 1: Response time**
> "When a new lead comes in, how long does it typically take your team to call them back?"

**Listen for:**
- "15-30 minutes" or longer = problem (67% of clients choose first responder)
- "We have 24/7 intake" = perfect fit for live transfer
- "Depends on the day" = inconsistent, could benefit from SC handling first contact

**If slow response:**
> "That makes sense — most firms take 20-30 minutes. Industry data shows that 67% of potential clients sign with the first firm that answers. We have a premium tier where we call within 90 seconds and either qualify them for you or transfer them live. Would that be valuable?"

---

**Question 2: Conversion rates**
> "What conversion rate do you typically see on exclusive MVA leads?"

**Listen for:**
- "10-15%" = standard (expected)
- "<10%" = problem (poor lead quality or slow intake)
- "15-20%" = above average
- "Don't know" = they're not tracking (opportunity to differentiate with reporting)

**If low conversion:**
> "That's pretty standard for web leads — 10-15% is the industry average. Our premium tier delivers live transfers that convert at 40-60% because we qualify them before your team ever picks up the phone. It's a higher upfront cost, but your cost per signed case is often lower. Want me to show you the math?"

---

**Question 3: Intake team structure**
> "How many people handle intake for MVA cases?"

**Listen for:**
- "2-3 people" = mid-size (good fit)
- "10+ people" = enterprise (may not need premium tier)
- "Just me" or "1 person" = solo/small (may not afford premium, but AI tier could work)

**If mid-size team:**
> "Got it. So your team is spending a lot of time calling back leads, many of which don't sign. Our premium tier pre-qualifies them, so your team only talks to leads we've already verified have cases. It's a time-saver for your intake staff — they can focus on closing instead of cold calling. Sound interesting?"

---

**Question 4: Current vendors**
> "Are you working with any other lead vendors right now? What's your experience been like?"

**Listen for:**
- "We use [competitor]" = opportunity to differentiate
- "Low conversion" = premium tier solves this
- "We also use a call center for intake" = SC integrates both (one vendor)
- "We're trying AI tools" = SC has AI tier built-in

**If using multiple vendors:**
> "So you're managing a lead vendor plus a call center? We actually combine both — exclusive DMA leads plus live qualification and transfer — from one vendor. One relationship, one invoice. Simplifies things quite a bit. Want me to walk you through how it works?"

---

### Introducing Premium Tier (Discovery Call)

**Script:**

> "Let me tell you about our premium lead nurturing tiers — this is totally optional, but it's become really popular with firms that want higher conversion and less intake work.
>
> We offer three tiers:
>
> **Tier 1 is our standard service** — exclusive web leads, pre-qualified via form logic, delivered in real-time. You call them. That's $[X] per lead in your market. Converts at about 10-15%, which is industry standard.
>
> **Tier 2 is AI-qualified** — our AI calls them within 90 seconds, runs through our intake script, verifies they have a case, and delivers a full transcript and structured notes. It's 24/7, so we never miss a lead. That's base price plus $200. Converts at 15-25%.
>
> **Tier 3 is human live transfer** — Sasha or I personally call within 90 seconds, qualify the case, and transfer them live to your intake team while they're still on the phone. If your team isn't available, we deliver detailed notes and flag it as urgent. That's base plus $1,200. Industry data shows live transfers convert at 40-60%.
>
> Most clients start with standard and then add premium tier for a portion of their leads once they see the quality. Does one of those options sound interesting to you?"

## 5. Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "That's too expensive. I'm paying $200/lead elsewhere."

**Don't say:** "Well, our leads are better quality..."

**Do say:**
> "I hear you. Let me ask — what's your conversion rate on those $200 leads? [They say 8-10%] So your cost per signed case is around $2,000-$2,500. Our live transfers convert at 40-60%, which puts cost per signed case at $3,000-$3,750. You're paying 20-50% more per signed case, but you need 75% fewer leads to hit the same case volume. The question is: do you want to manage 100 leads yourself to get 8-10 cases, or have us handle 30 leads and deliver 12-18 cases? It's really about time and efficiency, not just cost per lead."

---

### Objection 2: "Why not just use a call center?"

**Don't say:** "Call centers suck..."

**Do say:**
> "You absolutely could — and some firms do. The challenge is you're managing two vendors: a lead vendor and a call center. If something breaks, whose fault is it? We integrate both, so there's one throat to choke. Also, call centers typically charge $300-$500 per call with 60-90 second duration minimums. We charge $1,200 for a qualified transfer, no duration games. And we know your DMA, your case criteria, your exclusivity. A call center is calling leads for 50 different firms. We're only calling for you in your territory."

---

### Objection 3: "Can I just pay per signed case?" (CPA model)

**Don't say:** "We don't offer that..."

**Do say:**
> "We've thought about that model. The challenge is it shifts 100% of conversion risk to us, which means we'd need to charge $4,000-$5,000 per signed case to make the economics work. At $1,500 per live transfer with 50% conversion, your cost per signed case is $3,000. With CPA pricing, you'd pay $4,000-$5,000 for the same case — and you'd wait 2-6 weeks for payment processing. You're actually better off with pay-per-lead. Plus, you control which cases you sign — with CPA, the vendor controls that decision."

---

### Objection 4: "What if they don't convert at 40-60%?"

**Don't say:** "They will, trust me..."

**Do say:**
> "That's a fair question. Industry data shows live transfers convert at 40-60% — that's not our number, that's the market benchmark from multiple vendors. We track conversion by tier and report weekly. If after 30 days your live transfers aren't beating your standard lead conversion by at least 2x, we'll credit you the premium difference on your next invoice. That's how confident we are in the model. Want to try 10 live transfers to prove it out?"

---

### Objection 5: "We have our own intake team. We don't need this."

**Don't say:** "But our team is better..."

**Do say:**
> "That makes sense — you've invested in intake. The question is: how fast are they calling leads back? If it's within 5 minutes, you're doing better than 90% of firms and might not need premium tier. But if it's 15-30 minutes, you're losing 67% of potential clients to the first firm that answers. Our premium tier isn't replacing your intake team — it's the first touch. We qualify them, then transfer them live to your team. Your team still does the close. It's really about speed to lead, not replacing your staff."

---

### Objection 6: "Why don't you just include this in the base price?"

**Don't say:** "Because we want to charge more..."

**Do say:**
> "We could, but not everyone needs it. Some firms have lightning-fast intake teams and prefer to call leads themselves. Some firms are budget-conscious and want the lowest cost-per-lead. Premium tier is for firms that value conversion rate over upfront cost. We wanted to give clients the choice rather than forcing everyone into one model. You can start with standard and upgrade later if it makes sense. Does that flexibility work for you?"

---

### Objection 7: "I need to think about it." (Stall)

**Don't say:** "Okay, let me know..."

**Do say:**
> "Absolutely. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it budget, or do you have questions about how the service works? [Listen] Got it. Here's what I'd suggest: let's start with standard tier — no premium, just base leads. Test the quality for 30 days. If you like what you see, we can layer on premium tier for a portion of your leads. That way you're not making a big decision upfront. Does that feel like a lower-risk way to start?"
