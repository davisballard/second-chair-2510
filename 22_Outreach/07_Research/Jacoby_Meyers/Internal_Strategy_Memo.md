## The Partner Firm / Intake Routing Question

J&M operates as a **lead aggregator** in some markets — buying leads and routing them to partner attorneys (e.g., Larry H. Parker served CA leads in the Lead Faucet era; Denver leads went to J&M even though it wasn't clear they had a local office — though jacobymeyers.com does list a Denver office).

**This matters for FCC 2025 compliance.** The consent form must name the specific entity that will contact the consumer. Two compliant paths:

**Path 1 (Recommended): J&M handles all initial intake**
- SC delivers leads to J&M's intake team
- J&M contacts the consumer (consent names "Jacoby & Meyers")
- J&M then refers to local partner as an attorney-to-attorney referral
- No additional consent needed — standard legal referral
- SC doesn't need to know who the end attorney is
- **This is the simplest path and what we should propose**

**Path 2: SC names the end attorney per DMA**
- J&M tells SC who the serving attorney is in each DMA
- SC's dynamic consent form shows that attorney's name
- More complex to manage but cleaner consumer experience
- Requires J&M to share their partner network mapping

**On the call, ask:** *"When a lead comes in from a market where you have a partner firm rather than your own office, does your intake team handle the first call, or does the lead go directly to the partner?"*

This determines which path we propose. If J&M's intake team handles everything centrally, Path 1 is clean and simple. If leads go directly to partners, we need Path 2.

**Flag for Saul:** Get compliance sign-off on Path 1 (J&M intake → attorney referral) before the call. Confirm that FCC 2025 consent to "Jacoby & Meyers" remains valid if J&M then refers to a partner attorney.

## Negotiation Boundaries

### Revised Discount Framework (March 2026)

**The old approach** (15% off everything for enterprise deals) was leaving money on the table. Buying DMAs scattered across 7 states gives SC zero operational efficiency — each DMA is independent creative, compliance, and campaign work.

**The new approach:**

| Scenario | Discount | Rationale |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **California full lockdown** (all 7+ CA DMAs) | **15% off** CA DMAs only | Only state in J&M's footprint with enough high-value DMAs to justify it. Real operational efficiency: one compliance framework (SB37), shared creative system, consolidated campaigns. |
| **Everything else** | **No discount** — list price | Each DMA in a different state is independent work for SC. No operational efficiency from buying NYC + Miami + Denver. The value is exclusivity and a single national partner, not a price break. |

**Applied to J&M:**
- California (7 DMAs): **15% off** → ~$320 blended (all prices rounded to nearest $5)

- Full 17-DMA portfolio blended: **~$383/lead**

**The pitch language:**
> *"California is the one state where we offer a lockdown discount — seven DMAs, one compliance system under SB37, one creative framework. That earns 15% off. Every other market is priced at what it costs to operate there. NYC is a $460 market because it's the most competitive PI market in the country. That price is the price."*

### Other Terms

| Parameter | Floor | Target | Notes |
|-----------|-------|--------|-------|
| **Contract term** | 6 months | 12 months | Could accept 6-month pilot for a single state/DMA |

### Lines We Don't Cross

1. **No discounts for geographic spread.** Buying DMAs in 7 states doesn't earn a per-lead discount. It earns a relationship.
2. **No flat state fees.** The price is always per-DMA based on MOI score.
3. **No shared exclusivity.** One firm per DMA. If they want LA, no other firm gets LA. That's the product.
4. **No guaranteed case values.** We deliver qualified leads. Signing and case outcomes are on their attorneys.
5. **No competitor data.** We don't share which DMAs other firms hold or are negotiating for.
