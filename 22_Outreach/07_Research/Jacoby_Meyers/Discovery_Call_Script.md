## PHASE 3: PROCESS WALKTHROUGH (8 minutes)

**Goal:** Answer Tommie's email question: "Are you lead gen or creative?" Answer: both — and here's how. This is where Davis and Alex tag-team.

### How We Build Campaigns (Davis)

> *"Before we run a single ad in your market, we study it. Not the surface — the people. Who lives in this DMA. What they do for work. What corridors they drive. What keeps them up at night after an accident."*

> *"Every market has different archetypes. A working mom in the Inland Empire commuting 45 minutes on the I-10 is not the same person as a tech worker in San Francisco who got hit on a bike. They respond to different messages. We build advertising for each of those people specifically."*

> *"Within each DMA, we split targeting by ZIP code combinations — speaking to specific communities, not a whole metro generically. Through deep research, not cliches. This is what separates real advertising from templates. Most lead gen vendors take one national ad, swap in a city name, and call it local. We don't do that."*

### The Process (Alex leads, Davis adds context)

> *"Here's how a lead gets to you, start to finish."*

**Step 1 — Advertising.** *"We create the consumer-facing advertising — Meta, Google, YouTube, TikTok. Built specifically for each market. Different audience, different visuals, different copy per DMA."*

- **The why (Davis adds):** *"After iOS 14, third-party targeting data collapsed. Creative quality is now the only reliable way to train the platform's algorithm toward the right audience. Generic creative produces permanently expensive, blurry audience models. Specific creative self-selects the right people. That's a structural advantage that compounds over time."*

**Step 2 — Consumer funnel.** *"The ad drives to our consumer brand. 6-question qualification quiz: accident type, fault, injuries, medical treatment, timing, contact info."*

**Step 3 — Qualification and scoring.** *"Every lead gets scored for fit and quality. We're filtering for legitimate cases, not just contact records."*

**Step 4 — Routing.** *"The lead goes exclusively to the firm that holds that DMA. One lead, one firm. No sharing, no auction, no ping-post."*

- **Data point:** *"Shared leads convert at 2.5-5% to signed case. Exclusive leads convert at 15-25%. That gap is the difference between buying contact records and buying real cases."*

**Step 5 — Delivery.** *"Portal, email, or webhook — whatever fits your intake workflow. Full data package: contact info, accident details, injury type, qualification score, consent proof via TrustedForm."*

- **Data point:** *"79% of legal consumers hire the first attorney who responds meaningfully. Firms that respond within 60 seconds convert at 391% higher rates than those who wait 5 minutes. Our delivery is real-time."*

**Step 6 — Dashboard.** *"Real-time tracking of CPL, volume, lead quality, and cost per signed case. All your creative lives there too. You see exactly what we're running, and if you want to approve ads before they go live, we set that up."*

- **The differentiator:** *"No vendor in the PI lead gen space publishes cost-per-signed-case data with methodology. We track it from dollar one. 65% of vendors don't even publish their pricing. We show you every number."*

### If Tommie Asks About Compliance

**Alex takes this:**
> *"FCC 2025 requires one-to-one explicit consent — the consumer has to see your firm's name on the form before they check the consent box. One firm, one checkbox, unchecked by default. Every consent moment recorded with a TrustedForm certificate — video replay of exactly what they saw and clicked. Full audit trail, 5 years retention."*

> *"Most of the leads in your CRM right now from other vendors are probably non-compliant under these rules. If someone challenges a lead's consent, you need proof. We have it on every single lead."*

### If Tommie Asks About the Pixel / Optimization

> *"The longer we run in your markets, the smarter the pixel gets. Meta's learning phase starts with CPL elevated 30-50% above steady-state. Once we hit ~50 optimization events per ad set per week, costs drop significantly. By 90 days the campaigns are dialed in."*

> *"And through CAPI — Conversions API — we can pass signed case events back to Meta server-to-server. The algorithm stops optimizing for form fills and starts optimizing for actual case outcomes. That's how the leads get better over time, not just cheaper."*

### If Tommie Asks About Intake Routing / Partner Firms

> *"When a lead comes in from a market where your intake team handles the first call, the consent names Jacoby & Meyers. If you work with partner firms in certain markets and the lead goes directly to them, we name that partner in the consent form. **Goal:** Explain the DMA model. Be ready to present specific prices for whatever markets Tommie asks about. Davis leads.

> *"The core of what we sell is territory exclusivity. When you're our client in a DMA, no competing PI firm can buy from us in that market. That's not 'exclusive leads' — that's an exclusive market."*

> *"The data on this is clear. Shared leads convert at 10-15% contact rate and 2.5-5% to signed case. Exclusive leads — where you're the only firm receiving them — convert at 40-60% contact rate and 15-25% to signed case. That's not a marginal difference. It's a different product."*

### DMA Pricing — Wait for Him to Ask

**Let Tommie tell you which markets he's interested in.** Have `JM_Markets_Client_Facing.md` and `DMA_Pricing_Table_Client_Facing.md` ready to share on screen if he wants to see the system.

**If he asks about CA, CO, NV, WA:**

> *"California has seven DMAs where you have offices. When you lock down the entire state, we apply a 15% discount — one compliance framework under SB37, one creative system. That brings the blended California rate to about $320 per lead."*

> *"Colorado is Denver at $315. Nevada is Las Vegas at $325 and Reno at $270. Washington is Seattle at $335 and Spokane at $255."*

**If he wants to see how pricing works:**

> *"Every DMA has a Market Operating Index — a composite score based on six factors: competition and media costs, legal complexity, market size, case value competition, uninsured rates, and market quality factors. **Share `DMA_Pricing_Table_Client_Facing.md` on screen** — the MOI methodology + full 121-DMA table. Tommie's engineering brain will appreciate seeing the system.

### Anchoring — CPSC Before CPL

**Always anchor on cost per signed case before revealing per-lead prices:**

> *"The number that matters most isn't cost per lead — it's cost per signed case. At our rates with a 10% lead-to-sign conversion, you're looking at $3,200 to $4,600 per signed case depending on the market. Industry median from lead gen vendors is about $2,700. TV and radio runs $10,000 to $16,000. Google search $12,000 to $20,000."*

> *"At a median MVA case settling for $25,000 to $50,000 — generating $8,300 to $16,500 in attorney fees — that's a 3.5 to 7x return on your lead gen spend before case expenses."*

### If He References a Lower Price From Another Vendor

> *"A flat rate across all markets means the vendor is either averaging — overcharging you in cheaper markets to subsidize expensive ones — or selling shared territory. We price each DMA at what it costs to operate there. LA has 5,000+ PI firms competing for attention. Denver has a few hundred. Those are fundamentally different costs."*

> *"And the thing embedded in our price that a flat-rate vendor can't offer: exclusivity. Your competitor in LA can't buy from us while you hold that market. That protection shows up in your conversion rate."*

### If He Pushes for Lower Prices

> *"The California lockdown discount exists because there's real operational efficiency when we build one compliance framework, one creative system for the whole state. That's 15% we pass through. For individual markets in different states, each one is independent work. The price reflects what it costs to operate there."*

> *"What we can talk about is starting focused — pick the markets that matter most, prove it works at 45 days, then expand."*

Generic creative produces expensive, blurry audiences. Ours self-selects the right people."*

> *"Third — we track cost per signed case, not just CPL. No vendor in this space publishes CPSC with methodology. We do. Because that's the number that actually tells you whether your lead gen is working."*
