## PHASE 3: PROCESS WALKTHROUGH (8 minutes)

**Goal:** Answer the question Tommie already asked by email: "Are you a lead gen company or a creative production firm?" Answer: both — and here's how.

**Alex leads this section. Davis adds creative context where relevant.**

### The Flow

> *"Here's how a lead gets to you, start to finish."*

**Step 1: Advertising**
> *"We create the consumer-facing advertising — digital ads across Meta, Google, YouTube, TikTok. The creative is built specifically for each market we serve. A Denver ad doesn't look like an LA ad — different audience, different visuals, different copy."*

**Step 2: Consumer Funnel**
> *"The ad drives to our consumer brand — Get A Fair Case. The consumer fills out a 6-question qualification quiz: accident type, fault, injuries, medical treatment, when it happened, contact info."*

**Step 3: Qualification + Scoring**
> *"Every lead gets scored for fit and quality. We're filtering for legitimate cases, not just contact records. By the time a lead reaches you, it's been through qualification."*

**Step 4: Routing**
> *"The lead is routed exclusively to the firm that holds that DMA. One lead, one firm. No sharing, no auction, no ping-post."*

**Step 5: Delivery**
> *"You get the lead via portal, email, or webhook — whatever fits your intake workflow. Full data package: contact info, accident details, injury type, qualification score, consent proof via TrustedForm."*

**Step 6: Dashboard**
> *"Real-time dashboard tracking CPL, volume, lead quality, and — this is the important one — cost per signed case. We track from dollar one all the way to signed client."*

### If Tommie Asks About Compliance (He Will)

**Alex takes this:**
> *"FCC 2025 requires one-to-one explicit consent — the consumer has to see the specific firm name before they check the consent box. We built that in from day one. Dynamic consent flow, TrustedForm certificate on every lead, full audit trail. This isn't retrofitted — it's how the system was designed."*

**This is where Alex and Tommie connect as engineers.** Tommie built compliance-driven software for CA courts. Alex built compliance-driven software for securities trading. Let them talk.

### If Tommie Asks About Intake Routing / Partner Firms

> *"When a lead comes in from a market where your intake team handles the first call, the consent names Jacoby & Meyers and the lead goes straight to you. If you work with partner firms in certain markets and the lead goes directly to them, we'd name that partner in the consent form. Our system handles both — we just need to know the routing per market."*

**Goal:** Explain the DMA model. Be ready to present specific prices for whatever markets Tommie asks about.

> *"The core of what we sell is territory exclusivity. When you're our client in a DMA, no competing PI firm can buy from us in that market. That's not 'exclusive leads' — that's an exclusive market. ### Anchoring — CPSC Before CPL

**Before giving per-lead prices, anchor on cost per signed case:**

> *"The number that matters most isn't cost per lead — it's cost per signed case. At our rates with a 10% lead-to-sign conversion, you're looking at roughly $3,200 to $4,600 per signed case depending on the market. Industry average from lead vendors is $8,000 to $14,000."*

**Then per-lead prices feel justified in context.**

### If He References a Lower Price From Another Vendor

> *"A flat rate across all markets tells me the vendor is either averaging — overcharging cheaper markets to subsidize expensive ones — or selling shared territory. We price each DMA at what it costs to operate there. LA has 5,000+ PI firms competing for attention. Denver has a few hundred. Those are different markets with different costs."*

> *"The other thing embedded in our price is exclusivity. With a flat-rate vendor, your competitor in LA is probably buying from the same source. With us, that can't happen."*

### If He Pushes for Lower Prices

> *"The California lockdown discount exists because locking down an entire state gives us real operational efficiency — one compliance framework, one creative system. That's a 15% discount we pass through. For individual markets in different states, each one is independent work. The price is the price because the cost is the cost."*

> *"What we can talk about is starting focused — pick the markets that matter most, prove it works at 45 days, then expand."*

## OBJECTION PLAYBOOK (Tommie-Specific)

### "We're paying less per lead with our current vendor."

> *"That's fair. The question I'd ask is: what's your cost per signed case? If you're signing 10% of those leads, what does the all-in cost look like? Our pricing is higher per lead, but our leads are territory-exclusive, FCC compliant, and generated through market-specific advertising. The sign rate tends to be higher because the lead was honestly earned."*

**Don't trash any other vendor.** Tommie respects fairness and integrity. Compete on quality, not on attacking competitors.

### "What makes you different from every other lead gen company?"

> *"Territory exclusivity. Every other vendor in this space sells exclusive leads — meaning they won't sell the same contact twice. But two competing firms in your market can both be their clients, buying from the same pool. We sell exclusive territory. When you hold a DMA, your competitor cannot buy from us. Period. That's a fundamentally different product."*

### "We need to think about it."

> *"Of course. What specifically would be most helpful for me to send you? I want to make sure you have what you need to evaluate this properly."*

**Tommie will tell you exactly what he needs.** He's organized and direct. Listen.

### "This is more expensive than what we're used to."

> *"I understand that. Two things worth considering: first, cost per signed case — at a 10% sign rate, our leads produce signed cases at $3,200 to $4,600. Industry average is $8,000 to $14,000. Second, exclusivity. No one else in your market can buy these leads. That protection has value that shows up in your conversion rate over time."*

### "Can you do a flat rate across all markets?"

> *"We price each market at what it costs to operate there. LA and Denver don't cost the same — LA has 5,000 PI firms competing for attention, Denver has a few hundred. A flat rate either overcharges you in cheap markets or undercharges in expensive ones. Our pricing reflects each market's reality — and includes territory exclusivity that flat-rate vendors can't offer."*
