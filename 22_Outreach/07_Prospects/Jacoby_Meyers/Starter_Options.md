## How It Works: Lead Packages

Leads are sold in packages per DMA — like credits. The client pays upfront for a set number of leads in each market. We deliver them as fast as we generate them. When the package is used up, they buy more.

- **First package minimum: 100 leads per DMA**
- **Renewal packages: 50 leads minimum per DMA**
- Delivery rate averages 1-3 leads/day per DMA (some days more, some days zero — this is an average, not a guarantee)
- A 100-lead package fills in roughly 5-7 weeks at average delivery

---

## Why 100 Leads Per DMA Is the Right First Package

This isn't arbitrary. It's driven by how campaigns actually perform during ramp-up.

| Leads Delivered | Timeline (~) | What's Happening |
|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **1-30** | Weeks 1-2 | **Learning phase.** Algorithm finding the audience. CPL elevated 30-50% above steady-state. Lead quality being established. These are the most expensive leads in the package. |
| **30-60** | Weeks 3-4 | **Stabilization.** CPL declining. Creative optimization beginning. Real data emerging on what's working. |
| **60-100** | Weeks 5-7 | **Early optimization.** Algorithm locked in. Enough site traffic to launch retargeting (people who visited but didn't convert). Lead quality improving. This is where the real performance shows up. |

**At 100 leads, the client has seen the full arc** — learning, stabilization, and early optimization. They can evaluate based on real, representative data — not just the expensive learning-phase leads.

**Why NOT 50 for the first package:**
- A 50-lead package fills in ~3-4 weeks — entirely during learning + early stabilization
- The client judges SC on the worst performance window and never sees optimized results
- Not enough site traffic for retargeting, not enough data to know which creatives win
- The client walks away thinking the leads are mediocre, when they were actually pre-optimization
- It's not enough data for the client to draw meaningful conclusions about sign rate or CPSC

**Why renewals can be 50:**
- After the first 100, campaigns are past learning phase
- The pixel is trained, retargeting audiences are active
- Every subsequent lead benefits from the optimization of the first 100
- 50-lead renewals are clean, affordable top-ups that keep the pipeline flowing

## The Renewal Cycle

Once the first 100-lead package is filled in a DMA:

1. We notify them before the last leads are delivered — no surprises
2. They purchase a renewal package (50+ leads per DMA)
3. Campaigns continue running without interruption
4. Each renewal benefits from the optimization of every lead that came before — better targeting, lower CPL, higher quality

**The flywheel:** First package = ramp + prove it. Every renewal = optimized performance at better economics. The longer they stay, the better it gets.

## If He Wants a Smaller First Package

**If Tommie asks for 50 per DMA to start:**

> *"I understand wanting to start lean. The challenge is that the first 30-40 leads come during the algorithm's learning phase — that's when costs are highest and targeting is least precise. At 50 leads, you'd be evaluating us based almost entirely on pre-optimization performance. At 100, you see the full arc — learning, stabilization, and early optimization. By lead 100, you're seeing what the campaigns actually produce when they're dialed in. That's a much fairer test for both of us."*

**If he insists on 50:** Accept it. 50 is thin but workable. The risk is his evaluation is skewed toward learning-phase performance. Mitigate by setting expectations: *"The first 30 leads will be learning phase — don't judge quality there. Leads 30-50 is where you'll start to see real performance."*

**If he asks for 25:** Push back harder. 25 leads is not enough to exit learning, build any audience data, or draw conclusions. It's a waste of money for both sides.

> *"At 25 leads, the algorithm never exits learning phase. We'd be spending money without the platform ever optimizing — higher cost, lower quality, and not enough data for either of us to evaluate. I'd rather do fewer markets at 100 than more markets at 25."*
