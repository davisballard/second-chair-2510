## The Short Answer

The first ~30 leads come during the algorithm's learning phase — when costs are highest and targeting is least precise. If you only buy 50, you're evaluating us almost entirely on pre-optimization performance. At 100, you see the full arc — learning, stabilization, and early optimization. By lead 100, the campaigns are producing what they'll actually produce long-term.

---

## How It Works

When we launch campaigns in a DMA, we run one ad set with 3-5 different creatives — different angles, different hooks, different formats. We target the full DMA geography and let the creative self-select the right audience. A Spanish-language narrative reaches Hispanic families. An educational angle reaches information-seekers. A direct opportunity play reaches people ready to act.

The algorithm watches who responds to which creative and learns who to show the ads to. The more people who convert, the smarter it gets — finding more people within the DMA whose behavior matches the people who already converted.

**The 100-lead timeline at our delivery rate (~1-3 leads/day):**

| Leads | ~Timeline | What's Happening |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| 1-30 | Weeks 1-2 | Algorithm experimenting. Costs elevated 30-50%. Testing which people, times, placements work. |
| 30-60 | Weeks 3-4 | Costs declining. Algorithm stabilizing. We can see which creatives are winning. |
| 60-100 | Weeks 5-7 | Early optimization. Algorithm locked in. Enough site traffic to launch retargeting — reaching people who visited but didn't convert the first time. |

**At lead 100, you have:**
- Real data on which creative angles work in your market
- Real lead quality you can evaluate at intake
- Real sign rate data (at 10% sign rate, ~10 signed cases)
- A campaign that's past learning and producing at steady-state performance
- Enough site traffic to launch retargeting ads that bring back people who showed interest

**At lead 50, you'd have:**
- Mostly learning-phase data (expensive, imprecise)
- ~5 signed cases — too few to draw conclusions
- A campaign that hasn't shown what it can actually do

## After the First 100

Renewals are 50+ leads per DMA. At that point the campaigns are already optimized — every renewal benefits from everything we learned in the first 100. The creatives are refined. The algorithm knows who to target. Costs are lower. Quality is higher.

The first 100 is the investment. Everything after that is the return.
