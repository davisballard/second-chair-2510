## The Guarantee Terms (As Stated by J&M)

- SC delivers 100 leads at $315/lead (Denver) = **$31,500 upfront**
- J&M expects **$3,300 cost per signed case**
- J&M's average case value: **$35,000** (pre-litigation, 33% contingency = ~$11,667 fee)
- If CPSC exceeds $3,300 within the first 100 leads, SC delivers **additional free leads** until CPSC comes down to $3,300
- J&M has 24/7 intake and calls within 4 minutes

## If the Guarantee Is Missed: Free Lead Delivery Math

**Formula:** Total signed cases needed = $31,500 / $3,300 = **9.5 cases**

| Actual Conversion | Cases from 100 paid leads | Additional cases needed | Free leads to deliver | SC media cost for free leads |

## The Learning Phase Problem

The first 100 leads include the **learning phase** — when CPL is elevated 30-50% and targeting is least precise. This means:

- SC's actual cost for the first 100 leads in a new DMA is likely **$150-$250/lead**, not $75
- Learning phase leads are the lowest quality in the entire lifecycle
- Guaranteeing CPSC on a first package is guaranteeing on the worst possible performance window
- The guarantee would work much better on a SECOND package (50 leads) where campaigns are already optimized

## Recommended Protections If You Offer This Guarantee

### 1. Response Time Requirement
*"Guarantee contingent on leads being contacted within 5 minutes of delivery."*

J&M says they call within 4 minutes with 24/7 support. Put it in writing. If they let leads sit, the guarantee voids. This is the single biggest variable in conversion that SC can't control.

### 2. Cap the Free Leads
*"If CPSC exceeds $3,300 within the first 100 leads, SC delivers up to 50 additional leads at no charge toward the guarantee target."*

This caps SC's downside at ~$7,500-$3,750 in media cost. Without a cap, a 3% conversion rate could cost SC $32,000+ in free media.

### 3. 90-Day Evaluation Window
*"CPSC measured at 90 days from first lead delivery."*

PI cases take 2-6 weeks to sign from lead delivery. Without a time bound, J&M could claim leads haven't signed yet indefinitely. 90 days gives enough time for all cases to resolve.

### 4. Define "Signed Case"
*"Signed case = executed retainer agreement between Jacoby & Meyers (or designated partner firm) and the claimant."*

Not "we're working with them." Not "they're interested." Signed retainer. Clear, binary, verifiable.

### 5. Consider Offering the Guarantee on Package 2, Not Package 1
The first 100 leads include learning phase — the worst performance window. A CPSC guarantee on the SECOND package (50 leads, campaigns already optimized) is much less risky and more defensible:

*"First package of 100 leads is the optimization period — no CPSC guarantee. Second package (50+ leads) carries the $3,300 CPSC guarantee because campaigns are past learning and producing at steady state."*

## The Bottom Line

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Is $3,300 CPSC achievable on Meta?** | Yes — at 10% conversion. But 10% is the top of the Meta range (5-10%), not the middle. |
| **Is it risky for a first package?** | Yes — learning phase leads are the most expensive and lowest quality. |
| **Can SC absorb the downside?** | At $75/lead cost, yes even at 3% conversion. At $150/lead (realistic for new DMA), only profitable above 5% conversion. |
| **Should you offer it?** | If you do, protect yourself with response time requirement, free lead cap, 90-day window, and signed case definition. Better yet, offer it on package 2 instead of package 1. |
| **What does J&M get for $3,300 CPSC on a $35K case?** | $11,667 in fees, ~$2,000 in case expenses, $3,300 in lead cost = ~$6,367 profit per case (54.6% margin). That's strong for J&M. |
