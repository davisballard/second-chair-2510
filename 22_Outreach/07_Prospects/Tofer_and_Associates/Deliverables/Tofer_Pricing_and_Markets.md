## How We Price: The Market Operating Index (MOI)

Every market has a different cost to operate in. Los Angeles has 5,000+ PI firms competing for attention and the strictest advertising compliance regime in the country. Bakersfield has a few hundred firms. The lead quality is the same everywhere — the price reflects what it costs us to reach and qualify people in that specific market.

We quantify this with the **Market Operating Index (MOI)** — a composite score from 1.0 to 10.0, built from six weighted factors:

| Factor | Weight | What It Measures |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Competition & Media Cost | 25% | Cost per click for PI keywords, number of competing firms, CPM environment |
| Legal Viability Rate | 25% | What percentage of motor vehicle accidents become viable cases |
| Market Size | 15% | DMA population and annual accident volume |
| Case Value Competition | 15% | Average settlement values and jury verdicts in the market |
| Uninsured Motorist Rate | 10% | Percentage of at-fault drivers with no insurance |
| Market Quality Factors | 10% | Tourism, transient populations, seasonal patterns |

Each factor is scored 1–10, weighted, and combined. The MOI maps directly to price:

**Price = $175 + ($36.11 × (MOI − 1.0))**, rounded to the nearest $5.

| MOI Range | Price Range | What It Means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 8.0 – 9.0 | $425 – $460 | Highest operating cost — extreme competition, complex legal screening |
| 6.0 – 7.9 | $355 – $420 | Above-average cost — strong market fundamentals, meaningful competition |
| 4.0 – 5.9 | $285 – $355 | Moderate cost — real volume, manageable competition |
| 2.0 – 3.9 | $210 – $285 | Lower cost — less competition, favorable economics |

Every MOI score is transparent and auditable.

## Non-Soft-Tissue Option — High-Value Cases Only

We also offer a non-soft-tissue-only product: trucking/commercial vehicle, moderate-to-severe MVA (fracture, TBI, spinal, surgery), motorcycle, pedestrian/cyclist, DUI victim, rideshare, wrongful death. No whiplash, no minor sprains, no minor bruising.

### Why Non-Soft-Tissue Costs More — And Why It's Worth It

Non-soft-tissue cases represent roughly 15-20% of all injury accidents. Our funnel filters aggressively at the injury severity step — the majority of people who click our ads and start the qualification quiz get screened out before they ever reach you. We pay for every one of those clicks. That narrower addressable pool, combined with more targeted creative production and longer optimization timelines, drives the per-lead price up approximately **50%** over the standard rate for the same market.

On your side, the math works in your favor. The minimum median settlement on non-soft-tissue cases is $75,000 vs. $20,000 for soft tissue — nearly 4x the case value. At a 33% contingency, that's a minimum expected attorney fee of $25,000 per case vs. $6,600 for soft tissue. You're paying 50% more per lead for cases that are worth nearly 4x more — and none of your intake team's time gets burned on cases you'd reject.

### Non-Soft-Tissue Pricing

Non-soft-tissue leads are priced at approximately **1.5× the standard rate** for the same market. Because non-soft-tissue volume is much smaller, most individual DMAs don't have enough case volume to support a filtered campaign on their own. Non-soft-tissue campaigns typically work best at the **state level** — aggregating volume across multiple DMAs to give the algorithm enough signal to optimize.

The exception is very large DMAs (like Los Angeles) that have enough non-soft-tissue incident volume on their own.

## Projected Attorney Fee → Projected ROAS

Every lead we deliver carries a dollar-denominated projected value from day one — built from our Fit Score model × sign probability by lead grade × median attorney fee by case type. Aggregate the cohort, divide by spend, and you have Projected ROAS before any case has signed. No other vendor in this space projects per-lead value like this.

### The Formula

```
Projected Attorney Fee per Lead = Expected Attorney Fee × Sign Probability

  Expected Fee = f(injury severity, case type, medical treatment, fault, recency)
  Sign Probability = f(Lead Grade: A / B / C / D)

Projected ROAS = Σ(Projected Attorney Fee per Lead) / Total Lead Cost
```
