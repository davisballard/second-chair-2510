## PHASE 2 — MEASUREMENT DISCOVERY (8-10 min)

**This is the Tofer-specific lead. Not creative visibility first — measurement visibility first.**

**Davis-declarative opening frame:**
> *"One thing that's central to how we think about this — every lead we deliver has a projected attorney fee value attached to it on day one. Dollar-denominated. Before any case signs. I've never seen another vendor in this space do it, and most of the clients we talk to have never seen it. Before I walk through what that looks like — I'm curious what you're actually seeing from your current vendors on performance and value reporting."*

### Q1 — The measurement gap
> *"What does your current vendor hand back to you on performance, Carlos? CPL, sign rate, lead grade — anything projecting value on the lead before it's worked?"*

**(silence 4+s — Carlos is a data guy, he'll answer substantively)**

**Expected response range:**
- *"CPL and volume mostly"* — most likely. Bridge: *"Yeah — that's the industry norm. CPL is what vendors can compete on. Projected value per lead isn't a standard deliverable."*
- *"We track sign rate on our side but the vendors don't provide it"* — common. Bridge: *"Right, so you're doing the measurement work for them. And sign rate only shows you part of the picture — because a signed soft-tissue case and a signed trucking case aren't the same."*
- *"We get grade/score data from one vendor"* — less common. Bridge: *"Good. How often does the grade predict the sign?"* → probe quality of their current grading.

### Q2 — The CPSC flip
> *"Out of 100 leads you buy from your biggest current vendor, roughly how many turn into signed cases?"*

If he has the number (he will — he runs the dashboard):
> *"So at [X%] sign rate and [Y] CPL, you're paying roughly [$Z] per signed case. Does that feel like it's telling you what you actually need to know — or does the real question become what the *value* of those signed cases is?"*

### Q3 — The creative visibility gap (lighter touch)
> *"One thing Sasha mentioned in the emails — SB-37. Given you've been in CA intake for a while, I assume you already know the exposure landscape. So I'm not going to walk you through what SB-37 is. But — one question. Are your current vendors showing you the actual creative they're running for Tofer?"*

**(silence 4+s)**

**Release valve:**
> *"Yeah — it's just how the industry's built. Vendors keep creative in a black box. You probably saw plenty of that at Legal Soft Solution — I'd bet that's part of why you're open to a conversation like this one."*

*(Tactical note: naming Legal Soft Solution is a judgment call. Use ONLY if he's warmed up and mentioned it himself or given a clear signal. If in doubt, stay generic — "from your intake background.")*

## PHASE 4 — THE SC STORY (3 min, permission-based)

**Ask:** *"Based on what you just described — can I walk you through how we think about this? Just so you have the comparison in your head."*

### The 4 bullets — REORDERED FOR TOFER (value-first, not creative-first)

1. **Projected Attorney Fee → Projected ROAS** — *"Every lead we deliver has a Projected Attorney Fee attached — built from the Fit Score × median attorney fee by case type × sign probability. Aggregate the cohort, divide by spend, and you see Projected ROAS on day one. Before any case has signed. No other vendor projects per-lead value like that — they report CPL or CPSC after the fact. Plugs directly into your Looker stack."*

2. **We run our own advertising (not a marketplace)** — *"Every ad that runs under Tofer's name is built by us, visible to you in the dashboard, SB-37 compliant from day one, with mandatory disclosures and no prohibited content. You approve before it runs if you want to."*

3. **DMA-specific creative** — *"LA metro has four distinct creative zones — Westside, South Bay, Valley, Inland Empire edge. Spanish-language where the market demands it. Truck-accident creative for the I-10/I-15 corridors — which ties to Tofer's #1 Truck vs. Car LA 2024 win. Every ad is built for the specific people in the specific sub-market."*

4. **One firm per DMA** — *"Territory exclusivity. Your competitor in LA can't buy from us while you hold LA. That's the product — not 'exclusive leads,' exclusive territory."*

**Flip back to questions:**
> *"Does that framing — the measurement layer being the actual product, with creative and exclusivity as supporting infrastructure — does that resonate with how you'd want to show this to Alex?"*

Then:
> *"If you brought Alex a one-pager showing Projected Attorney Fee and Projected ROAS for a 100-lead LA cohort, would that be the kind of thing he'd engage with, or is there a different number that gets his attention first?"*

*(His answer IS the post-call deliverable structure. Write it down verbatim.)*

## PROJECTED ROAS — ONE PARAGRAPH

> *"Every lead we deliver has a Projected Attorney Fee attached to it — built from injury type, case type, treatment status, fault position, and recency. Across a 100-lead cohort in LA, we project the average attorney fee value to land somewhere between $1,800 and $3,200 per lead depending on the case mix. At $425 per lead cost, that's a projected 4.5 to 7.5 times return on ad spend — visible from day one, before anyone has signed anything. No vendor in this space reports like this. They report CPL or CPSC after 6 to 12 months once cases close. Projected Attorney Fee is the only metric that gives you a real read on day one, because it's computed from what we actually control: the quality of the leads we generate."*

**If he asks about confidentiality / NDAs on case data:**
> *"Projected Attorney Fee doesn't depend on you sharing case outcome data. It lives on our side of the relationship. You share only what you can — typically just sign/no-sign on each lead, which doesn't touch confidentiality. The projection validates and tunes against actuals over time."*

**If he asks about Looker / Zapier integration:**
> *"Webhook delivery with full lead payload — contact, accident, injury type, Fit Score grade, Projected Attorney Fee, consent proof. Drops straight into your Zapier routing, populates your Looker dashboards natively. Alex can walk you through the technical piece in detail on Call 2 — that's his domain. We designed it to be additive, not a replacement."*
