## PROJECTED ATTORNEY FEE → PROJECTED ROAS

### Day-One Anchor (verbatim)
> *"Every lead we deliver has a Projected Attorney Fee attached — built from injury type, case type, treatment status, fault position, and recency. Across a 100-lead cohort in {{PRIMARY_DMA}}, we project ${{LOW}}-${{HIGH}} per lead. At ${{PRICE}}, that's {{N}}-{{N}}× projected ROAS — visible day one."*

### The "How" (if they ask)
> *"Published median settlement values × sign probability tied to our lead grade — A 20-30%, B 12-20%. Model you can audit line by line. As your team reports sign rate, we tune."*

### Differentiator
> *"No vendor reports per-lead value like this. Most report CPL or CPSC after 6-12 months."*

### CPSC-Is-Misleading
> *"CPSC averages every signed case. Ten soft-tissue cases = same CPSC as nine soft-tissue + one trucking, even though revenue is completely different."*

### Confidentiality-Honest
> *"We know PI settlements have NDAs. Projected Attorney Fee doesn't depend on you sharing case outcome data — it lives on our side."*

## "WE'RE NEW" REFRAME

See `../../01_Playbooks/Negotiation_Playbook.md` → Trust Reframe

**Accusation audit:** *"You're probably thinking, 'four months, why trust them with {{market}}?' That's exactly what I'd ask."*
