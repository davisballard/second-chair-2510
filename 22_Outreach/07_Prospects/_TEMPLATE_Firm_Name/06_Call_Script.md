**Three beats:**

1. **Appreciate the time + gentle disarm.**

You obviously get pitched by lead gen companies all the time, and you've got relationships that are working or they wouldn't still be there — so just want to say upfront, we're not here to critique your current setup.    > *"[Sasha/I] mentioned [SB-37 / the specific thing from their email] — {{why it matters for THIS firm}}. Wanted to dig into that with you."*

3. **Soft frame-set.**
   > *"And one thing before I start asking some questions — on initial calls like this, we like to run it less as us telling you about what we do, and more as figuring out exactly what you want and what's going on on your end. Sound good?"*

Team intro — **30 seconds max**. No resume dump.

## PHASE 2 — CREATIVE DISCOVERY (8-10 min)

**Davis-declarative opening frame:**
> *"One thing that's central to how we work — showing clients the actual creative before it runs, and on an ongoing basis. It's non-negotiable for us. Partly for brand coherence, partly for {{compliance angle specific to their state}}. That's basically our whole model. So I'm curious how things work on your side."*

**Q1:** *"Are your current lead vendors showing you the ad creative they're running for {{FIRM_NAME}}?"*
*(silence 4+s)*

**Release valve (industry-blame, not contact-blame):**
> *"Yeah — honestly, that's the industry norm. Most vendors keep the creative in a black box. They run what converts. Nobody in your role gets shown the creative as a matter of course. That's just how the job is built."*

**Q2 — denial close:**
> *"Look — for all we know, the creative your vendors are running is completely on-brand and clean. That's possible, right?"*
*(wait)*
> *"Right. So the real question isn't whether it's clean — it's how you'd know. If someone walked in tomorrow and asked you to show them everything that ran under your firm's name this week, is that something you could pull together?"*
*(silence)*
