## Service Definition

**Premium Lead Nurturing** is Second Chair's differentiated offering that transforms standard web leads into live-qualified or AI-qualified leads through immediate phone contact, case qualification, and warm transfers to law firm intake teams.

**What it is:**
- Real-time qualification calls (human or AI) within 90 seconds of form submission
- 7-question intake script verifying case viability
- State-specific screening (no-fault thresholds, SOL, liability standards)
- Live transfer to firm (when available) or warm handoff with detailed notes
- Call recordings, transcripts, and structured intake data

**What it's NOT:**
- Not a separate call center service (integrated with lead generation)
- Not a SaaS product (we provide the service, not software)
- Not a cost-per-acquisition model (pay-per-transfer, not per signed case)

### Tier 3: Human Live Transfer

**What client receives:**
- Everything in Tier 1, PLUS:
- Second Chair agent (Sasha or Davis) calls lead within 90 seconds
- Completes 7-question intake qualification script (human conversation)
- Attempts live warm transfer to firm's intake line
- If transfer succeeds: Lead is on phone with firm immediately
- If firm unavailable: Detailed intake notes, urgent priority flag, firm calls back within 5 minutes
- Call recording (full audio)
- Structured intake notes
- State-specific threshold verification

**Pricing:** Base + $1,200 premium  
**Example:** $315 base (Denver) + $1,200 = $1,515 total  
**Example:** $460 base (NYC) + $1,200 = $1,660 total  
**Availability:** M-F 9am-6pm PT (or custom hours)  
**Conversion expectation:** 40-60% (live transfer industry standard)  
**Client action required:** Answer phone when transfer comes in (or SC delivers warm notes)

**Pay-per-transfer model:** Client billed flat rate regardless of whether transfer succeeds — as long as lead was qualified and transfer was attempted.

### Qualification Standards

**7-question intake script:**
1. Were you injured?
2. Did you see a doctor?
3. When did the crash happen?
4. Who was at fault?
5. Is the other driver insured?

**Compliance:**
- Federal: Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA)
- State: Two-party consent laws in applicable states

## Qualification Standards

### Case Acceptance Criteria

**Standard (all states):**
- ✓ Injury occurred
- ✓ Medical treatment (or clear intent to seek treatment)
- ✓ Within statute of limitations
- ✓ Other driver at fault (or acceptable shared fault under comparative negligence)
- ✓ Other driver insured OR claimant has UM coverage
- ✓ No existing attorney

**State-specific criteria:**

**No-fault states (FL, NY, MI, MA, MN, HI, UT, KS, ND):**
- Must meet threshold: permanent injury, fracture, hospitalization, $X in medical bills
- FL/NY: Strictest (permanent injury or 90/180-day disability)
- MA: Easiest ($2,000 in medical bills)

**Choice no-fault states (PA, NJ, KY):**
- Must have "full tort" OR meet serious injury threshold
- ~75% of drivers have limited tort (threshold required)

**Contributory negligence states (DC, MD, VA, NC, AL):**
- Claimant must be 0% at fault (any fault = bar to recovery)
- Strictest liability standard in US

**At-fault states (TX, IL, GA, CA, AZ, WA, CO, etc.):**
- Standard comparative negligence rules apply
- Most plaintiff-friendly (any fault % can recover proportionally or with <50/51% bar)

## Risk Analysis

### Risk: Clients balk at $1,500 price point

**Probability:** Medium (some firms will reject on price alone)

**Mitigation:**
- Lead with conversion rate math (cost per signed case, not cost per lead)
- Offer 10-lead trial at discounted premium ($600 vs. $1,200)
- Position AI tier ($200 premium) as lower-cost option
- Target right clients (mid-size, growth-focused, premium markets)

**If pricing proves too high:**
- Monitor adoption rate (target: 20-30% of clients opt into premium)
- If <10% adoption after 6 months: Consider $1,000 premium (vs. $1,200)
- If >40% adoption: Pricing is validated, consider premium increase in Year 2

---

### Risk: Conversion rates don't hit 40-60%

**Probability:** Low (industry benchmarks are strong)

**Mitigation:**
- Track actual conversion rates by tier
- Weekly reporting to clients (transparency)
- If conversion <30% after 30 days: Review qualification accuracy
- Offer conversion guarantee: "If live transfers don't convert at 30%+, we'll refund premium difference"

**Root cause analysis if conversion is low:**
- Qualification accuracy problem? (SC qualifying bad leads)
- Firm intake problem? (firm rejecting good leads, slow follow-up)
- Market problem? (DMA has unique characteristics)

---

### Risk: Premium service doesn't scale profitably

**Probability:** Very low (margins are 89%, even with dedicated staff margin is 99%)

**Mitigation:**
