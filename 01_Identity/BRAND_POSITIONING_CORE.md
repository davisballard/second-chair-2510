# Second Chair — Brand Positioning Core

**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Locked — aligned to Brand Architecture (Session 3)
**Canonical source:** `13_Brand_Strategy/03_Brand_Architecture/Brand_Architecture.md`
**Audience:** PI law firms (solo practitioners through mid-size practices)
**Co-Founders:** Davis Ballard (Creative & Strategy Systems), Alex DuBelko (Product & Compliance Systems), Sasha Zinshtein (Performance Media)

---

## Governing Principle

*"Second Chair must feel trustworthy before it feels impressive."*

The category is full of impressive claims. The attorney has heard them all and doesn't believe them. What moves an attorney to try a new vendor is the feeling that this company understands their world and isn't trying to manipulate them. Every brand decision — message, character, voice, proof — is evaluated against this standard.

---

## PART I: POSITIONING STATEMENT (Locked)

**For whom:** PI attorneys who control their firm's lead generation spend — typically the managing attorney or sole practitioner — who have bought leads before and been burned by advertising that exploited the claimant's vulnerability, and who are now looking for a vendor they don't feel they need to be defended against.

**Who find that:** Every major PI lead gen vendor generates leads from advertising that manipulates the claimant's vulnerability to produce volume — which means the claimant arrives at the intake call with inflated expectations the attorney must manage down before the real conversation can begin.

**Second Chair is:** The only PI lead gen company that builds advertising that honestly persuades claimants, producing leads who arrive with accurate expectations and the readiness to engage — because the advertising earned their response rather than manufacturing it.

**Because:** Davis Ballard's 18 years of brand strategy and creative direction, Alex DuBelko's compliance-grade product engineering, and Sasha Zinshtein's Fortune 500 programmatic media expertise and financial-fluency sales practice are integrated from founding into the same company — which means the creative, the intake pipeline, the compliance architecture, and the attorney relationship are all calibrated to the same single outcome: a claimant who was honestly won and who arrives ready.

**So that:** The attorney can evaluate the advertising before they commit, trust that every claimant was served honestly, report on cost-per-signed-case as the primary relationship health metric, and finally work with a lead gen vendor they don't need to verify every month.

---

## PART II: SINGLE-MINDED PROPOSITION (Locked)

*"The leads PI attorneys buy should be as honestly won as the cases they take."*

**What "honestly won" means:**
A case is honestly won through genuine advocacy — the attorney believed in it, worked it, earned the outcome. A lead is honestly won through advertising that earns the claimant's response: specific, accurate information about what they have, emotional resonance that is true to their real situation, no manufactured urgency, no false claims about outcome.

**The claim chain:**
Honestly won → claimant arrives with accurate expectations → emotionally ready to engage → converts at a higher rate → the ethical choice and the commercial choice are the same

Honesty is not a constraint on results — it is the mechanism that produces them.

## PART III: B2B MESSAGE ARCHITECTURE

### Brand Belief
*"The attorney who fights for people deserves leads that were fought for honestly."*

### Primary Message
*"Second Chair builds advertising you can evaluate before you commit — because we built it to be worth showing."*

This works because:
- It makes an offer no other PI lead gen vendor has made
- It implies confidence without stating it
- It requires zero trust to engage — the attorney evaluates, they don't believe in advance
- It is verifiable in the first conversation

### Supporting Messages (Three, in priority order)

**1. The performance proof:**
*"Claimants who were honestly persuaded arrive with accurate expectations and convert at a higher rate. The ethical choice and the commercial choice are the same."*

Proof: The intake call from a manipulated claimant vs. an honestly persuaded one. The difference is visible and felt immediately.

**2. The metric proof:**
*"We give you your cost-per-signed-case — not CPL. CPL tells you what you paid to talk to someone. CPSC tells you what you paid to sign them."*

Proof: CPSC reported as the primary metric on every account by default. No other PI lead gen vendor does this without being asked.

**3. The infrastructure proof:**
*"The creative, the compliance infrastructure, and the media execution are run by the same founding team. There's no vendor handoff — no layer where the claim breaks down."*

Proof: Three founders' specific roles — Davis (creative/strategy), Alex (product/compliance), Sasha (media/sales) — operated with shared accountability to the same metric (CPSC).

### Objection Responses

| Surface Objection | Underlying Fear | Second Chair's Response |
|---|---|---|
| "I've been burned before." | Will I lose money again? | "Month-to-month. No contracts. Bad leads replaced. Before you spend anything, I want to show you the advertising we run so you can evaluate it yourself." |
| "How is this different?" | Show me, don't tell me | "Can I show you the advertising we run before I explain anything else? Then you can evaluate the difference." |
| "How many leads can you get me?" | I need volume guarantees | "We'd rather start at a volume that lets you verify quality, then scale when you're satisfied. The goal is CPSC clarity before we talk about scaling." |
| "Your leads are probably garbage." | Do you generate leads honestly? | "Here's the advertising. Here's the compliance record — TrustedForm certification, origin documentation, the full stack. You're evaluating the product, not trusting our claim." |
| "I need proof before I commit." | Give me proof without risk | "Here's a firm in a comparable market. Here's the CPSC data from their last 90 days. Here's the pilot structure — minimum commitment, easy exit if the data doesn't support continuing." |
| "It's too expensive." | Does the ROI math work? | "What's your current cost-per-signed-case? Most attorneys don't have this number because their vendors don't report it. Let's do the math together before we talk price." |
| "I need to talk to my partner." | I'm not ready yet | "Absolutely. Here's the one-page — the advertising, the compliance record, and the CPSC reporting format. That's usually what partners want to see." |

### The Four Buying Moments

**Moment 1: The 2 AM Ceiling Math**
The managing partner alone with the numbers. Maximum buying readiness, zero defenses. SC's presence: podcast content, LinkedIn, digital touchpoints that speak to this private anxiety without manufacturing it.

**Moment 2: The Peer Hallway Recommendation**
"I've been using Second Chair and it's actually working." Most efficient buyer conversion. SC's strategy: every satisfied attorney is a systematically activated referral source.

**Moment 3: The Switching Trigger**
The attorney who discovered their "exclusive" leads were sold to three other firms. Active buyer for 2–4 weeks. SC's strategy: content that surfaces during crisis-mode searches.

**Moment 4: The Sales Call That Understands the Practice**
Sasha's call. Demonstrates SC knows what a contingency fee is, why Q1 is slow, what "soft tissue" means. The attorney experiences a peer conversation, not a vendor pitch.

## PART IV: B2C MESSAGE ARCHITECTURE

Messages differentiated by timing tier. The advertising must speak to who the claimant is in that specific moment.

### Tier 1 — Days 0–30 (Decision Fully Open)

**Who:** The working person who had an accident, got a call from the insurance company, isn't sure what to do. Not "a personal injury claimant" in their own mind.

**Recognition:** *"If you were in an accident recently — you'll want to hear this before you respond to the insurance company."*

**Evidence:** *"People who consulted an attorney before accepting an offer received significantly more than those who didn't. One conversation. Ten minutes. No cost."*

**Action:** *"Before you respond to anything — one free call."*

### Tier 2 — 1–6 Months (Accumulation Phase)

**Who:** The claimant who accepted a first offer and wonders if they made a mistake, or is still negotiating with bills accumulating.

**Recognition:** *"Your bills are still coming in. The settlement you received might not cover them."*

**Evidence:** *"Most accident victims who accept the first offer don't know they had other options. Some did. Some still do."*

**Action:** *"Find out where you stand. One call. No commitment required."*

### Tier 3 — 6+ Months to SOL (Deadline Approaching)

**Who:** The claimant who hasn't acted and may not know there's a deadline. Needs a fact, not inspiration.

**Recognition:** *"There's a deadline on your right to pursue a claim. Most people don't know when it is."*

**Evidence (state-specific):** *"In [state], you have [X] years from the date of your accident to file. After that, the option closes."*

**Action:** *"If you've never spoken to an attorney about your accident — find out if you still can."*

### The 99%/1% Mandate

Under Meta Special Ad Categories, demographic and interest targeting is eliminated. Creative is the only targeting mechanism. The advertising must be specific enough that the 1% of people who had a recent accident self-select in, while the 99% who didn't self-select out. This is creative self-selection, not platform targeting.

### What B2C Advertising Must NOT Do
- Manufacture urgency that isn't real
- Overclaim what a lawyer can do
- Talk down to the claimant
- Make them feel like they're being sold something
- Use fear-based hooks or manipulative patterns
- Promise dollar amounts that can't be substantiated

## PART V: BRAND CHARACTER (Locked)

Four traits. Each defined by what it IS and what it IS NOT.

### Trait 1: Expert, Not Arrogant
**Is:** Confident in the craft because the craft is real. Names what has been done and why it matters. Demonstrates something specific competitors can't match.
**Is not:** "Industry-leading," "best-in-class," or "cutting-edge." Does not ask for trust in advance of earning it.
**Sounds like:** *"Here's the advertising. Tell us what you think."*

### Trait 2: Honest in the Specific, Not the Abstract
**Is:** Uses exact numbers, real situations, specific processes. When uncertain, says so.
**Is not:** Values performance. Does not say "we're transparent" — is transparent: shows the advertising, shows the compliance record, shows the CPSC number.
**Sounds like:** *"Your cost-per-signed-case from our leads last quarter was $X. Here's what drove that number."*

### Trait 3: Respectful of Intelligence — Theirs and Ours
**Is:** Treats the attorney as a capable evaluator who has seen every vendor pitch. Speaks the language of case acquisition economics, not marketing.
**Is not:** Selling. No urgency tactics, competitor fear, or scarcity mechanics.
**Sounds like (B2B):** *"What's your current cost-per-signed-case? We'd like to understand the baseline before we make any proposals."*
**Sounds like (B2C):** *"Before you respond to anything, make one call. Ten minutes. Free."*

### Trait 4: Weighted, Not Heavy
**Is:** Serious in register without being stiff. Carries weight because the work has weight. A vendor that speaks like a principal, not a salesperson.
**Is not:** Corporate jargon. Not "disruption." Not "storytelling." Not "zealous advocacy."
**Sounds like:** *"The intake call is the first impression an attorney makes on a potential client. We try to make sure it starts right."*

## PART VI: TONE OF VOICE

### The Core Principle
The attorney who hears Second Chair speak should feel: *this person understands my world and is not trying to manipulate me.* The register is earned peer, not vendor pitch.

### B2B Voice Principles
1. **Fewest words with full weight.** Every extra word is debt.
2. **One idea per sentence.** When it's doing two, split or cut.
3. **Specificity is credibility.** "Our leads convert better" is not credible. A visible, specific difference is.
4. **Write for the attorney who has already been burned.** Don't explain why lead gen matters.
5. **Never end with a question.** State. Don't ask.

### B2C Voice Principles
1. **One specific fact. One specific action.** Not a pitch. Not a story.
2. **Never manufacture urgency that isn't real.** The SOL deadline is real — state it precisely.
3. **The claimant is not a victim who needs rescue.** Write to their agency, not their vulnerability.
4. **Tier-specific. Always.** Each ad must know which timing tier it's speaking to.

### What Second Chair Never Says

**B2B:**
- "We're committed to..." / "We're dedicated to..." / "We're passionate about..."
- "Best-in-class" / "industry-leading" / "cutting-edge" / "innovative"
- "Solution" (standalone noun) / "Ecosystem" / "Synergy" / "Leverage" (as verb)
- "Game-changing" / "revolutionary" / "transformative"
- "We get results" (without immediately specifying what, for whom, how)

**B2C:**
- "You could be owed THOUSANDS"
- "Don't let them get away with it"
- "You may have a case!" (may + exclamation point)
- "Call now before it's too late!" (without genuine imminent deadline)
- "We'll fight for you!" (promise the attorney makes, not the advertising)
- Any dollar amount that can't be specifically substantiated

## PART VII: PROOF POINTS

### Pre-Launch — Provable Now

**Primary proof mechanism:** Second Chair shows the advertising before commitment. No other PI lead gen vendor does this.

**Compliance architecture:**
- TrustedForm full stack on every lead
- Fail-closed DNC/litigator screening
- No shared leads

**Reporting:**
- CPSC as primary metric by default
- Dual scoring engine: Conversion Score and Case Value Score

**Founding team credentials (verifiable):**
- Davis Ballard: 18+ years across Disney, Google, Samsung, NFL, NBA, Uber, Taco Bell, The Macallan, Optum
- Alex DuBelko: TradeStation mobile platform (1M users, FINRA/SEC compliance), Compass, Breakwater Management ($550M+)
- Sasha Zinshtein: 14+ years programmatic media, 18+ Fortune 500 brands at 33across, restricted-category advertising at The Flower Agency, financial underwriting at Riverpoint Capital

**Month-to-month terms.** No long-term contracts required.

### Post-Launch — Format to Be Populated
- Average CPSC by DMA and case type
- Intake conversion rate: SC leads vs. prior vendor (90-day and 6-month marks)
- TrustedForm compliance rate (target: 100%)
- Attorney retention rate at 3, 6, 12 months
- Named references per active DMA

---

## PART VIII: THE NON-NEGOTIABLES

1. Never runs misleading advertising
2. Never shares leads between attorneys
3. Never hides the advertising from attorney partners
4. Never reports CPL as the primary metric
5. Never takes a partner whose intake practices contradict what the advertising promises
6. Never pitches volume as the primary value
7. Never uses fear, artificial urgency, or manufactured scarcity in B2C advertising
8. Never claims to be something it isn't — yet
