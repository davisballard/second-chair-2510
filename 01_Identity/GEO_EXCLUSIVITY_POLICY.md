# Geo-Exclusivity Policy

> **Type:** Internal policy — not for external distribution  
> **Status:** Active
> **Date:** March 2026 (updated from February 2026)

---

## The Policy

**Market (Designated Market Area) is the standard territory unit.**

Clients are sold geo-exclusivity at the Market level — one PI law firm per Market. No competing firm in that Market can become a Second Chair client while a firm holds that territory.

**No exceptions. No grandfathered arrangements. One firm, one Market.**

---

## Current Client Roster

Second Chair is launching with zero active clients. There are no legacy arrangements, no inherited exclusivity deals, and no grandfathered exceptions to honor. Every territory is open.

## The Three Product Tiers

### Tier 1 — Standard: Per-Market Exclusivity
One firm locks one Market. Pricing is set by the **Market Operating Index (MOI)** — a composite score (1.0–10.0) based on six weighted factors: competition & media cost (25%), legal viability rate (25%), market size (15%), case value competition (15%), uninsured motorist rate (10%), and market quality x-factors (10%).

The MOI maps linearly to lead price:

| MOI Score Range | Lead Price Range | Markets |
|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| **8.0–10.0** | $425–$500 | NYC ($460), LA ($425), Miami ($420) |
| **6.0–7.9** | $355–$425 | Chicago ($390), Orlando ($385), SF ($380), Philly ($375), Houston/Dallas ($365), Atlanta ($360) |
| **4.0–5.9** | $285–$355 | Riverside ($340), San Diego ($340), Seattle ($335), Phoenix ($315), Sacramento ($310), San Antonio/Austin ($300) |
| **2.0–3.9** | $210–$285 | Fresno ($270), McAllen/El Paso ($270), Bakersfield ($260), Rockford ($260), Tucson/Spokane ($255), Peoria ($250) |

Full methodology and per-Market scoring: `07_Research/02_Sales_Intelligence/Market_Analysis/Market_Baseline_Pricing_Framework.md`

### Tier 2 — Bundle: Multi-Market or Regional
For firms with multiple offices or regional reach — sell them the specific Markets they operate in, bundled:
- 2–3 Markets in the same state: 10% discount vs. individual Market pricing
- All Markets in a state: 15% discount vs. individual Market pricing
- Each Market must be explicitly named in the contract

### Tier 3 — Enterprise: Statewide or Multi-State (Exception Only)
For firms that genuinely run statewide campaigns and want every lead in a state — this exists but is a premium exception, not the standard:
- Priced as the sum of all Markets in that state, minus a 10–15% bundle discount
- Never a flat per-state fee (avoids the trap of pricing large and small states identically)
- Minimum 12-month contract; annual review
- Requires explicit approval — statewide deals should be rare and deliberate

## What to Say on the Website / FAQ

Keep it vague. Do not explain the Market model in detail publicly. Suggested language:

> "We offer territory-exclusive arrangements — per Market, per state, or custom — depending on the nature of the deal. One firm per territory, always."

Or for an FAQ:

> "How is your exclusivity structured?"  
> "We offer flexible territory arrangements — by Market, by state, or custom — based on the size and scope of a firm's market. Our team will walk you through what makes sense for your geography on a call."

---

## What to Say on Sales Calls

This is where you get specific. The discovery question that opens it:

> "Tell me about the markets you operate in. Are you running in one city, a few cities, or statewide?"

From there:
- **Single city / one Market:** "We'd lock the [City] Market for you. Here's what that means and what it costs."
- **Multiple cities, same state:** "We'd lock the specific Markets where you operate. Here's the bundle pricing."
- **Statewide:** "We do offer statewide arrangements, but it's priced as the sum of the Markets in that state. Let me show you what that looks like for [State]."

**The key distinction to name in every call:**

> "Most lead gen companies sell exclusive leads — meaning they won't sell the same contact twice. We sell exclusive territory — meaning no competing firm in your Market can buy from us at all. Two firms in the same city can both be clients of another lead gen company. Only one can be a Second Chair client."

## Why Market, Not MSA or ZIP or State

| Unit | Why Not Standard |
|------|-----------------|
| ZIP code | Too granular — PI firms serve full metro areas, not ZIP codes |
| County | Better, but not how attorneys think about their markets |
| MSA | More granular than Market (382 vs. 210 nationally) but less intuitive to PI attorneys |
| State | Too broad — caps us at 50 clients nationally; blocks markets firm never serves |
| **Market** | **How PI firms already think about and buy media (TV, radio, CTV, streaming). Published Nielsen map = unambiguous boundaries. 210 markets nationally = real scale.** |

## Revenue Ceiling Comparison (Why This Matters)

| Territory Unit | Max US Clients | ARR Ceiling |
|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| Per State | 50 | $40M–$60M |
| Per Market | 210 | $84M–$168M |
| Per MSA | 382 | $115M–$248M |

The Market model opens the revenue ceiling to 2–3x the per-state model while keeping territory language that clients immediately understand.
